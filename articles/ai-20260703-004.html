Offshore wind curtailment signals highlight grid integration gaps

Operational signals from balancing areas show localized curtailment and dispatch friction for offshore wind, underscoring transmission bottlenecks and the need for faster dynamic reactive support and HVDC coordination.

Field indicators suggest curtailment events are clustering when multiple offshore arrays ramp together into constrained coastal nodes. These conditions expose limits in existing reactive compensation and synchronous inertia resources. Expectations include accelerated interest in HVDC links with coordinated control, grid-forming inverters and large synchronous condensers in coastal reinforcement projects. Scenario watch: sustained curtailment could pressure developer returns and shift project economics toward hybridization with storage. What to monitor: scheduling patterns, reactive power shortfalls during ramp events and HVDC commissioning timelines. Grid operators are signaling clearer needs for control protocols that accommodate high offshore ramp rates. The medium-term remedy set centers on targeted transmission reinforcements and faster commissioning of dynamic grid support assets.

What to watch
  • HVDC commissioning schedules and control coordination tests
  • reactive power margins during offshore ramp periods
Action

Prioritize reactive support and HVDC coordination in offshore project studies to reduce curtailment exposure.
