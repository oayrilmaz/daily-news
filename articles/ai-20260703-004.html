Renewable output and curtailment risk amid interconnector constraints

Expectations of increasing curtailment episodes are emerging as variable renewables ramp and cross-border flows interact with limited interconnector capacity, posing operational and commercial stress points.

Periods of high wind or solar generation can expose bottlenecks in cross-border and regional transmission capacity. These episodes create downward pressure on wholesale prices and force curtailment as a stability tool. Scenario watch: persistent curtailment could shift project revenue models and accelerate storage co-location to capture otherwise lost energy. What to monitor are interconnector flow patterns, transmission outage schedules, and price dispersion between adjacent market areas. Policymakers may consider allocation mechanisms or targeted reinforcement to reduce systemic exposure, but permitting and construction timelines stretch years. For project developers, storage or flexible demand integration becomes a more attractive hedge. Operationally, enhanced forecasting and dynamic curtailment rules may be piloted to reduce system-level costs. Market participants should update revenue sensitivities to account for higher curtailment probabilities.

What to watch
  • interconnector flow and outage notices
  • market price spreads between regions during high renewable output
Action

Stress-test project revenue under higher curtailment scenarios and prioritize storage or flexibility options.
