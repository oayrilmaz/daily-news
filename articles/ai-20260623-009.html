Interconnector congestion scenarios may reshape cross-border flows

Signals point to episodic congestion on key interconnectors as renewable patterns shift, producing scenarios where capacity allocation and congestion pricing will be pivotal for regional supply security.

Cross-border flows have become more dynamic with asymmetric generation profiles across regions, raising the likelihood of congestion during high-VRE conditions. Expectations include price differentials that incentivize storage and demand response on either side of bottlenecks. Scenario watch: persistent congestion could prompt reallocation of capacity rights, updated scheduling procedures, or expedited reinforcement projects. What to monitor are interconnector utilization reports, congestion rent patterns, and redispatch notices. Operators may need to increase coordination on forecasting and market coupling to mitigate unexpected imbalances. The intelligence posture is that short-term market signals will drive investment decisions in flexibility and transmission reinforcement.

What to watch
  • Interconnector utilization and congestion rents
  • Redispatch and countertrading notices
Action

Assess exposure to cross-border congestion and evaluate flexibility investments to hedge redispatch risk.
