HV switchgear manufacturing bottlenecks keep lead-time risk high

Signals indicate persistent lead-time variability for HV switchgear driven by production capacity and component sourcing, creating a scenario where project schedules may slip without proactive procurement strategies.

EPCs and asset owners continue to flag longer and less-predictable lead times for gas-insulated and air-insulated switchgear assemblies. Manufacturing capacity constraints and specialized component backlogs are recurring themes. Some firms are prioritizing high-margin or long-term OEM customers, which can leave project buyers with limited negotiating leverage. The scenario risk is amplified for bespoke designs or integration-heavy packages that require factory acceptance testing and field commissioning windows. To mitigate, project teams are exploring standardization, early procurement, and modular assembly approaches. Suppliers that can offer inventory buffers or accelerated production slots will capture premium demand. Projects that cannot accommodate alternative equipment specifications may face schedule and cost escalation. Monitor supplier communications and consider staged delivery strategies where critical path items are prioritized.

What to watch
  • OEM capacity statements
  • project change orders citing lead times
Action

Lock critical-equipment procurements early and evaluate alternate specifications to reduce schedule exposure.
