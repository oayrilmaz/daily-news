AI-driven predictive maintenance adoption accelerates for HV assets

Signals indicate OEMs and EPCs are accelerating pilots of AI-based predictive maintenance for high-voltage transformers and switchgear, aiming to reduce forced outage risk and optimize spare parts logistics.

Engineering teams are integrating condition-monitoring feeds like dissolved gas analysis, temperature, and vibration into machine-learning models to predict failure windows. Pilot programs are reporting improved lead indicators for insulating oil degradation and bushing issues. EPC procurement strategies are being revised to incorporate data-driven replacement prioritization rather than calendar-based plans. There is active discussion on data ownership and compatibility between OEM diagnostic platforms and utility asset management systems. Cybersecurity and model governance are becoming central to contract language as analytics take on operational influence. Some pilots are demonstrating reduced unplanned downtime in short samples, but scalability questions remain. Capital planners are evaluating whether predictive insights justify revisions to spare inventories and service contracts. The effect could be a shift from reactive inventory stocking to just-in-time logistics for critical components.

What to watch
  • Pilot performance reports and scalability studies from OEM/EPC programs
  • Contractual language updates addressing data ownership and cybersecurity
Action

Pilot analytics on a narrow asset class and assess integration and governance implications before wide rollout.
