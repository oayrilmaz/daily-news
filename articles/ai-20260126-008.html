Modular HV equipment and factory automation reshape EPC risk allocations

Signals point toward greater adoption of factory-built, modular high-voltage skids to shorten field work, shifting risk from on-site integration to factory QA and supply-chain resilience.

EPCs are increasingly specifying modular HV substations to accelerate commissioning and reduce weather and labor exposure. Expectations include standardized interfaces and reduced site civil scopes. There is a scenario where factory defects or shipping disruptions concentrate risk at earlier stages and create complex contractual disputes. OEMs are investing in digital twins and automated testing to reduce variability. Insurers and lenders may demand more robust factory QA evidence before funding. What to monitor are warranty structures, acceptance testing protocols, and logistics contingency plans.

What to watch
  • Factory QA documentation, digital-twin test records, and enhanced acceptance protocols.
  • Logistics contingencies and insurance terms for ocean and overland transport of modular skids.
Action

Include factory acceptance criteria and transport contingencies in contracts to transfer and mitigate concentrated risks.
