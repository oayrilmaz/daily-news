Manufacturing automation seen as mitigation to equipment lead times

Expectations are that increased automation and modular assembly in high-voltage equipment manufacturing could reduce lead times, but adoption will depend on capital investment cycles and certification processes.

Signals from procurement planners indicate interest in factory modernization that emphasizes modular builds and plug-and-play substation components. Automation promises consistent quality and potentially faster throughput for core components, but requires significant up-front capital expenditure. Certification and type-testing cycles for new modular designs are time-consuming and create a scenario drag on rapid scale-up. OEMs and EPCs are assessing where standardization yields the most schedule benefit without compromising grid compatibility. Labor market shifts are also influencing the calculus, as automation reduces skilled assembly demand while increasing requirements for robotics and control experts. Scenario-watch: pilot factory lines with modular products could demonstrate lead-time reductions within 12–24 months if certification pathways are prioritized.

What to watch
  • Pilot modular substation product announcements
  • Type-testing and certification timelines for new designs
Action

Engage with OEMs on modular options and clarify certification requirements early to capture potential lead-time benefits.
