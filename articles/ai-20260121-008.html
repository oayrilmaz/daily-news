HV switchgear and semiconductor lead times shaping EPC sequencing

Signals of prolonged lead times for HV switchgear components and power-semiconductor modules are prompting EPCs to reconsider sequencing and prequalification for multi-site builds.

Manufacturing capacity constraints for certain switchgear classes and power semiconductors are creating scenarios where late procurement risks delay entire scopes. EPC teams are experimenting with parallel procurement and early critical-equipment long-lead buys to de-risk schedules. Qualification of second-source vendors is adding procurement overhead. There is a growing expectation that modular-build approaches will be used to isolate delayed components without halting entire projects. Warranties and acceptance testing are being revisited to account for diverse component origins. OEMs are under pressure to increase transparency on production ramp plans and to offer better visibility into allocation frameworks. This dynamic may favor developers who can commit to volume offtakes or longer-term supply agreements. Strategic inventory strategies and finance for inventory carry are becoming part of bid models.

What to watch
  • monitor vendor allocation notices for long-lead HV equipment
  • track qualification timelines for alternative suppliers
Action

Lock critical-equipment procurements early and build alternative-vendor qualification into project plans.
