Curtailment signals rise where new solar pairs with constrained transmission

Signals point to increasing curtailment risk in systems where rapid solar-plus-storage additions outpace transmission reinforcement, prompting market and dispatch friction.

Rapid deployment of solar plant clusters, often with co-located storage, is improving firming potential but is also concentrating export needs on limited corridors. Operators are testing intra-day dispatch arrangements that prioritize storage cycling for market revenue over local stability under specific scenarios. Expectations include temporary curtailment windows during flexibility shortages and higher ancillary service demand to manage intra-hour variability. Policy choices that accelerate local grid upgrades or incent distributed storage will materially change the curtailment profile across affected regions. Scenario watch includes temporary revenue impacts for project owners and potential renegotiation of interconnection agreements when congestion persists. What to monitor are transmission outage schedules, curtailment notices to generators, and regulatory moves on congestion management and queue reforms.

What to watch
  • generator curtailment notices
  • transmission outage postings
Action

Map export corridors for new projects and include curtailment risk clauses in commercial contracts.
