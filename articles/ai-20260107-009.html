Distribution operators exploring dynamic line ratings and real-time congestion management

Signals show distribution operators are piloting dynamic line ratings and real-time congestion tools to extract latent capacity on existing assets.

Pilots appear aimed at reducing the need for expensive reinforcement by leveraging weather and conductor thermal models. Expect deployments to target constrained feeders that service rapid load growth or distributed generation pockets. Scenario watch includes coordination challenges between distribution operators and transmission planners as boundary conditions change more frequently. Benefits may include deferred capital expenditure and increased hosting capacity for renewables and EV charging. Reliable sensor networks and validated models are prerequisites to scale these programs safely. Cybersecurity and data governance will become more prominent as operational data is used to influence switching and re-dispatch decisions.

What to watch
  • Pilot results on hosting capacity increases
  • Agreements between distribution and transmission planners
Action

Identify feeders where dynamic ratings could defer upgrades and begin sensor readiness assessments.
