HV testing standard divergence and interoperability scenarios

Signals from procurement and test labs indicate expectations that divergent testing standards for HV equipment across regions may create interoperability and warranty risk for projects with multinational supply chains.

HV equipment being sourced from multiple jurisdictions can be subject to differing test regimes and certification thresholds. Divergent acceptance criteria increase the likelihood of rework at commissioning or warranty disputes post-installation. Project owners and EPCs need to harmonize requirements early and require vendor attestations against project-specific test matrices. Interoperability issues are particularly acute for protection relays, instrument transformers, and control communications. Testing capacity constraints in some regions can also delay project milestones if re-testing is required. Mitigation scenarios include third-party witness testing, pre-shipment factory acceptance expansions, and clearer contractual testing obligations. This is a scenario assessment rather than a statement that specific equipment will fail interoperability tests.

What to watch
  • Vendor test certificate variations
  • Factory acceptance test schedules
  • Protection and control interoperability checks
Action

Define project-specific test matrices in procurement documents and require vendor attestations to reduce interoperability risk.
