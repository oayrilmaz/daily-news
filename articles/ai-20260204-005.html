Ancillary services tightening as renewable penetration grows

Market signals point toward increasing value for fast-response ancillary services as system inertia changes with higher renewable shares, creating scenarios where new market products and procurement methods may emerge.

System operators appear to be recalibrating ancillary-service requirements to reflect lower synchronous inertia in several grids. Fast frequency response and synthetic inertia are becoming more valuable to grid stability. Market operators are experimenting with shorter procurement windows and finer temporal granularity to attract battery and inverter-based resources. Price signals for contingency reserves have shown episodic spikes in simulations and test markets. Traders and asset owners are repositioning portfolios to capture these services, altering traditional merchant generation valuations. The near-term scenario includes more frequent market interventions and evolving settlement rules. What to monitor are rule-change proposals and pilot program results for sub-second services.

What to watch
  • rule-change filings for ancillary services and pilot program announcements
  • price movement and activation frequency in fast-response markets
Action

Assess asset eligibility for fast-response market products and update valuation models accordingly.
