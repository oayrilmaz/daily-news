OEMs signal wider AI deployment for predictive maintenance amid workforce gaps

Signals of accelerated AI adoption for predictive maintenance and asset health are tempered by persistent skilled labor shortages and integration complexity for EPCs and OEMs.

Vendors are increasingly positioning AI-enabled analytics as a way to extend asset life and reduce unplanned outages. Early deployments are focused on transformers, circuit breakers, and rotating equipment where sensor density is increasing. Integration challenges remain, particularly around data normalization and vendor interoperability. Scenario watch: organizations that accelerate sensor deployments and data governance will capture maintenance efficiencies faster than those with fragmented data estates. Workforce shortages mean OEMs are also investing in automated manufacturing and remote commissioning tools. Customers are demanding clearer ROI cases and standardized APIs to reduce integration risk. Expect a wave of partnership-level collaborations between analytics providers and equipment manufacturers. The near-term competitive edge will go to suppliers who can combine domain expertise with scalable AI operations.

What to watch
  • deployments that publish clear ROI or pilot results
  • announcements of standardized data APIs or joint vendor integrations
Action

Prioritize data governance and pilot partnerships to validate AI maintenance use cases before scaling.
