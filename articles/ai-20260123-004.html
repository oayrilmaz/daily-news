Transmission bottlenecks drive localized solar curtailment signals

Operational signals from several Asian grids point to persistent solar curtailment driven by limited long-distance transmission capacity and lagging queue management reforms.

New large-scale solar plus storage projects are constrained by congestion on critical export corridors that remain underbuilt. Curtailment rates are rising in certain provinces and are beginning to show up in project revenue models and developer planning assumptions. Grid operators are experimenting with dynamic dispatch and localized storage aggregation to reduce spill, but scale remains limited. Interconnection queues are long and processes for queue prioritization are a source of developer uncertainty and capital scheduling risk. Policy and market interventions being discussed could shift curtailment economics but are likely to take months to implement. Scenario watch: if storage deployments do not accelerate, developers may increasingly seek merchant deals in nearby load centers or accept lower capacity factors.

What to watch
  • Curtailment statistics reported by regional grid operators over the next 72 hours.
  • Policy statements on interconnection queue reform or transmission build acceleration.
Action

Factor curtailment risk into ROI models for new solar projects and prioritize interconnection risk mitigation in site selection.
