Ancillary service pricing scenarios shift as storage and demand response enter markets

Expectations are that increasing storage and aggregated demand response participation is reshaping ancillary service price dynamics, creating scenario effects on procurement and asset valuation.

As more storage assets bid into frequency and fast-ramping products, price formation for those services may become more competitive and volatile. Aggregated demand response can provide similar capabilities at different costs and contractual structures. The scenario consequence is a potential compression of margins for legacy peaker plants and new opportunities for flexible assets. Market operators may recalibrate minimum bid sizes or accreditation rules to manage reliability and participation fairness. For project financiers, changes to ancillary revenue expectations affect asset returns and contract structures. Short-term signals include clearing price movements in fast-response products and rule changes on aggregation. The likely near-term outcome is a period of adjustment as market rules catch up with new resource capabilities.

What to watch
  • clearing prices for fast-response ancillary services
  • regulatory changes on aggregation and accreditation of flexible resources
Action

Revisit ancillary service revenue models and test sensitivity to increased competition from storage and DR resources.
