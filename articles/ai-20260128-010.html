What to monitor: microgrid integration and diesel phase-out signals in Latin America

Project planners in parts of Latin America are signaling increased interest in integrated microgrids combining storage, renewables, and controls as a pathway to reduce diesel reliance in data centers and critical facilities.

Stakeholders are focused on resilience given grid reliability concerns in certain regions. Hybrid microgrids are being evaluated both for cost benefits and emissions targets. Fuel-cycle economics for diesel versus renewable-plus-storage are being re-run under different fuel-price scenarios. Local regulations and interconnection rules are significant determinants of feasibility. Developers are also testing demand-management strategies to lower peak consumption and reduce backup duration needs. Grid services monetization, where available, is being included in business cases for microgrids. The primary implementation frictions are financing structures and permitting for on-site generation. If pilots demonstrate reliable operations, scaling could accelerate across new and retrofit builds.

What to watch
  • pilot microgrid commissioning results and runtime hours
  • changes in interconnection rules or incentives for on-site generation
Action

Evaluate microgrid pilots and refine business-case models incorporating grid service revenues and local regulatory constraints.
