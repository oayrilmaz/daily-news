Generative AI pilots for asset management invite both opportunity and model-risk

Signals indicate widespread piloting of generative and predictive AI tools for maintenance planning, but there is an expectation of model drift and false-positive risks that could create operational and contractual friction if not managed.

Generative AI is being trialed to synthesize field reports and suggest workpacks for technicians. These trials are signals of potential efficiency gains in documentation and planning.

However, model outputs can hallucinate or overconfidently assert root causes, which creates a scenario where unvetted recommendations could lead to misdiagnosis and unnecessary interventions.

Asset owners are expected to implement layered validation, combining AI outputs with rule-based checks and expert review. This hybrid approach is likely to be a common mitigation strategy.

Contracts with vendors will need clauses addressing liability, explainability, and performance thresholds for AI-generated recommendations. Procurement teams should anticipate negotiation on these points.

Operational integration will require clear audit trails and versioning of model logic to trace decisions during outages or warranty disputes. That is an expectation for robust deployment governance.

What to monitor are pilot performance metrics, vendor transparency on training data and validation protocols, and any incident reports linked to AI-driven maintenance actions.

What to watch
  • pilot metrics and incident reports tied to AI recommendations
  • vendor disclosures on model training and validation
Action

Require human-in-the-loop validation and include explicit performance and liability terms when contracting AI maintenance tools.
