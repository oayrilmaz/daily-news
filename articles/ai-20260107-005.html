Capacity market friction as retirements outpace builds

Signals suggest capacity market tensions are rising where thermal retirements are outpacing near-term sustained capacity additions, potentially stressing reserve margins and price signals.

Market dispatch and capacity auction results indicate tightening reserve expectations in some regions. Retirement announcements for older thermal plants are accelerating capacity attrition in specific zones. New-build timelines for renewables and storage often lag interconnection and permitting, creating timing gaps. This dynamic can push locational marginal prices and capacity payments higher in constrained nodes. Contract negotiations for generation and storage projects are showing premium pricing for deliverability. Regulators may face pressure to adjust market rules or fast-track capacity procurements. Scenario responses include short-term capacity contracts and demand response expansions. Market participants should reassess exposure to node-level price volatility.

What to watch
  • Auction outcomes and cleared capacity prices
  • Announcements of fast-track procurements or rule changes
Action

Stress-test contract portfolios against higher locational capacity prices and evaluate demand response as an alternative capacity source.
