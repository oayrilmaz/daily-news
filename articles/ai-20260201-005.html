Capacity and ancillary markets signal tightening ahead of seasonal peaks

Market indicators suggest potential tightening in capacity and ancillary service markets ahead of seasonal peaks, creating expectations of elevated price volatility and stress tests for reliability constructs.

Price signals in forward capacity auctions and ancillary services are being watched for indications of stressed margins. Regions that rely on thermal retirements and limited demand response capacity are particularly exposed. The interplay of inverter-based resources and traditional synchronous inertia is affecting ancillary requirements and procurement strategies. Market operators may need to accelerate reforms to incent fast-ramping resources and synthetic inertia solutions. Retail contracts and wholesale price pass-throughs could expose vulnerable consumers to spikes. Scenario watch: if scarcity pricing events occur, expect expedited regulatory dialogue and potential temporary market rule adjustments to preserve reliability.

What to watch
  • Forward auction price movements and scarcity price triggers
  • Announcements of emergency market rule changes or additional procurements
Action

Model exposure to ancillary price volatility and consider contracting fast-ramping or storage assets to hedge capacity risk.
