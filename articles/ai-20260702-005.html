Short-term price volatility and settlement risks

Market signals point to a potential confluence of supply-side stress and extreme weather forecasts that could amplify intraday price swings and create settlement exposure for hedged portfolios.

When forecast uncertainty rises, market participants often tighten positions and reduce liquidity, which magnifies price moves. Settlement mechanisms may expose retailers and aggregators to basis risk if real-time prices diverge sharply from day-ahead expectations. Portfolio hedges that do not account for distribution-level congestion may underperform during localized stress events. Counterparty credit exposure increases when volatility forces margin calls and liquidity pressures across trading desks. Smaller market participants can be squeezed by extreme spreads and the operational costs of rapid rebalancing. Scenario planning for traders should include stress tests that model correlated outages and weather-driven demand surges. Regulators and market operators may consider temporary rule changes to manage extreme events, affecting liquidity further. This is a watch for treasury and risk teams to review intraday exposure and contact counterparties about contingency positions.

What to watch
  • intraday price spikes and liquidity drops
  • margin call notifications
Action

Run intraday stress tests on hedged portfolios and confirm available liquidity for margin calls.
