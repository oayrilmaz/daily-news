HVDC corridor planning faces scenario-based interconnection trade-offs

Scenario analysis suggests planners are reassessing HVDC corridor priorities as long-distance flows interact with local flexibility resources and evolving market rules.

Planners are running scenarios that weigh the benefits of large bulk transfers against investments in distributed flexibility and storage. Scenario watch: in some modeled futures the marginal value of new HVDC capacity falls if localized BESS and demand response scale faster than anticipated. What to monitor are capacity allocation proposals and cost-benefit assumptions used in recent planning rounds. Project timelines and financing are sensitive to these scenario outcomes, which could shift attention to hybrid solutions that combine HVDC with strategically placed storage. Interoperability of control schemes across AC-DC interfaces is a technical risk to be validated. If policy and market signals favor decentralized resources, corridor timing and scale may be revised down. Conversely, if cross-border reliability concerns dominate, HVDC could remain a prioritized build.

What to watch
  • planning assumptions on storage uptake and demand response
  • interconnection cost-benefit analyses that affect corridor sizing
Action

Run scenario sensitivity on corridor economics that vary storage and DR penetration to inform design flexibility.
