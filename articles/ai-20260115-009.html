Expectation: HVDC interconnector projects face execution complexity and stakeholder pushback

Signals from planning corridors and stakeholder engagements suggest HVDC interconnector projects carry elevated execution risk related to siting, permitting, and marine logistics, potentially delaying capacity needed to balance renewables.

Project proponents are encountering complex stakeholder landscapes that can extend timelines beyond initial expectations. Marine route availability and cable-laying windows remain constrained, creating seasonal execution windows that can slip. Onshore converter station siting often triggers local permitting challenges and environmental reviews that increase schedule risk. The combination of long lead-time components and narrow installation windows forms a scenario where interconnector availability lags renewable capacity growth. Cost escalation risk is heightened if vessels and specialized contractors are double-booked. System operators may need interim solutions to manage cross-border flows and congestion. Financing structures are being stress-tested for protracted delivery timelines. Expect increased focus on contingency planning and staged commissioning approaches.

What to watch
  • permits or consultations delaying converter station siting
  • cable-laying vessel availability notices
Action

Incorporate seasonal installation windows and vessel availability into interconnector schedules and funding plans.
