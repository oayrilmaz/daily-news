Growing demand signals for HVDC and FACTS in interconnection projects

Signals from grid planning forums indicate rising interest in HVDC and flexible AC transmission system (FACTS) technology for long-distance interconnections and renewables export, creating a scenario that will affect procurement, skills needs, and system design.

Planners are evaluating HVDC links to transport bulk renewable energy across long distances with lower losses and controlled flows. FACTS devices are being considered to manage power quality and dynamic stability where high renewable penetration exists. This technology tilt will require specialized procurement channels and skilled installation and commissioning teams. For vendors this represents an opportunity but also raises pressure to certify equipment to local standards and to maintain long-term service contracts. System operators will need updated protection schemes and operator training for converter-based assets. Financing structures may also change since HVDC involves different risk profiles than AC-only projects. What to monitor: procurement specifications for converter stations, local workforce training initiatives, and updates to protection and control standards.

What to watch
  • Procurement specs for converter stations
  • Local training initiatives for HVDC/FACTS commissioning
Action

Begin aligning procurement and training plans to account for converter-based transmission and FACTS integration needs.
