Interconnection and desalination demand create new load-flexibility scenarios

Signals of growing interconnection projects and expanding desalination loads suggest operators will need to model flexible supply scenarios that balance water-security demand with variable renewable integration.

Large industrial loads such as desalination present new scheduling and flexibility challenges for grid planners. Expectations include greater use of demand-side flexibility contracts and time-of-use scheduling to manage peak desalination draws. Interconnector projects create opportunities to smooth variability but also expose systems to cross-border contingency risk. Scenario planning should include fuel-switching options for desalination plants and the potential for on-site storage or captive generation. EPC scopes for desalination-linked power may expand to include hybrid renewable-plus-storage designs to reduce grid reliance. Operators and regulators will need clear protocols for prioritizing water versus electricity security during system stress events. Monitor procurement notices and interconnector commissioning updates for implications to local dispatch and reserve planning.

What to watch
  • power contracts tied to desalination operations
  • interconnector commissioning milestones
Action

Model industrial load flexibility options and evaluate hybrid on-site supply designs for desalination-linked projects.
