Cyber hygiene signals show gaps in remote access management

Signals collected from vendor and operator communities indicate recurring gaps in remote access controls for substations, creating expectations of elevated cyber risk in automated maintenance and vendor support workflows.

Remote access for diagnostics and firmware management is widespread for modern substations. There is an expectation that legacy VPNs and shared credentials persist in some environments. Scenario watch: exploitation of weak remote access pathways could affect both operational visibility and protection systems. What to monitor are privileged account inventories, third-party access certificates, and MFA adoption rates. Operators are being urged to adopt zero-trust micro-segmentation and strict vendor session auditing. Some pragmatic mitigations include jump servers with session recording and granular authorization. Short-term audits of remote access logs are recommended to surface anomalous patterns.

What to watch
  • Third-party access certificate expirations
  • Adoption rates of MFA and session recording
Action

Inventory remote access pathways, enforce MFA, and implement session recording for vendor connections.
