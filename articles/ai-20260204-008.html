EPC risk management shifts toward fixed-price scope and modular delivery

Signals from contracts and tendering activity suggest EPCs are favoring fixed-price scopes and modular delivery frameworks to manage supply and schedule risk.

EPCs are increasingly proposing phased construction and modular substation elements to mitigate long-lead equipment uncertainty. The expectation is a greater adoption of risk-sharing clauses and milestone-based payments to align incentives with suppliers. Scenario watch: tighter fixed-price contracts could transfer production and logistics risk back to manufacturers, potentially increasing disputes if backlogs persist. What to monitor are escalation clause insertions, performance guarantees, and arbitration frequency in recent contract templates. For owners and financiers, balancing fixed-price protection with manufacturer capacity risk will remain a key diligence area before award.

What to watch
  • inclusion of escalation and force majeure clauses in new contracts
  • spikes in contract amendments tied to manufacturer delays
Action

Reassess contract terms to ensure balanced risk allocation and consider staged acceptance criteria for modular deliveries.
