Supply-chain tension for HV switchgear and SiC power modules signals delivery risks

Procurement patterns and supplier commentaries are signaling continued tension in high-voltage switchgear deliveries and silicon-carbide module availability, framing potential delivery risks for near-term projects.

Manufacturing lead times for large SF6 and SF6-alternative breakers appear extended in several markets. Intelligence scenarios point to bottlenecks in insulation materials and precision machining capacity. On the power-electronics side, demand for SiC IGBTs is outpacing some fabrication and packaging roadmaps. EPCs may face milestone slips if alternative sourcing is not validated early. What to monitor are RFQ language changes indicating acceptance of equivalent technologies and escalation clauses in supplier contracts. The expectation is selective prioritization by OEMs toward larger contracts, leaving smaller projects to accept longer lead times or substitute technologies. This dynamic could increase project carry costs and complicate commissioning schedules.

What to watch
  • RFQ terms accepting alternate breaker technologies
  • Supplier lead-time change notices and prioritization policies
Action

Lock down critical long-lead procurements now and qualify alternate suppliers to mitigate delivery risk.
