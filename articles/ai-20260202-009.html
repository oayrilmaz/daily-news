HVDC interconnector stress scenarios as asynchronous flows and market signals interact

Signals suggest increased scenario complexity for HVDC interconnectors as asynchronous flows driven by price arbitrage and renewables variability could produce protection coordination and thermal management stress in peak windows.

HVDC links are crucial for cross-border balancing, but growing arbitrage activity can drive flow patterns that are less predictable than traditional scheduled transfers. This increases the need for dynamic thermal ratings and faster coordination protocols.

Protection coordination across converter stations must account for rapid reversals and converter control mode interactions. Unexpected interactions between converter control loops can create transient stability concerns.

Thermal management issues arise when flow patterns concentrate in narrow time windows, stressing cable and converter cooling systems. These systems were often specified for more stable duty cycles.

Scenario planning should include high-frequency market-induced flow reversals and simultaneous renewable ramps in adjacent areas. That combination increases the likelihood of converter derating or protective trips.

Signals include more frequent discretionary curtailments, dynamic rating adjustments, and vendor advisories about converter performance under fast reversals. These are indicators to watch rather than confirmed faults.

Operational mitigations include conservative dynamic rating policies and pre-established emergency coordination procedures with adjacent control areas.

What to watch
  • Frequency of dynamic rating adjustments and discretionary curtailments on interconnectors.
  • Converter vendor advisories about performance under fast flow reversals.
Action

Tighten dynamic rating policies and rehearse coordination protocols for rapid flow reversal scenarios with counterparty operators.
