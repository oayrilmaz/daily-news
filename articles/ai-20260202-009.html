Cross-border HVDC projects present operational and geopolitical scenario risks

Expectations around new HVDC interconnectors include benefits for balancing renewables, but signals suggest careful scenario planning for operational and geopolitical contingencies.

HVDC corridors are being considered to unlock distant renewable zones, but such projects create centralized dependency points that merit detailed contingency planning. Operators and planners are reportedly running joint procedures to define control handovers and emergency response for multi-jurisdictional faults. Scenario watch includes asymmetric operating policies or political friction that could complicate dispatch coordination during stress events. Technical interoperability—control protocols, converter station protection, and communication resilience—remains a focal area for risk reduction. Expectations are that contractual mechanisms and automated boundary controls will be tested to ensure stable operation under varying conditions. What to monitor are progress updates on converter station testing, cross-border operation agreements, and any diplomatic statements that would alter project timelines.

What to watch
  • converter station test results
  • cross-border operation agreements
Action

Establish joint operational playbooks and contractual outage protocols for cross-border HVDC projects.
