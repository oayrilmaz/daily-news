HVDC converter station staffing and timeline risks

Signals suggest risks to HVDC converter station timelines driven by shortages of skilled installation crews and specialist electrical engineers in key regions.

HVDC projects rely on niche skills for converter installation, cooling systems, and harmonic filtering configurations. Where labor markets are tight, mobilization windows can slip and increase charter and accommodation costs for specialist crews. Training pipelines for these skill sets remain limited, and relocation hurdles can slow workforce scaling. Project owners are exploring modularization and factory-assembled converter skids to reduce on-site labor intensity. However, regulatory and permitting frameworks may not be adapted to high degrees of modular delivery. The watch scenario is delayed energization windows that force re-sequencing of interconnected AC upgrades and extend temporary grid security arrangements.

What to watch
  • Availability of specialist converter installation crews
  • Permitting acceptance of modular converter deliveries
Action

Assess installation labor sourcing and consider modular assembly options while confirming local permitting for skid deliveries.
