Utility-scale solar-plus-storage market shaping in Latin America

Signals point to a scenario where utility-scale solar-plus-storage projects in Latin America are being structured to capture both energy arbitrage and ancillary services, challenging interconnection and regulatory frameworks.

Project developers are reportedly favoring hybrid configurations to maximize capacity value and revenue stacking potential. Transmission and distribution interconnection studies may need to model combined generation and storage behaviors rather than separate assets. Regulatory frameworks that do not clearly define hybrid participation in ancillary markets could create revenue uncertainty. Local content and grid code compliance for inverter-based resources may require additional testing and certification steps. Financiers may demand clearer modeling of stacked revenue streams before committing long-term capital. EPC teams should plan for integrated control systems and extended commissioning windows for hybrid plants. What to monitor: regulator guidance on hybrid asset market participation and updates to interconnection study templates for combined assets.

What to watch
  • regulatory guidance on hybrid participation
  • interconnection study updates for combined assets
Action

Model stacked revenue scenarios and engage regulators early on hybrid market participation rules.
