Rapid solar growth driving local congestion and curtailment risk

Signals from grid operators suggest accelerated solar additions in several markets are increasing congestion and curtailment risk, pushing expectations for more active grid management and storage co-location requirements.

High instantaneous solar output episodes are creating intra-hour imbalances that challenge traditional dispatch frameworks. Operators are increasingly considering dynamic line ratings and active curtailment frameworks to preserve system security. Scenario planning points to an uptick in mandated co-located storage or strict curtailment clauses in PPAs for new solar projects. EPC and OEM bids may need to include optional BESS scopes or firming packages to win contracts in constrained regions. Market signals could drive investment into more flexible transmission upgrades and substation reinforcements in priority corridors. Developers and financiers should expect contract negotiations to tighten around deliverability and curtailment compensation mechanisms. Monitor grid constraint reports and curtailment statistics for early signs of shifting project economics.

What to watch
  • curtailment data releases from operators
  • new PPA clauses addressing deliverability
Action

Stress-test ongoing and planned solar projects for deliverability and include storage options in bids for constrained regions.
