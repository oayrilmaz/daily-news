High-VRE integration scenarios strain intra-day balancing in Southeast Asia

Scenario indicators point to growing curtailment risk in systems rapidly adding VRE without matching flexibility; intra-day markets and regional interties emerge as potential mitigations.

Rapid solar rollouts in several Southeast Asian grids increase the probability of mid-day surplus events in plausible scenarios. Existing grid flexibility, largely provided by thermal ramping, is increasingly insufficient for high-VRE variability. Short-duration storage and demand-side shifting are frequently cited as near-term mitigation in planning discussions. Developers and planners signal interest in regional interconnection as a mechanism to smooth localized surpluses. Market-side reforms such as intraday trading windows and faster settlement could improve dispatch efficiency but face implementation friction. Grid codes and curtailment compensation rules are areas to monitor for policy action. Transmission build timelines and permitting remain the long-lead constraint on large-scale integration solutions. Expect incremental operational measures while structural market and transmission reforms proceed slowly.

What to watch
  • Market rule consultations on intraday trading or settlement speed
  • Permitting/approval updates for regional transmission corridors
Action

Prioritize flexible resource procurement and engage on intraday market design discussions.
