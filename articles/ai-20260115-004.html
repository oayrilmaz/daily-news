Storage shortfalls could increase curtailment scenarios for distributed renewables

Signals suggest distributed renewable fleets may face elevated curtailment risk in near-term scenarios due to storage project slowdowns and constrained interconnection capacity.

Developers are reporting that delayed storage deployments are increasing periods where there is more variable generation than absorptive capacity on distribution networks. This increases the expectation of curtailment during low-demand, high-generation intervals. Curtailment changes revenue certainty and alters contracted offtake profiles. Grid planners are likely to use market signals to prioritize storage interconnections, but permit and supply-chain hurdles remain prerequisites. In some regions, distribution system operators may implement more active curtailment schemes as a short-term balancing tool. That approach shifts the commercial risk onto generators unless contracts specify compensation. Scenario watch revolves around seasonal demand troughs and rapid overnight wind ramps that outpace available flexible loads. Monitoring should focus on storage commissioning schedules and DSO curtailment policy updates. Developers with flexible asset portfolios will be positioned to arbitrate price signals. Policy responses could include incentives for behind-the-meter storage adoption or expedited approvals for community batteries. These measures would ease absorption constraints but require time to implement. Grid modernization projects that increase controllability can reduce curtailment but will not remove near-term capacity gaps. Financially, investors may demand higher risk premiums for merchant renewables exposed to heightened curtailment risk. Power purchase agreement terms may be renegotiated to include more granular dispatch and compensation clauses. Project returns will be sensitive to these contractual adjustments. Operational mitigation includes demand-side management, virtual power plant aggregation, and targeted storage retrofits. Developers should stress-test revenue models against increased curtailment scenarios and explore flexibility markets where available.

What to watch
  • Storage commissioning schedules in high-penetration areas
  • DSO curtailment policy notices
Action

Stress-test renewable revenue models for increased curtailment and pursue flexibility market access where feasible.
