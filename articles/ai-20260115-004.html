Signals of rising curtailment risk where transmission expansion lags

Operational signals point to increasing curtailment risk in regions where new renewable builds outpace transmission upgrades, creating economic and reliability trade-offs.

Developers are encountering more frequent dispatch limitations in high-resource zones, signaling a mismatch between generation additions and export capacity. Grid operators are exercising market and emergency curtailment mechanisms more often in those constrained corridors. This dynamic creates scenarios where renewable project revenues and financing profiles face greater volatility. Some system planners are re-prioritizing transmission reinforcements to key export nodes, but permitting and siting remain hurdles. Hybridization with storage is becoming a commercial hedge against curtailment risk but requires additional capital and interconnection capacity. Local congestion is pushing market participants to consider alternative connection points or behind-the-fence solutions. Expect increased dialogue between regulators, system operators, and developers on cost allocation for strategic reinforcements. The longer-term outcome depends on policy shifts to streamline transmission build timelines.

What to watch
  • market curtailment notices for high-resource zones
  • planning updates on priority transmission reinforcements
Action

Evaluate curtailment exposure for generation assets and model storage or alternative interconnection strategies as hedges.
