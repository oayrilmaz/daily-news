Expectation: increased on-site generation and storage bids from hyperscale buyers

Signals indicate hyperscale and AI-focused data center operators are prioritizing greater on-site resilience through combined on-site generation and long-duration storage procurement—impacting local transmission demand profiles.

Operators are modeling portfolios that reduce dependence on strained local grids during peak AI training loads. This is generating expectations of higher procurement for medium-duration batteries and tiered on-site generation assets. The change alters load shape forecasts for distribution feeders serving large campuses and increases the likelihood of demand charge negotiations. Local utilities may face revenue shifts and new interconnection requests that require studies separate from traditional IT load assumptions. Microgrid and black-start capabilities are being evaluated more frequently as operational requirements tighten. The move could accelerate permitting and zoning requests for fuel storage in constrained jurisdictions. Equipment lead times, particularly for switchgear and power conversion systems, are a consistent operational concern. This scenario elevates the importance of coordinated planning between data center developers and grid planners.

What to watch
  • large interconnection studies filed by hyperscale projects
  • permits seeking on-site fuel or long-duration storage
Action

Engage with local grid planners early if serving large compute loads to align interconnection and resilience strategies.
