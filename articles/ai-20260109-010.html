Solar-plus-storage rollouts face transmission queue and interconnection signals

Signals indicate a growing backlog at transmission interconnection queues in several Latin American markets as solar-plus-storage projects accelerate.

Queue metrics show clustering of projects around the same substations and corridor nodes, increasing the risk of extended negotiation and network-upgrade delays. Expectations are that some projects will need to revise dispatch profiles or accept staged interconnection. Scenario watch: sequential approval of upgrades could advantage projects with earlier commercial operation dates and disadvantage late entrants. What to monitor are transmission upgrade cost-allocation announcements and interconnection timeline revisions. There are indicators that developers are exploring hybridization of existing plants and informal local network reinforcements to mitigate delays. Financial close timelines may be affected if interconnection risk alters bankable deliverability assumptions. Policy clarity on cost-sharing for network upgrades will be a key determinant of development pace.

What to watch
  • Transmission upgrade cost-allocation and queue milestone updates
  • Staging or dispatch limitations imposed by system operators
Action

Reassess project timelines against interconnection queue positions and engage early on upgrade cost-sharing.
