Extreme heat scenarios stress distribution circuits and contingency plans

Signals of prolonged high ambient temperatures are prompting scenario planning across distribution operators for increased thermal overloads and protection miscoordination.

Operators are reportedly running scenario models that assume longer-duration peak loads driven by air-conditioning and EV charging. These scenarios highlight hotspots on older feeders and secondary networks where conductor ampacity and transformer thermal limits could be breached. Protection settings are being reviewed in jurisdictions where reclosing on hot lines risks equipment damage. Field crews are staging spares and mobile transformers in modeled high-risk corridors as an operational hedge. There is growing attention on dynamic thermal rating pilots as a soft mitigation against conservative static ratings. However, implementation timelines and telemetry coverage remain uneven across utilities. Regulatory flexibility on emergency switching and incremental demand response is appearing in some planning discussions. These actions are best read as elevated preparedness rather than confirmation of imminent outages. Continued monitoring of ambient forecasts, feeder load telemetry, and dispatchable demand-response availability will clarify risks over the next 72 hours.

What to watch
  • feeder load telemetry increases
  • activation of staged mobile transformers
Action

Review critical feeder ampacity margins and confirm plans for mobile transformer deployment and crew staging.
