Rooftop PV growth and net-metering adjustments under scenario watch

Signals from regulatory filings and distributor engagement suggest pressure to restructure net-metering frameworks as rooftop PV penetration grows, creating distribution-level revenue and technical questions.

Utilities are signaling the need to rebalance tariff structures and compensation to maintain distribution cost recovery. Scenario watch highlights potential moves toward time-differentiated credits or export tariffs to manage midday reverse flows. Technical concerns rise around hosting capacity, voltage rise, and protection coordination on feeders with high distributed generation. What to monitor are pilot programs that allocate export compensation based on time-of-day or feeder-level constraints. EPCs and installers may see demand shift toward behind-the-meter storage bundles to preserve self-consumption economics. The expectation is incremental regulatory adjustments rather than abrupt revocations of net-metering, with phased approaches to minimize political and social backlash. Stakeholders should prepare modeling that demonstrates distribution impacts under alternative compensation schemes.

What to watch
  • Pilot tariff designs with time-differentiated export compensation
  • Hosting capacity studies on feeders with high DG penetration
Action

Model distribution impacts under alternative net-metering and incentivize bundled storage options for customers.
