Ancillary markets evolving to reward fast-response assets

Expectations are that ancillary service products will continue to adapt to favor assets that can provide millisecond-to-minute responses, reshaping revenue stacks for storage and fast-acting generation.

Market operators are experimenting with shorter-duration products and faster settlement windows as system needs change. This evolution increases the value proposition for batteries, flywheels, and responsive inverters relative to slow-ramping thermal plants. There is a scenario watch for integration friction where market signals outpace telemetry and control readiness at asset sites. Smaller participants may face barriers due to telemetry and bidding platform requirements, concentrating benefits among better-capitalized actors. What to monitor are regulatory filings on market product definitions, settlement rule changes, and pilot auction results. Developers should reassess business cases to include fast-response revenue streams. Grid planners must consider how these market shifts influence capacity adequacy models and reserve margins. Confidence is medium-high since trials and product changes have been increasingly visible across regions.

What to watch
  • regulatory filings on market product design
  • pilot auction outcomes for short-duration reserves
Action

Revisit asset revenue models to include potential fast-response market products and telemetry compliance needs.
