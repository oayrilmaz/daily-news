Behind-the-meter batteries used for frequency response by hyperscalers

Operational and contractual signals indicate an uptick in hyperscalers in the UK deploying behind-the-meter battery assets to participate in frequency response and ancillary services markets, which may change demand profiles and commercial relationships with distribution utilities.

By aggregating behind-the-meter batteries, hyperscalers can capture ancillary service revenues and reduce peak grid exposure. This shift creates new interface requirements with local DNOs and may require firmware and control standardization.

These arrangements could reduce stress on local grids during peaks but also complicate visibility for distribution operators unless telemetry and coordination agreements are implemented.

Data-center operators and regulators should clarify telemetry requirements and settlement mechanisms to ensure system-level benefits and accurate market participation.

What to watch
  • Changes to DNO telemetry and settlement requirements
  • New market participation filings by large consumers
Action

Engage with local DNOs to align telemetry and settlement processes before deploying behind-the-meter batteries for ancillary services.
