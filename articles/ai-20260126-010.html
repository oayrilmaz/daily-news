Testing capacity and partial-discharge monitoring influence HV acceptance

Expectations suggest partial-discharge monitoring and factory test capacity are becoming differentiators in HV equipment acceptance and long-term asset health strategies.

Project owners are adding continuous partial-discharge monitoring as a condition of acceptance for new transformers and switchgear. These monitoring systems provide early degradation signals and reduce reliance on infrequent offline diagnostics. However, integrating continuous sensors adds procurement and warranty complexity, including data ownership and alarm thresholds. Factory test bays for HV equipment are in tight supply in some regions, pushing owners to accept staged testing or witnessed sampling regimes. The combination of increased monitoring expectations and constrained FAT capacity is prompting owners to negotiate clearer acceptance criteria tied to on-site verification and long-term monitoring commitments. Vendors that can demonstrate integrated PD monitoring, remote diagnostics, and reliable FAT scheduling are gaining a commercial edge. For asset managers, the shift offers improved lifecycle visibility but demands new analytics and maintenance workflows.

What to watch
  • Contract clauses requiring continuous PD monitoring and data-sharing terms.
  • Factory test bay booking announcements and regional test-capacity constraints.
Action

Include PD monitoring and FAT scheduling guarantees in procurement contracts and budget for analytics integration post-acceptance.
