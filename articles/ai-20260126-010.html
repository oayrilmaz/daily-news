AI-driven trading tools reshape ancillary markets engagement

Signals suggest more asset owners and aggregators are piloting AI-driven trading and dispatch tools for ancillary services, creating scenarios around model explainability, compliance, and market risk exposure.

AI-driven dispatch can identify short-window revenue opportunities in fast markets. Model opacity raises questions for compliance teams and auditors. Backtesting often underestimates edge-case stress events that affect ancillary performance. Market operators may request explainability or audits before permitting algorithmic participation at scale. Aggregators could face increased collateral requirements if models increase bid volatility. Vendors are starting to offer explainability toolkits and scenario-testing services. Asset owners should establish governance for model updates and human override. Monitor regulatory feedback on algorithmic market participation and any pilot allowances.

What to watch
  • regulatory statements on algorithmic participation
  • vendor explainability and scenario-testing offerings
Action

Implement model governance and stress testing before deploying AI-driven bids in ancillary markets.
