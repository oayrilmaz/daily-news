Intra-day volatility and battery arbitrage scenarios rise as markets adapt

Signals show increasing intra-day price dispersion in fast-growing markets, enhancing near-term arbitrage opportunities for storage but raising settlement and forecasting complexity.

Market indications point to higher frequency swings as variable renewables and industrial loads expand without matching flexibility. Battery operators are modeling higher potential returns from intraday stacking but flag execution and calendar-risk complexity. There is an expectation that market design tweaks, such as sub-hourly settlement or new ancillary products, will be discussed to clarify value streams. Scenario watch: rule changes or delays could materially affect project returns and contract structures for merchant storage. What to monitor are discussion papers from market operators and changes in settlement granularity. Traders and schedulers may need to upgrade optimization stacks to capture shorter-duration spreads. The interplay between congestion on transmission and price divergence will be a critical determinant of where storage is most economic.

What to watch
  • Market operator proposals on settlement granularity and ancillary products
  • Intraday price volatility and congestion patterns at key nodes
Action

Reassess merchant storage models for shorter settlement periods and invest in optimization capability for intraday trading.
