Resilient power demand rising in data center builds

Signals from procurement and site assessments suggest data center developers are prioritizing diversified, on-site resilient power architectures amid concerns over grid volatility and generator fuel logistics.

Developers are increasingly specifying dual-fuel and large UPS systems to mitigate short-duration grid disturbances. There is growing appetite for distributed energy resources like behind-the-meter batteries coupled with CHP for resilience. Site selection teams are re-weighting grid reliability metrics alongside fiber and land costs. Power purchase agreements are being restructured to include firming services and capacity guarantees. Some operators are accelerating deployment of digital twin models to simulate failure modes and optimize generator/UPS coordination. These are emerging expectations and project specifications rather than universal standards. Watch for tender clarifications and revised technical requirements in upcoming RFPs to confirm direction.

What to watch
  • RFP amendments referencing fuel-flex generators or larger UPS capacities
  • Interconnection study outcomes for planned sites
  • Local fuel logistics and storage regulations
Action

Revisit power architecture specs for upcoming bids and include DER integration and fuel contingency planning.
