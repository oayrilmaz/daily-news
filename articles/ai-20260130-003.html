Data centers signal faster adoption of on-site storage and DERs for resilience and cost control

Data center operators are showing signals of accelerating integration of on-site battery storage and distributed energy resources to manage power costs, resilience expectations, and increasing compute-driven load variability.

Operators are experimenting with larger battery banks to flatten demand peaks and capture ancillary revenue streams. Integration with on-site gas gensets and fuel cells is being rebalanced to prioritize low-emissions profiles while maintaining backup reliability. There is a rising emphasis on standardized grid-interactive controls that let data centers provide fast frequency response and demand flexibility. Scenario watch: tighter market signals for capacity or time-of-use tariffs could make storage payback timelines materially shorter. What to monitor: procurement of grid-interactive inverters, interconnection requests that cite export-capable systems, and tariff pilots in major markets. Smaller operators may opt for third-party microgrid service agreements to de-risk capex and operation. Coordination with local grid operators is increasingly necessary to manage export and black-start scenarios safely. Expect conversations between data centers and utilities to intensify around demand flexibility programs.

What to watch
  • interconnection filings for export-capable systems
  • tariff changes impacting large C&I customers
Action

Model storage and DER scenarios against local tariff structures and prioritize grid-interactive controls.
