Modular GIS and digital twins gain traction for aging networks

Expectations are building that factory-assembled gas-insulated substations and digital twin deployments will be used to shorten outage windows and manage complex asset renewals in congested urban networks.

Signals point to increased procurement interest in modular GIS to avoid prolonged site construction and to reduce civil works in dense urban footprints. Digital twins are being considered to simulate commissioning and to support predictive maintenance, particularly for oil-free switchgear and control systems. Adoption scenarios vary by utility budget cycles and existing asset condition, with early adopters prioritizing projects where access and space are constrained. A scenario watch should consider whether standardization across vendors accelerates or stalls, which will influence interoperability and spare parts strategies. The balance between on-site customization and factory standardization will affect delivery timelines and capex profiles. Utilities will likely pilot integrated digital twin workflows before committing fleet-wide.

What to watch
  • procurement notices for factory-built substation modules
  • pilot digital twin commissioning reports
Action

Map current fleet components to modularization compatibility and pilot a digital twin on a critical asset within six months.
