Transformer spare-part signals point to concentrated risk

Signals suggest a growing concentration of risk tied to aging large power transformers and long lead times for replacements, a scenario that should be on asset managers' radar.

Inventory reports and procurement lead-time signals indicate long waits for large power transformers and certain HV components. Expectations are that constrained supply could force extended outage durations if failures occur. Scenario watch includes simultaneous failures or weather-driven damage in constrained regions that would strain contingency plans. What to monitor: spare count by critical voltage class and nearby vendors' production schedules. Operators should evaluate temporary load transfer plans and modular replacement options. EPC teams may need to pre-qualify multiple vendors and consider short-term rental or mobile transformer solutions.

What to watch
  • lead times for large transformers
  • spare inventory by voltage class
Action

Audit transformer spares and update contingency plans to account for extended replacement lead times.
