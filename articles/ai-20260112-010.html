Battery second-life and recycling pilots signal a path to lower raw-material exposure

Signals from pilots and procurement cycles show growing strategic value in battery second-life reuse and recycling, offering a scenario-based lever to blunt long-term raw-material demand and reduce lifecycle emissions for storage projects.

Manufacturers and recyclers are running pilots to recover critical materials and to repurpose EV batteries for stationary storage second-life applications.

These pathways could materially change demand curves for certain raw materials under scenarios where recovery rates and economics scale favorably.

Regulatory clarity on recycled-content standards and environmental controls will influence investment and offtake decisions for these supply chains.

Second-life batteries introduce variability in performance and certification requirements, so integration strategies favor modular, monitored systems.

Recycling economics depend on collection logistics and local processing capacity, creating regional disparity in how quickly the benefits accrue.

Stakeholders should model multiple uptake scenarios to understand how recycled supply could offset primary material exposure over a multi-year horizon.

What to watch
  • Regulatory proposals on recycled-content standards
  • Commercial-scale recycling plant announcements and pilot outcomes
Action

Assess pilot partnerships for second-life reuse and incorporate recycled-content scenarios into long-range materials planning.
