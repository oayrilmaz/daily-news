Investment interest rises for substation refurb and remanufacture in constrained markets

Signals suggest rising interest among project financiers and local operators in substation refurbishment and remanufacturing as a pragmatic response to constrained supply chains and capital efficiency needs in some African markets.

Refurbishment can stretch available equipment and avoid multi-year procurement waits for new transformers and switchgear. Local remanufacturing initiatives are attracting attention as a way to reduce import dependency and create faster turnaround for replacement parts. These programs require upskilling of local technicians and investments in test facilities to ensure safety and performance. Donors and development banks appear to be evaluating blended-finance mechanisms that could underwrite capacity-building tied to refurbishment projects. Operator risk tolerance varies, and acceptance of remanufactured equipment often depends on demonstrated factory testing and warranty frameworks. Scenario watch: if global lead times continue to pressure capital projects, refurbishment and remanufacturing may shift from niche to mainstream in constrained regions.

What to watch
  • Announcements of blended-finance programs targeting remanufacturing facilities.
  • Local test-facility openings and technician training initiatives.
Action

Evaluate refurbishment as a contingency option and identify partners for local testing and warranty support.
