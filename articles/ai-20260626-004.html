Offshore cable reliability concerns could shift HVDC commissioning strategies

Signals from project trials indicate cable failure modes and jointing challenges that may lead developers and grid operators to re-evaluate HVDC commissioning and redundancy practices.

Recent project testing and asset performance chatter highlight jointing integrity and thermal management as recurring technical themes for long subsea cables. Developers are increasingly considering sectionalized redundancy and spare capacity in scheduling to avoid prolonged outages. Procurement for specialized jointing teams and test equipment is lengthening project timelines. Grid operators face scenario choices between accepting later in-service dates or imposing tighter pre-commissioning testing that could delay connection but reduce early-life incidents. Insurance terms and warranties for cable systems are becoming more detailed, with greater attention to installation conditions and thermal load envelopes. These dynamics may raise capital costs and encourage modular offshore array strategies that limit single-point cable exposure. Expect more conservative acceptance testing regimes in upcoming tenders.

What to watch
  • tender language changes requiring extended pre-commissioning tests
  • procurement timelines for specialized subsea jointing crews and test equipment
Action

Build contingency into HVDC commissioning schedules and specify additional pre-commissioning tests in contracts.
