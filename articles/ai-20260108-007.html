Resilience Standards and EPC Scope Creep as a Scenario

Expectations are growing that evolving resilience standards could broaden EPC scopes, creating contractual and cost implications for transmission and distribution projects.

Regulatory dialogues increasingly emphasize resilience metrics, which can translate into retrofit or hardening requirements for projects under development. EPCs may face scope creep if owners incorporate new resilience criteria mid-project, complicating schedules and cost allocations. There is a scenario watch that procurement documents will start embedding minimum resilience performance, shifting design baselines. Financing structures could respond by requiring resilience provisions as conditions for funding, affecting overall project economics. Manufacturers and suppliers may be asked to deliver higher-spec components, pressuring lead times and pricing. Owners and EPCs that proactively model resilience scenarios are likely to reduce negotiation friction later in the project lifecycle. What to monitor: draft policy language on resilience metrics and any guidance linking compliance to financing terms.

What to watch
  • Draft regulatory guidance on resilience metrics in major markets.
  • Language in financing term sheets tying funding to resilience compliance.
Action

Incorporate resilience scenario planning into project contracts and align with financiers early to avoid later scope disputes.
