Grid-code evolution expectations center on synthetic inertia and testing

Regulatory signals point toward tighter grid-code expectations for synthetic inertia, inverter testing, and certification processes that could affect project compliance timelines.

Drafting activity and stakeholder consultations are signaling a shift toward explicit synthetic inertia requirements for new connections. Expectations include new testing regimes and extended commissioning windows for inverter-based plants. Scenario watch: accelerated rule adoption without transitional allowances could create compliance bottlenecks for late-stage projects. What to monitor are consultation papers, technical requirements for type testing, and certification timelines that might be imposed. EPCs and OEMs should align product development roadmaps with emerging test criteria. Intelligence indicates that early engagement in consultations will yield better alignment and fewer retrofits. Projects should plan for potential additional testing resources and timeline buffers. Regulatory clarity will be a key driver of capital allocation decisions for new generation assets.

What to watch
  • consultation papers and draft technical requirements
  • type-testing and certification schedules
Action

Engage with regulators and adapt commissioning plans to include potential new testing and certification steps.
