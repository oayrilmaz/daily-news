EPC bid structures may be shifting as developers seek more supply-chain risk transfer

Signals from tender behavior in large renewable and transmission projects suggest developers are increasingly seeking EPC packages that transfer greater supply-chain and delivery risk to contractors, which could change bid pricing and contract terms in the near term.

Developers facing component lead-time uncertainty and financing constraints appear to prefer fixed-price, turnkey solutions. EPC firms may respond by tightening contingencies, seeking supplier guarantees, or pushing back on scope flexibility.

This shift could increase initial bid prices but reduce developer execution risk; both parties should reassess risk allocation and contractual remedies for delays or scope changes.

What to watch
  • Changes in tender terms and allocation of supply-chain liabilities
  • EPC bid contingency levels and proposed supplier guarantees
Action

Review and clarify risk allocation in EPC tender documents and prepare to negotiate supplier guarantee structures.
