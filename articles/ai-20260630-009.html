HVDC and interconnect scenarios: planning for long-distance renewable corridors

Signals point to renewed interest in HVDC interconnects as planners consider long-distance renewable corridors and cross-border balancing, creating strategic planning implications for transmission owners and developers.

HVDC offers low-loss links that support bulk power transfers over long distances. Corridor planning introduces right-of-way, permitting, and multi-jurisdictional governance challenges. Scenario planners should factor in long lead times for converter stations and specialized equipment procurement. Interconnects can enable geographic smoothing of variable renewables and reduce overall curtailment. However, they require coordination on operational rules and fault management. What to monitor are high-level system studies and multinational framework discussions that outline dispatch and cost-allocation principles. For EPCs, converter station integration expertise and logistics for heavy equipment will shape competitive positioning.

What to watch
  • regional system studies and planning documents referencing HVDC corridors
  • multinational framework talks on cost allocation and dispatch rules
Action

Map converter-station procurement timelines and engage early on cross-border governance and cost-sharing models.
