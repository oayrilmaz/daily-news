Interconnection queue reform remains a scenario driver for project timelines

Expectations around regulatory action on interconnection queue priorities and cost allocation could reshape project economics and schedules, especially for distributed resources and storage co-located with renewables.

Regulators considering reforms may change how network upgrades are allocated between developers and incumbents, shifting risk profiles. That change would materially affect EPC contracting and financing terms for late‑stage projects. Queue-clearing mechanisms and potential fast-track windows for storage could re-order commercial priority and influence which projects proceed. Developers with flexible designs may gain relative advantage. Scenario watch: a policy shift that accelerates small-scale storage interconnection could increase patchwork upgrades, raising complexity for transmission planners. That fragmentation typically elevates coordination costs and delivery uncertainty. What to monitor are filings and public comment periods at regional regulators and any pilot programs for reformed interconnection. Early signals from administrative rulings are often followed by market re-pricing. Project sponsors should review contractual clauses related to network upgrade cost responsibilities and prepare for renegotiation scenarios. Finance teams should model variant outcomes for interconnection costs. Stakeholders should also prepare for potential litigation or appeals in contested rule changes, which can extend uncertainty and delay implementation timelines.

What to watch
  • regulatory filings and public comment deadlines
  • pilot program announcements for storage interconnection
Action

Reassess interconnection cost allocations in contracts and model multiple regulatory outcomes for financing plans.
