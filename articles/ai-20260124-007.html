Ancillary markets and DER participation: volatility signals

Signals from real-time markets suggest growing volatility as distributed energy resources increasingly participate in ancillary services, potentially changing price dynamics and resource dispatch behavior.

Aggregation platforms are maturing and enabling more DERs to bid into ancillary markets. Increased participation raises the likelihood of intra-day price swings as small resources cluster into large effective capacity. Market operators are adapting rules to preserve reliability while integrating DER behaviors, creating temporary frictions. Price signals may favor fast-ramping assets and storage, compressing revenues for slower thermal units. Measurement and verification standards remain a weak link and could produce settlement disputes that affect market liquidity. The net effect may be higher short-term price volatility but improved capacity diversity. Traders and asset managers should update calibration models to incorporate DER response profiles. Market design changes in response to these dynamics may follow in the medium term.

What to watch
  • Rule-change proposals for DER participation and settlement windows
  • Intra-day price spikes correlated with DER dispatch events
Action

Update market participation models for DER aggregation and test settlement scenarios under high-volatility conditions.
