Incentive signal for HVDC and interties could accelerate regional transmission projects

Policy signals and draft guidance in several jurisdictions are highlighting incentives for HVDC links and interregional interties, creating scenario pressure favoring accelerated permitting and financing for long-distance projects.

Draft incentive frameworks are emphasizing the role of HVDC in decarbonization pathways and grid resilience planning. This policy framing increases the likelihood that project sponsors will pursue accelerated permitting tracks and blended financing structures. Grid planners may re-evaluate corridor studies with an eye toward HVDC advantages in capacity and controllability. Financing partners are likely to prioritize projects with defined incentives, potentially reshaping the developer landscape. Transmission owners must anticipate more complex stakeholder engagement around right-of-way and cross-jurisdictional approvals. Expect an uptick in pre-development activity for converter stations and associated AC reinforcements. Scenario risks include extended environmental reviews and supply-chain competition for specialized converter equipment.

What to watch
  • Finalization of incentive language and guidance documents.
  • Expressions of interest or pre-development filings for major intertie projects.
Action

Monitor policy finalization and prioritize early engagement on converter station siting and specialized equipment procurement.
