EPCs anticipate tighter schedules and offer modular HV solutions

EPCs are reportedly packaging modular high-voltage solutions to meet compressed project timelines, creating a scenario where prefabrication and factory testing become competitive differentiators.

Market conversations indicate EPCs are promoting factory-built substation modules to reduce on-site work and schedule exposure. The scenario is driven by congested supply chains and permitting delays that make on-site build time a liability. Factory testing promises faster commissioning and predictable quality, but transportation and site-access constraints can limit applicability. OEM partnerships focused on interface standardization and plug-and-play protection panels improve uptake. Owners may face trade-offs in customization versus speed. Warranty and liability clauses for modular components are becoming negotiation focal points. The move could compress the staffing profile required on site and shift risk toward logistics and integration testing.

What to watch
  • modular substation delivery timelines and site transport constraints
  • contract language on warranties for factory-tested modules
Action

Assess modular solution compatibility with site constraints and incorporate logistics risk into schedule contingencies.
