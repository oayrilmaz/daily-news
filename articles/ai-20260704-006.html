EPC contracts shift toward performance-based clauses for HV projects

Industry signals show growing interest in shifting EPC contracts toward availability and performance-based remuneration for high-voltage projects, reallocating certain operational risks to contractors and influencing project finance structures.

Clients appear to be negotiating contracts that tie payments to operational metrics such as transformer availability, protection performance, and commissioning timelines. The intelligence framing positions this as a reaction to historic schedule slips and the desire to align incentives with long-term reliability. Contractors are assessing how to price and hedge these new risk buckets.

This contractual evolution may prompt changes in warranty language, spare parts provisioning, and factory testing requirements. EPCs may require higher upfront mobilization or insist on client-provided contingencies for long-lead items to accept performance liability. Lenders and insurers will scrutinize performance guarantees when underwriting projects.

Scenario watch: widespread adoption of performance clauses could improve long-term reliability but increase EPC bid prices as contractors price risk into margins. Alternatively, a negotiated middle ground with joint risk-sharing mechanisms may emerge, including performance securities and insurance instruments.

Operational handoffs will need clearer digital and documentation standards, especially for protection settings and control logic. Clients will likely demand real-time telemetry for contract compliance monitoring. This pushes both parties toward interoperable data formats and standardized FAT reports.

For smaller EPCs and local contractors, absorbing performance risk may be infeasible without partnerships or reinsurance, potentially consolidating the market. Larger OEMs may offer packaged solutions that combine equipment sales with long-term service contracts to meet client demands.

Summary: EPCs are facing pressure to accept performance-based payment schemes, reshaping contract pricing and risk allocation. Stakeholders should prepare for tightened documentation and insurance scrutiny.

What to watch
  • Emergence of standardized performance KPIs in RFPs
  • Insurer appetite for long-term performance guarantees
Action

Review contract templates and assess the feasibility and cost of accepting availability-based payment terms.
