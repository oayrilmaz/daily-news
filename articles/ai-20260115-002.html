Increased scanning activity prompts substation cybersecurity scenario planning

OT teams are reporting elevated scanning and probing signals against substation IP ranges, prompting expectations that asset owners should refresh segmentation and logging strategies.

Current indicators show scans that map services and probe legacy remote access protocols. These activities appear exploratory rather than disruptive at this stage. Owners should treat this as an operational security signal and not a confirmed incident. That framing supports preemptive hardening without public alarm. Short-term priorities include isolating engineering workstations, enforcing MFA on remote access, and increasing ICS-specific telemetry capture. Visibility gaps in serial-to-IP gateways remain a weak point. Hardening those gateways or applying protocol whitelisting will reduce attack surface. Supply chain risk is a secondary vector. Vendors with remote maintenance access increase the probability of a chained compromise if not tightly controlled. Expect regulators and operators to underscore incident reporting and tabletop exercises in the coming weeks as part of a defensive posture refresh.

What to watch
  • sudden increase in failed authentication or remote sessions
  • vendor remote support requests and their authentication posture
Action

Run a prioritized checklist to segment engineering networks and increase ICS-specific logging for the next 72 hours.
