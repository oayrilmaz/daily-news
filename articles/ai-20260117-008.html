Project delivery risks as ESG and labor dynamics reshape timelines

Signals indicate rising complexity in large electrification projects as ESG requirements, labor availability, and localized content expectations combine to increase delivery risk for OEMs and EPCs.

Project sponsors are layering tighter ESG clauses into contracts, which can extend procurement cycles and change supplier selection dynamics. Labor markets in construction and specialized HV trades are tight in key regions, increasing schedule risk. Local content and community engagement expectations are adding scope to project management and compliance tasks. OEMs and EPCs may need to reprice risk or embed contingency buffers in schedules. There is an expectation that some smaller suppliers will be squeezed, creating consolidation pressure in supply chains. The net result is a scenario with longer project durations, higher contingency spend, and potential claims. Firms should monitor contract language trends and adjust resource planning accordingly.

What to watch
  • changes in contract clauses related to ESG and local content
  • labor market indicators for HV trades and construction
Action

Update project risk registers to include ESG-driven procurement and labor contingencies and engage key suppliers early.
