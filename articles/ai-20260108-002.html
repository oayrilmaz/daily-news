Lead-Time Signals for HV Switchgear Remain Elevated

Supply-chain signals continue to point to elongated lead times for high-voltage switchgear and related substation components, creating project schedule risk for mid-sized and brownfield upgrades.

Procurement activity and quoted delivery windows indicate suppliers are prioritizing large OEM contracts, leaving smaller EPCs with longer waits. Concentrated manufacturing capacity for specific insulation materials and control components is a scenario watch that could sustain delays. Owners contemplating retrofit work should expect schedule contingency to be necessary and may face higher expedited costs. Extended lead times complicate outage planning because windows for equipment replacement may need to be shifted by months. There are expectations that alternative suppliers and refurbished equipment markets will see increased interest as a stopgap. Standby strategies such as staged procurements and early factory acceptance testing are likely to mitigate some integration risks. What to monitor: quoted delivery dates from multiple suppliers and the availability of refurbished or rental breakers in key voltage classes.

What to watch
  • Changes in quoted delivery windows from multiple suppliers.
  • Availability of refurbished or rental breakers in target voltage classes.
Action

Add schedule contingency to substation projects and explore alternative sourcing or refurbished equipment options.
