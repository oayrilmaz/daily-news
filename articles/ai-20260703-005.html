Ancillary services demand rising in high-renewables pockets

Signals suggest ancillary services markets are tightening in regions with rapid renewable growth, creating potential near-term revenue opportunities and operational stress for conventional units.

Market operators appear to be soliciting more frequency and ramping products to manage variability. The intelligence points to higher clearing prices for fast-response resources and a premium for firming capacity. Scenario watch highlights potential new product definitions for very-fast frequency response and inertia-like services. Battery operators and fast-ramping gas units are in a favorable position under these dynamics. What to monitor are settlement rule changes and product-gating proposals at market operator forums. This environment could reshape procurement priorities for utilities and asset owners. Traders and aggregators may increase bidding activity to capture transient price spikes.

What to watch
  • market operator rule proposals for new fast-response products
  • ancillary price spikes during high renewable output periods
Action

Evaluate participation in fast-response markets and validate telemetry for rapid dispatch assets.
