Transmission constraints increasing curtailment risk in high-renewables zones

Grid expansion lags and localized congestion are showing signals of higher renewable curtailment in several Asian corridors; operators should plan for bridging solutions to preserve project economics.

Expectations are that new renewable capacity will continue to outpace transmission upgrades in constrained corridors during the next 12–18 months. Scenario watch: prolonged curtailment could push developers to delay commissioning or seek remote balancing agreements. What to monitor are transmission build approvals, right-of-way clearances, and the frequency and duration of constrained flow-hour windows. Short-term fixes include dynamic line ratings, flexible ramping agreements, and local storage deployments to shave peaks. There is also a sign that grid-scale storage procurement will accelerate where merchant pricing supports capture of curtailed energy. Policymakers and regulators may be pressured to prioritize critical tie-line projects to avoid sustained developer attrition and to keep investment flowing. Project developers should model curtailment risk into offtake discussions and consider hybridization with storage or grid services.

What to watch
  • transmission project approvals and right-of-way delays
  • frequency/duration statistics of constrained flow hours
Action

Incorporate curtailment scenarios into financial models and evaluate storage or flexible PPA structures to mitigate revenue risk.
