Distributed renewables stress local distribution dispatch models

Signals from distribution operators indicate rising complexity in managing high penetrations of rooftop PV and community batteries, prompting expectations of more granular voltage and constraint controls.

Distribution systems are beginning to experience simultaneous high inverter output and low load periods, driving voltage excursions. The intelligence suggests advanced inverter settings and grid-forming experiments are being prioritized to stabilize local networks. There is a scenario watch on curtailment policies as a blunt instrument that may reappear if control layers are not matured. Aggregators and DERMS vendors are expected to push integration pilots to provide non-wires alternatives. Regulatory tolerance for rapid experimentation will determine how quickly advanced control architectures can be adopted. Monitoring will focus on voltage management events and changes to interconnection technical requirements.

What to watch
  • voltage excursion reports
  • changes to interconnection technical requirements
Action

Engage DERMS and inverter vendors on grid-forming and dynamic voltage control capabilities as contingency planning.
