EPCs adapting modular builds to reduce site risk

Signals point to EPCs offering more modular and factory-integrated builds to reduce on-site assembly time and exposure to labor or supply disruptions on large projects.

EPC firms are framing modularization as a risk mitigation strategy to shorten field schedules and reduce weather and labor exposure. Expectations include wider use of pre-assembled switchyards, transformer skids, and integrated protection cabinets delivered with tested wiring harnesses. This approach shifts integration risk upstream to manufacturers and increases emphasis on factory acceptance testing documentation. Scenario watch: transport constraints and heavy-lift logistics can become the new gating factor for modular deliveries if ports or roads are constrained. Contracts may evolve to include clearer interfaces for staged energization and conditional commissioning acceptance. Operators should require detailed FAT records and transport-impact assessments to avoid surprises during site integration. EPCs and OEMs should coordinate logistics early and validate lifting and site access plans with modular dimensions in mind.

What to watch
  • Factory acceptance test (FAT) completeness
  • Transport and heavy-lift logistics constraints
  • Staged energization interface clauses
Action

Require comprehensive FAT documentation and coordinate logistics planning early for modular deliveries.
