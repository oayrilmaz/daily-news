Shift to SiC/GaN capacity creating short-term equipment bottleneck scenarios

Signals indicate manufacturers are retooling for wide-bandgap semiconductors, producing near-term capacity constraints for SiC and GaN devices that could slow inverter and converter deliveries.

Capital investment in SiC and GaN fabs is rising, but conversion of fabs and material supply chains takes time. The near-term scenario is constrained availability of qualified power modules for high-efficiency inverters. EPC schedules that rely on next-generation inverters may face delays or cost premia for expedited device sourcing. Another scenario is temporary redesigns that accept larger or less efficient legacy components to meet delivery timelines. The manufacturing pivot is also prompting increased qualification activity for module suppliers and tighter acceptance testing. For system integrators, this creates an operational friction point between desired performance gains and practical lead times. Expect OEMs to prioritize key customers and large volume contracts when allocating limited wafer capacity.

What to watch
  • capacity announcements from wafer fabs and module suppliers
  • component allocation notices from OEMs
Action

Secure allocation agreements and accelerate supplier qualification for wide-bandgap power modules.
