Recycling and secondary supply scenarios shape battery mineral availability

Signals point to accelerating investment in recycling infrastructure and secondary supply channels that could materially affect nickel and cobalt availability for grid-scale batteries over the medium term.

Growing interest in recycling creates an expectation that near-term material tightness could be eased if collection and processing scale effectively. Recycling scenarios reduce reliance on primary mining but require capital and regulatory clarity to become reliable feedstocks. There is a watchpoint on processing bottlenecks and reagent availability that could limit throughput even as feedstock volumes rise. Policy signals that favor circularity could incentivize investments in hydrometallurgical and direct recycling methods. OEMs and cell manufacturers may begin to reprice supply contracts to reflect increasing secondary-content requirements. Operators procuring battery systems should monitor recycled-content specifications and potential impacts on warranty and performance claims. Market signaling from recycled material transactions will be an early indicator of how quickly secondary supply can scale. Prepare procurement strategies that account for a mix of primary and secondary sources to reduce exposure to single-supply risks.

What to watch
  • recycling plant throughput reports and permitting updates
  • shifts in OEM contract language on recycled content
Action

Explore procurement flexibilities that allow mixed primary/secondary material sourcing and monitor recycling capacity buildouts.
