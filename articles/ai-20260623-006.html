Concentration in lithium and nickel supply chains poses production risk

Signals suggest concentrated sourcing and processing for lithium and nickel remain a strategic risk, with scenarios that could affect battery manufacturing timelines and project economics.

Downstream battery producers are exposed to chokepoints in refining and precursor production that can propagate through to cell availability. Expectations include price sensitivity to shipment disruptions and policy interventions in key supplier regions. Scenario watch: a localized strike, regulatory action, or logistics slowdown at a primary processing hub could ripple across cell supply and lengthen project timelines. What to monitor are export policy moves, processing capacity expansions, and announcements of new domestic refining projects. OEMs appear to be exploring alternative chemistries and second-life markets as hedges. The intelligence posture favors diversified sourcing, long-term offtake, and investment in regional processing to reduce single-point risks.

What to watch
  • Processing capacity announcements and export policy signals
  • Offtake agreements for new refining capacity
Action

Prioritize diversified offtake and accelerate assessment of alternative chemistries or domestic refining partnerships.
