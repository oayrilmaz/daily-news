Capacity market repricing and contracting dynamics

Market signals suggest shifting dynamics in capacity and forward contracting, with growing premiums for flexible and fast-ramping resources that create new merchant risk and contract structures for developers.

Forward price behaviour is showing increased value for flexibility during stressed hours, altering project revenue stacks. Developers are likely to chase hybridization and co-located storage to capture capacity and ancillary revenue streams. Capacity market repricing expectations could make long-term PPAs less attractive for some investors. Portfolio managers are evaluating hedges tied to ancillary services rather than pure energy volumes. These dynamics may accelerate merchant exposure for greenfield assets in constrained regions. Scenario watch: sudden policy clarifications on capacity mechanisms could re-rate project economics quickly. Traders and risk teams should monitor ancillary market price trends and capacity auction signals. Contract negotiators will need clauses that reflect ramping and operational performance payments.

What to watch
  • capacity auction results and ancillary price moves
  • changes in PPA structures favoring flexibility
Action

Review contract templates to incorporate flexibility and ancillary-service revenue terms for new projects.
