Scarcity pricing and battery co-optimization create merchant revenue scenarios

Market signals point to tighter scarcity-pricing dynamics in some regions, creating expectations that batteries will be co-optimized for capacity revenue and ancillary services, altering merchant project economics.

Traders and asset managers are reportedly modeling more frequent scarcity-price events into dispatch strategies, which increases the value of fast-response assets. Batteries are being framed as dual-revenue machines, with capacity accreditation and fast-ramping ancillary services both considered in investment cases. Market design changes, including auction rules and performance obligations, are likely to influence battery sizing and control strategies. Settlement granularity and settlement delays remain watch items that affect revenue capture. A plausible scenario is that batteries configured for co-optimization will outcompete single-purpose assets in merchant stacks. Projects dependent on merchant stacks should reassess revenue stacking assumptions under tightened scarcity expectations.

What to watch
  • ISO/RTO notices on scarcity pricing revisions and capacity accreditation changes.
  • Settlement rule updates that affect fast-response revenue capture.
Action

Re-evaluate revenue stacking and battery control algorithms to capture scarcity and ancillary revenues.
