Expectation: HVDC project procurement faces strategic trade-offs

Scenario analysis suggests HVDC procurement in congested corridors will increasingly weigh cost, delivery risk, and interoperability, prompting bespoke contract structures.

Planners are modeling trade-offs between turnkey HVDC solutions and multi-vendor approaches to control cost and schedule risks. Interoperability with existing AC systems and protection schemes is a recurring scenario constraint in technical procurement language. Financing scenarios consider phased revenue realization where segments of HVDC links become operational ahead of full commissioning. Manufacturing lead-times for converters and onshore stations are being stress-tested against aggressive commissioning timelines. Operations scenarios highlight the need for synchronized testing protocols and cross-border operational agreements in multi-jurisdiction projects. The procurement implication: contract flexibility and clear acceptance criteria reduce delivery uncertainty but can increase negotiation complexity.

What to watch
  • Tender language indicating turnkey or multi-vendor preferences
  • Timeline adjustments for converter station deliveries
Action

Define interoperability and phased-acceptance criteria early in procurement to reduce integration risk.
