Ancillary markets and co-optimization pressure

Market signals indicate increasing pressure to co-optimize energy and ancillary services as inverter-rich fleets change the shape of flexibility supply.

Expectations are that market operators will pilot more granular products aimed at fast-frequency response and synthetic inertia. There is a scenario watch where existing market rules fail to adequately remunerate fast-acting resources, creating underinvestment risks. Aggregators and large flexible loads may capture disproportionate value if design remains static. Vendors might propose bundled products to smooth participation. What to monitor are proposal filings and pilot timelines for new ancillary services. A secondary scenario suggests tensions between long-duration storage economics and high-speed inverter services as market signals evolve.

What to watch
  • Regulatory filings for new ancillary products
  • Pilot participation by aggregators and storage
  • Shifts in price signals for fast response
Action

Monitor regulatory filings and reassess participation strategies for assets that provide high-speed grid services.
