Volatility in short-term power markets tied to fuel and weather uncertainty

Expectations and signals point to elevated short-term price swings as fuel markets and weather-driven demand create tighter supply margins in several trading hubs.

Traders and asset managers are signaling increased use of hedging instruments to manage near-term exposure. Renewables output variability combined with colder-than-average pockets could produce localized scarcity pricing events. There is expectation that ancillary service markets will see more clearing for fast-ramping and inertia-like services. Scenario watch includes the potential for market rule tweaks that prioritize resource adequacy signals over nodal price signals. Settlement disputes and uplift payments may rise as system operators manage unanticipated dispatch patterns. What to monitor: changes in day-ahead/real-time spreads, ancillary services clearing prices, and any operator notices adjusting dispatch priorities.

What to watch
  • day-ahead to real-time price spreads
  • ancillary services clearing price movements
Action

Review hedging positions and evaluate opportunities in fast-response ancillary service markets.
