Scenario watch: inertia deficits as inverter-based resources rise

Scenario watch indicates growing system inertia concerns in regions rapidly adding inverter-based wind and solar, prompting operator interest in synthetic inertia and synchronous condensers.

Grid operators are being signaled to evaluate frequency response margins as conventional synchronous machines retire. The expectation framing shows multiple technical mitigations under consideration, including dynamic inverter controls and synchronous condenser deployments. What to monitor is how protection coordination is updated to reflect faster frequency dynamics. There are expectations that procurement of inertia-mimicking services will create a new ancillary market segment. Manufacturers of power electronics may promote firmware upgrades that provide fast frequency response, but real-world effectiveness can vary by site and by control parameterization. The intelligence framing suggests system planning studies will need higher fidelity models for inverter behavior. A misestimate of inertia needs could increase the probability of under-frequency events during large disturbances.

What to watch
  • tenders for synchronous condensers or inertia services
  • grid code changes on dynamic inverter behavior
Action

Review protection and governor settings with updated inverter models and engage in ancillary service market design discussions.
