Predictive maintenance pilots for GIS and relay fleets gain traction

Signals from pilot deployments suggest predictive analytics and edge AI are beginning to change asset health management for GIS and protection relays in substations, with operational trade-offs to assess.

Several utilities appear to be testing machine-learning models on PD, oil/insulation metrics, and relay event streams to extend maintenance intervals. The scenario watch highlights faster detection of degradation versus calendar-based replacement, but also points to increased false-positive rates when models are trained on limited failure datasets. Vendors are integrating edge inferencing to limit data egress and latency, which can improve situational awareness in remote stations. Expectations include blended approaches where analytics augment rather than replace visual inspections and diagnostic testing. There is potential operational friction as field crews adjust to predictive work orders and trust model outputs. What to monitor are changes to spares strategies, service contracts, and insurer requirements as predictive regimes scale. Adoption will be uneven across regions based on data maturity and regulatory acceptance. These developments are signals of an industry shift that merits cautious pilot expansion and rigorous validation.

What to watch
  • Model validation reports and false-positive rates
  • Changes in spares inventories and service-level agreements
Action

Expand pilots with dual-track validation: run predictive outputs alongside traditional diagnostics before changing maintenance intervals.
