Spare-parts and HV equipment lead times remain a constraint for substation projects

Expectations are that long lead times for GIS, breakers and large power transformers continue to distort substation project schedules and contingency planning.

EPCs are signalling that procurement pipelines are still absorbing backlog orders, which limits flexibility for emergent projects. Project managers are increasingly incorporating multi-year procurement windows into cost and schedule forecasts. Inventory strategies are shifting toward strategic spares for critical HV components, at the expense of working capital. The scenario to watch is coordinated delay across multiple suppliers leading to sequential commissioning slips on clustered projects. Insurance and warranty negotiation practices are being tested as OEMs push delivery certainty language into contracts. Operators may need to consider refurbishment, retubing and staged commissioning as interim mitigations for grid reliability.

What to watch
  • Revised OEM lead-time advisories
  • Procurement award timing slips
Action

Reassess procurement timelines and secure long-lead items now; increase focus on strategic spare policies.
