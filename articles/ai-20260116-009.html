Battery storage capacity growth scenarios alter dispatch planning

Emerging signals of accelerated battery storage procurement in parts of Latin America are changing dispatch and revenue models for renewables, shifting emphasis to co-located storage and flexible generation.

Developers and utilities are signaling stronger appetite for paired solar-plus-storage and grid-scale batteries to firm output and participate in ancillary markets. The scenario implication is that renewable projects without storage could face curtailment or lower merchant value in increasingly flexible grids. Storage procurement cycles and inverter integration plans are becoming critical path items for new projects. Market design changes that reward fast frequency response and capacity firming are likely to further accelerate storage demand. Financing models are adapting to include revenue stacking from arbitrage and ancillary services. Operators should watch for interconnection queue impacts as mixed-resource projects increase complexity. The expectation is a medium-term uplift in storage capacity but with near-term integration and market participation hurdles.

What to watch
  • Interconnection queue changes for co-located projects
  • Market rule updates affecting ancillary service compensation
Action

Incorporate storage options into project financial models and engage early on market participation rules for ancillary services.
