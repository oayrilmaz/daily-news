Substation cybersecurity signals

Signals are pointing to growing attention on remote access controls and supply-chain security for substation automation as operators bolster defenses against cyber threats.

Operators are reassessing remote access models and limiting broad VPN-based connectivity to protection and control devices. Expectations include increased segmentation and zero-trust principles applied to OT networks. Scenario watch: tighter remote access rules could complicate vendor support and remote diagnostics workflows. Utilities will likely demand higher cybersecurity assurances from OEMs and third-party service providers. Firmware and component provenance checks are being elevated in procurement criteria. Investments in detection and response for OT networks are increasing to reduce dwell time of potential intrusions. Training and tabletop exercises are being incorporated into commissioning plans to validate incident response.

What to watch
  • procurement requirements for cybersecurity posture from OEMs
  • changes to remote access and vendor support procedures
Action

Review OT network segmentation and vendor remote-access policies ahead of new deployments.
