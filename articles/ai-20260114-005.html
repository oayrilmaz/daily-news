Capacity and ancillary markets face winter volatility scenarios

Market signals point to elevated short-term price volatility in capacity and ancillary services as winter weather and supply variability intersect with higher demand, prompting scenario planning for procurement teams and traders.

Tight reserve margins and unexpected generator derates can compress capacity availability and force market-clearing prices upward. Ancillary services, particularly fast frequency response and reserves, may see price spikes if battery and flexible resources are not yet fully integrated. Market participants are likely to re-evaluate hedge positions and short-term procurement strategies to manage exposure. Price volatility also amplifies counterparty credit risk and may alter bid behaviors in day-ahead and real-time markets. These conditions increase the value of flexible assets and deepen the business case for storage providing fast-response services. What to monitor are nodal price spreads, ancillary service clearing prices, and capacity auction signals over the coming days.

What to watch
  • Real-time and day-ahead price spreads
  • Ancillary service clearing prices and volumes
  • Generator outage postings and derate reports
Action

Re-evaluate short-term hedges and test flexible asset dispatch strategies to capture ancillary revenue while managing downside risk.
