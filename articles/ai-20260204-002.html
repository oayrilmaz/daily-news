Digital-twin pilots raise cyber-physical integration questions at substations

Early digital-twin deployments for substation asset management are generating signals about integration complexity and new cyber-physical attack surfaces that utilities should monitor as pilots scale.

Operators running digital-twin pilots report improved planning fidelity for maintenance and fault simulations. These pilot systems often draw data from diverse telemetry and vendor systems, increasing integration overhead. The heterogeneity of protocols and middleware is creating patterns of configuration drift that can complicate model validation. Cybersecurity teams flag the potential for expanded access pathways when digital twins are linked to operational technology. Asset managers are weighing the trade-offs between simulation benefits and the need for strict boundary controls. Expect governance frameworks and secure data-handoff patterns to become central as deployments move from pilot to production.

What to watch
  • configuration drift incidents during pilot expansion
  • changes to OT network segmentation post-deployment
Action

Treat digital-twin rollouts as controlled experiments with strict OT/IT boundaries and formal change-management checkpoints.
