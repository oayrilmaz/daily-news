Curtailed wind and solar scenarios persist near congested interconnects

Signals of persistent curtailment are emerging near congested interconnects, prompting scenario planning for storage and grid-enhancement prioritization.

Grid congestion continues to show up in nodal output differentials between generation offers and actual dispatch in several coastal and island-connected regions. Developers are increasingly pricing storage co-located with new renewable builds to mitigate curtailment risk. System operators are exploring grid reinforcements, but timelines for transmission upgrades remain multi-year. Merchant projects face scenario risks where curtailed revenue materially affects project finance assumptions. Policy levers to accelerate interconnection queue processing are being discussed in multiple jurisdictions as a mitigation path. This intelligence framing highlights the operational and financial trade-offs developers and system planners face amid persistent congestion signals. The situation remains a watch scenario rather than confirmed mandated curtailments for specific projects.

What to watch
  • Interconnection queue movement and transmission reinforcement approvals.
  • Storage procurement and co-location announcements from major developers.
Action

Stress-test project revenue models with different curtailment and storage dispatch scenarios and revisit interconnection risk contingencies.
