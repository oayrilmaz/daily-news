Incentive shifts for renewables and storage procurement

Signals indicate regional authorities are revisiting incentive structures to accelerate storage deployment alongside renewables; policy pivots could alter project bankability.

Several jurisdictions are evaluating capacity payments, long-term contracts, and tax incentives targeted at storage paired with renewables. These policy conversations appear driven by expectations around system flexibility needs. Scenario watch: rapid policy shifts could change project timelines, favoring vertically integrated sponsors who can internalize revenue stacking. What to monitor: draft incentive schedules, procurement timelines, and clarity on grid-connection cost allocation. This is an intelligence expectation rather than a report of enacted measures. Developers and financiers should update underwriting assumptions to reflect possible incentive realignments.

What to watch
  • Draft incentive schedules and procurement announcements
  • Guidance on grid-connection cost allocation
Action

Revisit financial models to account for potential new storage incentives and altered revenue stacks.
