Distributed automation pilots focus on islanding and blackstart readiness

There is growing attention on distributed automation schemes that enable controlled islanding and faster blackstart responses, particularly in regions with large renewable portfolios.

Planners are exploring layered automation where local control systems can sustain critical loads during broader system disturbances. Signals show interest in combining microgrid controllers with automated restoration sequences.

Interoperability with legacy protection and coordination with system operators are the primary technical hurdles. Expectations are for extensive staged testing to prove safe interactions between distributed controllers and central dispatch.

Scenario watch: well-executed pilots could shorten restoration timelines and reduce load-shedding impacts, but miscoordination risks could create unexpected protection trips if not thoroughly validated.

What to monitor: test protocols for coordinated islanding, blackstart sequencing validation, and procedures for manual override and operator situational awareness.

This piece is an intelligence perspective to inform grid planning teams considering distributed automation for resilience and restoration capabilities.

What to watch
  • islanding test outcomes
  • blackstart sequence validation
Action

Develop coordinated test plans with system operators before deploying distributed automation pilots.
