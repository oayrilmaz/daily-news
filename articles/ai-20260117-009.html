HVDC converter station build signals for interconnect projects

Planning signals point to increased interest in HVDC converter station deployments to support long-distance interconnects and large renewable power transfers across regions.

Regional planners are signaling the need for converter stations as part of broader interconnect and export-focused renewable strategies. HVDC corridors present technical and commercial options for long-distance bulk transfer with lower losses. Siting and water-cooled converter designs are being assessed in hot-climate contexts, raising engineering considerations around cooling, reliability, and footprint. Supply-chain implications for power electronics and large smoothing reactors are being factored into project timelines. Developers are considering staged converter capacity and uprate pathways to match generation buildouts. The scenario indicates that early-stage converter station procurement and factory acceptance planning will be critical to avoid multi-year delays. Coordination between coastal and inland grid planners is emerging as a common challenge for these projects.

What to watch
  • project-level converter procurement timelines
  • technical studies on cooling and reliability for hot-climate converter designs
Action

Integrate cooling and procurement contingency planning into HVDC project schedules and engage vendors on staged delivery options.
