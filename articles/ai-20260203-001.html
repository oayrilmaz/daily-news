Winter demand variability increases ancillary service pressure

Signals point to higher-than-usual ancillary service deployments as short-term demand swings challenge reserve margins; operators are reportedly looking at fast-response options and market interventions.

Intelligence indicates greater reliance on frequency response and fast-start resources during recent cold snaps. Grid operators are under pressure to balance energy and reserves amid forecast uncertainty and distributed load behavior. Scenario watch suggests additional activations of demand response and peaker assets in constrained pockets. Expectations include tighter real-time markets and potential price spikes for ancillary products. System planners may accelerate procurement of fast-acting battery services and contracted grid-forming resources. What to monitor are intra-day reserves, start-up notices for thermal units, and DR activation patterns. These signals could influence near-term procurement and dispatch decisions for utilities and large consumers. Operational risk increases where transmission constraints prevent efficient redispatch. Interactions between retail DR aggregation and wholesale markets remain a second-order risk. If sustained, the pattern could push longer-term targeting of inertia substitutes and synchronous compensation measures.

What to watch
  • real-time reserve price movements
  • fast-start generator dispatch notices
Action

Review contingency activation plans for fast-response resources and validate telemetry for reserve-capable assets.
