Grid-forming inverter pilots broaden as a mitigation signal for inertia loss

Signals suggest a growing wave of pilots deploying grid-forming inverters to mitigate inertia loss as inverter-based resources reach material shares of supply in several North American balancing areas.

Multiple utility and aggregator pilots are reportedly expanding scope to test grid-forming inverter behaviors under fault and islanding conditions. These pilots are being framed as a way to restore synchronous-like responses without synchronous machines. Observers expect the pilots to focus on ride-through, frequency response, black-start capability, and coordinated control under high solar penetration. Scenario watch: if pilots show consistent stability benefits, regulators may push procurement preferences for grid-forming capabilities. What to monitor: interconnection queue filings referencing grid-forming specs and test results from demonstration projects. Operationalizing grid-forming inverters will require new protection settings and coordination protocols with transmission operators. Deployment timelines will likely be staggered, creating mixed-technology pockets that stress system operation in transition periods.

What to watch
  • watch for interconnection filings referencing grid-forming specs
  • monitor pilot test reports on frequency and fault response
Action

Review protection and control strategies for upcoming inverter-heavy connections and track pilot outcomes for spec updates.
