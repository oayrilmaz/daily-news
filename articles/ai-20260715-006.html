Signals of new bottlenecks in battery-grade cobalt and nickel

Expectations are building around renewed supply-side friction for battery-grade cobalt and nickel, with project delays and processing capacity constraints likely to propagate into the mid-term supply chain.

Exploration-to-production timelines remain elongated for high-grade battery mineral projects due to permitting, financing, and processing bottlenecks. Refining capacity that upgrades raw ore to battery-grade chemicals is a particular pinch point. Downstream manufacturers may face tighter feedstock availability, pressuring prices and lead times for storage and inverter suppliers that rely on these materials. Strategic stockpiling is becoming more common, but it raises working-capital and storage-cost considerations. Smaller players with flexible sourcing may gain near-term advantage, while large projects could face schedule shifts. Scenario watch: a cluster of refinery outages or permit delays could push OEM procurement windows and force design substitutions for some storage technologies.

What to watch
  • refinery capacity reports
  • permit and financing notices on major projects

Map critical-mineral exposure across your supply chain and explore alternative chemistries or recycled feedstock options.
