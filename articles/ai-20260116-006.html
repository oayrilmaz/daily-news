Procurement diversification to mitigate concentration risk

Signals indicate procurement teams and project developers are increasing diversification efforts across geographies and recycling pathways to hedge against concentrated supply risks for battery- and turbine-grade materials.

Companies are reportedly accelerating studies into alternative feedstocks and expanded recycling of cathode materials to reduce reliance on primary mined sources. Expectations include more long-term offtakes with a broader set of suppliers and increased investment in refining domestically. Scenario watch: geopolitically driven trade disruptions could still materialize and stress timelines for new projects, making short-term stockpiling more likely. Investors and OEMs may raise technical specifications to allow for a wider range of acceptable feedstock chemistries. Exploration and junior-miner signals suggest a staggered supply response that may not align quickly with demand surges. Policy incentives for domestic processing could shift project economics and attract capital into near-term capacity expansions. These dynamics may influence both capital allocation and contract structures across the supply chain.

What to watch
  • New refining capacity announcements and policy incentives.
  • Changes in long-term offtake patterns and stockpiling behavior.
Action

Map your critical-minerals exposure and accelerate testing of recycled and alternative feedstocks to broaden procurement options.
