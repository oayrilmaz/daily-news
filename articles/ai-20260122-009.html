Converter stations and HVDC readiness

Expectations are forming around increased investment in converter-station capacity to support long-distance renewables transmission and cross-border flows.

As remote renewables build out, converter stations become critical nodes that link asynchronous systems and long HVDC links. Technical complexity and skilled labor needs for commissioning converter stations present unique project risks. Testing regimes for protection coordination and harmonic management are more demanding than for conventional AC substations. Supply chain for power electronics and specialized transformers can be the pacing items in delivery schedules. Scenario watch: coordination between system operators on protection schemes and test plans is a leading indicator of commissioning risk. What to monitor: availability of skilled commissioning teams, specialized transformer lead times, and interoperability test outcomes. Project planners should factor in extended commissioning windows and staged energization plans to reduce go-live risk.

What to watch
  • Availability of specialized commissioning crews and test schedules
  • Lead times for power electronics and special transformers
Action

Include extended commissioning and interoperability testing in project timelines and contract terms.
