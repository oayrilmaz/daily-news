Extreme temperatures and cooling loads stress HV equipment ratings

Signals from operational corridors suggest extreme ambient temperature patterns are elevating transformer and switchgear cooling demands; expectations include temporary deratings and scenario watch for accelerated insulation aging in poorly ventilated yards.

Sustained high ambient temperatures raise oil and winding temperatures, pushing units closer to nameplate thermal limits and impacting loss-of-life calculations. GIS and air-insulated switchgear experience higher cooling loads that can reveal latent design margins or installation shortfalls. There is an expectation that some utilities will deploy temporary derating or cooling augmentation while planning retrofits for high-exposure sites. What to monitor are thermal rating advisories, offline diagnostic schedules for high-temperature-exposed units, and requests for additional load control. A scenario watch highlights the risk of insulation degradation accelerating failure probabilities if thermal excursions persist. EPCs and OEMs may be asked to verify rated cooling performance in site acceptance tests to validate as-built thermal management. Operators should prioritize thermal imaging and dielectric testing on units with historical high-load exposure to identify early degradation. Asset managers need updated end-of-life forecasts and potential replacement timing adjustments based on increased thermal stress.

What to watch
  • Thermal rating advisories for substations
  • Increased diagnostic or offline testing requests
Action

Increase thermal monitoring on exposed HV assets and validate cooling performance during site acceptance tests.
