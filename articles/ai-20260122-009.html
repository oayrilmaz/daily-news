HVDC interconnector scenarios and regional grid integration

Scenario signals point to renewed interest in HVDC interconnectors to balance renewable-rich areas with load centers, creating planning and converter-supply considerations for regional transmission builds.

Interconnector scenarios are framed around smoothing renewable variability and enabling cross-border energy trade. This requires careful coordination on converter station siting and grid code harmonization. HVDC converter lead times and integration complexity remain significant factors in project schedules. Expectations include pre-ordered converter modules and staged converter station commissioning to manage risk. Regional operators may need new operational agreements for power flows, congestion allocation, and emergency support to ensure reliable interconnector use. Converter technology choices—voltage-source converters versus line-commutated technologies—will have implications for controllability, fault response, and ancillary service provision across networks. Supply-chain clarity for power electronics and high-voltage DC insulation systems will be key, as these components often have constrained global capacity and long manufacturing lead times. Financial structuring will need to reconcile regulated transmission returns with merchant components if cross-border trade is allowed, making project bankability more complex. Monitor planning documents for interconnector capacity targets and procurement notices for converter equipment or turnkey HVDC packages.

What to watch
  • Procurement notices for HVDC converter stations or turnkey packages.
  • Draft operational agreements for cross-border power dispatch and congestion rules.
Action

Assess converter supply options and model interconnector operational scenarios in planning studies.
