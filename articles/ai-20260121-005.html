Capacity market signals may shift with higher storage participation

Market signals suggest storage is poised to influence capacity markets and price formation, creating an expectation that procurement mechanisms and auction designs will need rapid recalibration.

As storage assets become price-makers in short-duration capacity products, auction structures and qualification rules are under informal scrutiny.

Storage's revenue stack—capacity, ancillary services, and energy arbitrage—changes how operators and asset owners bid into seasonal and daily markets.

A scenario to monitor is capacity clearing prices compressing in zones with substantial short-duration storage, pressuring peaking assets and changing investment calculus.

Market operators may need to tighten testing regimes and delivery verification to prevent gaming of short-duration products.

Policy adjustments could include minimum-duration requirements or differentiated product definitions to preserve reliability signals for long-duration capacity.

Stakeholders should expect consultations on product design and be prepared to deliver operational test data demonstrating sustained availability.

What to watch
  • Auction rule discussions on minimum duration and delivery proofs
  • Operational test data requirements for storage qualification
Action

Prepare operational performance records for storage assets and engage in product design consultations to influence qualification rules.
