Transformer lead times push refurb market and alternative sourcing

Signals point to continued pressure on new transformer lead times, prompting more operators to evaluate refurbished units and modular substations as schedule hedges.

Primary equipment suppliers still face extended production schedules driven by specialized winding capacity and test-lab throughput. Grid owners are increasingly weighing refurbished or reconditioned transformers to bridge delivery gaps, particularly for medium-voltage distribution units. These secondary-market moves can restore capacity quickly but introduce variability in expected remaining life and warranty terms. Modular, skid-mounted substations are also gaining attention as a faster path to site energization, especially where civil works are the schedule driver. Procurement teams are adopting stricter acceptance testing protocols for used equipment to manage reliability risk. Financial teams are recalibrating capex-to-opex trade-offs when rental or interim units reduce outage durations. Scenario planning should account for longer procurement tails for bespoke high-voltage units and the operational impacts of mixed-new-and-refurb fleets.

What to watch
  • Award notices for transformer refurb contracts and rental agreements.
  • Regulatory guidance on acceptance testing for used HV equipment.
Action

Audit spare inventories and acceptance-test procedures, and include refurbishment options in near-term procurement plans.
