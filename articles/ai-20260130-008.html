EPC scheduling under material constraints

Signals indicate EPCs in several markets are adjusting schedules and contractor arrangements in response to material and logistics uncertainty tied to critical components.

Expectations are that phased delivery and modular construction will be emphasized to reduce exposure to single-shipment risks. There is a scenario watch that tight windows for grid tie-ins could shift commissioning sequences and incur cost escalation. EPCs may favor local sourcing where feasible to shorten lead times. Vendors could offer extended warranty and inventory-financing products to ease project cashflow pressures. What to monitor are change-order frequencies and vendor acceptance of phased commissioning plans. A potential scenario sees increased use of temporary generation or islanding approaches to meet contractual availability targets.

What to watch
  • Frequency of change orders and schedule slips
  • Adoption of local sourcing for critical components
  • Availability of vendor inventory-financing offerings
Action

Incorporate phased commissioning options into contracts and secure contingency financing for critical components.
