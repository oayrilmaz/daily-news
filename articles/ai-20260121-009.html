HVDC link projects face regulatory and permitting scenario friction

Signals point to continued regulatory and permitting friction for cross-border HVDC projects, creating a scenario where schedule certainty depends on early stakeholder alignment and risk allocation mechanisms.

HVDC projects offer strategic value for long-distance transfers and renewables integration, but permitting complexity remains a material schedule risk. These signals suggest that regulatory windows and public-consultation processes will continue to extend timelines. Scenario watch: misaligned national approval timetables that stall long-lead equipment procurement and strand converters in storage. Developers should monitor permitting milestones and secure conditional engineering procurement contracts where possible. Early socialization with stakeholders and transparent environmental planning can reduce the probability of late-stage objections. Transmission owners may use staged contracting to hedge against permit slippage. The commercial implication is a need for contract structures that fairly allocate permit and schedule risk across stakeholders.

What to watch
  • National permitting milestone dates and consultation outcomes
  • Progress on converter and cable procurement contracts linked to permit conditions
Action

Embed permitting contingency clauses in procurement contracts and begin stakeholder engagement early in project lifecycles.
