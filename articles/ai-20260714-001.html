Heat surge and PV ramp create reserve squeeze signals

Signals of elevated evening ramp risk are emerging as persistent heat and high distributed PV production compress reserve margins, prompting scenario planning across control rooms.

Operational indicators point to narrower reserves during late-afternoon to evening ramps as rooftop PV declines and air-conditioning demand holds. Grid operators are likely recalibrating contingency strategies to account for more frequent, sharper net-load swings. AI-driven forecasting tools are being tested to bridge gaps in situational awareness but introduce new OT integration risk vectors. Short-term thermal ratings and dynamic line rating pilots appear more attractive where corridor cooling limits are approached. The situation raises a scenario where faster-than-expected PV curtailment or localized congestion drives emergency redispatch actions. Market signals could tighten ancillary service prices if reserve shortages persist over several days. Prepare for increased coordination requests from transmission operators to distribution companies and potential temporary operational constraints on non-firm tie lines.

What to watch
  • Real-time reserve and ancillary market price spikes
  • Alerts on dynamic line rating or short-term rating activations
Action

Validate near-term contingency plans and confirm communication channels between TSO and DSO operations.
