Permitting and funding frictions slow interconnection upgrades

Policy signals indicate uneven progress on permitting and funding mechanisms for transmission upgrades that support large-scale renewables, creating a scenario where interconnection queues and project delays persist despite capital availability.

Regulatory committees are debating allocation of costs between developers and consumers. Local permitting timelines remain the dominant gating factor in multiple jurisdictions. Scenario watch includes extended queue queues and economic re-rating of late-stage projects. Expectations are that some projects will be restructured or re-scoped to manage interconnection risk. What to monitor are regulatory filings that redefine cost allocation or introduce expedited permitting tracks. Grid planners may need to prioritize incremental upgrades that unlock multiple projects rather than single large interventions. The political economy of funding will shape which regions accelerate and which lag.

What to watch
  • Regulatory filings on cost allocation and permitting reform.
  • Announcements of prioritized transmission upgrade lists.
Action

Engage with regulators on interconnection priorities and consider staged project designs to reduce queue exposure.
