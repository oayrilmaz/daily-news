Substation automation growth raises cybersecurity and integration signals

Expectations are rising for accelerated substation automation and remote control deployments, framing a concurrent scenario watch for cybersecurity exposure and vendor integration risks.

Utilities are signaling investments in SCADA upgrades and RTU deployments to enhance observability and remote operations.\nIncreased connectivity expands attack surfaces and elevates the need for robust cybersecurity hygiene and segmentation.\nVendor ecosystems are diversifying, which raises integration and interoperability challenges across protection and control systems.\nWhat to watch: patch management practices, multi-vendor integration tests, and cybersecurity incident reporting trends.\nOperators face trade-offs between rapid automation benefits and the maturity of operational security practices.\nTraining and change management for operations staff are critical to avoid configuration errors that could mimic cyber incidents.\nRegulatory attention to operational cyber-resilience is likely to grow as adoption accelerates.

What to watch
  • Vendor interoperability test results and cybersecurity compliance updates.
  • Reports of configuration errors or patch-related outages in automated substations.
Action

Strengthen network segmentation and require multi-vendor integration testing in automation procurements.
