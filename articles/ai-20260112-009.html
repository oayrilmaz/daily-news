Distribution automation pilots and DR aggregation

Distribution utilities in several markets are signaling pilots for automation and distributed resource aggregation to relieve local constraints and unlock flexibility services.

Expect pilots to focus on advanced volt/VAR control, feeder-level DER orchestration, and aggregated demand response. Scenario watch includes integration challenges between legacy SCADA systems and modern DERMS platforms. Data quality, latency, and cybersecurity emerge as operational risks as controls move closer to the edge. Regulatory frameworks for compensated DER aggregation are uneven, creating commercial uncertainty for aggregators. Utilities may prioritize feeder segments with high solar penetration to test curtailment reduction strategies. Success in pilots could inform tariff design and capacity market participation for aggregated resources. Equipment interoperability and standardized telemetry will be decisive for scalability. Near-term, manual overrides and fallback procedures will remain necessary during pilot phases. Broader rollout depends on demonstrable reliability and clear commercial pathways for participants.

What to watch
  • Pilot results on feeder control and DER participation rates
  • Regulatory updates on aggregator compensation and telemetry requirements
Action

Engage with utilities on pilot designs and ensure DER assets meet telemetry and control interoperability requirements.
