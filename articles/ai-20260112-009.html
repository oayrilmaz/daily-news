Converter station and HVDC commissioning schedules under scenario pressure

Expectations suggest HVDC and converter station projects in some corridors may face commissioning pressure from synchronizing timelines and supplier bandwidth constraints.

HVDC systems require tightly coordinated commissioning across converters, control systems, and AC interface equipment, increasing schedule fragility. Supplier bandwidth for power-electronics modules and specialized testing can become a bottleneck when multiple projects converge in time. Unexpected firmware integration issues between control platforms can extend commissioning windows, especially where interoperability tests are limited. Environmental and logistics constraints in remote converter locations further complicate the ability to accelerate remediation work. If commissioning slips, power transfer delays could ripple into contracted energy schedules and commercial arrangements between utilities. Owners and EPCs may lean on phased commissioning strategies to deliver partial transfer capability while final tests conclude. Risk mitigation through dedicated factory-acceptance testing and cross-vendor integration rehearsals reduces the likelihood of late-stage surprises. Monitor planned commissioning windows and supplier staffing models to assess the probability of slippage.

What to watch
  • Supplier staffing levels and factory-acceptance test schedules for converter modules.
  • Planned vs. actual commissioning milestones for major HVDC projects.
Action

Build phased handover plans and require detailed FAT evidence before site mobilization to lower commissioning risk.
