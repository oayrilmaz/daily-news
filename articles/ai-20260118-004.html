Curtailment signals persist where transmission upgrades lag renewables growth

Signals indicate continued curtailment pressure in European regions where fast-paced wind and solar additions are outstripping transmission upgrade timelines, creating operational and market scenarios that could shape procurement and siting decisions.

Rapid renewables deployment in favorable resource pockets has produced pockets of high instantaneous generation relative to local demand. Transmission project schedules and permitting bottlenecks are a limiting scenario for evacuation capacity in several corridors. Curtailment economics are starting to factor into project-level yield expectations and offtake contract designs. Energy storage procurement is increasingly considered a hedge to reduce lost energy and firm generation profiles. Policymakers and regulators may face pressure to accelerate grid expansion or incentivize grid-supporting storage. What to monitor are interconnector commissioning timelines and grid reinforcement procurement notices.

What to watch
  • transmission reinforcement procurement and timelines
  • storage tenders aimed at congestion relief
Action

Factor potential curtailment into project financial models and engage on grid reinforcement planning to protect renewable yields.
