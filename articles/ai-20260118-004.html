Expectation: Storage co-location with renewables gains traction as congestion hedge

Multiple planning scenarios show increased interest in co-locating battery storage with wind and solar projects to manage both market volatility and local grid congestion.

Co-located storage is being modeled to arbitrage short market spreads and to firm output during constrained transmission periods. Developers are including storage as a tool to meet contractual availability windows for offtakers and to capture ancillary service revenues. Interconnection studies are increasingly assessing combined project impacts rather than treating storage and renewables separately. Permitting and land-use scenarios are evolving as projects aim to optimize shared balance-of-plant and grid connection points. Financial models are testing staggered commissioning where storage comes online ahead of full renewable build to secure longer-term offtake pathways. The scenario implication: integrated project design teams should align technical, regulatory, and commercial timelines early.

What to watch
  • Interconnection study approaches treating hybrid projects jointly
  • Of-take agreements specifying firm capacity with storage
Action

Prioritize integrated interconnection studies and align commercial structures to capture storage value streams.
