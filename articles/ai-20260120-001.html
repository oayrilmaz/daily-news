AI control systems expand but increase operational and cyber scenarios

Deployments of model-based and AI-assisted grid controls are showing benefits in efficiency and congestion management, while signaling new operational and cyber-risk scenarios for operators and vendors.

Signals from multiple deployments indicate AI models are improving short-term load and congestion forecasts, enabling closer-to-real-time dispatch adjustments. These systems can tighten margins on reserves and reduce fuel use, but they also create new dependencies on data quality and model availability. Operators may face scenarios where model drift, data poisoning, or degraded telemetry lead to suboptimal actions if fallbacks are immature. There is an expectation that vendors will publish more guidance on validation, explainability, and defensive measures in coming quarters. Security teams should watch for attempts to probe ML inference paths or to manipulate training data via upstream sensors and meters. Regulators and grid planners may need to update operating practices to include model validation cycles and incident playbooks specific to AI-assisted decisions. What to monitor in the next 72 hours are vendor advisories, grid operator bulletins, and any reported anomalies in control room tools that reference AI modules.

What to watch
  • vendor model validation advisories
  • control-room anomaly reports
Action

Inventory AI/ML-enabled control systems and validate fallback procedures and data integrity checks within 30 days.
