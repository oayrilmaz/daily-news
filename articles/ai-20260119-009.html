Operational technology cybersecurity signals prompt scenario readiness

Signals of increasing OT-targeted activity against energy infrastructure are pushing operators to re-evaluate substation cybersecurity posture and incident response readiness as a priority.

Operators are reporting more reconnaissance-style activity that targets legacy OT protocols and remote access vectors.

This increases the expectation that older substation control systems are a likely attack surface if not segmented and monitored.

Scenario planning should assume attempts at reconnaissance could precede disruptive actions and therefore prioritize detection and containment.

Investment in network segmentation, robust remote access controls, and anomaly detection is being signaled as an immediate mitigation set.

Training and tabletop exercises that include OT incident scenarios will improve operational readiness and reduce recovery time.

Procurement specifications for new protection relays and RTUs should include cybersecurity baseline requirements to avoid introducing new exposures.

What to monitor are indicators of compromise on control networks and vendor patch advisories affecting OT components.

Near-term risk reduction often comes from configuration changes and improved monitoring rather than wholesale equipment replacement.

What to watch
  • indicators of compromise in control networks
  • vendor advisories and OT patch releases
Action

Prioritize network segmentation and anomaly detection for substations and run OT tabletop exercises this quarter.
