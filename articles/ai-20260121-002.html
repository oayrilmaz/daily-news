OT/IT convergence at substations increases attack-surface scenarios

Signals point to accelerating OT/IT integration in substations, which could improve operational visibility while expanding the scenario set for cyber and supply-chain risk.

Utilities and contractors are reportedly integrating more IP-based sensors, edge compute, and cloud analytics at substation perimeters. That integration promises faster fault detection and predictive maintenance use cases. However, it also connects legacy protection systems to broader networks in ways that were previously constrained. These expanded interfaces create potential vectors for cyber intrusion, though widespread exploitation is not claimed. Supply chain dependencies for critical relay firmware and secure gateways are becoming more visible in procurement planning. Patching and lifecycle management scenarios grow complex as heterogeneous devices proliferate. Insurer and regulator attention may follow as these signals strengthen, potentially prompting baseline cybersecurity requirements. The overall scenario balances operational gains against elevated systemic risk from interconnected components.

What to watch
  • Procurement notices for IP-based substation gear and edge compute.
  • Regulatory or insurer guidance on substation cybersecurity baselines.
Action

Assess current segmentation, firmware management, and vendor SLAs to reduce exposure from OT/IT convergence.
