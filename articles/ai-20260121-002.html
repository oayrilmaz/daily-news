Aging protection relays and cyber-physical convergence create layered risks

Signals show that a mix of legacy protection relays and new digital IEDs in substations is creating a convergence of cyber and physical vulnerabilities that warrants a dedicated inspection and upgrade strategy.

Inventory mismatches between legacy electromechanical relays and modern intelligent electronic devices are appearing in several regional asset lists. This mixed-architecture posture increases complexity for both protection coordination and incident response. Cybersecurity updates often lag firmware compatibility testing, producing windows where patches cannot be applied without service risk. Scenario watch: simultaneous firmware backlog and physical equipment aging could extend outage durations during certain fault types. There is an observable push toward standardized hardware-module replacements in procurement signals. Expectation: accelerated interest in microgrid-islanding and local protection schemes to mitigate exposure. Operators are being advised to prioritize relay testing and spares rationalization. This briefing is scenario-based and intended to surface risks to substation resilience.

What to watch
  • inspect relay firmware versions and patch backlog
  • validate spare parts inventory for legacy relay models
Action

Prioritize a phased relay modernization plan and require cyber-compatible patch windows in maintenance schedules.
