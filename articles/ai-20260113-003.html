Data center operators exploring behind-the-meter microgrids

There are signals that large data-center operators are piloting behind-the-meter microgrids and DER aggregation to secure resilience and control marginal energy costs, creating a scenario where distribution networks see different load profiles and reliability requirements.

Operators framed microgrids as a way to reduce exposure to distribution outages and wholesale price spikes. Early configurations tend to pair short-duration storage, gas gensets for extended outages, and sometimes on-site PV to lower daily energy spend. Expect negotiations with local utilities to focus on interconnection terms and visibility of exported energy. Scenario watch: wider adoption could change utility planning assumptions around peak growth and stranded asset risk. Integration pain points include harmonizing transfer schemes, anti-islanding protection, and coordination with distribution automation. Permitting and local grid codes will influence deployment timelines. Keep an eye on pilot locations and whether projects pursue bilateral service agreements or market participation.

What to watch
  • interconnection contract language and export limits
  • utility requirements for telemetry and control
Action

Engage early with utilities on telemetry and control expectations when designing microgrids to avoid late-stage scope changes.
