Solar-plus-storage deployment reshapes evening load stress scenarios

Expectations from developers and system planners indicate that paired solar-plus-storage deployments in parts of Asia are altering evening ramp needs and creating new stress patterns for substations and interconnects.

Signals point to projects that prioritize dispatchable capacity during evening peaks, reducing midday curtailment but increasing synchronized charging windows late in the day. This pattern increases thermal cycling on transformers and may elevate short-duration overload events for feeders not designed for bidirectional flows. Grid planners should consider distribution asset ratings and protection coordination as charging and discharging patterns evolve. There is an expectation that interconnection studies will need to model aggregated inverter behavior and frequency response during sunset ramps. OEMs and EPCs might face revised product specifications emphasizing dynamic thermal ratings and rapid frequency support. Monitor whether regional grid codes update requirements for storage participation in voltage and frequency regulation. Operational readiness exercises that include high solar-plus-storage penetration scenarios will be important.

What to watch
  • Interconnection study language on aggregated inverter behavior and dynamic thermal ratings
  • Grid code updates on storage participation in system services
Action

Re-run interconnection and thermal models for feeders with expected synchronized BESS charging/discharging windows.
