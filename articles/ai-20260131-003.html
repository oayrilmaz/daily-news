Modular ESS and hybrid cooling rising in new data center designs

Design teams are signaling a shift toward modular energy storage and hybrid cooling to manage power resilience and PUE as procurement cycles tighten.

Data center operators are favoring containerized battery systems that can be scaled in discrete phases during site commissioning. Hybrid cooling approaches combining air economizers with liquid loops are being evaluated to reduce chiller loads and improve PUE in warmer regions. Scenario watch highlights integration complexity between modular ESS, on-site generation and existing UPS controls during staged builds. Expectations include higher scrutiny of BESS thermal management and lifecycle replacement scenarios in procurement reviews. What to monitor are supplier roadmaps for containerized batteries with second-life reuse pathways and standardized interconnects. Operators may prioritize modularity to de-risk schedule slips and to localize failure domains when commissioning new halls. Regulatory engagement on fire and safety codes for containerized systems will matter for permitting timelines.

What to watch
  • Permitting clarifications for containerized ESS
  • Supplier interoperability standards for modular systems
Action

Require standardized interconnect specs and early engagement with permitting authorities for containerized ESS and hybrid cooling systems.
