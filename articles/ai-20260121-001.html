Operational AI models introduce new control-layer risk scenarios for transmission operators

Expectations are rising that AI-driven operational tools, while improving situational awareness, will create novel failure modes that require new oversight and validation regimes.

Signals suggest more ISOs and large utilities are piloting models for load forecasting and contingency assessment. These models can speed decisions but may embed hidden biases from training data, producing confidence where it is unwarranted. Scenario watch should include model drift during atypical weather and rare equipment outages. Operators may face combinatorial errors when automated recommendations interact with traditional protection systems. Liability and governance frameworks are not yet standardized across regions, increasing operational ambiguity. Risk-mitigation playbooks that emphasize human-in-the-loop validation and conservative fallback settings are likely to expand.

What to watch
  • Unexpected model outputs during extreme weather events
  • Policy statements on AI governance from major ISOs
Action

Review AI governance and incident-response procedures and prioritize human override mechanisms in near-term deployments.
