Recycling and circular-economy signals could ease raw-material pressure

Signals point to acceleration in recycling and circular-economy initiatives for battery and transformer materials, which could moderate raw-material pressures over the medium term.

Industrial actors are reportedly scaling trials of material recovery processes for lithium, copper, and rare-earth-bearing components. There is an expectation that commercial-scale recycling will take several years to meaningfully alter primary demand dynamics. Scenario watch: policy incentives and producer responsibility schemes could accelerate recycling adoption, shifting investment priorities. What to monitor are pilot results on recovery rates, capital commitments to recycling capacity, and supportive regulatory frameworks. Short-term impacts on project schedules are likely limited, but medium-term supply dynamics could improve if recycling reaches scale. Manufacturers and project owners should incorporate secondary-material sourcing options into procurement strategies to hedge primary-supply risk.

What to watch
  • recovery rate disclosures
  • producer responsibility policies
Action

Map secondary-material sourcing options and monitor recycling pilot outcomes for procurement planning.
