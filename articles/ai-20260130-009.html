HVDC converter station maintenance demand rising

Signals point to rising maintenance demand for HVDC converter stations as capacity expands, creating a scenario where specialized outage windows and spare-part logistics will become more consequential for transmission planning.

The ramp of cross-border and offshore HVDC projects appears to be increasing scheduled converter maintenance cycles. Expectations include more frequent needs for specialized spares and offshore intervention vessels. Scenario watch: constrained maintenance windows could force higher-cost emergency work during critical seasons. What to monitor are outage notices for converter stations and long-lead part orders. Grid operators will need more coordinated planning across interconnect corridors. OEMs may offer bundled maintenance and monitoring services to capture recurring revenue. Training and certification of specialist crews will be a limiting factor in near-term maintenance capacity. Insurance and contracting models may evolve to account for HVDC-specific outage risk.

What to watch
  • converter station outage notices
  • orders for long-lead HVDC spares
Action

Coordinate cross-border maintenance planning and validate spare-part inventories for HVDC assets.
