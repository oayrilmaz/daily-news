Battery recycling capacity signals shift in secondary supply expectations

Investment signals in battery recycling hint at growing expectations for secondary supply to contribute materially to cathode feedstock availability in coming years.

Recycling offers a pathway to reduce reliance on primary extraction and to mitigate geopolitical concentration risks in upstream supply chains. Scaling recycling requires secure grid access for energy-intensive hydrometallurgical and pyrometallurgical processes. Policy incentives and producer responsibility rules are expected to accelerate feedstock volumes available to recyclers. EPCs with experience in chemical processing and high-voltage plant integration may see new opportunities for turnkey facilities. However, the timing of meaningful secondary supply depends heavily on collection rates, economics, and permitting timelines. Scenario watch: faster-than-expected scaling of recycling could relieve some pressure on raw material markets, but only if sorting and refining bottlenecks are resolved.

What to watch
  • New permits or financing announcements for large-scale recycling facilities.
  • Policy moves on producer responsibility and recycling targets.
Action

If planning recycling projects, prioritize energy supply agreements and integration with high-capacity grid connections.
