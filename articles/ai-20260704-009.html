Remote substation cyber and physical security remain a growing scenario risk

Remote and distributed substations are increasingly exposed to combined cyber and physical security threats, creating scenarios where coordinated attacks could amplify restoration complexity.

Remote access tools and telemetry systems have improved operational visibility but also present larger attack surfaces if not segmented properly. Physical security at smaller sites often lags the sophistication of OT protection, enabling multi-vector scenarios. Coordinated incidents that combine physical sabotage with targeted cyber intrusions can complicate diagnostics and extend restoration times. Utilities are exploring hardened communication paths, segmented control networks and recovery playbooks that assume degraded telemetry. What to monitor are unusual credential usage patterns, maintenance requests for access to critical devices, and procurement of ruggedized edge hardware. Investments in local automation that permits safe islanding without central control are emerging as a mitigation preference.

What to watch
  • Unusual remote access or credential use reports
  • Procurement notices for ruggedized edge devices and segmentation tools
Action

Harden segmentation, monitor remote access behavior, and exercise degraded-control recovery playbooks.
