Permitting backlogs signal delays for solar-plus-storage growth in key markets

Signals from interconnection queues and permitting offices suggest that solar-plus-storage projects face administrative delays that could push commercial timelines and influence investment returns.

Project developers report longer-than-expected wait times for environmental and grid-connection approvals in several jurisdictions. Interconnection queues are lengthening as more projects seek to pair storage with renewables to capture flexible revenues. Municipal and regional permitting resources are often under-staffed, creating bottlenecks particularly for larger-scale proposals. Financing models that assume near-term COD may need revision to accommodate administrative slippage and carrying costs. Storage adds complexity to permitting packages due to safety and fire-suppression requirements, increasing review time per project. Delays can create knock-on effects for equipment procurement, contractor scheduling, and tax-incentive qualification windows. Developers should engage local authorities early and consider phased permitting or pilot pathways to de-risk timelines. Scenario risk includes longer lead times for interconnection clusters and potential re-pricing of deals if market conditions shift.

What to watch
  • monitor queue movement and estimated approval timelines at regional grid operators
  • watch local permitting office staffing and policy changes affecting review processes
Action

Engage permitting authorities early and structure project milestones to absorb potential approval delays.
