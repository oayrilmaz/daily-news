Distributed generation complicates protection coordination

Signals from distribution operators suggest growing scenarios where high penetrations of distributed generation challenge existing protection schemes, increasing the risk of misoperation during faults.

The rising share of rooftop solar and behind-the-meter inverter systems alters fault current profiles and islanding behaviour. Traditional relay settings and coordination margins were not designed for bi-directional flow at scale, producing nuisance trips or delayed fault clearing in stress scenarios. Upgrading protection schemes requires both hardware changes and revised protection philosophies that incorporate inverter characteristics. EPCs and utilities face sequencing challenges to retrofit protection selectively without widespread outages during transitions. The operational learning curve for inverter-based resource behaviour in fault conditions is an ongoing concern that influences commissioning checklists. Short-term mitigation may rely on inverter firmware limits and stricter interconnection controls, which in turn affect customer export limits. Track interconnection queue changes and protection upgrade orders as leading indicators.

What to watch
  • Orders for protection relay upgrades and selective retrofit programs
  • Changes to interconnection rules limiting export or requiring ride-through capabilities
Action

Inventory protection schemes in feeder sections with rising DG penetration and plan targeted relay upgrades and coordination studies.
