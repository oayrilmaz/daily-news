Remote access signals raising relay security concerns

Signals indicate an uptick in vendor-facilitated remote access and maintenance sessions at substations, creating a scenario watch for cyber-physical exposure in protection relays and configuration management.

Several operators are expanding remote maintenance capabilities to reduce field visits and speed fault analysis. These expansions often rely on vendor remote-desktop, VPN, or cloud gateways that vary in authentication rigor. Expectations: inconsistent patching and credential management across third-party vendors increases the attack surface. Scenario watch: a misconfigured vendor access path could allow unauthorized changes to relay settings or firmware updates. What to monitor: inventory of vendor access accounts, multi-factor enforcement, and recent remote-session logs. Small changes to relay settings can materially alter protection coordination, so even incidental misconfigurations matter operationally. Remediation windows for protective-device discrepancies are often tight, which increases risk when remote sessions are frequent.

What to watch
  • Vendor remote-session volumes and authentication methods
  • Unusual relay configuration changes or unexpected firmware flashes
Action

Audit vendor access paths and enforce MFA and session recording for all remote maintenance.
