AI predictive maintenance for HV equipment shows promise but raises data-quality scenario risks

Signals from trials applying AI to transformer and breaker health detection indicate improved early warning capabilities, while also exposing model drift and data-quality risks that could undermine reliability if unaddressed.

Early deployments of AI for PD detection, thermal anomaly spotting, and vibration analysis are reportedly catching faults earlier than traditional thresholds. Expectations are that asset owners will adopt hybrid human-AI processes to validate alerts and avoid false positives. Scenario watch: model drift from sensor degradation or changing operating regimes could produce misleading signals, potentially prompting unnecessary interventions. What to monitor: reported false positive/false negative rates from pilot programs and strategies for retraining models. Data governance, sensor calibration schedules, and labeled failure datasets are emerging as critical enabling assets. Integration into existing SCADA and asset management workflows remains a practical barrier. Vendors offering closed-loop retraining and explainability features may have a commercialization advantage.

What to watch
  • track pilot performance metrics and model retraining cadences
  • monitor sensor calibration and labeled-failure dataset availability
Action

Implement governance for AI models, including retraining schedules and human validation gates before automating maintenance actions.
