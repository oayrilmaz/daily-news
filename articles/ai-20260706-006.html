Lithium and nickel supply signals indicate potential bottlenecks for battery scale-up

Upstream project delays and geopolitical supply signals suggest potential bottlenecks in lithium and nickel availability that could constrain battery manufacturing ramp plans if not mitigated.

Exploration-to-production timelines for key battery metals remain long and capital intensive, creating sensitivity to project delays. Signals include permitting hurdles, funding cycles, and refining capacity constraints that affect downstream feedstock availability. Nickel that meets strict battery-grade specifications is a particular bottleneck in several scenarios. Recycling and secondary supply are emerging as partial mitigations but require scale and regulatory alignment. These constraints could raise input price volatility and influence OEM sourcing strategies. Manufacturers may prioritize chemistries with different metal mixes or accelerate recycling integration to reduce exposure. Policy interventions to unlock domestic refining capacity are one possible scenario to ease pressure but would take time. Close tracking of project milestones and refinery throughput will offer near-term visibility on risk trajectories.

What to watch
  • Refinery throughput and commissioning schedules for battery-grade processing facilities.
  • Permitting and financing milestones for major upstream projects.
Action

Stress-test battery procurement and consider alternative chemistries or recycled feedstocks in sourcing plans.
