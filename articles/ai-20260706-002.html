Remote substation access and asset aging raise combined physical-cyber scenario risk

Signals point to a convergence of aging transformers, increased remote access, and constrained maintenance windows that could amplify outage scenarios at regional substations.

Aging HV transformers remain concentrated in key mid-Atlantic and Midwest corridors and may coincide with deferred maintenance cycles. Operators are expanding remote monitoring to address workforce limits, but increased connectivity introduces new cyber-attack surface. Scenario watchers note that physical failure modes can be exacerbated by interrupted monitoring or delayed on-site response. Insurance and resiliency planning are adjusting to account for compound risks rather than single-point failures. Vendors are promoting predictive analytics, but data quality and integration across legacy SCADA systems are uneven. Prioritization frameworks for targeted replacement versus asset rehabilitation are emerging in operator strategy sessions. Rapid mobilization plans for transformer unavailability remain a critical but under-tested contingency.

What to watch
  • spikes in procurement requests for spare transformer units
  • operational advisories on remote access hardening or SCADA segmentation
Action

Prioritize inventory of critical spares and test remote access segmentation and incident playbooks under realistic degraded-communications scenarios.
