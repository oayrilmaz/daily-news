DER proliferation increases protection and inertia questions

Signals suggest growing distributed energy resource (DER) injections are changing system inertia and protection dynamics, raising operational risks for operators and asset owners.

Operational data patterns indicate more inverter-based generation on feeders previously dominated by synchronous machines. Grid inertia expectations are therefore shifting, with implications for frequency response and relay settings. Protection coordination frameworks were designed for unidirectional flows; expectations are that bidirectional power flows can create miscoordination windows unless relay settings and fault detection logic are updated. This is a scenario watch for increased nuisance tripping during high DER export periods. System operators are reportedly evaluating aggregated DER controls and synthetic inertia schemes as mitigations. Implementation timelines and interoperability remain uncertain, and real-world performance may vary across vendors and firmware versions. Distribution utilities face resource constraints to reprogram relays and deploy adaptive protection; asset owners should expect multi-year workstreams to fully address miscoordination risks. Prioritization will likely be based on feeder criticality and load density. Market mechanisms that value fast frequency response could change dispatch incentives, shifting operational behavior at the distribution edge. Watch for changes in tariff signals and interconnection requirements that alter DER availability during stress events. This trend also raises training and situational-awareness requirements for control-room staff. Expect a growing demand for real-time DER visibility and testing frameworks that simulate high-penetration conditions. Coordination gaps between transmission and distribution entities are a potential blind spot. Scenario planning that models aggregated DER behavior under contingency conditions will be an important near-term risk-reduction measure.

What to watch
  • Unusual frequency excursions during high DER export windows
  • Interconnection policy updates that tighten ride-through requirements
  • Firmware updates from major inverter vendors impacting ride-through
Action

Audit relay coordination on key feeders and engage with DER aggregators to improve visibility and ride-through testing.
