On-site renewables and microgrids are increasingly part of sustainability and resiliency debates

Signals show on-site renewables and microgrids entering the center of conversations about both sustainability goals and resilience expectations for critical power users.

Deploying on-site PV, storage, and microgrid controls changes the dependency mix between grid and self-supply. Decision drivers include contractual grid reliability, sustainability commitments, and corporate risk tolerance. Expectations include more complex interactions with distribution operators regarding interconnection and islanding rules. Financial models must account for capital expenditure, operational savings, and potential revenue from grid services. Microgrid projects also require new capabilities in control, cybersecurity, and maintenance. Scenario teams should map regulatory constraints on export and islanding that could limit expected benefits. Adoption is likely to accelerate where grid reliability is perceived as weak or where incentives align with decarbonization objectives.

What to watch
  • Local interconnection rules and islanding permission changes.
  • Incentive programs that materially improve project paybacks for microgrids.
Action

Assess microgrid designs against local interconnection constraints and include cybersecurity in control-system specifications.
