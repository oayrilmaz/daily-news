OEMs face sustained lead-time pressure as SiC demand reshapes semiconductor roadmaps

Signals indicate OEMs and EPCs are managing persistent lead-time and qualification pressure driven by accelerated demand for silicon carbide power devices and automation in assembly.

SiC adoption for inverters and traction systems is increasing thermal and efficiency expectations but complicates procurement and qualification cycles. Vendors are prioritizing wafer capacity and long-term supply contracts, creating a buyer market for qualified device volumes. EPC timelines can be squeezed when key semiconductor deliveries slip, affecting commissioning and warranty start dates. Automation in switching and assembly is being presented as a partial hedge to semiconductor constraints by improving throughput elsewhere. Qualification testing for new SiC modules is extending acceptance windows and increasing test-lab utilization. Near-term scenarios include staggered commissioning schedules and acceptance amendments tied to semiconductor delivery milestones. Stakeholders should expect tighter coordination between procurement, test labs, and project schedules to mitigate risk.

What to watch
  • Supplier capacity updates and long-term supply agreement announcements
  • Qualification test backlog and lab capacity reports
Action

Reconcile project schedules with semiconductor delivery windows and include semiconductor milestones in acceptance criteria.
