Grid integration complexity rises with high offshore wind forecasts

Signals indicate that high offshore wind supply forecasts are increasing short-term transmission and balancing complexity, prompting scenario-level contingency planning by TSOs.

High variability scenarios are driving discussions on dynamic line rating and curtailment policies. There is growing expectation of local congestion at coastal transmission corridors during peak generation windows. Grid operators are signaling more frequent reliance on synchronous condensers and fast-acting storage to manage stability. Market mechanisms for curtailment compensation remain a key unresolved variable. Investment in subsea cable redundancy is being framed as a medium-term mitigation. The interplay between interconnector flows and domestic balancing creates additional operational uncertainty.

What to watch
  • Signals of increased curtailment events
  • Announcements of dynamic line rating pilots
Action

Assess transmission constraint scenarios for coastal zones and prioritize fast-acting stability resources.
