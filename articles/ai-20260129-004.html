Scenario signals for solar curtailment and smart-inverter grid services

Signals show growing operational scenarios where solar curtailment and localized grid constraints are motivating utilities to seek smart-inverter solutions for active grid support.

Solar-rich regions are reportedly experimenting with dynamic inverter setpoints to manage local voltage excursions and thermal constraints. These trial scenarios emphasize grid services such as volt-var and ride-through rather than pure energy delivery. OEMs of inverters are being asked for firmware and telemetry upgrades that enable centralized visibility and control at distribution scale. EPCs installing large PV plants face expectations to include grid-support packages and coordination rules with distribution system operators. Market frameworks being tested could remunerate these services, creating a pathway to reduce curtailment while improving local reliability. What to monitor are pilot program notices and interconnection requirement updates that embed active inverter functionality. The scenario is likely to accelerate demand for interoperable communication platforms and cybersecurity assurances for field devices. This shift would also influence procurement specifications for transformers and switchgear in PV balance-of-system designs.

What to watch
  • pilot program notices for volt-var control or active inverter dispatch
  • updates to interconnection technical requirements that mandate grid-support features
Action

Update inverter and EMS offering roadmaps to include grid-support firmware and robust telemetry compatible with distribution operator requirements.
