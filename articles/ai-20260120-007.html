Regulatory drafts hint at tighter interconnection and inverter performance requirements

Signals from regulatory consultations point to increased technical requirements for inverter-based resources and tighter interconnection study expectations to preserve system stability.

Consultation signals suggest regulators are prioritizing grid stability as inverter-based capacity grows. Expectations are that new rules will formalize ride-through, fault-ride-through, and synthetic inertia obligations. Scenario watch: stricter requirements could increase project upgrade costs and extend interconnection timelines if existing plants must retrofit controls. What to monitor are draft rule language and deadlines for compliance that may affect projects in development or early operations. Grid-code harmonization efforts are being discussed to limit regional fragmentation, but implementation timelines remain uncertain. Equipment qualification processes for inverters and control systems are likely to lengthen. Developers and OEMs are engaging early to align test protocols and demonstrate compliance pathways. Market participants are exploring contractual mechanisms to allocate retrofit risk between developers and investors.

What to watch
  • draft rule publications and comment deadlines
  • test protocol changes for inverter certification
Action

Review project compliance gaps and begin technical validation against likely updated grid-code requirements.
