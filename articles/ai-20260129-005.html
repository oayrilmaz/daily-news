Frequency ancillary markets reshape dispatch strategies for thermal assets

Signals suggest deeper participation in frequency ancillary markets is changing how thermal plants schedule maintenance and optimize dispatch, with potential impacts on availability during high-congestion windows.

Plants that can provide fast frequency response are finding new revenue streams that affect short-term dispatch economics. This is prompting rescheduling of planned outages to preserve capability during periods of high ancillary value. Scenario watch flags increased wear from frequent start-stop cycles if economic signals favor rapid response without corresponding maintenance adjustments. Market operators are updating product definitions to better align state-of-charge, ramp capability, and performance settlement. Thermal generators, batteries, and aggregated demand response are competing in layered markets that now value rapid contingency response. System planners should monitor how shifting ancillary revenues alter capacity availability forecasts during peak congestion events. Operators need integrated O&M strategies to avoid hidden reliability erosion from market-driven operational profiles.

What to watch
  • Settlement rule changes for frequency products
  • Outage reschedules by key thermal fleets
Action

Reconcile maintenance scheduling with ancillary market participation to prevent reliability degradation from increased cycling.
