AI-driven trading and DER aggregation add volatility to ancillary markets

Signals suggest that increased participation by AI-optimized trading bots and aggregated distributed energy resources is amplifying short-term price volatility in ancillary and intraday balancing markets.

Algorithmic agents and aggregated DER stacks are optimizing for microsecond-to-minute market signals, changing the supply-demand balance for ancillary services. These actors can rapidly shift position in response to price signals, increasing intraday price swings. Market operators face a choice: impose slower dispatch gates, increase telemetry requirements, or redesign product granularity. Each choice carries trade-offs between efficiency, participation, and reliability. For grid participants, this environment raises counterparty and settlement risk as rapid re-pricing occurs. Clearing mechanisms and reserve sizing assumptions may need revision to reflect higher-frequency resource behavior. Monitoring market depth and response patterns will indicate whether volatility is transient or structural.

What to watch
  • Spikes in intraday reserve prices and rapid bid/offer churn rates.
  • Regulator or operator consultations about telemetry or market product redesign.
Action

Review exposure to ancillary market volatility and consider hedging or adjusting bidding algorithms to account for faster-moving counterparties.
