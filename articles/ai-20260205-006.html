Upstream concentration risks for battery metals influencing procurement planning

Signals of upstream concentration in certain battery metals markets are prompting buyers to reassess procurement timelines and certification pathways to protect supply for HV equipment and energy storage programs.

Procurement teams are noting increased geopolitical and operational concentration in key mineral supply chains that feed battery and conductor manufacturing. This is elevating the value of diversified sourcing and long-term offtake arrangements. There is an expectation that more stringent ESG and traceability requirements will lengthen qualification timelines for new suppliers. Scenario watch: expedited demand from storage and EV programs could outpace certification workflows and create temporary shortages. What to monitor: mine production reports, certification scheme timelines, and long-term contract announcements.

What to watch
  • certification and ESG traceability timelines
  • major production and offtake announcements
Action

Initiate alternative-supplier qualification and extend procurement lead times where feasible to reduce exposure.
