Transmission bottlenecks threaten renewable project timelines

Procurement and interconnection queue signals indicate transmission and substation capacity is becoming a primary gating constraint for large-scale wind and solar projects in multiple LATAM markets.

Developers are reporting delays tied to EHV line buildouts and substation upgrades, which creates an expectation that project commissioning windows will slip absent accelerated permitting or investor-led mitigation measures. The scenario watch includes an increased incidence of contractual renegotiations and force-majeure claims where interconnection lead times exceed commercial forecasts. That could affect project valuations and financing terms. Regional procurement constraints for large transformers and switchgear compound the timeline risk. When combined with limited contractor capacity for EHV construction, the result is a multi-factor bottleneck. Solutions being signaled include staged energization, temporary radial connections, and merchant-funded grid reinforcements. These are pragmatic but may introduce operational constraints and higher costs. Policy responses such as priority grid corridors or expedited permitting are plausible, but implementation cycles are uncertain and often contentious. Developers and off-takers should therefore expect a period of elevated negotiation. The intelligence picture favors early engagement between project planners, transmission owners, and regulators to identify feasible interim solutions that preserve project economics and reliability.

What to watch
  • Interconnection queue position changes and EHV construction start notices
  • Regulatory programs for expedited transmission permitting
Action

Engage transmission owners early, evaluate staged energization options, and factor interconnection timing into financing covenants.
