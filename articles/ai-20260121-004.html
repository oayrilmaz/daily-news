Grid-forming inverters move from pilots toward procurement

Expectations are growing that grid-forming inverter technology will transition from demonstration projects to procurement specifications as operators seek synthetic inertia and better low-inertia event performance.

Grid operators and planners are signaling interest in resources that can stabilize frequency without synchronous machines. Demonstrations have highlighted benefits in black-start capability and ride-through performance during disturbances. Procurement cycles and revised interconnection standards could begin to codify requirements for grid-forming capability, especially in regions with high inverter-based resource penetration. Equipment OEMs are iterating firmware and testing profiles to meet evolving utility expectations. Scenario watch: early adopters may confront interoperability and testing challenges that slow commercial-scale deployment. What to monitor: updates to interconnection manuals, test protocols, and procurement language that references grid-forming functionality. The transition will likely be phased, focusing first on critical nodes and new build farms. Operationally, system operators should budget for commissioning and modeling work to integrate these new dynamic characteristics.

What to watch
  • Interconnection manual revisions
  • Procurement language changes for inverters
Action

Start including grid-forming capability in planning studies and procurement scoping to align with potential upcoming interconnection requirements.
