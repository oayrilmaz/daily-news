Short-term curtailment dynamics reshape storage value signals

Signals from grid operations suggest rising localized curtailment is altering short-duration storage economics and prompting strategic shifts in co-located asset deployments.

Areas with rapid renewables build and constrained local network capacity are experiencing increased curtailment windows that reduce revenue certainty for energy-only projects. Short-duration batteries are seeing revised business cases where arbitrage is supplemented by firming and grid services to manage curtailment risk. Scenario watch includes accelerated pairing of storage with generation to capture ancillary revenue and reduce exposure to curtailment. What to monitor are new procurement tenders that prioritize co-located storage and any changes in grid connection offers that include curtailment clauses. Developers may pivot to longer-duration or hybrid storage to access capacity-like revenues where available. This evolution could pressure finance structures and require renegotiation of PPA terms to reflect variable dispatch expectations. Expect closer engagement between regulators and system operators on compensation frameworks for curtailment and flexibility provision.

What to watch
  • connection offer revisions
  • storage-inclusive procurement tenders
Action

Review PPA curtailment language and evaluate storage pairing or alternative dispatch architectures for new builds.
