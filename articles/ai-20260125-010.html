Storage-backed renewables projects gaining procurement edge amid curtailment concerns

Signals from recent solicitations suggest hybrid renewables-plus-storage proposals are gaining preference in procurement scenarios that aim to reduce curtailment risk and firm variable output, creating an expectation that hybrid bids will win a larger share of new contracts.

Procurement teams are increasingly valuing dispatchability and reduced curtailment when scoring proposals. Developers that couple PV or wind with battery systems can present tighter firming profiles and better compliance with off-take terms. Storage also improves grid integration by smoothing output and providing intra-hour flexibility that mitigates local congestion. Cost declines in batteries and more favorable financing for hybrid projects are nudging evaluation criteria toward bundled offerings. However, project complexity and interconnection studies for hybrids remain more involved and can stretch development timelines. The scenario to watch is a procurement tilt toward hybrid solutions that accelerates storage deployment but requires more sophisticated EPC coordination. Grid planning outcomes will depend on how quickly interconnection studies adapt to hybrid models.

What to watch
  • tender outcomes favoring bundled renewables-plus-storage bids
  • interconnection study timelines for hybrid projects
Action

Prioritize development of hybrid proposals and coordinate early with grid planners to shorten interconnection lead times.
