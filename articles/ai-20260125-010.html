Forecasting models and AI reshaping load-management practices

Load forecasting models augmented with AI are producing signals that could change day-ahead scheduling and reserve allocation, prompting operations teams to reassess model validation and vendor lock-in risks.

Enhanced forecasting tools are being tested to improve intraday scheduling and reduce reliance on contingency generation. These models often rely on proprietary feature engineering and training data, raising questions about portability across sites and vendors. Accuracy improvements in AI forecasts can reduce reserve procurement costs but increase dependency on vendor updates and data pipelines. Operations teams must balance the efficiency gains with the need for transparent error bounds and fallback procedures. Scenario planners are considering joint procurement or open-model approaches to avoid single-vendor dependencies. Regulatory frameworks that require explainability for operational models could shape adoption paths. The net effect is likely to be improved short-term scheduling efficiency accompanied by new governance and contractual considerations.

What to watch
  • Vendor releases describing model explainability features or API portability.
  • Incidents where forecast errors materially altered reserve usage or costs.
Action

Implement validation regimes and fallback procedures before relying on AI forecasts for day-ahead and intraday scheduling.
