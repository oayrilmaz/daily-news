Prefabrication signals ease labor constraints on HV projects

Signals indicate prefabrication and modular substation components are gaining traction as a response to skilled labor shortages and site schedule risk.

EPCs are increasingly trialing factory-built substation modules and containerized switchgear to reduce on-site labor hours. The signal suggests a strategic shift toward off-site assembly for HV projects.

This approach shortens critical-path activities on congested sites and reduces exposure to weather and local labor availability. Expectations are that prefabrication can improve schedule certainty and reduce rework.

However, the scenario includes transport and site-integration trade-offs: oversized modules require specialized transport and lift plans that may constrain route approvals and increase coordination costs.

OEMs that standardize modular interfaces and provide turnkey installation services will be advantaged. Procurement teams should assess vendor track records on factory acceptance testing and integrated site commissioning.

There is also an economic signal: while unit costs may be higher for prefabricated modules, total installed cost and schedule certainty can lower project risk premiums and financing costs.

Monitor RFPs that specify modular delivery and any announcements from EPCs expanding prefab-capacity as indicators of broader adoption.

What to watch
  • RFPs specifying modular or factory-built substation components
  • EPC capacity announcements for prefab assembly
Action

Evaluate prefabrication options and update transport/lift risk assessments in project schedules.
