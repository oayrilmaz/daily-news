Regulatory signals favor grid planning reforms and capacity mechanisms

Signals from consultations indicate policy attention on capacity remuneration and coordinated grid planning to manage renewables integration risks.

Regulators are exploring mechanisms to value long-duration and fast-response capabilities in capacity frameworks. Grid planners are being encouraged to accelerate interregional reinforcement and to formalize DER visibility requirements. Policy talks emphasize aligning market signals with reliability objectives to reduce out-of-market interventions. Stakeholders are being asked to submit data on project timelines, which may change queue prioritization under new frameworks. The policy environment is signalling potential transitional support for storage and firming resources during buildouts. Scenario watch: targeted capacity mechanisms could improve near-term investment incentives but may alter marginal returns for energy-only assets. Market participants should prepare for rule changes that could reshape procurement timelines and revenue stacks.

What to watch
  • Publication of consultation outcomes or draft rule changes
  • Requests for data submissions that affect interconnection prioritization
Action

Engage with consultations and reassess project revenue models under potential capacity remuneration changes.
