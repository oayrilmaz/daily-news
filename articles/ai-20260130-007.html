High-renewables market dynamics creating volatility and negative-price scenarios

Market signals point to more frequent intraday volatility and negative-price events in areas with high wind and solar penetration, influencing merchant asset economics and dispatch planning.

Prices during low-demand, high-output windows are increasingly dipping below zero in some regional markets, creating challenges for conventional and storage asset dispatch economics. These price patterns can flip revenue models for peakers and storage if duration and frequency of negative-price hours increase. Developers and operators are reassessing hedge structures and contract terms to account for higher short-duration variability. Market operators may respond with new ramping products or capacity mechanisms to stabilize revenues. Scenario planning should evaluate how pricing granularity and settlement rules affect short-term signals to assets. Watch for proposals that change settlement intervals or introduce scarcity pricing triggers. These market shifts will affect investment decisions for flexible resources and grid services.

What to watch
  • settlement interval and scarcity pricing proposals
  • frequency and duration of negative-price events
Action

Reevaluate hedges and revenue models for flexible assets to reflect increased short-duration price volatility.
