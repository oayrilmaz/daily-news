Distributed solar-plus-storage signals rapid behind-the-meter adoption

Signals of accelerating distributed solar and storage adoption are framing LATAM as a region where behind-the-meter assets will play a larger role in capacity adequacy and peak management.

High retail tariffs and increasing grid stress are creating expectations for commercial and industrial customers to adopt self-generation and storage. There is a scenario watch for regulatory responses that introduce net billing changes or export limits. Utilities may face revenue pressure and must plan for different load shapes as behind-the-meter penetration rises. EPCs and integrators could see strong demand for standardized BTM packages and O&M offerings. Financing models that pair hardware with energy services are likely to gain traction. Grid planners should model aggregated BTM behavior to understand impacts on distribution-level voltage and protection schemes. This trend may also encourage microgrid development for critical infrastructure and remote communities. Policymakers will need to weigh resilience and reliability trade-offs as BTM scales.

What to watch
  • changes to net billing or export regulations
  • financing programs targeted at commercial BTM projects
Action

Model distribution-level impacts of aggregated BTM adoption and engage regulators on export and net billing frameworks.
