Design stressors for HV equipment in desert data center projects

Scenario watch indicates that designers and OEMs must adapt HV equipment and cooling strategies for desert data centers where ambient extremes and dust exposure challenge traditional specifications.

Desert deployments increase corrosion, thermal derating, and particulate ingress risks for switchgear and transformers. Design responses include higher ingress protection, enhanced filtration, and derating assumptions that affect equipment sizing and cost. A procurement signal is stronger specification clauses around heat-exchanger sizing and maintenance intervals to preserve uptime in harsh conditions. Operationally, increased maintenance frequency and spare inventory near sites could become necessary to meet SLAs for hyperscale customers. EPCs may need to negotiate revised warranty terms that reflect tougher operating envelopes and more aggressive environmental degradation assumptions. Monitor specification trends and OEM guidance updates that reference desertized product lines and service programs tailored for high-dust environments.

What to watch
  • Equipment specifications referencing desert or high-dust ratings
  • OEM desertized product announcements
Action

Incorporate harsher derating and maintenance plans into procurement and warranty negotiations.
