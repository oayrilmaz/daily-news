Interconnection reform expectations gaining traction

Signals from regulatory filings and stakeholder filings suggest interconnection reform is gaining momentum as a global policy theme, with potential to reshape project timelines and transmission planning.

Regulators appear to be more active in exploring queue reform and cost-allocation adjustments to unblock constrained projects. There is an expectation that some jurisdictions will move toward more centralized or standardized interconnection processes. This shift could materially change project risk profiles and financing timelines. EPC and OEM planning will need to adapt to potential acceleration or reprioritization of transmission projects. Scenario watch: rapid policy changes could create short-term alignment pain between developers and grid operators. What to monitor are consultation timelines and draft rule language that affects cost allocation and queue management.

What to watch
  • what to monitor: draft rulemaking schedules and consultation outcomes
  • expectations: shifts in cost-allocation and queue management frameworks
Action

Map active projects against potential interconnection reform timelines and adjust risk assessments.
