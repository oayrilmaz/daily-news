Regulators preparing clearer rules for DER aggregation and visibility

Expectations suggest European regulators are moving toward standardized requirements for aggregator visibility and telemetry to integrate DERs safely into balancing and congestion management frameworks.

Regulatory working groups are reportedly focused on data standards, settlement integrity, and operational visibility. The intelligence framing emphasizes system security and fair market access as drivers for reform. Aggregators may face new telemetry obligations and certification steps to participate in system services. Improved visibility will likely reduce operational uncertainty but raise compliance costs for smaller aggregators. Grid operators see benefits from clearer visibility that enable more reliable dispatch and reduce reliance on conservative operational margins. The expected outcome is a gradual ramp of obligations with phased compliance timelines to avoid market disruption.

What to watch
  • consultations on aggregator data standards and certification frameworks
  • pilot programs testing telemetry-based dispatch models
Action

Prepare for enhanced telemetry requirements by auditing aggregator data capabilities and planning certification steps.
