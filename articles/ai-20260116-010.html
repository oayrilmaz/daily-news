HV equipment aging and maintenance backlogs create reliability watch scenarios

Signals suggest aging high-voltage assets combined with maintenance backlogs could elevate outage risk in regions where expansion has prioritized new capacity over refurbishment.

Maintenance deferral in favor of expanding capacity can leave critical assets operating beyond optimal inspection and replacement cycles. Aging oil-filled transformers and switchgear have failure modes that are sensitive to thermal stress and insulation degradation. Spare parts inventories for legacy equipment are often limited, and long lead times complicate rapid field replacements. EPC and OEM partnerships focused on refurbishment and targeted modernizations are emerging as near-term reliability mitigations. Remote monitoring and advanced diagnostics offer detection improvements, but many legacy assets lack compatible instrumentation. A scenario to watch is clustered equipment failures during heat extremes or system disturbances that reveal underinvestment in refurbishment. Monitor asset health reporting and procurement actions that prioritize refurbishment RFPs or condition-based replacement programs.

What to watch
  • Utility asset health reports and refurbishment tenders.
  • Supplier inventories and lead-time disclosures for legacy equipment spares.
Action

Prioritize condition-based inspections and secure critical spares to reduce exposure from aging HV assets.
