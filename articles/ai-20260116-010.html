DER aggregation stresses distribution planning and visibility

Signals from distribution planners and aggregators indicate that rapid adoption of DER aggregation is creating visibility gaps and complicating feeder-level planning assumptions.

Aggregators are bundling residential and commercial DERs to provide grid services, but distribution operators report difficulty reconciling aggregated bids with nodal voltage and thermal constraints. The mismatch can increase operational uncertainty.

Telemetry limitations at the distribution edge create blind spots. Many DERs report only energy meter-level data that lacks real-time inverter state and ramp capability information.

Planning assumptions that historically treated distributed resources as passive are being re-evaluated; planners need new models that incorporate active DER behavior and aggregator response dynamics.

Interconnection studies are becoming more conservative, and curtailment clauses are being tightened as operators seek to preserve feeder reliability in high-DER scenarios.

Market and regulatory frameworks that define visibility and telemetry requirements for aggregators will materially affect how aggregations are valued and how quickly they can scale safely.

If visibility gaps persist, distribution planners may require additional telemetry or impose operational constraints, slowing aggregator market expansion and affecting DER revenue streams.

This is a system-integration watch: technical fixes exist but require coordinated policy and investment in distribution observability.

What to watch
  • Regulatory moves defining telemetry or performance requirements for aggregators.
  • Interconnection study trends showing new constraints or curtailment clauses.
Action

Engage with aggregators on telemetry standards and revise planning models to include active DER behavior and uncertainty margins.
