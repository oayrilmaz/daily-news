Off-grid renewables and microgrids for data centers: transmission-deferral scenarios

Signals of increased microgrid deployments for data centers in LATAM are creating plausible transmission deferral scenarios while concentrating risk at feeder and point-of-interconnection nodes.

Developers are pairing solar, storage and backup generation to meet resiliency targets where grid reliability is inconsistent. This reduces peak demand on transmission in some corridors and creates opportunities for deferral of upgrades. Expectations include more requests for dynamic interconnection agreements and specialized protection schemes. Scenario watch flags concentrated risk where multiple large consumers island simultaneously during grid events, stressing local distribution. What to monitor are interconnection modification filings and requests for islanding-capable infrastructure. OEMs supplying microgrid controls are iterating on standards to improve coordination with utilities. The net system effect could be localized relief on transmission needs but elevated complexity for distribution planners. Preparedness requires updated protection studies and contractual clarity on islanding operations.

What to watch
  • interconnection modification filings and islanding-capability requests
  • protection-study updates for feeders with clustered microgrids
Action

Coordinate with distribution planners to update protection studies and define clear islanding protocols with data-center operators.
