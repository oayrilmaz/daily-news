Model risk grows as AI expands in asset maintenance

The expansion of AI-driven predictive maintenance across OEM and EPC workflows is increasing exposure to model risk, creating a need for systematic validation and governance rather than blind operational reliance.

AI models are being used to predict failures, prioritize inspections, and optimize spare parts stocking. These models improve efficiency but introduce risks from data bias, label drift, and untested edge cases. OEMs and EPCs are beginning to codify model governance, including retraining cadences, threshold alerts, and human-in-the-loop review for high-consequence actions. There are signals that different asset types require bespoke models and that one-size-fits-all approaches underperform. Auditability and provenance of training data are becoming procurement considerations for large asset owners. Model deployments without adequate validation can produce false confidence and deferred maintenance that increases failure probability. The recommended approach is staged deployment with conservative action thresholds and continuous performance monitoring. Expect an industry push toward third-party model certification and clearer contractual language on AI liability.

What to watch
  • performance degradation metrics for deployed maintenance models
  • contract clauses addressing AI model liability and data provenance
Action

Implement staged AI deployments with conservative action thresholds and continuous model performance monitoring.
