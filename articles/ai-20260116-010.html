Urban mining and second-life battery initiatives signal longer-term supply relief

Procurement and R&D signals indicate that urban mining and second-life battery initiatives are progressing from pilot stages toward scaled demonstrations, offering a potential structural hedge against primary supply shocks over time.

Several commercial pilots for battery recycling and second-life reuse are moving into demonstration-scale deployments, focusing on recoverable cobalt, nickel, and lithium streams. Second-life batteries are increasingly attractive for lower-duration storage services and behind-the-meter applications, providing a bridge as recycling capacity scales. Expectations are that technical recovery rates and collection logistics will improve, but economic viability depends on policy incentives and sustained feedstock flows. Scenario watch includes an accelerated recycling ramp that meaningfully offsets primary material demand in a multi-year horizon. Local industrial policy and investment signals are important determinants of pace. The intelligence takeaway is urban mining will become an element of procurement diversification strategies, but it will not fully substitute for primary mining in the near term.

What to watch
  • Announcements of scaled recycling facilities and feedstock collection programs
  • Deployments of second-life fleets in commercial storage applications
Action

Include recycled content and second-life options in sourcing plans to diversify supply and reduce upstream exposure.
