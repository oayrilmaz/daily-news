OEMs recalibrating procurement and vertical partnerships amid component constraints

Signals point to OEMs and EPCs deepening supplier relationships and considering vertical partnerships to stabilize access to IGBTs, power modules, and other constrained components.

Component-level constraints are prompting OEMs to lock multi-year supply agreements and co-invest in manufacturing capacity. Engineering teams are redesigning for flexibility across available semiconductors and power electronic options. Some EPCs are adopting modular designs to decouple long-lead components from overall project schedules. Financing models are adapting to include component-delivery risk and escrow-style arrangements. This structural response may alleviate shortages but increases capital tied up in supply commitments. Scenario watch: a coordinated industry push into component manufacturing could rebalance supply but likely with lead times measured in quarters to years.

What to watch
  • Long-term supply agreements or joint-venture announcements with semiconductor manufacturers
  • Design-spec changes reflecting alternative component sets
Action

Inventory component exposure across projects and engage suppliers on multi-year hedges or co-investment options.
