Frequency response market reforms could reallocate revenue for fast resources

Signals of market-rule reform discussions suggest frequency response and fast-acting ancillary services may be repriced, creating scenarios where batteries and inverter controls capture a larger share of system revenues.

Regulators and independent system operators are reportedly examining bid structures and deliverability metrics for fast frequency services. Market designs that better value speed and accuracy would advantage inverter-based resources with rapid ramp capability. The expectation is an iterative reform path, with pilot auctions preceding broader rule changes. Capacity market participants may face revenue churn as new product definitions emerge. Traditional thermal peakers without fast-response capability could see reduced capacity market competitiveness over time. For investors, clearer product definitions and settlement mechanisms will be decisive for project returns. Vendors that can demonstrate speed and predictability in live grid conditions will likely gain commercial traction.

What to watch
  • pilot auction results and settlement rule updates
  • announcements of new product definitions for fast frequency response
Action

Model revenue sensitivity to evolving ancillary service definitions and update merchant project underwriting assumptions accordingly.
