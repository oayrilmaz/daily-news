Ancillary service value shift: fast reserves commanding premium

Market signals suggest an upward revaluation of fast-response ancillary services as grids with high inverter-based resources increasingly prize rapid ramping and frequency response.

As inverter-dominated fleets grow, system operators are creating or refining markets that compensate speed and accuracy of response rather than just megawatt capacity. This changes the revenue streams for assets that can deliver sub-minute response. Battery storage and improved fast-ramping gas units appear positioned to capture value, but participation rules and measurement methodologies remain in flux across jurisdictions. That regulatory variance creates uneven market opportunities. Aggregators and virtual power plant operators are experimenting with stacked services to monetize fast response across capacity, frequency, and congestion products. Operationalizing this stacking requires precise telemetry and firm contractual terms. Pricing volatility in these markets can be high, reflecting scarcity during extreme events and low utilization otherwise. Asset owners should model asymmetrical revenue profiles rather than relying on flat capacity payments. Market design choices, such as telemetry granularity and settlement intervals, materially influence which technologies win. Some grid operators are already trialing faster settlement windows to better reflect rapid response value. For market participants, the emerging signal implies a shift in asset valuation toward speed and reliability, supporting investments in controls, telemetry, and hybrid assets that can deliver guaranteed fast reserves.

What to watch
  • New ancillary service product rollouts and settlement rule changes
  • Telemetry and certification requirements for fast-response providers
Action

Evaluate asset control upgrades and telemetry investments to participate in fast-response ancillary markets.
