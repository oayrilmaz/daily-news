Modular substations and prefabrication gain traction with schedule pressures

Signals indicate EPCs and OEMs are increasingly proposing modular, shop-built substation components to shorten site schedules and reduce weather and safety risk.

The expectation is that prefabricated modules will compress construction timelines and reduce onsite labor intensity. Scenario watch: modular approaches require stricter early-stage design coordination and carry change-order risk if site conditions differ from assumptions. Supply-chain benefits include factory-quality testing and reduced field commissioning time. However, transport logistics and on-site lifting capacity become gating factors for very large modules. Standardization of module interfaces could create economies of scale for OEMs that capture repeatable designs. What to monitor are bid packages that prefer modular delivery and any changes in insurance terms tied to factory-tested assemblies. Contractors should update risk registers and logistics plans to account for oversized-load constraints. Early engagement between design, factory, and site teams will be decisive in realizing promised schedule gains.

What to watch
  • RFPs specifying modular delivery
  • Transport and lifting capacity constraints
Action

Pilot modular components on low-complexity sites and update logistics and interface-management plans.
