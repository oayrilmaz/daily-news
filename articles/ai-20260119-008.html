EPCs consider modular and factory-built substation components to shorten onsite time

Signals show EPCs are piloting modular substation and factory-assembled components as a strategy to compress onsite schedules and mitigate local labor constraints.

Modular prefabrication offers predictable lead times and quality control advantages that are attractive under current scheduling pressures. EPCs are testing shipment sequences that allow partial energization while other modules remain in transit. The expectation is a trade-off between higher upfront fabrication cost and reduced contingency exposure onsite. Local regulatory frameworks for assembled substations are being assessed for compliance and inspection cadence. One scenario to watch is that permitting authorities will require new inspection protocols for factory-built modules. Another consideration is logistical complexity in transporting large modules across constrained corridors. If modular approaches scale, suppliers will need to standardize interfaces and spares planning. Long-term, modularization could reshape labor needs toward factory skillsets rather than field crews. The near-term action is expanding vendor qualification to include factory and transport capabilities.

What to watch
  • Permitting updates related to factory-built substations
  • Vendor capabilities in transport and on-site integration
Action

Include modular assembly capability and transport logistics in vendor evaluations to preserve schedule flexibility.
