AI tools for asset management scale with caveats on hallucinations and data quality

Signals show rapid uptake of large-model AI tools for asset management and documentation summarization, but scenario watch flags stubborn risks from hallucinations and inconsistent data records.

Asset owners are trialing generative AI to extract insights from maintenance logs, test reports, and vendor manuals to accelerate decision making. These tools can dramatically reduce time spent searching documents and generate maintenance checklists, creating efficiency upside. At the same time, hallucination risks where models fabricate part numbers, procedures, or dates are a practical hazard for engineering teams. Good outcomes depend on well-structured, curated training datasets and human-in-the-loop validation workflows. Integration into ERP and CMMS systems is a next step but requires strict access controls and provenance tracking. Vendors are starting to offer domain-constrained models and retrieval-augmented generation to reduce fabrication risks, though not uniformly effective. The scenario to monitor is over-reliance on AI outputs without verification, which can propagate errors into procurement or field procedures.

What to watch
  • deployments of retrieval-augmented systems
  • audits revealing AI-generated errors
Action

Pilot AI summarization with strict human verification gates and preserve original document provenance for audits.
