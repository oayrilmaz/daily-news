Distributed PV-plus-storage adoption accelerating, grid friction follows

Signals show rapidly rising adoption of distributed PV paired with batteries across parts of LATAM, presenting both resilience gains and localized grid integration challenges.

Host-utility interactions are being reshaped as prosumers add storage to manage consumption and export. This increase in two-way power flows can stress distribution protection schemes and require rethinking substation settings. Scenario watch: utilities may adopt export limits or new tariff structures to manage distribution impacts, potentially altering the economics of some installations. EPCs and inverter OEMs are adapting product lines for higher islanding and anti-islanding performance. Financing models that depended on predictable net-metering may need renegotiation if export rules change. What to monitor: distribution operator pilot programs and proposed changes to net-export compensation mechanisms.

What to watch
  • utility pilot programs on export limits
  • proposed tariff reforms affecting net-export compensation
Action

Include potential export restrictions in financial models and validate inverter anti-islanding capabilities early.
