Floating solar and hybridization signals

Signals indicate developers are increasingly evaluating floating solar paired with hydropower reservoirs as a hybrid resilience and generation optimization pathway.

Expectation is that floating PV can reduce evaporation and smooth seasonal output when co-located with existing hydro assets. Scenario watch: hybridity could change reservoir management priorities and require new coordination processes between generation and system operators. EPCs may need to adapt marine installation capabilities and O&M plans for floating structures. There is also an expectation that environmental reviews will focus on aquatic impacts and grid connection capacity. What to monitor are pilot deployments, environmental assessment outcomes, and utility contractual terms for hybrid dispatch and revenue sharing.

What to watch
  • Environmental assessment findings for floating PV pilots
  • Utility contracts defining hybrid dispatch rules
Action

Explore pilot opportunities for floating PV on suitable reservoirs and prepare environmental and operational coordination plans for hybrid dispatch.
