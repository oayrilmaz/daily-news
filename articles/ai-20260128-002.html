Substation digital twin trials highlight spare-parts forecasting gaps

Early digital twin deployments at substations are surfacing gaps in spare-parts forecasting that could lengthen outage recovery if inventories and procurement pathways are not adjusted.

Digital twins are improving failure-mode visibility and predictive maintenance signal quality in trials. However, simulated failure forecasts point to long lead times for certain high-voltage components if replacements are required. The modelling scenarios suggest a mismatch between predictive maintenance timing and current procurement cycles. Organizations that rely on single-source suppliers or small inventory buffers may face extended downtime in a stress scenario. The twin-based insights can be used to prioritize parts stocking and diversify supply chains. There is also an opportunity to update maintenance contracts and SLAs to reflect predictive alerting. Risk management decisions now hinge on whether procurement flexibility is increased rapidly enough to match predictive capabilities.

What to watch
  • Inventory levels and procurement lead times for key HV components
  • Contract clauses enabling accelerated delivery or alternative sourcing
Action

Map critical components against lead times and update procurement playbooks to include rapid-sourcing options informed by twin outputs.
