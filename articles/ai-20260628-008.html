EPC schedule risk from component bottlenecks

Signals of staggered component deliveries are emerging as a material schedule risk for EPCs, prompting scenario planning around phased commissioning and modular strategies.

EPC firms report uneven arrival of long-lead electrical components, which complicates sequencing and on-site labor planning. This pattern elevates the strategic value of modular and preassembled skids that can be slotted into delayed schedules. Contractors are exploring split-scope and temporary work solutions to maintain progress while awaiting critical deliveries. Contractual terms related to liquidated damages and change orders are becoming focal negotiation points. The cost of expedited shipping and rework is a rising scenario cost center. Some EPCs are signaling increased reliance on local fabrication where quality standards permit. Expect tighter integration between procurement teams and site planners to manage cascading impacts.

What to watch
  • Change-order frequency and claims tied to late deliveries
  • Shifts toward local fabrication or modular prefabrication contracts
Action

Integrate procurement and construction planning early and evaluate modular or split-scope approaches to reduce schedule exposure.
