Permitting and export policy remain a watch for lithium and cobalt projects

Signals from project timelines and stakeholder communications suggest permitting delays and evolving export considerations could slow select lithium and cobalt developments in parts of Africa.

Local permitting processes are showing longer cycles in several jurisdictions as environmental and community review intensify. Export policy discussions at the national level are increasingly focused on value retention and local processing requirements. Developers may face capital reallocation decisions if timelines extend beyond planned milestones. Downstream OEMs are evaluating alternative sourcing and recycling pathways to reduce exposure. International investment gestures and diplomatic engagement could alter the trajectory, but near-term uncertainty remains elevated. The supply outlook for some battery-critical minerals now includes an elevated probability of phased production starts rather than immediate full-scale output.

What to watch
  • Regulatory guidance from national mining authorities
  • Community consultation milestones for major projects
Action

Map alternative sourcing and recycling pathways and build schedule contingencies into mineral-dependent project plans.
