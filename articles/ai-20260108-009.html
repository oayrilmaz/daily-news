Storage-integrated HV substations present integration and protection coordination questions

Signals from pilot projects show that integrating large-scale batteries with HV substations raises protection coordination and islanding scenario complexities that merit cautious operational testing.

Battery systems provide fast response but introduce bidirectional flows that protection schemes were not originally designed to handle. Expectation: protective relays and system studies will need recalibration to account for inverter-based fault contribution. Scenario watch: protection malcoordination during high charge/discharge cycles may trigger nuisance trips or delayed fault clearing. What to monitor: results from staged fault injection tests, inverter fault-current contribution modeling, and relay setting revisions. EPC teams are coordinating closely with TSOs on staged commissioning to reduce live-system risk. The outcome will shape future substation design standards where storage is co-located.

What to watch
  • Staged fault injection test schedules and outcomes
  • Relay setting change notices and coordination studies
Action

Plan staged commissioning with fault-injection tests and update protection coordination studies before full storage dispatch.
