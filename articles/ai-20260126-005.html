Market design signals point to volatility as new flexibility assets scale

Expectations are that the rapid addition of flexible resources—storage, demand response, and hydrogen—will create transient volatility in energy and ancillary markets until rules and price signals mature.

Market operators are seeing scenario signals of price compression during high-renewable periods and sharp spikes when flexibility is scarce. Traders and asset owners are recalibrating risk models to account for fast-acting distributed resources and the valuation of grid services. Settlement rules and measurement standards for non-traditional resources remain an area of active discussion. The interaction between day-ahead, intra-day, and real-time markets is under stress as new resources provide sub-hourly services. Financial market participants are hedging against increased short-term volatility while seeking instruments that reflect emerging flexibility value. Scenario-watch: inconsistent market rules across jurisdictions could prolong price divergence and create arbitrage opportunities.

What to watch
  • Changes to settlement or measurement standards
  • Announcements of new market products for flexibility
Action

Review trading and hedging strategies to incorporate sub-hourly volatility and flexibility value.
