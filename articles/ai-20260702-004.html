Curtailment economics push co-located storage in constrained markets

Signals from generation and grid planners suggest curtailment economics are making co-located battery additions the default mitigation in several constrained markets rather than large merchant storage bids alone.

Developers are modeling co-location as a way to capture higher value energy and reduce negative pricing exposure. Expectations are incremental deployment of behind-the-meter and co-located storage at new project sites. Scenario watch: permitting and interconnection sequencing will determine which projects stack storage successfully. What to monitor are changes in grid connection agreements that alter curtailment compensation or charging rules. The financial case is strongest where renewable output frequently hits local transmission constraints. OEMs report improved integrated inverter options for factory-coupled PV-plus-storage solutions. Operations teams are refining charge-discharge strategies to arbitrate energy and ancillary services revenue. Co-location also shifts maintenance and warranty responsibilities to combined EPC models.

What to watch
  • interconnection policy changes affecting charge/discharge rules
  • EPC contract language trends for integrated PV+storage
Action

Re-run project financials with co-located storage scenarios and verify interconnection terms related to charge/discharge and curtailment compensation.
