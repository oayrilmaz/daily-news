Offshore wind-grid connection bottlenecks remain a scenario risk for buildout timelines

Signals from interconnection planning point to persistent bottlenecks in offshore wind grid integration, creating a scenario that could compress project timelines and increase curtailment risk.

Transmission build complexity for offshore arrays continues to drive planning uncertainty. Cable routing, landfall permitting, and coordinated onshore upgrade sequencing are recurring constraints in planning scenarios. Grid operators face trade-offs between incremental acceptance and waiting for coordinated capacity, which affects merchant and contracted projects differently. The risk of protracted grid reinforcement timelines increases the chance that asset owners will accept higher curtailment rates or staged commissioning. In parallel, interest in HVDC modular islands and subsea switching is rising as a mitigation scenario. These technologies reduce some routing constraints but introduce cost and manufacturing lead-time considerations. Expect developers and system planners to increasingly model staged integration scenarios and contractual protections against delayed transmission availability.

What to watch
  • Updates to offshore transmission planning timelines or staged connection frameworks
  • New procurement signals for subsea switching or modular HVDC solutions
Action

Incorporate staged integration and curtailment scenarios into project finance models and consider HVDC options where feasible.
