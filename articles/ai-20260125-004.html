Inverter lead-times and SiC demand may slow new solar-wind output ramp

Procurement signals suggest inverter and silicon-carbide (SiC) module demand could create near-term friction for renewable project commissioning schedules.

Inverter vendors are increasingly prioritizing higher-efficiency SiC-equipped product lines to meet grid-code needs for fast response and DER controls. This expectation could widen lead times for conventional Si-insulated inverters as production retooling occurs. A scenario watch: clustered commissioning windows for large solar and wind farms may compete for limited SiC supplies and fabrication capacity. Grid synchronization demands and reactive support capabilities are making advanced inverter specs a gating factor for interconnection approvals. Project developers may be forced to re-specify equipment or accept phased commissioning to match available supply slots. Material and semiconductor suppliers may see a near-term premium on SiC wafers and power modules if demand concentration persists. Operators and EPCs should monitor procurement queues and consider flexible inverter architectures that accept retrofit modules. What to monitor: vendor lead-time updates, SiC wafer availability indicators, and milestone slippage on expected COD dates.

What to watch
  • vendor lead-time notices and production slot changes
  • commissioning milestone adherence for large projects
Action

Reassess inverter specs and consider early procurement or modular retrofit options to mitigate SiC-related schedule risk.
