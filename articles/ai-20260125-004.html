Solar-plus-storage procurement picks up

Signals indicate a renewed wave of solar-plus-storage procurement in constrained markets as planners seek to firm variable resources and manage evening peaks.

System planners are framing scenarios that require storage to provide capacity value during late-day ramps and extended cloudy periods. Procurement signals emphasize LCOS and integration services, with developers bundling dispatch optimization and grid services into bids. Interconnection queues show a higher proportion of hybrid projects compared to prior cycles, reflecting a scenario preference for dispatchable renewables. Manufacturing lead times for battery cells and PCS equipment remain a variable, influencing project phasing and contract terms. Developers are increasingly including automated controls tuned for frequency and voltage support as part of project deliverables. Regulatory frameworks that recognize storage as capacity are a key scenario driver for near-term project bankability.

What to watch
  • new capacity market rules recognizing storage
  • announced module or inverter lead-time changes
Action

Ensure project bids include detailed dispatch and integration guarantees and check battery supply timelines against commercial milestones.
