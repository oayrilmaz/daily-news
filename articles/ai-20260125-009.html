Solar-plus-storage financing signals strain on bankability terms

Signals from financiers and developers point to tightening bankability terms for utility-scale solar-plus-storage projects in parts of Latin America, creating a scenario watch on contract structures and off-taker credit risk.

Currency volatility and evolving dispatch patterns are influencing lender risk appetites for long-duration storage paired with solar assets. Underwriting is reportedly becoming more conservative, with stricter covenants and larger debt-service reserves requested. Developers may need to offer more robust guarantees or accept higher costs of capital in certain markets. Off-taker creditworthiness and contract duration remain pivotal to securing favorable financing. There is growing discussion about blended finance structures and political-risk insurance to bridge gaps, but these instruments add complexity and pricing pressure. Scenario analyses should test sensitivities to lower-than-expected capacity payments and prolonged commissioning timelines. Projects that can demonstrate diversified revenue stacks and firming-service contracts may retain preferential access to capital.

What to watch
  • changes in lender covenant requirements for storage projects
  • new blended-finance or insurance offerings for off-taker risk
Action

Stress-test financing models under adverse currency and off-taker scenarios and prioritize diversified revenue contracts.
