Adoption signals of condition-based monitoring change substation maintenance scenarios

Signals show accelerated adoption of online condition-based monitoring in high-voltage substations, creating scenarios where predictive maintenance alters outage planning and spare-part strategies.

Condition-based monitoring systems are being rolled into substations to provide continuous asset health telemetry. Expectations are that earlier fault indicators will reduce unplanned outages and allow more granular maintenance scheduling. Scenario watch includes a transition period where mixed fleets of monitored and unmonitored assets require hybrid maintenance strategies. What to monitor are false-positive rates from analytics, integration fidelity with asset-management systems, and training uptake among field crews. Utilities should expect to reconfigure spare-part inventories toward condition-driven needs and revise outage coordination processes. This is intelligence to guide deployment strategy rather than a statement that all monitoring initiatives are performing uniformly. Project leads should pilot CBM integration and quantify reliability improvements before broad rollout.

What to watch
  • analytics false-positive and false-negative rates
  • integration progress with asset-management platforms
Action

Pilot condition-based monitoring on critical assets and use results to resize spare inventories and maintenance windows.
