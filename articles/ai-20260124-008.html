Contract terms and capex pacing adjust to longer equipment lead times

Signals from EPC tender language and OEM proposals suggest contract terms and capex pacing are being adjusted to reflect prolonged equipment lead times and material cost uncertainty.

EPCs are reportedly writing more detailed force majeure and schedule relief clauses into contracts to account for material and component delays. Expectations are that financiers will demand clearer milestones and contingency allowances in project financing. Scenario watch: projects without flexible procurement clauses may face rebalancing of risk or renegotiation. What to monitor are shifts in milestone-linked payments and retention structures in new RFQs. There is uncertainty about whether insurers will accept broader delay risk at current premium levels. Firms that lock in long-lead equipment early and maintain transparent supply-chain data may gain competitive advantage. The signal emphasizes contractual adaptation as a pragmatic response to persistent lead-time volatility.

What to watch
  • RFQ and contract template changes from EPCs
  • Financiers' milestone and contingency requirements
Action

Review contract templates to ensure schedule relief and procurement protections are in place before tendering.
