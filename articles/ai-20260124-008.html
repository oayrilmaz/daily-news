OEMs shifting production mix toward modular and retrofit solutions

Manufacturers are signaling a strategic pivot to modular equipment and retrofit services to capture near-term market demand while full-scale factory expansions remain capital intensive.

OEMs face a bifurcated market: long-term demand for full-scale transformers and short-term demand for rapid deployment solutions. Modular designs reduce lead time but require standardized interfaces and certified installation partners. Retrofit service lines offer higher margin and quicker revenue recognition for manufacturers. These shifts change talent requirements, emphasizing field services and systems integration over heavy manufacturing. EPC firms will find new partnership opportunities with OEMs that offer certified retrofit kits. Quality assurance, warranty definitions, and certification processes will be critical to scaling these offerings. Expect OEMs to publish updated product roadmaps focused on interoperability. The market is likely to see consolidation among service providers that can deliver end-to-end retrofit execution.

What to watch
  • New product roadmaps and certified retrofit programs from major manufacturers.
  • Mergers or partnerships between OEMs and field-service EPCs.
Action

Engage OEMs early on retrofit certification and align EPC capabilities with modular product interfaces.
