Grid stability concerns as wind and solar penetration rises

Signals from system operators suggest rising concern about inertia and voltage control as wind and solar reach higher penetration levels, increasing interest in synthetic inertia and grid-forming inverters.

Higher non-synchronous generation levels are reducing conventional inertia on several systems. System operators are evaluating synthetic inertia, fast frequency response, and grid-forming inverter strategies as mitigations. Procurement of synchronous condensers is being considered in constrained areas. Scenario watch: delayed deployment of grid-forming capabilities could necessitate more conservative curtailment practices. What to monitor: tender activity for synchronous condensers, grid-forming inverter pilots, and changes to ancillary service requirements. Project planners should build capability roadmaps for grid-forming and firming resources.

What to watch
  • tenders for synchronous condensers and grid-forming pilots
  • changes to ancillary service requirements
Action

Incorporate grid-forming and synthetic inertia scenarios into system planning and procurement roadmaps.
