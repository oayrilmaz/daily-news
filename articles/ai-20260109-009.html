HV equipment lifecycle and testing signals inform maintenance priorities

Signals from asset-management systems and testing programs point to an elevated focus on high-voltage equipment lifecycle management as an operational priority in parts of the Middle East, with testing cadence and spares planning under review.

Asset records and test schedules suggest operators are tightening maintenance windows for critical switchgear and transformer fleets. These actions are indicative signals that lifecycle risk is being actively managed rather than evidence of widespread imminent failures. Test-lab throughput and contractor availability are scenario watch factors that could affect planned maintenance execution. What to monitor: upcoming test campaigns, spare parts requisitions, and outage coordination notices that might reflect shifting priorities. Proactive oil and DGA testing, plus updated protection settings reviews, are likely outcomes of this trend. Operators should reconcile field-testing capacity with planned outage windows to avoid slippage. Inventory rationalization for long-lead spares is a pragmatic mitigation under these expectations.

What to watch
  • Upcoming DGA and oil testing campaign schedules
  • Spare-part requisition and procurement activity
Action

Prioritize critical-equipment testing and align contractor availability ahead of planned outage windows.
