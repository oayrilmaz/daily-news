High-voltage asset monitoring: expectation of expanded sensor networks

Operators are signaling plans to expand sensor networks across high-voltage assets to improve situational awareness and enable predictive maintenance ahead of peak cooling season.

Sensor deployments targeting bushing partial discharge and oil dielectric trends are reportedly being evaluated. The scenario framing favors distributed, low-power sensors that can integrate with existing SCADA or edge-analytics stacks. These deployments aim to reduce surprise failures and optimize maintenance windows. Integration challenges include cybersecurity of edge devices and data governance across utility domains. There's an expectation for OEMs to offer bundled sensing and analytics subscriptions to accelerate adoption. Conditional on pilot performance, broad rollouts could follow within 12–24 months. Treat these as operational modernization signals rather than immediate mass deployments.

What to watch
  • Pilot performance metrics for PD and oil-dielectric sensors
  • Edge-device cybersecurity assessments
Action

Pilot targeted sensor packages on critical HV assets and develop data governance and cybersecurity plans before scaling.
