EPC margins pressured by logistics and specialized labor demand

Signals from procurement and project execution indicate EPCs are facing margin pressure due to elevated logistics costs and competition for specialized installation labor; scenario planning should include cost escalation risk.

Logistics chains remain sensitive to episodic chokepoints, increasing freight and insurance costs for long-haul shipments. Skilled labor for high-voltage installation and complex civil works is in apparent short supply in some markets. EPCs may respond by offering higher overtime rates or subcontracting to specialist crews, increasing overall project cost. There is an expectation that firms will push for schedule padding and change-order clauses to cover these risks. Clients may seek fixed-price guarantees, which introduces counterparty risk for EPCs. What to monitor are labor market indicators, freight rate movements, and the incidence of change orders on active projects. Suppliers offering prefabricated or modular solutions may gain uptake as a way to reduce onsite labor needs. Contracting teams should re-evaluate escalation clauses and contingency allocations.

What to watch
  • Change-order frequency and value in active projects
  • Freight and insurance rate movements
Action

Revisit contract escalation clauses and increase contingency budgets for projects in high-logistics regions.
