Supply-chain geopolitics shaping cobalt and nickel project prioritization

Signals indicate financiers and OEMs are reprioritizing projects in regions with clearer governance and logistics, altering expectations for development timelines in some African jurisdictions.

Market participants appear to be favoring deposits with integrated processing or with-offtake assurances to mitigate geopolitical execution risk. This suggests an expectation that capital will flow more readily to lower-perceived sovereign and logistical risk profiles. The scenario watch concerns the potential for localized supply tightness if high-grade projects in higher-risk jurisdictions face delays. There is a risk that such concentration could produce price shocks for battery raw materials if demand forecasts stay robust. What to monitor are announcements of new processing plants, export-regime changes, and insurance premiums for mining operations. Downstream manufacturers are reportedly expanding recycling pilots to reduce exposure. Investors should account for longer permitting cycles and social-license factors in financial models.

What to watch
  • Changes in export or royalty regimes in producing jurisdictions
  • New processing/refining capacity announcements
Action

Factor geopolitical and logistics risk into supply contracts and pursue diversified sourcing and recycling pathways.
