Battery precursor constraints: scenario of pinch points in precursor chemicals and refining

Signals suggest pressure on precursor chemical supply chains that feed battery cathode and anode production, potentially slowing battery cell ramp rates for storage and EVs in certain scenarios.

Upstream refiners and chemical processors are being scrutinized for their capacity to convert mined concentrates into battery-grade precursors. Bottlenecks in conversion capacity can decouple mining output from downstream cell manufacturing. Investments in electrolytic refining and precursor synthesis are underway, but commissioning delays could create transitory gaps. Recycling streams are not yet scaled to materially offset precursor demand. Producers may prioritize high-margin contracts, reordering supply allocation across geographies. The short- to mid-term scenario is a staggered scale-up in cell production as precursor capacity comes online intermittently. This dynamic favors projects with secured offtake or vertical integration. Policymakers considering support measures for refining capacity could materially alter the trajectory.

What to watch
  • Commissioning schedules for precursor plants
  • Offtake agreements that prioritize specific cell manufacturers
Action

Assess precursor supply exposure in procurement contracts and consider diversifying to vertically integrated suppliers or recycling partnerships.
