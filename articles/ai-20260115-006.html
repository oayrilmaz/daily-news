Supply-chain diversification scenarios for battery feedstocks

Signals indicate ongoing scenario planning among OEMs and miners to diversify battery feedstock sources, with Africa playing a pivotal role in some supply chains.

Producers and consumers are modeling multi-sourced feedstock strategies to insulate projects from single-supplier disruptions.

Investment scenarios increasingly favor projects with near-term mine-to-refine pathways to reduce geopolitical and logistical risk.

Downstream OEMs are signaling interest in offtake-linked financing to accelerate mine development and secure critical mineral flows.

Infrastructure constraints, including grid access and transport corridors, remain a scenario risk that could slow ramp timelines.

Environmental and social governance (ESG) considerations are being stress-tested as part of due diligence in sourcing decisions.

Intelligence suggests companies should evaluate contractual flexibilities and partner options to maintain feedstock optionality over multi-year procurement cycles.

What to watch
  • New offtake-linked mine financing announcements
  • Logistics and grid-access developments near key mines
Action

Assess multi-source procurement routes and include logistics contingencies in feedstock planning.
