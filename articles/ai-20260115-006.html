Materials demand signals favor SiC and spherical graphite for next-gen power electronics

Signals suggest near-term procurement interest is tilting toward silicon carbide and spherical graphite as designers prioritize efficiency gains and cell form factors in power electronics and batteries.

Manufacturers are evaluating SiC for inverters and traction applications due to efficiency and thermal benefits. Scenario watch: supply chain bottlenecks for refined precursors could create short-term tightness even as long-term plans expand capacity. What to monitor are offtake language shifts and financing activity around precursor processing capacity. For battery anodes, interest in higher-purity spherical graphite appears linked to demand for faster charged and higher-energy cells. OEMs are signaling design moves that could increase per-unit content of these materials. Geographic concentration of refining and the capital intensity of scaling remain key constraints. Strategic inventories and multi-sourcing are likely to become more common among tier-one buyers.

What to watch
  • offtake contract structures for precursor/refined materials
  • announcements of new refining or precursor-processing capacity
Action

Assess supplier concentration risk for SiC and spherical graphite and secure staggered offtakes where feasible.
