Firmware and comms risks for modern protective relays

Signals indicate a rising cadence of firmware rollouts for protective relays and substation controllers, creating a narrow window for compatibility testing and increasing the risk of configuration mismatches during commissioning or routine updates.

Multiple vendors are pushing firmware updates to address interoperability and cybersecurity hardening. Field teams report compressed schedules to validate release candidates in live-tier test rigs. Scenario watch includes partial protection miscoordination after asynchronous updates. Expectations include a period of elevated test-failure rates and longer commissioning cycles where strict version control is not enforced. What to monitor are vendor advisory timelines and operator patching policies. Substation OT teams will likely need to increase roll-back capability and staging inventories of validated firmware. The consequence of rushed updates could be protection blinding or nuisance tripping.

What to watch
  • Vendor firmware advisories and mandatory update deadlines.
  • Any spike in relay misoperations or nuisance trips after updates.
Action

Institute strict staging and rollback procedures for relay firmware and coordinate update windows with operations.
