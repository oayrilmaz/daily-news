Upstream investment signals shift in critical minerals posture

Signals show increased capital flow into upstream critical minerals projects in Africa, presenting scenarios of both supply stabilization and new geopolitical friction points.

Exploration and development activity is rising where geology supports battery-metal deposits. This trend could ease supply-side bottlenecks over a multi-year horizon if projects reach production. The scenario also raises questions about local value capture and processing capacity. Investments in regional processing would shorten supply chains but face permitting, infrastructure, and energy constraints. What to monitor are financing arrangements, offtake structures, and commitments for local processing. Social license and community agreements will be a persistent operational risk. Downstream converters and battery makers may accelerate tie-ups to secure feedstock. Expect timeline variance; many projects will still face multi-year execution risk.

What to watch
  • Financing and offtake announcements tied to upstream projects
  • Plans for regional mineral processing facilities
Action

Map supply chain exposure and consider securing upstream optionality through diversified offtake or financing participation.
