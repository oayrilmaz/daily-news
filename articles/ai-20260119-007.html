Momentum for interoperability and HVDC standards builds as interconnection projects multiply

Signals suggest regulators and standards bodies are accelerating talks on interoperability for HVDC links and converter controls to reduce integration friction for cross-border and long-distance projects.

As interconnection projects scale, the complexity of converter control interactions and protection coordination grows. Standardization conversations are taking shape to ensure interoperability and reduce bespoke engineering. Expectations include a push for reference architectures and common comms protocols to simplify multi-vendor integration. Scenario watch highlights that lack of common standards increases project risk through longer engineering cycles and integration testing. Regulators may offer incentives or procurement preferences for projects adopting interoperable designs. OEMs that align early with emerging standards may secure preferred roles in major interconnection programs. Developers should monitor draft standards and engage in working groups to influence practical requirements. Clear interoperability frameworks could also reduce lifecycle O&M costs by easing spares management and training.

What to watch
  • draft interoperability specifications for converter controls
  • regulatory incentives tied to standard compliance
Action

Engage with standards bodies or consortia to align designs and avoid rework during future interconnection projects.
