Capacity and energy markets showing increased winter volatility in forward signals

Market signals point to elevated winter price volatility in capacity and day-ahead energy markets, influenced by tight thermal availability and evolving renewables profiles.

Market participants are preparing for higher winter volatility driven by variable fuel availability and the seasonal correlation between demand and renewable output. Expectations are that hedging costs and capacity contract prices will reflect this elevated risk. Scenario watch: a colder-than-expected winter or generator outages could trigger sharp price spikes and trigger emergency market measures. What to monitor are boiler and gas-supply availability notices and any reserve margin updates from system operators. Demand-response and storage aggregations are being evaluated as near-term tools to mitigate price exposure. Retail suppliers may increase forward purchases or seek structured products to cap exposure. Regulators could shift toward temporary market interventions if volatility translates to consumer impacts. Market design discussions on capacity remuneration and deliverability constraints are gaining fresh attention.

What to watch
  • reserve margin updates from system operators
  • fuel-supply and outage advisories
Action

Reassess hedging positions and increase monitoring of reserve margin and fuel notifications.
