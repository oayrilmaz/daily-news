Curtailment and transmission bottleneck signals increase

In several high-renewable regions, signs point to rising curtailment driven by constrained transmission corridors and uneven geographic resource distribution.

Renewable generation builds continue to outpace regional transmission upgrades in multiple jurisdictions. The mismatch creates pockets of oversupply at certain nodes and chronic underutilization of resources. Curtailment trends reduce project revenue certainty and complicate offtake economics for new builds. Markets may respond with enhanced grid investment or by modifying dispatch and congestion pricing rules to incentivize efficient flows. Scenario watch: if transmission builds lag, developers may shift investment to storage-coupled projects or different geographies. What to monitor: congestion reports, re-dispatch events, and regulatory moves to prioritize transmission siting.

What to watch
  • node-level curtailment and congestion data
  • policy changes on transmission prioritization
Action

Re-evaluate site selection economics to include curtailment risk and prioritize storage-paired models where feasible.
