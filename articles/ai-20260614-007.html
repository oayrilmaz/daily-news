Regulatory scrutiny of AI in critical infrastructure is rising in draft discussions

Signals from multiple policymaking forums indicate growing interest in defining governance and audit requirements for AI use in critical infrastructure, which could shape deployment timetables and vendor obligations.

Regulators are reportedly discussing frameworks that emphasize explainability, incident reporting, and human-in-the-loop requirements for AI systems operating in power and grid contexts. The expectation is that compliance burdens will increase for both operators and vendors, particularly around audit trails and model validation. Scenario watch: tighter rules may slow procurement cycles and favor established suppliers able to demonstrate compliance. Smaller vendors and startups could be pushed to partner with larger integrators or focus on niche, non-operational offerings. There is also potential for cross-border divergence in standards, complicating multi-national procurement. Legal teams will need to update contract clauses to address algorithmic liability and insurance coverage. For operators, governance structures and oversight functions may need rapid expansion to meet reporting and validation requirements.

What to watch
  • Draft regulatory texts and consultation windows related to AI in infrastructure.
  • Procurement RFPs adjusted to include explainability and audit clauses.
Action

Audit your AI deployments for traceability and prepare documentation that demonstrates validation and human oversight capabilities.
