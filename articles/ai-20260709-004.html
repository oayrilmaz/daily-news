Transmission bottlenecks signal continued curtailment risk

Signals indicate persistent transmission constraints in several European regions, sustaining a scenario where renewable curtailment and congestion management become routine operational considerations.

Grid operators are expected to increasingly manage high instantaneous renewable shares with local curtailment and redispatch. This dynamic raises the value of flexible assets and storage at the distribution-transmission interface. The expectation is that project developers will price for curtailment risk in offtake contracts. There is a scenario where accelerated HVDC interties reduce constraints over a multi-year horizon. In the nearer term, however, expect intermittent congestion events tied to weather-driven output. Market designs that reward flexibility will influence investment in storage and fast-ramping generation. What to monitor are transmission project approvals and queue progress. Permitting delays remain the primary gating factor for long-run relief.

What to watch
  • Transmission project approvals and milestones
  • Market signals rewarding flexibility and fast response
Action

Assess exposure to curtailment in current renewable offtake arrangements and model scenarios with higher curtailment rates.
