Regulatory signals favoring resilience investments could shape capex plans

Signals from recent consultations indicate regulators may prioritize reliability and resilience funding mechanisms, which could unlock new capital for transformers, grid hardening, and distributed resilience projects.

Stakeholder feedback appears to be tilting toward targeted resilience incentives and adjusted cost-recovery approaches. The scenario watch is that regulatory frameworks could evolve to allow faster depreciation or specific recovery for hardened assets. Utilities are likely to model multiple outcomes to optimize capex timing and project scope. This could accelerate projects that were previously marginal under traditional rate constructs. The regulatory change process can be lengthy, so near-term effects will depend on interim guidance and pilot program approvals. Watch for draft rule language and consultation outcomes to gauge timing and scope.

What to watch
  • Monitor draft regulatory guidance and pilot-program approvals.
  • Watch utility capex re-prioritizations and rate-case filings.
Action

Model multiple regulatory outcomes in capex planning and engage in consultation processes to shape cost-recovery mechanisms.
