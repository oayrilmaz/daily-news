Interconnection reform discussions create queue-resolution expectations

Regulatory signals indicate interconnection queue reform is a scenario many stakeholders are preparing for, with implications for project timelines and curtailment risk.

Discussions around fast-tracking studies and standardized cluster studies are gaining traction as a means to reduce developer backlog. Expectations are that reforms would prioritize firm capacity commitments or staged capacity milestones. Such changes could alter project financing timelines and bankability assumptions. Scenario analysis for developers should incorporate both accelerated and delayed-path outcomes for interconnection. Distribution-level hosting capacity visibility remains an operational constraint requiring parallel investments. The balance of transmission upgrades versus DER integration is central to the policy trade-offs under review.

What to watch
  • Regulatory filings on interconnection study methodology
  • Announcements of pilot cluster-study programs
Action

Update project timelines to include alternative interconnection outcomes and engage on pilot-study opportunities.
