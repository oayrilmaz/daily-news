Rising curtailment signals in high‑VRE grids expose transmission bottlenecks

Expectations are building that accelerated renewable buildouts in some Asian markets are leading to higher curtailment signals, reflecting transmission constraints and the need for more flexible grid services.

Rapid additions of solar and wind near load pockets are producing periods where generation exceeds local transfer capacity. Curtailment signals are becoming a scenario to plan for, especially in areas with delayed transmission reinforcements. Developers are increasingly considering hybridization with storage and co‑location of ancillary services to reduce revenue volatility from curtailment. Grid operators are weighing market reforms to better value flexibility, but implementation timelines remain uncertain. The continued mismatch between generation placement and transmission expansion raises merchant risk for new projects. Some project sponsors are shifting toward behind‑the‑meter or distribution‑connected models to mitigate exposure. This pattern suggests near‑term pressure on market design and transmission investment priorities.

What to watch
  • announcements of transmission reinforcements or delays
  • developer tender design changes favoring storage or hybrid projects
Action

Model curtailment scenarios into project financials and consider storage pairing to protect revenue streams.
