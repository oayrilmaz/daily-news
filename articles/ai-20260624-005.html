Day-ahead volatility remains elevated around heat-driven demand spikes

Market indicators suggest day-ahead and ancillary markets will stay volatile as heat-driven demand compresses generating margin and reshuffles reserve needs.

Price formation is being influenced by short-notice ramping needs and uncertain renewable output during peak windows. Traders and asset owners are signaling tighter spreads between day-ahead and real-time products. There is an expectation of expanded use of fast-start gas and storage assets for balancing. Market operators may tighten scarcity pricing triggers or revisit reserve definitions in response to repeated high-price intervals. The scenario of sustained volatility could compress merchant project returns and increase hedging costs. Participants are watching for regulatory tolerance of emergency interventions during extreme events. Volatility also elevates counterparty credit exposure in forward markets.

What to watch
  • emergency pricing or scarcity trigger activations
  • changes to reserve or scarcity price mechanisms
Action

Stress-test merchant revenue scenarios under elevated volatility and review hedging positions.
