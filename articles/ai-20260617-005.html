Heat-driven demand and transmission constraints hint at near-term price volatility in ancillary markets

Signals point to potential upticks in ancillary and capacity market price volatility as heat events raise demand while transmission bottlenecks limit supply mobility.

System peak demand correlated with heat waves increases reliance on fast-start units and reserves, stressing ancillary markets. Constraints on transfer capacity reduce the marginal supply pool available to constrained regions and magnify price spikes. Market participants are positioning portfolio hedges and offering more flexible resources to capture scarcity rents during these windows. Grid operators may deploy emergency reserve activations or constraint relaxations that change market-clearing outcomes in real time. Participants should expect compressed dispatch durations and faster ramping requirements for resources to be economically rewarded. What to monitor are real-time reserve activation notices, price dispersion across hubs, and operator dispatch advisories during heat peaks. Scenario planning should include short-duration volatility events that materially shift expected nodal settlement outcomes.

What to watch
  • Real-time reserve activations and nodal price spreads
  • Operator advisories on constrained transfer capability
Action

Stress-test trading and hedging positions for short-duration scarcity events and monitor nodal spreads closely.
