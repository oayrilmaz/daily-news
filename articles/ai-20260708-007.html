Regulatory signals stress AI/OT cybersecurity in critical infrastructure

Regulators and standard-setting bodies are signaling increased scrutiny of AI integration into OT environments, focusing on explainability and resilience controls for critical grid assets.

Policy drafts and consultation signals emphasize the need for deterministic fail-safes when AI models influence operational decisions. There is a clear push toward mandatory logging, rollback capabilities, and human-in-the-loop requirements for AI systems that affect safety-critical functions. Standards groups are discussing certification pathways for models and data handling consistent with existing OT security frameworks. Procurement officers are being advised to demand evidence of model testing under adversarial and degraded-data scenarios. Insurance markets are adjusting their underwriting criteria for projects that deploy AI in operations. Scenario planning suggests phased compliance timelines that could affect ongoing pilots and procurement contracts.

What to watch
  • Consultation papers and draft standards referencing AI-in-OT controls
  • Insurance underwriting updates for AI-enabled operational systems
Action

Review AI governance, logging, and rollback capabilities in operational AI pilots to align with emerging regulatory expectations.
