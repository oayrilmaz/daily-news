Onsite renewables and microgrids gaining commercial traction

Signals indicate growing commercial interest in onsite renewable microgrids in LATAM as operators seek to hedge rising fuel and grid instability risks.

Developers and operators are reporting increased RFP activity for hybrid onsite microgrids that integrate PV, storage and controllable loads. Fuel cost volatility and occasional grid reliability issues are driving the economics for onsite investments rather than reliance on utility supply. Local regulatory frameworks for behind-the-meter generation are evolving, with pilot incentives and clearer interconnection rules in some markets. Financing structures are experimenting with shared-savings or energy-as-a-service models to reduce upfront capital strain. Contractors face scaling challenges in skills and local supply chains for integrated systems. The scenario to monitor is a clustered adoption in high-demand corridors that strains local installation capacity and increases component lead times. Operational teams should prepare for coordination with local utilities on protection and anti-islanding requirements. Intelligence recommends prioritizing early-stage procurement and local partner development to secure deployment windows.

What to watch
  • Local interconnection rule updates and pilot incentive announcements.
  • RFP activity and contractor availability for integrated microgrid projects.
Action

Lock in local EPC partners and begin procurement for key components to secure deployment timing.
