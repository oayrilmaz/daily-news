Curtailment risk rises as grid bottlenecks meet record output windows

Signals of recurring high-output renewable windows suggest scenarios where transmission constraints may increase curtailment and pressure market revenues for developers.

Periods of high wind and solar output are aligning with transmission constraints in several regions, prompting scenario analysis for increased curtailment.

Grid reinforcement timelines often lag generation commissioning schedules, creating temporary oversupply pockets that depress merchant revenues.

Developers are evaluating hybridization and storage co-location as hedges against curtailment risk and market value erosion.

Capacity support markets and flexibility tenders are becoming more attractive for projects seeking predictable revenue streams in this environment.

Policy signals on expedited permitting for grid upgrades could relieve some constraints, but right-of-way and build timelines remain significant hurdles.

Expect project economics to bifurcate: those with storage or contracted offtake hold value while pure merchant assets face higher variance in near-term revenue scenarios.

What to watch
  • announcements of expedited grid upgrade approvals
  • new tenders for flexibility or capacity contracts
Action

Stress-test project models against higher curtailment scenarios and accelerate storage co-location planning where feasible.
