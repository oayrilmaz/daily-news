HV procurement signals for desalination and grid tie-ins

Signals point to coordinated HV equipment procurement tied to desalination and new industrial loads, creating a scenario where substation upgrades become a near-term programmatic focus.

Large water and industrial projects are expected to require dedicated HV feeders and step-down substations, increasing demand for GIS and large transformers. Coordination between plant integrators and transmission owners will be essential to meet ramp schedules without stranding generation assets. Procurement cycles for switchgear and protection relays could compress as developers align to plant commissioning timelines. Grid-stability considerations will push for advanced protection schemes and reactive power compensation at interconnection points. Local content rules and export-control timing may influence vendor selection and schedule risk, especially for specialized HV equipment. What to monitor: procurement notices from industrial projects, protection-scheme specifications, and interconnection timing updates.

What to watch
  • industrial project procurement notices
  • interconnection timing bulletins
Action

Engage early with project integrators to align protection-scheme requirements and confirm vendor local-content capabilities.
