Distributed solar plus storage projects facing interconnection and net-metering uncertainty

Signals from regional filings point to a scenario where changing net-metering and interconnection protocols could alter the value proposition for distributed solar-plus-storage, affecting small-scale developers and commercial consumers.

Distributed assets often depend on stable net-metering rules to capture export value. When tariff structures or compensation methodologies shift, the economics for behind-the-meter storage can change rapidly.

Interconnection studies for aggregated distributed energy resources are still evolving in many LATAM markets. Unclear aggregation rules create execution and revenue risk for virtual power plant strategies.

Commercial customers evaluating battery installs may find payback assumptions sensitive to tariff reform, leading to project deferrals or contract renegotiations with third-party owners.

Service providers that offer bundled energy-as-a-service deals may need to include contract clauses protecting against abrupt policy changes, which could increase pricing for end customers.

A scenario to monitor is a region announcing export compensation reductions during peak solar seasons, prompting a wave of new storage activations to preserve onsite value and avoid export penalties.

Financing partners will likely require policy-contingency assessments and potentially higher returns to accept regulatory risk in project underwriting.

Developers and C&I buyers should model a range of net-metering outcomes and consider hybrid revenue stacks that do not rely solely on export compensation.

What to watch
  • Regulatory consultations on net-metering and aggregation rules
  • Interconnection queue guidance for DER aggregations
Action

Re-run distributed solar-plus-storage models with alternative net-export compensation scenarios and include policy-contingency clauses in contracts.
