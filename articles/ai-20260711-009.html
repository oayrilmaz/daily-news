HVDC corridor development scenarios

Signals indicate renewed attention on HVDC corridors to transfer bulk renewables across regions; watch how corridor prioritization and financing scenarios could reshape interregional balancing.

Governments and system planners are reportedly modeling HVDC links to move offshore wind capacity to load centers. Financing and permitting scenarios will determine corridor timing and scale. The scenario includes trade-offs between merchant financing and regulated models that alter risk allocation. What to monitor are corridor prioritization lists and public-private partnership frameworks. For operators, HVDC availability timelines will affect local congestion and curtailment planning. EPCs and OEMs should assess manufacturing cadence for HVDC converter stations and cable systems. The operational benefit is greater geographic smoothing, but construction phasing may create temporary reliability constraints.

What to watch
  • Corridor prioritization and financing frameworks
  • Manufacturing cadence for converters and cable systems
Action

Incorporate HVDC timing scenarios into congestion and curtailment forecasts and engage early on financing models.
