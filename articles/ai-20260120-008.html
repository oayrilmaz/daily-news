Lead times for large transformers and switchgear remain a chokepoint

Market indicators continue to show long lead times for large power transformers and HV switchgear, suggesting project planners should incorporate supply risk into schedules and contract terms.

Signals point to factory capacity constraints and limited specialist labor as primary drivers of extended delivery windows. Expectations are for continued premium pricing on critical path equipment for projects targeting 2026–2028 commissioning. Scenario watch: a cluster of large project starts concentrated in a short window could materially stretch OEM delivery capacity. What to monitor are OEM production schedules, allocation announcements, and any moves to prioritize certain offtakers or geographies. EPCs should consider design modularity, temporary grid reinforcements, or alternative procurement strategies to reduce dependency on single vendors. There is also a signal that secondary markets for refurbished transformers and reconditioned spares are gaining traction as stopgap solutions. Contract terms that include clear liquidated damages and delivery milestones will be increasingly important.

What to watch
  • OEM production schedule updates and allocation notices
  • secondary market activity for refurbished HV equipment
Action

Secure long-lead equipment early, diversify suppliers, and include delivery protections in contracts.
