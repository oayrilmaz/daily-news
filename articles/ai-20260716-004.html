Inverter-grid interactions and curtailment signals rise with rooftop growth

Rapid distributed PV growth is increasing instances where inverter settings, reactive power export, and protection coordination create localized voltage and stability scenarios requiring updated operating practices.

Operators are observing higher frequency of localized voltage excursions during high PV export periods. These events are prompting review of inverter ride-through and reactive support settings. Protection schemes originally designed for passive loads may not perform as expected when distributed resources supply local faults. This creates scenarios where coordination studies are needed across many small generators. Curtailment strategies are being tested more often to preserve system stability, but curtailment creates revenue and political tensions, raising pressure on faster grid upgrades and smarter DER controls. Standards for inverter behavior and telemetry expectations are evolving, but deployment lags; owners must balance cost of retrofitting firmware with the risk of increased curtailment. Aggregators and distribution operators are piloting virtual power plant controls and DERMS integrations to provide system services, but scaling these controls introduces cybersecurity and market-design questions. Expect a near-term focus on targeted non-wires alternatives and grid-edge operational playbooks while transmission and distribution upgrades catch up with DER penetration.

What to watch
  • Changes in inverter default settings or firmware recall-notices.
  • Distribution-level curtailment alerts and pilot VPP rollouts.
Action

Prioritize visibility into inverter telemetry and coordinate protection studies with DER aggregators ahead of peak export seasons.
