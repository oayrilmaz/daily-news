AI-enabled O&M for offshore wind appears to be moving from pilot phase to limited deployments

Current indications suggest an uptick in procurement interest for AI-enabled predictive maintenance and drone-based inspection services for offshore wind fleets, potentially lowering operating costs but increasing reliance on specialized providers and data pipelines.

Operators are signaling interest in end-to-end inspection workflows that combine automated visual inspection, anomaly detection models, and prioritized maintenance scheduling.

If deployments scale, expected near-term benefits include targeted repairs, reduced unplanned downtime, and lower vessel usage. However, dependence on third-party analytics and remote sensing introduces data governance, bandwidth, and integration challenges, particularly for legacy SCADA systems.

Project owners should pilot open integration standards, require clear SLAs for detection accuracy and false-positive rates, and plan for secure data transport and edge-processing to reduce latency and bandwidth exposure.

What to watch
  • procurement notices for AI inspection services
  • SLAs specifying detection accuracy or false-positive thresholds
Action

Require interoperable integration and clear detection SLAs when contracting AI inspection and predictive-maintenance services.
