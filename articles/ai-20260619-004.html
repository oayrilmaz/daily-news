Solar curtailment signals rise alongside storage procurement

Signals show growing instances of solar curtailment in fast-expanding markets even as large storage procurements move from announcement to early delivery phases, suggesting short-term balancing frictions.

Rapid solar additions are creating temporal supply peaks that exceed local network absorption capacity during midday. These signals are manifesting as increased curtailment notices and redispatch events at the distribution-transmission interface.

Procurement signals for storage systems are strengthening, with multiple tenders and PPA addenda indicating storage to be co-located or sited nearby high-penetration zones. Delivery timing remains staggered, which creates an interim curtailment window.

Expectation: once storage capacity comes online, midday curtailment should decline, but the transitional period will require pragmatic operational coordination and updated dispatch rules to prioritize resource use.

What to monitor: interconnection queue movements, storage commissioning schedules, and redispatch cost trends. These metrics will provide leading indicators of when curtailment pressure will ease.

Risks include mismatch between contractual PPA settlement periods and operational curtailment windows, which could create market-dislocation pressures or project revenue shortfalls if not managed.

Implication: asset owners and system planners should coordinate site-specific storage commissioning and demand-response initiatives to minimize lost renewable energy and to smooth the transition to higher renewable penetration.

What to watch
  • storage delivery and commissioning timelines
  • interconnection queue updates in high-penetration zones
Action

Align storage commissioning with peak curtailment sites and update dispatch logic to prioritize co-located resources.
