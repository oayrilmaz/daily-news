Inverter fleet growth and inertia gap: synthetic inertia deployment scenarios

Rapid inverter-based generation growth is shifting short-duration frequency dynamics, creating signals that accelerate adoption of synthetic-inertia solutions and grid-forming strategies.

Inverter-connected resources dominate new capacity additions, changing how grids respond to fast disturbances. This reduces natural synchronous inertia and shortens windows for primary control actions. Expectations include increases in fast-frequency response procurements and pilots of grid-forming inverters. Scenario watch highlights coordination complexity when multiple vendors implement different synthetic-inertia algorithms. What to monitor are frequency nadir margins in stressed windows and procurement notices for fast-response contracts. Grid codes are evolving to require advanced capabilities, but retrofit paths for existing inverters are mixed. There is also an opportunity for aggregated DERs to provide coordinated synthetic inertia if control standards converge. The near-term outcome depends on procurement speed and operational testing.

What to watch
  • frequency nadir reports during high-renewable windows
  • procurements for fast-frequency response and grid-forming trials
Action

Assess inverter fleet capabilities and prioritize pilots for grid-forming control across a representative set of assets.
