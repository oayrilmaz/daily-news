Renewable curtailment signals drive storage co-location experiments

Persistent signs of renewable curtailment in constrained corridors are prompting experiments in co-locating storage and flexible loads to improve utilization and commercial returns.

Operators in several regions are signaling higher-than-expected curtailment rates during high wind and solar stretches. Co-located storage is emerging as a technical and commercial lever to capture value that would otherwise be lost to curtailment. Developers are testing contractual structures that share storage value between project owners, offtakers, and grid operators. Grid congestion and limited transmission remains the primary driver; permitting timelines for new lines lag project buildouts. Scenario watch: if storage permitting accelerates, developers may prioritize brownfield co-location over greenfield transmission-dependent projects. The merchant economics for co-located storage hinge on capacity markets, balancing markets, and clearer revenue stacking rules. Expect policy discussions to intensify around priority dispatch, curtailment compensation, and incentives for storage adjacent to congested nodes. OEMs that offer integrated storage-inverter-transformer packages could capture a growing retrofit and brownfield market.

What to watch
  • storage co-location tenders
  • regulatory moves on curtailment compensation
Action

Evaluate co-location options for constrained assets and engage early with regulators on revenue stacking and compensation frameworks.
