Grid capacity signals and curtailment risk for new wind and solar

Signals suggest rising volumes of intermittent generation entering interconnection queues are creating scenarios where near-term curtailment risk is a material commercial consideration.

Project developers and grid planners are reportedly debating siting and dispatch strategies to mitigate congestion-driven curtailment.

The intelligence framing notes that while long-term capacity expansion exists in scenarios, near-term network constraints are the operational reality for many sites.

Energy storage pairing and dynamic curtailment hedges emerge as frequent mitigation scenarios in project-level planning.

Market participants may seek location-specific contracts that account for expected curtailment patterns rather than assuming full dispatchability.

Regulatory signals on connection charges and queue reform will shape how quickly new projects can economically dispatch into constrained areas.

If storage costs continue to fall, hybrid projects that co-locate batteries could outcompete standalone renewable builds in congested zones.

Monitoring interconnection queue changes and DSOs' short-term congestion forecasts will reveal which projects face the highest curtailment risk.

What to watch
  • Interconnection queue updates and proposed queue reforms
  • Distribution-system operator congestion forecasts for high-development zones
Action

Incorporate curtailment scenarios into financial models and prioritize storage co-location or flexible contracting in congested regions.
