Hybrid wind-solar projects prompt reevaluation of interconnection studies

Developers and system planners are signaling increased deployment of co-located wind-solar hybrids, creating new expectations for interconnection studies and curtailment modeling.

Early signals suggest hybrid sites complicate traditional interconnection studies due to correlated output and shared grid assets. Planners are expected to adapt thermal and stability analyses to reflect combined variability and potential for complementary generation profiles. Scenario discussions include updated curtailment algorithms and revised capacity factor assumptions for financial models. Grid operators may request additional meteorological and short-term forecasting data to support reliability assessments. OEMs for inverters and plant controllers are likely to pitch solutions that enable independent dispatch of sub-assets while presenting a unified grid-side profile. Project timelines could extend as studies add complexity and new data requirements. The near-term competitive advantage will go to developers who can supply high-resolution operational data and integrated control strategies.

What to watch
  • monitor interconnection study requirements in key ISOs
  • request high-resolution site data for hybrids early in RFPs
Action

Include hybrid-specific assumptions in financial models and prepare for extended interconnection study timelines.
