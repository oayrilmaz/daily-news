Offshore wind grid integration and reactive support strategies

Expect growing focus on reactive power services from offshore wind as system operators seek tools to manage voltage and dynamic stability with high inverter-based resource penetration.

Offshore wind parks are increasingly being designed with capability for dynamic reactive support and grid-forming controls. This trend responds to signals of declining synchronous generation in some coastal grids. Scenario watch: insufficient reactive capability could drive operational curtailment or require costly onshore reinforcements. What to monitor: interconnection studies referencing reactive support, procurement of dynamic reactive devices, and firmware upgrades for converters. This brief frames likely near-term responses rather than documenting completed upgrades. Turbine and converter OEMs may play a pivotal role in enabling grid-support compliance at scale.

What to watch
  • Interconnection study updates citing reactive power shortfalls
  • Converter firmware release notes enabling grid-support modes
Action

Confirm reactive capability requirements in contracts and test plans for offshore converter fleets.
