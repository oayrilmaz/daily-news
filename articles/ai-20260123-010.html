Corporate offtakes and grid access friction shape project siting choices

Signals show corporate buyers are increasingly factoring grid access and curtailment risk into offtake decisions, shifting project siting to less-congested nodes and prompting new commercial structures.

Corporate offtakers are requesting clearer locational risk allocation in PPAs to avoid unexpected curtailment impacting voluntary emissions targets. Developers are responding by prioritizing sites with stronger interconnection prospects or by bundling storage to firm deliveries. Grid congestion in key export corridors is influencing developer site selection and capex profiles. New commercial constructs, including guaranteed delivery tranches and asymmetric curtailment sharing, are appearing in negotiations. Scenario watch: if congestion persists, developers may favor incremental repowering and behind-the-meter corporate supply to satisfy demand. Expect more detailed grid-impact analyses during bid rounds and tighter negotiation of curtailment compensation clauses.

What to watch
  • PPA negotiations specifying locational curtailment allocation
  • Developer preference signals for sites with lower congestion risk
Action

Build curtailment scenarios into PPA financial models and consider storage or staged delivery guarantees to protect offtake value.
