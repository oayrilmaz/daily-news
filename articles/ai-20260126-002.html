Aging GIS and switchgear backlog emerges as a near-term maintenance choke point

Signals from maintenance networks suggest an accumulating backlog of GIS and high-voltage switchgear replacements, creating a scenario watch for outages and delayed refurbishments across multiple European utilities.

Inspection cycles are uncovering more cases where older GIS modules approach end-of-life thresholds, increasing urgency for replacement or major overhaul. Procurement timelines for replacement modules remain extended in many vendor networks, raising the prospect of temporary workarounds instead of full replacements. Utilities may prioritize critical nodes and defer lower-risk assets, which shifts operational risk to less-monitored locations. Logistics friction for oversized components and specialized labor availability is contributing to schedule slippage. There is an expectation that planned maintenance windows will face compression, increasing the frequency of out-of-hours work. This environment elevates the importance of contingency planning and spare parts inventory management. Over the next several weeks, watch for notifications of extended maintenance windows and temporary configuration workarounds.

What to watch
  • Public or regulatory filings about extended maintenance timelines or emergency works
  • Vendor shipment advisories and labor availability statements
Action

Audit critical substation asset end-of-life dates and prioritize spares and contingency plans for the next maintenance cycle.
