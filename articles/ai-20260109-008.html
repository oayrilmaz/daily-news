OEM consolidation and aftermarket service strategies — market implications

Signals point to continued consolidation among equipment OEMs and a pivot toward aftermarket services as margins on new large-equipment sales compress.

Several scenario analyses indicate OEMs are expanding field-service footprints and investing in digital service platforms to capture recurring revenue. This shift is altering competitive dynamics, with smaller EPCs seeking partnerships to access OEM diagnostic tools. Aftermarket contracts increasingly bundle predictive analytics, spare parts provisioning, and SLA guarantees. Buyers must weigh the convenience of integrated service offerings against potential vendor lock-in and pricing opacity. Consolidation may also reduce supplier diversity for long-lead components, amplifying supply-chain risk. Procurement teams are therefore revising contract terms to preserve interoperability rights and spare-parts sourcing flexibility. For project owners, service-led OEM strategies change lifecycle cost calculations and necessitate tighter SLAs around availability and replacement timeframes.

What to watch
  • New service-platform rollouts from major OEMs
  • Contract clauses on interoperability and spare-parts
Action

Negotiate interoperability clauses and multi-sourcing options when engaging with OEM service contracts to limit lock-in.
