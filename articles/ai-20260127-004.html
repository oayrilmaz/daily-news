Connection queues and curtailment risk shaping project economics

Signals point to a growing squeeze between renewable project deployment and available grid capacity in several European markets, creating a scenario where curtailment risk and delayed connections could materially shift PPA economics and project schedules.

Connection queuing is increasingly shaping the cashflow profiles of new renewable projects. Operators and developers are weighing rightsization versus waiting for deeper grid reinforcements. Curtailment expectations are influencing offtake pricing and risk allocation in PPAs. Some corporate buyers are reportedly adjusting procurement timelines in response to market volatility. Grid reinforcements and storage co-locations are being discussed as mitigations, but these raise capital complexity. Policy decisions on prioritization could accelerate or delay projects at scale. The current picture is built from signals and scenario assessments rather than comprehensive confirmations. Developers should model extended connection timelines into near-term project finance assumptions.

What to watch
  • Monitor transmission reinforcement approvals and expected in-service dates
  • Track PPA pricing adjustments and buyer procurement pauses
Action

Re-run financial models with extended connection and curtailment scenarios and explore storage co-location options.
