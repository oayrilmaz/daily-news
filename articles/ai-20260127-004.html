Curtailment risk rising as offshore wind growth bumps transmission limits

As offshore capacity expands, intelligence indicates transmission constraints and congestion are likely to increase curtailment risk absent near-term operational or permitting interventions.

Grid balancing signals show increasing seasonal congestion on coastal transmission corridors. Developers and system operators are said to be considering stepped curtailment models to manage overload events. There is an expectation that market mechanisms may be adjusted to prioritize commissioned assets or firm contracts during peak export windows. Scenario watch: prolonged curtailment could distort merchant revenue forecasts and delay FID timelines for new projects. Operational workarounds under discussion include phased grid reinforcements and dynamic curtailment schedules aligned to market signals. What to monitor: transmission outage plans, incremental connection approvals, and any short-term redispatch notices from system operators. The intelligence suggests value in commercial hedges for projects exposed to curtailment.

What to watch
  • Transmission outage schedules in coastal corridors
  • Redispatch or network reinforcement notices from TSOs
Action

Assess curtailment exposure in project revenue models and explore contractual hedges or staged commissioning options.
