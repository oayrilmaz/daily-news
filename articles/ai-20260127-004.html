Curtailment risks and synthetic inertia drive interconnect planning

Expectations are emerging that higher variable renewable penetration will force grid planners to prioritize synchonous-inertia substitutes and dynamic grid codes in upcoming capacity plans.

Signals from market feedback and engineering proposals suggest curtailment risk is being internalized into project valuations. The scenario assumes more frequent periods where thermal dispatch is constrained and renewables output must be managed through active measures. Synthetic inertia, fast frequency response and grid-forming inverter strategies are becoming central engineering conversations. Transmission expansion is still necessary but may not fully remove near-term constraints, creating a layered approach of local inverter-based solutions and selective reinforcements. Developers are factoring curtailment scenarios into PPAs and merchant revenue forecasts. Grid-code changes that require specific inverter functions are a near-term policy watch. Technical validation of large-scale grid-forming inverter behavior under stressed conditions is a recurring engineering caveat.

What to watch
  • Regulatory signals on mandatory inverter ride-through and synthetic inertia requirements.
  • Developer contract terms that include curtailment compensation or dispatch limits.
Action

Revisit inverter specifications and PPA clauses to account for expected grid-code tightening and curtailment scenarios.
