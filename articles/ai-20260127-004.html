Grid flexibility becoming the constraint for new solar-plus-storage in congested regions

Developers signal that interconnection queues are increasingly constrained by transmission flexibility rather than pure capacity, pushing more projects toward co-located storage and advanced grid services.

In several congested transmission corridors, the marginal constraint is not just MW capacity but the ability to manage ramping and reverse power flows. This is prompting project teams to design solar PV with co-located batteries to offer firming and ancillary services. Market frameworks that compensate fast-ramping and congestion relief services are shaping commercial models. Expect more negotiation around interconnection agreements to include visibility into storage dispatch rules and telemetry. EPCs must consider additional protection and anti-islanding controls when pairing inverters and BESS at scale. Scenario watch focuses on curtailment risk reduction and revenue stacking from capacity, energy, and grid services. Developers should monitor evolving interconnection study criteria and tariff changes that affect storage valuation.

What to watch
  • changes to interconnection study rules
  • tariff updates for ancillary services
Action

Design new PV projects assuming co-located storage and negotiate interconnection terms that preserve dispatch flexibility.
