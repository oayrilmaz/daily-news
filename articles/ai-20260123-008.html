EPC financing and contractor risk under a tighter credit regime

Expectations are emerging that tighter financing conditions could alter contractor behavior on large HV and renewable EPC projects, leading to more conservative bidding and extended mobilization timelines.

Signals from deal pipelines indicate contractors are pricing higher risk premiums and demanding advanced milestone payments. Lenders appear to be scrutinizing balance-sheet exposure to long-tail construction and commissioning risk more closely. This dynamic is prompting sponsors to rebalance risk allocation or to offer enhanced guarantees to secure delivery. Procurement schedules are stretching as contractors seek to de-risk through staged mobilization or higher contingency lines. Scenario watch: a material credit event in the contractor sector could cascade into broader project delays and renegotiations. What to monitor: changes in bid bond sizes, requests for higher advance payments, and amendments to standard EPC contract terms.

What to watch
  • increases in bid bonds and advance-payment requests
  • amendments to standard EPC contract terms in new tenders
Action

Revisit contract terms and financing contingencies to ensure sponsor and lender expectations are aligned with current contractor risk pricing.
