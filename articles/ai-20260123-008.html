Signals: OEMs push digital-twin services to lock customers into lifecycle revenue

Signals point to OEMs packaging digital twins and predictive services with equipment sales as a differentiation play that could alter procurement and warranty dynamics.

Digital twins offer value in performance optimization and predictive maintenance when data fidelity is high. OEMs are positioning these services as part of integrated solutions that include spare parts and update services. That bundling can shorten procurement cycles but may create vendor lock and constrain interoperability. Buyers should expect contract clauses that tie performance guarantees to continuous data flows and software updates. There is also a practical consideration: digital twin efficacy depends on field-calibration and data governance, which many projects under-resource. From an EPC perspective, integration of twin outputs into asset management systems requires upfront planning. This trend reshapes project economics toward lifecycle O&M and away from pure hardware sales.

What to watch
  • Warranty and data-access clauses in OEM service agreements
  • Interoperability requirements for twin integrations
Action

Negotiate data access and interoperability terms when accepting bundled digital-twin services to avoid vendor lock-in.
