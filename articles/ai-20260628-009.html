Digital twin pilots for substation lifecycle management gaining traction

Signals point to growing pilots of digital twins for substation asset lifecycle management, offering scenario benefits in predictive maintenance and spares optimization but raising data governance and integration questions.

Utilities and owners are reportedly piloting digital twin layers that integrate SCADA, protection settings, and commissioning records. These pilots aim to reduce unplanned outages through predictive maintenance and improved spare parts forecasting. Scenario risks include data mismatches between legacy asset records and live telemetry, which can reduce model utility or produce false positives. Integration complexity across vendors and proprietary formats is a common friction point in these tests. Cyber hygiene of the twin environment remains a key consideration as models often require access to sensitive configuration data. Successful pilots could materially improve lifecycle costing and planning accuracy, but scaling remains non-trivial. Track API standardization efforts and pilot-to-production pathways as signals of broader adoption.

What to watch
  • Announcements of pilot completions or moves to production for digital twin platforms.
  • Standards activity or API releases addressing vendor interoperability.
Action

Run integration readiness assessments and tighten data governance before scaling digital-twin pilots to production.
