AI-driven predictive maintenance signals reshaping O&M for substations and data centers

Adoption signals for AI-based predictive maintenance are rising, offering scenario pathways to lower unplanned outages but introducing new data-integration and cybersecurity considerations.

Early deployments of AI models for transformer and UPS anomaly detection show promise in reducing mean time to detection and improving maintenance scheduling. These projects generate signals about recurring data-quality challenges and the need for edge analytics to limit latency. Scenario planning must now account for model drift, training-data biases, and integration with existing SCADA and asset-management systems. Cybersecurity is a growing concern as predictive systems expand attack surfaces and require robust identity and access controls. There is an expectation that proof-of-concept projects will move to scaled pilots in the next operational cycle if data-readiness thresholds are met. Monitor pilot performance metrics and change-control processes for model updates.

What to watch
  • pilot performance metrics and model drift indicators
  • integration status with SCADA and asset-management systems
Action

Start small-scale pilots with clear data governance and cybersecurity requirements before scaling predictive-maintenance solutions.
