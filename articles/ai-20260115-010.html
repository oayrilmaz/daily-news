Cross-border interconnector financing gap creates project timeline scenarios

Signals point to persistent financing and political risks for major interconnection projects in Latin America, creating scenarios where project phasing or public guarantees become necessary to proceed.

Interconnector projects offer operational and market benefits but require complex multi-jurisdictional financing arrangements. Scenarios model delayed timelines when sovereign guarantees or multilateral bank support are absent. Political cycle changes in participating states often introduce renegotiation windows that can stall progress. Private capital appetite exists but typically demands risk mitigation mechanisms that are not uniformly available. Currency and regulatory harmonization issues raise economic viability questions in plausible adverse scenarios. Design phasing, where initial capacity is smaller and expanded later, emerges as a commonly discussed mitigation. Monitor statements from regional development institutions and any new blended-finance instruments as early indicators. Project proponents should prepare multiple contracting and currency-risk hedging paths.

What to watch
  • Announcements of blended-finance instruments or sovereign guarantees
  • Political or regulatory changes in participating countries
Action

Prepare phased-design and blended-finance options and engage with development financiers early.
