Battery recycling and urban-mining signals shift supply dynamics

Signals of accelerating investment in battery recycling and urban-mining technologies are creating a scenario where secondary supplies could meaningfully diversify feedstock sources for EV and storage value chains over the medium term.

Recycling pilots and scaling efforts are showing potential to recover critical elements from end-of-life batteries and manufacturing scrap. If these projects scale, they could reduce pressure on primary mining supply and shorten lead-times for some materials. The scenario requires robust collection networks, standardized chemistries, and recycling economics that survive commodity price swings. Policymakers and OEMs may use incentives or closed-loop procurement to accelerate material circularity. There are upstream implications for ore-processing investment decisions if recycled streams become more predictable. Environmental, health and safety considerations will remain central to permitting and siting of recycling facilities. What to monitor: announced scaling of recycling capacity, regulatory incentives for circularity, and changes in feedstock composition reported by processors.

What to watch
  • Scaling announcements for recycling facilities
  • Regulatory incentives for material circularity
Action

Evaluate partnerships with recycling providers and consider procurement strategies that incorporate secondary material streams.
