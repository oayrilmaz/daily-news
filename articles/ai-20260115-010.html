Hybrid plant plus storage scenarios reshape grid connection strategies

Signals of growing investment in solar-plus-storage and wind-solar hybrids are creating operational scenarios that change grid connection and curtailment strategies in high insolation regions.

Developers are modelling hybrid architectures to increase firm energy delivery and reduce curtailment risk at constrained interconnection points. This expectation is driving interest in co-optimized dispatch algorithms and contractual structures that value capacity as well as energy. Signals suggest grid operators will test new interconnection rules to accommodate hybrid plants and behind-the-meter storage aggregation. There is a scenario watch for redefined grid codes and inverter requirements to support hybrid plant grid services like voltage control and ramp management. What to monitor are pilot trials for co-located control systems and any regulatory updates to interconnection requirements for storage-coupled renewables. OEMs that can supply integrated power-electronics and energy-management systems will have an advantage in delivering turnkey hybrids. Strategically, hybridization reduces merchant exposure while increasing technical complexity that must be addressed in design and O&M planning.

What to watch
  • regulatory updates to interconnection rules for hybrid and storage-coupled projects
  • pilot deployments of co-located renewables and energy-management systems
Action

Assess hybrid plant options and engage with regulators on proposed interconnection and inverter requirements.
