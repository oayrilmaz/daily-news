Substation cybersecurity and AI risk scenarios

Signals indicate rising attention to AI-related cyber risk in substations, prompting scenario planning for anomalous model outputs and automated control interactions.

Operators are reportedly conducting tabletop exercises that include AI model compromise scenarios and false-positive detection chains. The scenario framing emphasizes the need for model validation, access controls, and fail-safe engineering. Vendors are being asked to provide audit trails and secure telemetry assumptions as part of pilot scopes. There is an expectation that integration of AI into protection or control loops will be staged behind rigorous human oversight. Incident response playbooks are being updated to include model isolation and data rollback steps. What to monitor are procurement clauses mandating explainability, secure enclaves, or third-party model audits.

What to watch
  • Procurement clauses requiring model explainability and audits
  • Updated incident response playbooks referencing model isolation
Action

Incorporate AI-failure modes into cyber incident exercises and require vendors to demonstrate secure model lifecycle controls.
