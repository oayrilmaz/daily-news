Solar+storage finance faces currency and policy tail risks in key LATAM markets

Expectations are growing that financing terms for solar-plus-storage projects in LATAM will be increasingly impacted by currency volatility and policy unpredictability, prompting scenario planning among sponsors and lenders.

Developers and lenders are reportedly layering currency hedges and political-risk insurance into financial close strategies more frequently. This is an expectation that unhedged revenue streams from merchant offtake and local-denominated contracts carry meaningful downside. The scenario watch centers on the interaction between sovereign policy shifts and contracted revenue; changes to energy-market rules could reprice project returns rapidly. There is a risk that higher hedging costs will compress project returns or stall late-stage financings. What to monitor are central bank interventions, subsidy adjustments, and changes to taxation or local content rules. Sponsors are increasingly structuring blended revenue stacks and seeking hard-currency offtake where possible. If the currency and policy environment remains volatile, expect a rise in developer equity contributions and longer tenor requirements from cautious financiers.

What to watch
  • Central-bank and fiscal policy moves in key LATAM jurisdictions
  • Shifts in local content or taxation rules affecting project economics
Action

Include currency and policy-stress scenarios in financial models and pursue hard-currency revenue or hedging strategies where feasible.
