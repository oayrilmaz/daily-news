Ancillary-services volatility expected in inverter-dominated markets

Market signals point to higher short-term volatility in ancillary-services prices where synchronous inertia has declined significantly, creating opportunities and operational stresses for fast-response assets.

As inverter-based generation increases share, frequency and ramping events produce sharper price dislocations in reserve and regulation markets. Fast-response assets such as batteries and fast-start gas are likely to capture elevated prices during transient periods, but revenue windows may remain unpredictable. Expectation: procurement models will increasingly favor aggregation and flexibility contracts to smooth revenue streams for distributed resources. Market operators may introduce new product definitions or procurement windows to manage volatility, but implementation timelines can be protracted. Scenario watch: a concentrated loss of a large synchronous unit could trigger multi-market price cascades and liquidity stress for smaller participants. What to monitor: real-time price volatility, product rule change consultations, and aggregator capacity announcements.

What to watch
  • Real-time reserve and regulation price spikes
  • Regulatory consultations on new ancillary products
Action

Assess participation strategies for fast-response assets and consider aggregator partnerships to stabilize short-term revenue exposure.
