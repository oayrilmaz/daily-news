Short-term capacity market volatility scenarios

Market signals indicate heightened short-duration price volatility in capacity-constrained regions, which could affect procurement strategies for peak shaving resources and ancillary services.

Price spikes for short-duration capacity can create arbitrage opportunities for battery resources but also increase operational risk for buyers dependent on fossil peakers. The elasticity of supply in those hours is limited.

Expectation: traders and aggregators may expand participation in fast frequency response and reserve products to capitalize on short window scarcity. That will push assets toward faster dispatch capabilities.

The scenario of sudden price volatility may lead to contractual disputes if performance obligations are unmet during stress hours. That risk is higher for newly aggregated distributed assets with limited historical performance.

Market design changes that accelerate settlement times and incentivize fast-response services could reduce volatility but require system operator upgrades. Implementation timelines are typically multi-year.

Monitor real-time price dispersion and frequency deviation metrics as early indicators of stress. Those signals can precede formal scarcity declarations.

Participants without flexible hedging strategies may face materially higher costs during sustained volatility episodes.

What to watch
  • Real-time price spikes
  • Frequency deviation trends
Action

Re-evaluate hedging strategies for peak exposure and consider fast-response assets for ancillary revenue capture.
