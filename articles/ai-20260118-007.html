Grid-code modernization under way for inverter performance

Expectations are that several jurisdictions in the region are advancing grid-code updates focused on inverter performance, testing, and certification to secure network stability as inverter-based generation scales.

Regulatory discussions emphasize measurable inverter outputs such as synthetic inertial response and voltage support. The intelligence portrayal suggests harmonization efforts to avoid fragmented requirements that could stifle equipment markets. Test-bed facilities and certification labs are being explored to de-risk deployments. Developers and manufacturers face potential rework costs if firmware and hardware must be upgraded to meet new criteria. There is a policy push to balance reliability needs with cost impacts on renewable projects. Enforcement mechanisms and timelines remain a subject of negotiation, creating uncertainty for near-term projects. The scenario outcome could be staggered compliance windows and transitional derogations for legacy fleets.

What to watch
  • draft consultation documents and comment deadlines
  • development of regional certification labs
Action

Engage in consultations and align procurement specifications with emerging grid-code expectations to avoid retrofit costs.
