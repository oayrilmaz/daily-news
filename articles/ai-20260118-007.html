Draft interconnection standard discussions create near-term policy uncertainty

Signals from regulatory dockets indicate active discussion about interconnection standard changes that could alter technical requirements and queue management, creating planning uncertainty for developers and utilities.

Early-stage proposals appear focused on clarifying technical studies, cost allocation, and timelines for queue processing. Stakeholders are articulating competing expectations about fairness, speed, and system impact. There is an expectation that any change will shift project economics and could impose retrofit obligations on projects in advanced stages. Scenario watch includes transitional rules that apply unevenly across projects and raise rework costs. What to monitor are published draft proposals, comment periods, and working group schedules. Utilities may need to realign interconnection engineering resources and update standard agreements. The intelligence implication is that developers should model multiple interconnection outcome scenarios and consider contractual protections for queue re-prioritization.

What to watch
  • Publication of draft standard text and comment deadlines
  • Utility notices on queue processing and study timelines
Action

Model alternate interconnection outcomes for active projects and update contractual risk clauses.
