AI for protection and control: benefits and correlated-failure risk scenario

Scenario watch: AI-enhanced protection and control algorithms promise faster fault detection and adaptive protection, but shared model adoption could introduce correlated-failure modes that require governance and simulation-based validation.

AI can improve discrimination between fault and non-fault events, reducing unnecessary tripping and improving service continuity. Adaptive protection with learned thresholds can optimize relay coordination in evolving grid topologies. However, if many assets use similar models trained on overlapping datasets, the risk of simultaneous misclassification under an unusual system state increases. This creates a scenario where model-driven responses could unintentionally magnify disturbances. Governance frameworks, diverse-model ensembles, and rigorous failure-mode simulations are being signaled as needed mitigations. Standards bodies and operators may require explainability, version control, and rollback mechanisms before wide deployment. Engineering teams should adopt digital twins to stress-test ML-driven protection under rare but plausible contingencies.

What to watch
  • Proposals for standards on AI model explainability and rollback mechanisms in protection systems.
  • Field pilots integrating ML protection with legacy relay architectures and their failure-mode reports.
Action

Establish model-governance requirements, run digital-twin failure-mode simulations, and require rollback capabilities for ML-driven protection deployments.
