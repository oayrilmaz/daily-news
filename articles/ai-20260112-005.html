Volatility in power markets expected amid weather and gas-price swings

Signals from futures and OTC conversations indicate heightened near-term volatility in power markets as weather-driven demand and natural gas price shifts create non-linear price dynamics in several regions.

Market participants are signaling wider bid-ask spreads and tighter liquidity in certain seasonal products. Expectations include intermittent price spikes during cold snaps or heat waves, particularly where gas-fired generation is marginal. Scenario watch includes cascade effects on ancillary services pricing as operators procure reserves at short notice. What to monitor are forward curve movements, spark spreads, and reserve procurement notices from ISOs. Hedging desks are increasingly favoring shorter-tenor instruments and flex contracts tied to weather indices. For project finance, fluctuating merchant revenue assumptions could prompt renegotiation of covenants or adjustment of capital buffers. Traders and risk managers should account for potential correlation shifts between gas and power that could erode traditional hedging effectiveness.

What to watch
  • Forward curve movements and spark spread shifts
  • Reserve procurement and ancillary services notices from ISOs
Action

Tighten short-term hedging and liquidity plans, and model stress scenarios for correlated gas-power shocks.
