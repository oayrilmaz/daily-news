Capacity and ancillary market volatility as renewables expand

Market signals point to rising short-term volatility in capacity and ancillary service prices as variable renewables displace thermal capacity without equivalent fast-acting resources.

Expect higher price dispersion in real-time and ancillary markets, especially in regions with rapid renewable penetration. Scenario watch includes increased opportunity for flexible resources and battery providers to capture scarcity rents. Market design frictions, including slow procurement cycles for inertia and fast frequency response, could exacerbate price spikes. Settlement risk and disputes may rise as participants contest credit and performance obligations under stressed conditions. Regulators may be pressured to adjust market parameters or create new products for long-duration flexibility. Investment signals from volatile pricing could accelerate deployments of storage and demand-response if revenue certainty improves. However, financing terms could tighten if perceived merchant risks remain high. Data quality and forecasting improvements will be critical for market participants to manage exposure. Overall, transitional market turbulence is a credible expectation before product and policy adjustments align with resource mix.

What to watch
  • Intraday/real-time price spikes and reserve scarcity events
  • Regulatory consultations on new market products for inertia and fast response
Action

Model exposure to ancillary market volatility and evaluate opportunities for flexible assets to participate in scarcity markets.
