Hydro variability and distributed solar ramp present operational scenarios

Signals suggest drought-driven hydro variability combined with rapid distributed solar growth will create scenarios requiring enhanced flexibility and new procurement of peaking resources.

Hydropower variability can shift reserve needs toward thermal or battery peakers in certain basins, particularly during dry seasons. At the same time, rooftop solar growth is changing midday net load shapes and reducing daytime wholesale prices in some markets. Scenario expectations include ramping stress in early evening periods and greater reliance on flexible gas units or batteries. Market and procurement signals could include targeted capacity tenders for fast-start resources and incentives for demand response. What to monitor are reservoir level reports, distributed generation registration trends, and dispatch sequencing changes. Grid operators may need to refine forecasting tools to account for high distributed PV penetration. Long-term planning might prioritize diversified flexibility portfolios and transmission investments to manage basin-to-basin transfers.

What to watch
  • Reservoir level and inflow reporting for major hydro basins.
  • Registrations and interconnection queues for distributed PV and DERs.
Action

Incorporate seasonal hydro scenarios and distributed PV growth into short-term procurement and flexibility planning.
