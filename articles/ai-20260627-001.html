AI operational tooling expands; cyber+control tradeoffs surface

Operators increasingly pilot AI agents for load forecasting and dispatch, but early signals suggest a widening tradeoff between operational gains and new control-plane exposures.

Utilities report exploratory deployments of ML-driven situational awareness and automated dispatch agents as a scenario to cut response times and optimize asset use. These pilots often sit atop legacy SCADA and newer telemetry, creating hybrid control planes that may not share consistent security postures. The intelligence posture shows frequent small anomalies in command sequences that could be benign automation artifacts or early indicators of misconfiguration. Expect a growing emphasis on explainability and human-in-the-loop controls as operators balance efficiency against risk. Vendors are likely to present bolt-on security features, though integration complexity could delay protective benefits. Scenario watch: look for correlated rises in anomalous access logs and automated control actions during peak tests as a precursor to operational risk.

What to watch
  • Anomalous command sequences during automation tests
  • Vendor integration timelines for AI explainability features
Action

Validate human-in-the-loop controls and increase logging fidelity for AI-driven dispatch trials.
