Ancillary services volatility expected as storage and EV load patterns shift

Expectations of changing load shape from EV charging and the rapid deployment of storage are producing signals of higher short-term volatility in ancillary service markets.

Market participants are modeling more frequent and sharper dispatch swings as aggregated EV charging coincides with storage cycling. That dynamic could widen spreads in frequency regulation and reserve products. Operators and market designers are reportedly evaluating new product granularity and faster settlement mechanisms to ensure reliability and proper price signals. There is a scenario where price spikes incentivize rapid storage arbitrage but also raise integration complexity for aggregators. Watch for market rule filings and pilot programs testing faster procurement intervals. The commercial implication is a need for trading and asset management systems to handle higher temporal granularity. Participants should prepare for more active participation requirements in ancillary markets.

What to watch
  • market rule filings on faster settlement
  • pilot programs for high-frequency ancillary procurement
Action

Upgrade market operations tooling to handle higher cadence settlements and prepare aggregators for active participation.
