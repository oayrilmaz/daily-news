Short-term volatility expected as renewables surge and thermal retirements proceed

Market signals point toward wider intra-day price swings in regions where thermal capacity is retiring faster than flexible replacements arrive, creating scenario risk for balancing and ancillary markets.

The pace of thermal retirements in some markets is outstripping commissioning of flexible gas and storage, increasing reliance on demand-side flexibility. That gap is expected to widen intraday price spreads and raise ancillary service costs during steep ramps. Traders and asset owners may reprice risk, increasing hedging premiums for capacity that can provide rapid response. There is a scenario where increased curtailment and negative prices occur during high renewable output hours, followed by sharp price spikes during evening ramps. Market operators could respond with temporary product changes, capacity adders, or emergency procurement. Participants should watch out for reconfiguration of bidding zones and changes to scarcity pricing rules. Monitor day-ahead vs real-time spreads as an early indicator of stress.

What to watch
  • day-ahead to real-time price spreads and scarcity pricing signals
  • authorities' proposals for temporary market product changes
Action

Stress-test market exposure across high-renewable ramp scenarios and review hedging strategies for fast-response capabilities.
