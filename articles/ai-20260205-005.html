Ancillary Markets See Growing Demand Signals

As flexibility needs increase, signals suggest ancillary markets will expand and evolve procurement methods to secure fast-response resources and aggregated distributed resources.

Market operators appear to be exploring products and procurement windows tailored to sub-second and minute-scale responses. This evolution could elevate the commercial value of batteries, fast-ramping thermal assets, and aggregated DERs. There is an expectation that new bidding formats and settlement rules will emerge to reflect speed and accuracy of response. Aggregators and software providers may find commercial opportunities to bundle compliance services for smaller assets. The change may also pressure conventional capacity markets to better reflect operational flexibility rather than static capacity. Trading desks and asset owners will likely need to adjust valuation models to include new revenue streams from speed-based services. Regulatory clarity on measurement and verification will be a key near-term enabler. The net effect may be a reallocation of revenue within generation and storage portfolios.

What to watch
  • market operator consultations on new fast-response products
  • aggregator participation filings and pilot results
Action

Assess asset participation potential in emerging speed-based ancillary products and update valuation models accordingly.
