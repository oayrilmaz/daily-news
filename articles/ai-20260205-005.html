Ancillary markets face structural change from distributed flexibility

Signals point to growing interest in aggregating distributed energy resources to compete in ancillary markets, prompting market operators to reassess participation rules and settlement mechanisms.

Aggregators and platform providers are reportedly conducting pilots to stack value from frequency response, reserves, and capacity. The scenario watch is that market design will need to address telemetry requirements, settlement granularity, and double-counting risks. Expectations include phased relaxations to participation thresholds alongside strengthened telemetry and verification protocols. System operators may require tighter cybersecurity and data-integrity audits for aggregated assets. Another signal is that clearer price signals could accelerate DER investment if market entry costs are reduced. What to monitor are rulemaking comments and pilot outcomes that define minimum asset size and verification standards. Changes in these parameters will affect business models across DER aggregators, utilities, and OEMs.

What to watch
  • Market operator papers on telemetry and verification for aggregated assets.
  • Pilot outcomes from aggregator participation in frequency and reserve markets.
Action

Prepare for stricter telemetry and audit requirements if entering ancillary markets as an aggregator or asset owner.
