Project financing and risk allocation adjustments in large EPC contracts

Signals indicate EPC contracts are being restructured with revised risk allocation and financing terms, as sponsors and lenders recalibrate exposure to supply-chain and schedule risk.

EPC contractors are reportedly seeking more favorable force majeure and schedule relief clauses to reflect persistent component lead times. Expectations include increased use of milestone-based payments and escrow arrangements to mitigate contractor liquidity risk. Scenario watch should consider the impact on project timelines and the appetite of insurers for political and supply-chain risk cover. Lenders may demand more conservative draw schedules or higher contingency reserves. There will be commercial tension between owners wanting fixed-price certainty and contractors seeking flexibility. Engineering timelines and procurement strategies may shift to prioritize long-lead procurement and vendor diversification.

What to watch
  • Contract amendments with revised force majeure or schedule relief language
  • Lender and insurer commentary on project risk allocation
Action

Review contractual risk allocations on upcoming EPCs and consult lenders on acceptable structures.
