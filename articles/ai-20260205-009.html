HV procurement and geopolitical risk

Signals suggest procurement strategies for HV equipment are being recalibrated to account for geopolitical friction and export controls, creating scenarios where regional inventories and alternative supplier qualification become priorities.

Procurement teams are modelling multi-supplier sourcing to reduce single-vendor exposure. There is an expectation that lead times and compliance checks will increase for equipment crossing multiple jurisdictions. Some operators are pre-qualifying secondary suppliers and building local spares depots as contingencies. Training and certification programs for local technicians are being accelerated to reduce reliance on external vendor support during outages. A scenario to monitor is export-control driven pauses that force mid-project supplier changes with potential technical compatibility issues. Insurance terms are also being revisited to capture geopolitical interruption risk. EPCs are incorporating more rigorous clause sets to manage force majeure and sanctions-related delays. Tracking regulatory export-control notices and supplier certification updates offers an early indicator of shifting procurement risk. Over the medium term, a diversified supplier base and local capability build-outs are likely defensive strategies.

What to watch
  • Supplier qualification updates and export-control notices.
  • Changes to insurance terms covering geopolitical interruptions.
Action

Qualify secondary suppliers and plan spare depots to reduce single-supplier exposure for critical HV equipment.
