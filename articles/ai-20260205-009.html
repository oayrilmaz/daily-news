Maintenance deferral scenario: wildfire and thermal stress interactions

In some jurisdictions, deferred substation maintenance combined with seasonal wildfire and thermal stress creates a scenario warranting closer operational monitoring and contingency planning.

Deferred vegetation management and postponed equipment servicing are potential contributors to elevated risk during hot, dry periods. Wildfire-driven outages and thermal derating of equipment are interacting variables that increase the likelihood of forced outages in stressed networks. Operators may be more reliant on temporary reconfiguration and rapid restoration plans as mitigations. This framing is a scenario to watch rather than a statement that failures are occurring. Increased coordination with emergency services and pre-positioning of crews are plausible near-term responses. Insurance and replacement-part logistics could become pressure points if multiple incidents coincide. Asset managers should prioritize critical-path inspections and consider third-party surge support arrangements.

What to watch
  • utility advisories on planned maintenance deferrals
  • regional wildfire alerts coinciding with heatwaves
Action

Prioritize inspections of high-voltage assets in fire-prone corridors and secure mutual-aid agreements.
