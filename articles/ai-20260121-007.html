Transmission planning under new scenario-based mandates

Expectations are that regulators will increasingly require scenario-based transmission planning that explicitly accounts for high-renewable futures and electric-vehicle demand patterns, altering project prioritization.

Regulatory conversations are trending toward requiring planning exercises that examine multiple decarbonization and electrification pathways. Planners are being asked to justify investments against a wider set of futures, including high distributed energy resource penetration and rapid EV adoption. This shift could prioritize transmission projects that enable bulk renewable transfers and resilient interconnection nodes. Funding and cost-allocation debates are likely to follow, influencing which projects advance. Scenario watch: accelerated approvals for high-priority interregional lines could create short-term capacity bottlenecks in manufacturing and permitting. What to monitor: draft planning criteria, stakeholder filings, and cross-jurisdiction coordination mechanisms. Utilities may need enhanced scenario analysis capabilities and stakeholder engagement strategies. The operational implication is more frequent re-scoping of multiyear programs to remain aligned with updated futures.

What to watch
  • Draft planning orders from regulators
  • Cross-jurisdiction coordination actions
Action

Enhance scenario modeling capabilities and prepare stakeholder materials that justify transmission investments across multiple futures.
