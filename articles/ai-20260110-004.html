Converter reliability becomes focal in large offshore wind builds

Expectations are rising that converter and HVDC subsystem reliability will drive project schedules and O&M profiles for the next wave of large offshore wind arrays, creating a scenario where electrical balance-of-plant risk eclipses turbine availability.

As projects scale in capacity and distance from shore, the electrical export system's complexity grows materially. Subsea cable faults remain high consequence, but converters and valve-level failures now attract more attention due to repair lead times. OEMs and owners are considering modular designs and staged commissioning to isolate electrical risks. The economics of spare converter modules versus expedited offshore intervention are under active assessment. Scenario watch: converter failures during high-load winter conditions could constrain export capacity and require curtailment decisions. Enhanced condition monitoring, digital twins, and firmware standardization are being discussed as mitigations. What to monitor: procurement language for spare power-electronics modules and tender terms that shift O&M risk to contractors.

What to watch
  • Procurement clauses for spare converter modules
  • Announcements of extended O&M response times for HV systems
Action

Reassess export-system spares and condition-monitoring plans for offshore projects now.
