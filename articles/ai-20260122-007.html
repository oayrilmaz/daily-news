Grid-code updates expected to prioritize dynamic capability and DER integration

Expectations are rising that upcoming grid-code revisions will emphasize dynamic capability and distributed energy resource integration; scenario watch highlights potential acceleration of inverter interoperability and telemetry requirements.

Regulators are signaling a shift toward performance-based codes that reward dynamic response and fast frequency support from inverter-based resources. This would likely increase interoperability and telemetry requirements for inverters and grid-edge controllers during commissioning and operation. A scenario watch shows added testing and certification timelines for DER vendors, which could slow deployments in the short term. What to monitor are consultative papers, draft code language, and vendor readiness statements about new test standards. System operators may begin pilot programs that require enhanced telemetry uplinks and remote-control capabilities from aggregated DERs. There is an expectation that grid-code changes will favor designs that reduce inertia shortfalls through synthetic inertia or fast reserve functionalities. EPCs and OEMs should prepare for adjusted procurement specs and potentially more rigorous factory acceptance testing regimes. Stakeholders should engage in consultations to shape practical timelines and avoid unintended deployment bottlenecks.

What to watch
  • Draft grid-code consultation papers
  • Vendor certification readiness announcements
Action

Engage regulators and update procurement and testing plans to reflect likely new telemetry and interoperability requirements.
