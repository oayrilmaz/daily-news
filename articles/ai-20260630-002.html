Partial-discharge monitoring signals suggest GIS maintenance windows

Emerging patterns in partial-discharge telemetry could elevate the need for targeted gas-insulated substation maintenance, particularly where long-retired assets remain in service.

Several networks are reporting incremental rises in partial-discharge activity on medium- and high-voltage GIS, a common early signal of dielectric stress. These measurements often precede insulation degradation and can warrant focused inspection. Operators face a trade-off between immediate intrusive maintenance and planned replacement. Intrusive work risks short-term outages that are increasingly costly during peak demand periods, while deferral increases failure probability. Supply constraints for replacement GIS modules and accessories complicate rapid remediation. Long-lead items and specialized labor availability mean that decisions now have downstream schedule implications. A scenario watch is heightened outage risk if partial-discharge trends accelerate during peak-season stressors like heat or storm activity. That scenario could force reactive dispatch and longer restoration times. For asset owners, enhancing remote monitoring and prioritizing condition-based interventions can reduce unnecessary outages and optimize use of scarce vendor resources. Firmware and sensor calibration checks are immediate low-cost actions. Monitor incoming vendor advisories on spare parts, condition-monitoring alerts, and outage scheduling to understand near-term exposure and plan alternative routing where possible.

What to watch
  • Increase in PD alarm rate at critical substations
  • Vendor lead-time advisories for GIS components
Action

Prioritize condition-based inspections and confirm availability of critical GIS spares and skilled technicians.
