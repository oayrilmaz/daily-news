EPC schedules pressured by component lead times

Project developers and EPC firms are reportedly adjusting schedules as HV transformer and switchgear lead times fluctuate, framing near-term delivery risk for greenfield projects.

Manufacturing bottlenecks and capacity reallocation toward high-margin products are expected to influence delivery windows for large electrical equipment. EPCs are exploring alternative suppliers and stockpiling critical components to maintain project timelines. This approach increases working capital needs and may shift bid strategies. Scenario watch: longer lead times could force phased commissioning or temporary workaround solutions. What to monitor are vendor capacity announcements and any shifts in fabrication center allocations.

What to watch
  • vendor capacity and allocation notices
  • fabrication center schedule changes
Action

Build component contingency plans into project timelines and evaluate phased commissioning options.
