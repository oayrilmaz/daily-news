SCADA and OT cybersecurity remains an elevated operational risk

Signals indicate increased attention to cybersecurity posture for substation OT systems as integration with cloud and AI control layers accelerates.

Operators are balancing faster communications and AI control with legacy SCADA systems that were not designed for modern threat models. Patch cycles and system upgrades are constrained by operational windows and supplier qualification processes. This creates a scenario where new connectivity improves operational capability but temporarily increases attack surface. Incident response readiness and asset inventory accuracy are becoming top priorities for grid owners. What to monitor are advisories on vulnerabilities and utility-led OT security pilots. The expectation is a phased hardening approach, with near-term exposure during transition and longer-term resilience gains if investments are sustained.

What to watch
  • Vulnerability advisories relevant to legacy SCADA protocols
  • Utility OT modernization and incident response exercise announcements
Action

Prioritize asset inventory, patch management windows and incident response exercises when planning SCADA integrations.
