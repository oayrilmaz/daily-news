Data centers push on-site resilience and flexible generation options

Signals point to a rising interest among large data center operators in diversified on-site generation—battery plus gas, hydrogen pilots and modular GTs—to meet resilience targets and grid participation objectives.

Operators are increasingly evaluating non-traditional on-site fuel mixes to decouple critical loads from grid interruptions. Interest in hydrogen-ready turbines and dual-fuel configurations is emerging as a contingency for extended outages and decarbonization roadmaps. Battery energy storage remains central for instantaneous backup and for participating in ancillary markets, but duration limits drive interest in hybrid systems. Scenario watch: Adoption of hydrogen or dual-fuel systems at scale could shift local fuel logistics and permitting dynamics near data center campuses. Local distribution constraints and utility interconnection terms remain the principal gating factors for on-site infrastructure expansion. Power purchase and resilience contracts will likely evolve to include fuel-supply guarantees and site-level emissions clauses. Expect closer coordination between data center firms and utilities over staged islanding strategies and fast-ramp generation interfaces. Technical standards for integrating diverse on-site generation with UPS systems will be an area of near-term engineering focus.

What to watch
  • Permitting developments for on-site hydrogen or dual-fuel generation.
  • Utility interconnection term changes affecting behind-the-meter generation.
Action

Reassess interconnection plans and fuel logistics assumptions for critical facilities and negotiate clearer resilience clauses with utilities.
