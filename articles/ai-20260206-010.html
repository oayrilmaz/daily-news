Solar+Storage Financing Signals Reveal Offtake and Merchant Risk Tensions

Project finance signals in solar+storage deals are highlighting tension between long-term offtake certainty and merchant revenue upside, creating new diligence priorities for financiers.

Developers are structuring deals that blend contracted capacity with merchant exposure to capture higher potential returns, which shifts revenue risk profiles. Financiers are signaling more granular cashflow stress tests and shorter tenor structures when merchant exposure rises, reflecting uncertainty in price trajectories. Scenario watch: prolonged low wholesale prices or policy changes to capacity payments could degrade merchant revenue and affect debt service metrics. Credit committees may require deeper market hedging, staged debt tranches, or sponsor support mechanisms to close deals with higher merchant content. Project owners should be prepared to document robust dispatch and revenue-optimization strategies that account for curtailment and transmission constraints. Monitor offtake contract language trends, merchant exposure levels in deal announcements, and evolving lender covenants for early risk signals.

What to watch
  • shifts in typical PPA tenor and merchant exposure clauses
  • lender covenant changes or increased equity requirements
Action

Design offtake and hedging structures aligned with lender expectations and stress-test merchant upside under conservative price scenarios.
