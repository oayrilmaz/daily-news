Modular GIS and prefabrication attract EPC interest for rapid builds

Signals show EPCs and owners evaluating modular gas-insulated switchgear (GIS) and prefabricated substation modules to compress on-site schedules and reduce skilled labor dependencies for fast utility and industrial projects.

Modular approaches shift scope from field assembly to factory verification, which can shorten on-site commissioning time. EPCs note advantages in quality control and repeatability, especially for desert and remote locations with limited skilled labor pools. The trade-off includes higher lift-and-transport logistics and potential single-supplier dependency for modules. Owners focused on schedule certainty are giving weight to integrated factory acceptance testing and full-system FATs. The shift could reshape tender specifications and warranty structures. There is a scenario where modular standardization accelerates deployment but creates longer lead-times for bespoke components. Project teams should balance module benefits against transport and integration risks.

What to watch
  • RFPs specifying modular solutions
  • Logistics and heavy-lift infrastructure availability
Action

Evaluate modular prefabs on a total-cycle basis including transport, lifting and factory acceptance testing before committing to standard designs.
