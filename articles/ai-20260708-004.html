Transmission constraints continue to signal renewable curtailment risk

Operational signals and queue patterns suggest that wind and solar curtailment risk remains elevated where transmission upgrades lag behind generation additions, creating revenue and integration stress for projects.

Interconnection queues still show concentrations of variable generation in regions where new transmission investment is delayed, signaling a growing mismatch. Operational curtailment spikes are being observed during low-load, high-output windows; these are being managed but not fully mitigated. Storage deployment is increasing but may not be scaling fast enough to absorb near-term oversupply during these windows. EPCs face complex dispatch coordination challenges when linking storage, generation, and existing transmission rights, increasing project execution complexity. Markets with flexible tariff structures are experimenting with localized incentives to encourage behind-the-meter storage and demand response to reduce curtailment. This environment creates a scenario where early movers in co-located storage capture higher value, while late adopters face revenue erosion from frequent curtailment. Intelligence posture: follow interconnection cluster movements, curtailment frequency metrics, and transmission upgrade permitting as leading indicators.

What to watch
  • Interconnection queue concentrations and shifts
  • Curtailment frequency data and transmission upgrade milestones
Action

Reassess project revenue models for new RE builds and prioritize co-located storage or dispatch agreements to mitigate curtailment exposure.
