Offshore wind integration and transmission bottlenecks create curtailment signals

Signals of rising curtailment and constrained export capacity in coastal regions suggest transmission expansion timelines are not keeping pace with offshore wind commissioning, forcing operational and market adaptation.

Grid injection patterns from new offshore assets are producing periods of localized overgeneration during low-load hours. Expectations are that without accelerated transmission upgrades, curtailment rates will stay elevated, reducing merchant revenue for generators. Scenario watch: developers may shift to co-located storage and flexible contracting to mitigate revenue risk. What to monitor are transmission reinforcement project milestones and constrained-area LMP differentials. System operators could deploy dynamic line ratings and topology optimization as interim measures. Financing structures for future offshore projects may evolve to include transmission risk allocation. Long-term planning will need to align permitting, seabed infrastructure, and onshore substation expansion to avoid recurring integration bottlenecks.

What to watch
  • Transmission reinforcement project timelines
  • Locational price spreads near coastal nodes
Action

Model curtailment risk into project returns and consider storage or contractual mitigations for new offshore assets.
