Behind-the-meter generation adoption accelerates for resilience

Scenario signals suggest accelerated adoption of behind-the-meter generation and storage across Asia as data centers and industrial loads hedge against local grid volatility and price risk.

Market signals show more projects bundling diesel or gas gensets with battery systems to create layered resilience profiles rather than relying solely on utility backs. This hybrid approach is being framed as a pragmatic response to both transient grid reliability risks and to price exposure during peak events. Regulators in several markets are testing streamlined permitting for such behind-the-meter assets, a development that could shorten deployment cycles. Operators are also exploring fuel-switch and dual-fuel strategies to manage fuel supply volatility. The scenario could change distribution load patterns and require closer coordination with DN operators to manage islanding events. Watch for new aggregated behind-the-meter offerings and changes in interconnection rules that facilitate controlled islanding.

What to watch
  • Permitting changes for on-site generation and storage
  • New product offerings combining gensets and batteries
  • Distribution operator guidance on controlled islanding
Action

Assess behind-the-meter options for critical sites and engage regulators early to expedite permitting and interconnection agreements.
