Battery-material processing concentration increases scenario risk for supply security

Signals show processing and refining capacity for lithium and nickel remaining concentrated in a few regions, elevating scenario risk for downstream battery and EV supply chains.

Upstream mining production is expanding, but the bottleneck in some value chains is processing and refining. Expectations are that any disruption in processing hubs will propagate to cell and pack manufacturing timelines. Strategic stockpiling and forward offtake agreements are being discussed more widely as mitigation, but they carry cost and capital implications. Scenario watch: export or trade policy shifts could trigger rapid re-pricing. Investment in new processing plants is underway but faces permitting, capital and technology-improvement hurdles that lengthen lead times. Signals from financing rounds and plant commissioning timelines should be monitored. Downstream sectors including data centers, EV fleets and utility-scale storage could see project timing impacts if material flows are constrained. Owners should stress-test procurement schedules and substitute chemistries where feasible. Recycling and second-life markets are presented as medium-term buffers, but current volumes are insufficient to offset primary demand. Expectations are that recycling scale-up will be gradual. Companies should evaluate critical-mineral exposure and consider diversified sourcing, metal-swap clauses and longer lead-time procurement to reduce concentration risk.

What to watch
  • commissioning timelines for new refineries
  • trade-policy announcements affecting mineral exports
Action

Map critical-mineral exposure and implement multi-source procurement and offtake flexibility.
