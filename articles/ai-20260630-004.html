Curtailment risk and grid integration costs shaping new renewable project economics

Signals from interconnection queues and developer planning cycles suggest curtailment expectations and behind-the-meter congestion charges are being folded into project economics, altering route-to-market strategies in crowded regions.

Developers are increasingly modeling higher curtailment scenarios and incorporating them into P50/P90 assumptions. This expectation is driving some projects to pursue co-located storage or firming power-purchase agreements to maintain offtake value. Grid congestion at key transmission interfaces is prompting more active engagement with distribution operators to explore non-wires alternatives. The scenario watch flags that absent network reinforcements, merchant revenue streams for new capacity could be more volatile than previously modeled. Equipment choices may prioritize inverter features that support dynamic ramp management and aggregated dispatch. Investor underwriting is adjusting to reflect these operational uncertainties in projected returns.

What to watch
  • Interconnection queue studies that revise curtailment expectations
  • Formal requests for distribution-level non-wires solutions
Action

Incorporate higher curtailment scenarios into financial models and evaluate storage co-location as a hedge.
