AI-driven condition-based maintenance pilots for transformers show predictive potential

Signals from utilities and vendors suggest AI-based condition monitoring pilots for large transformers are improving early fault detection, framing a scenario where digital twins could extend asset life and optimize O&M spend.

Pilots reportedly combine dissolved gas analysis, thermal imaging, and vibration telemetry with machine learning to predict degrading conditions earlier than conventional intervals. The scenario shows potential for deferred capital replacements and targeted interventions that reduce unplanned outages. Key uncertainties include model generalizability across vintages and manufacturers, and the need for high-quality labeled failure data. Cybersecurity of telemetry streams and integration with existing maintenance workflows are practical hurdles. Regulators and auditors may require validation frameworks before credits for deferred replacements are allowed in asset valuation. Watch for pilot-to-scale transition announcements and performance metrics that validate ROI. If robust, the approach could materially change O&M planning for aging fleets.

What to watch
  • track published pilot performance metrics and ROI studies
  • monitor regulatory acceptance of CBM for asset accounting
Action

Pilot AI-based CBM on a subset of assets and establish data governance and validation processes before scaling.
