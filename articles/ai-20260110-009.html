Security and theft scenarios increase substation vulnerability

Signals from operational briefings suggest theft, vandalism, and opportunistic attacks are a growing scenario to factor into substation security and outage risk assessments in several LATAM corridors.

Asset owners are reporting increased scrutiny of perimeter defenses and detection systems in scenario planning exercises. Thefts of copper, meters, and smaller components are being modeled for their potential to cascade into longer outages when spare parts are limited. There is an expectation that insurance premiums and self-insurance considerations will reflect heightened security scenario exposure. Some operators are accelerating remote monitoring and rapid-response contracts to reduce restoration times. Community engagement and local policing coordination are being added to risk mitigation playbooks. What to monitor are security incident reports, police-resource allocations, and changes in theft-related insurance conditions.

What to watch
  • local security incident reports and police coordination notices
  • insurance condition changes related to theft or vandalism
Action

Conduct a rapid review of physical security measures and confirm rapid-response contracts for critical spares and repairs.
