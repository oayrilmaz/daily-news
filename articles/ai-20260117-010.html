AI inspection and digital twins in HV equipment maintenance

Signals suggest operators are trialing AI-driven inspection and digital twin scenarios to reduce O&M costs and predict failures in high-voltage equipment.

The intelligence framing is that AI-based visual inspection and sensor analytics are being integrated into maintenance workflows as pilot scenarios. Digital twins tied to asset telemetry could enable scenario planning for transformer aging and switchgear stress. These approaches change outage planning by enabling targeted interventions rather than broad scheduled replacements. The scenario also brings data governance, model validation, and sensor calibration challenges that must be managed. Vendors may bundle analytics subscriptions with monitoring hardware, shifting CapEx to OpEx for some owners. Cybersecurity of twin models and data integrity becomes an operational concern in the scenario. Success metrics will likely focus on mean-time-between-failures improvements and avoided outage costs.

What to watch
  • pilot outcomes showing predictive maintenance accuracy and ROI
  • contracts bundling analytics with hardware and data ownership terms
Action

Pilot digital twin and AI inspection on a representative asset class and define data governance and validation requirements up front.
