Ancillary services pricing under pressure as inverter-based resources grow

Expectations are forming that ancillary-services markets will face pricing volatility as inverter-based resources (IBRs) change provision profiles; market operators and traders should reassess procurement and settlement frameworks.

IBRs provide different inertia and fault-ride-through characteristics compared with synchronous machines, which alters the supply stack for reserves and frequency response. This shift is prompting market operators to refine product definitions for fast frequency response and synthetic inertia. Short-duration, high-value services are rising in prominence, which benefits storage but complicates revenue stacking for assets optimized for energy arbitrage. Pricing volatility is expected to increase in tight regions during low-load, high-renewable intervals when conventional providers withdraw. Market participants are exploring aggregator models to pool distributed assets into credible reserve offers. Settlement and verification for synthetic services remain an operational challenge and a potential source of disputes. Traders and asset owners should scenario-test portfolios against higher intra-day volatility and shorter-duration reserve events. Watch for pilots that modify metering or settlement windows as a signal of broader market redesign.

What to watch
  • fast frequency response pilots
  • settlement window changes
Action

Stress-test market positions for short-duration reserve events and evaluate aggregation strategies for distributed flexibility.
