Ancillary market repricing as inverter-based resources shift dynamics

Signals suggest ancillary service prices are re-pricing to favor sub-second and minute-resolution resources as inverter-based assets and batteries increase grid participation.

Market operators are under pressure to update product definitions and procurement windows to capture new fast-acting capacity. Price signals for frequency containment and fast frequency response appear to be widening in markets with high inverter penetration. Traditional thermal units face economic pressure as revenue streams for slower ramping services diminish in value. Market participants are experimenting with AI-driven bidding strategies to capture high-frequency market opportunities. Scenario watch: rapid product definition changes could create transitional volatility and opportunities for flexible asset owners. What to monitor are market rule change consultations and atypical price volatility in sub-minute ancillary products.

What to watch
  • Market rule change consultations on fast-response products
  • Sub-minute ancillary price volatility
Action

Model revenue sensitivity to sub-minute ancillary prices and test algorithmic bidding strategies.
