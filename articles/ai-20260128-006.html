Concentration of supply remains a strategic risk for EV and battery chains

Signals point to continued concentration of key battery metals in particular jurisdictions, creating a scenario where geopolitical shifts or permitting delays could ripple through equipment and battery assembly timelines.

Cobalt and nickel upstream supplies remain concentrated in a limited set of production regions. These are strategic signals that could translate into procurement volatility when combined with local policy changes.

Downstream processors and refiners are not evenly distributed, which creates chokepoints in material flows. The expectation is for increased investment in regional refining capacity to mitigate risk, though timelines are long.

Procurement teams may face price and availability swings that affect battery pack deliveries and, by extension, EV and storage project schedules. This scenario emphasizes the need for diversified contracts.

Recycling and circular-materials efforts are rising as a strategic hedge against primary supply risk. Commercial-scale recycling remains nascent in many regions, so near-term effects are limited.

Policy shifts or export controls in producing jurisdictions remain a scenario that could accelerate supply disruptions. Monitoring political signals and permit backlogs will be important for planners.

What to monitor are announcements of new refining capacity, changes to export or mining policy, and large strategic procurement agreements that could shift material flows.

What to watch
  • refinery capacity announcements and export policy signals
  • large off-take agreements and recycling facility plans
Action

Map critical-material exposure across projects and pursue diversified off-takes and recycling partnerships where feasible.
