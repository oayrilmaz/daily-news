Workforce and fabrication signals suggest continued bottlenecks for on-site commissioning

Signals from EPCs and fabrication yards indicate that skilled labor and on-site commissioning teams remain a constraint, potentially delaying energization windows even when equipment arrives on schedule.

Skilled commissioning crews for HV and substation works are in tight supply across multiple markets. Fabrication capacity for skid-mounted substations and packaged switchgear is near utilization in some regions. Logistics improvements do not fully mitigate the need for experienced commissioning personnel. Project timelines often hinge on sequence-critical commissioning steps requiring specialized crews. EPCs are experimenting with remote commissioning support but face regulatory and safety limits. Scenario outcomes include partial energization or temporary operational limits until full commissioning is complete. Contractors may reprice mobilization or include premium days for accelerated teams. What to monitor: crew availability notices and changes to on-site labor costs in bids.

What to watch
  • Notices of crew shortages or premium mobilization charges in contractor bids.
  • Announcements of expanded remote commissioning pilots or regulatory approvals.
Action

Include contingency labor plans and consider staged commissioning schedules to reduce single-point timing risk.
