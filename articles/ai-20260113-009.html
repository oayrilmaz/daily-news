AI-driven asset management signals efficiency gains and new procurement requirements

Early signals indicate AI-assisted operations and predictive maintenance are moving beyond pilots; this brief frames scenarios where asset lifecycles and OEM service offers could be reshaped.

AI models are being integrated to predict transformer and breaker degradation with higher lead-time than traditional methods. Adoption of predictive maintenance could reduce unplanned outages and alter spare-parts stocking logic for critical HV equipment. One expectation is that OEMs will bundle AI analytics with extended service contracts, changing total-cost-of-ownership calculations. A scenario to watch is accelerated warranty and performance contracting tied to AI-derived condition-based KPIs. Procurement teams should monitor data access terms, model transparency provisions, and cybersecurity controls embedded in OEM offers. There are signals that independent analytics providers will compete with OEM platforms, creating vendor negotiation leverage. What to monitor are pilot-to-scale transition metrics and any regulatory guidance on the use of AI in safety-critical asset decisions. Treat AI-based maintenance as a potential lifecycle cost reducer, but insist on auditability and clear contractual remedies.

What to watch
  • OEM contract terms on data access, model explainability, and cybersecurity
  • Pilot performance metrics that demonstrate extended failure lead-time
Action

Require data-access and model-audit clauses in AI-enabled service agreements and pilot test for multiple failure modes.
