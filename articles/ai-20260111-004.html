Curtailment and grid-forming inverter uptake shape renewable dispatch scenarios

Expectations suggest renewable curtailment patterns will persist in regions with constrained transmission, while grid-forming inverter adoption remains a key scenario variable for system stability.

Transmission congestion continues to be a primary signal driving renewable curtailment in fast-growing markets. Grid-forming inverter demonstrations are increasing but full-scale deployment timelines vary across project pipelines. Curtailment economics are pressuring developers to consider hybridization with storage or grid services as a mitigation strategy. System operators are evaluating new operational protocols to accommodate higher inverter-based generation shares. The interoperability of legacy protection schemes with inverter-dominant fleets is a scenario watch for fault performance. Equipment lead times for advanced inverters and power electronics may lag growth in installed capacity. Monitor interconnection queue changes and storage procurement notices for shifts in curtailment risk.

What to watch
  • interconnection queue updates and transmission upgrades
  • storage procurement announcements paired with new renewables
Action

Model curtailment scenarios and accelerate storage or hybrid designs where transmission expansion is uncertain.
