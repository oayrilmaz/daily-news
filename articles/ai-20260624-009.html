Colocation growth plans encounter local grid reliability and interconnection hurdles

Signals from developers and local operators point to grid reliability constraints and protracted interconnection processes in several LATAM markets, forcing project adjustments and more conservative commissioning schedules.

Expectations are that colocation providers will increase investments in on-site resilience, including energy storage and redundant feeds. Scenario watch: constrained utility capacity could delay large campus projects or push higher use of diesel during ramp-up. What to monitor are interconnection queue backlogs and local utility reinforcement plans. Intelligence suggests closer utility-developer coordination and contractual clarity on supply milestones will become standard. Developers should assess incremental capacity contracts and staged deployment models. Financial assumptions for new projects may need reworking to reflect resilience capital requirements.

What to watch
  • Interconnection queue updates
  • Utility reinforcement project timelines
Action

Plan for staged builds and include resilience capital for on-site power to mitigate interconnection delays.
