OEM capacity and digital-twin adoption shape delivery risk profiles

Expectations of constrained OEM factory capacity and faster adoption of digital-twin workflows are changing delivery risk profiles for large electrical equipment and substation projects.

Manufacturers are balancing capacity expansion with ongoing retrofit demand, creating timing uncertainty for new orders. Digital-twin and predictive-maintenance offerings are being pushed to the front of proposals to improve lifecycle value and reduce O&M costs. EPCs may need to revise schedules to integrate vendor digital models and updated factory lead times. This transition creates both operational upside and coordination risk: richer models improve planning but require upfront data handshakes and validation. What to monitor: firm factory lead times, digital model delivery SLAs, and vendor capacity windows. Scenario watch: delayed equipment deliveries could force temporary re-rating of assets or phased energization approaches.

What to watch
  • Vendor factory lead-time confirmations and change notices
  • Digital model delivery SLAs and integration checkpoints
Action

Lock down equipment delivery windows and require digital-model milestones in vendor contracts.
