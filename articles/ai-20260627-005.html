Capacity market signals shift toward flexibility premiums

Market signals are trending toward higher valuation for fast-ramping and duration-flexible resources as operators price the premium for short-notice reliability.

Capacity markets are showing indications of rebalancing toward attributes rather than pure MW capacity. Policymakers and market operators may consider nodal or attribute-based auctions to recognize flexibility and inertia contributions. Short-duration resources like batteries could capture higher revenues in certain constructs, while long-duration resources remain necessary for multi-day events. This transition could influence investment decisions and shape bidding strategies. Thermal generation that offers quick-start capabilities may see revised compensation structures. Ancillary service markets are also likely to evolve to internalize the value of ramp and sustained energy. Traders and asset owners are expected to refine offer stacks for mixed portfolios. Scenario watch includes new product launches that explicitly remunerate capacity firmness and ramping capability.

What to watch
  • Regulatory dockets proposing attribute-based capacity products
  • Auction results that show price separation for flexible capacity
Action

Review asset participation models to capture flexibility premiums and adjust bidding strategies accordingly.
