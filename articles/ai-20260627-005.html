Heat-Period Price Volatility Expected to Drive Short-Term Capacity Bids

Signals from price curves during recent warm periods suggest expectations of renewed short-term price volatility, likely prompting faster capacity bidding and hedging activity in organized markets.

Higher peak loads from heat coupled with variable renewable output are tightening intra-day markets during stress periods. Market participants are expected to submit more aggressive short-term capacity and ancillary service bids to manage exposure. Hedging desks may increase use of short-dated contracts and call options to mitigate sudden price spikes. There is a scenario watch for elevated locational marginal price spreads and for constrained areas to signal scarcity more frequently. Asset owners with flexible capacity and fast-ramping capability could capture upsized premiums in reserve markets. Market operators may consider temporary product adjustments or soft caps if volatility undermines orderly dispatch. Credit and margining practices could tighten as counterparties seek to limit exposure to extreme price movements.

What to watch
  • spikes in locational marginal prices during peak heat windows
  • announcements of temporary market product changes by operators
Action

Revisit short-dated hedging strategies and ensure flexible assets are contracted to capture reserve premiums.
