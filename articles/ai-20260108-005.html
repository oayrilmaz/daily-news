Ancillary services volatility as storage and AI bidding converge

Market behavior signals indicate increased volatility in ancillary services as distributed and utility-scale storage begin using automated bidding strategies informed by machine learning.

Storage assets can arbitrage energy and reserve markets, and algorithmic bidding increases the pace of price discovery. Machine-learned strategies tuned on historical volatility may perform unpredictably under regime change. Rapid entry of aggregated DER portfolios adds latency and coordination complexity for market operators. The result can be increased short-term swings in reserve prices and reduced predictability of capacity availability. Market rule adjustments could follow to curb gaming or systemic risk. Smaller market participants may struggle to compete or hedge emerging price dynamics. The scenario to watch is a sequence of high-volatility events that prompts emergency market interventions or rule changes.

What to watch
  • Abnormal ancillary price spikes in major ISOs
  • Regulatory statements on algorithmic bidding and market fairness
Action

Stress-test bidding algorithms across sharp regime shifts and engage with market operators on testing windows and guardrails.
