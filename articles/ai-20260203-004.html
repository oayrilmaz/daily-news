Offshore wind integration creates new transmission choke points

Rapid offshore wind buildouts in several Asian markets are creating transmission integration bottlenecks, prompting scenario-level adjustments to interconnection planning and merchant transmission thinking.

Signals from planning entities highlight a growing mismatch between offshore generation zones and onshore grid capacity. The consequence is a need for either accelerated transmission build or curtailment management strategies. Expectations are for renewed emphasis on coordinated grid planning and phased interconnection approaches. Developers and system planners are experimenting with clustering offshore projects around shared HVDC export corridors to reduce per-project onshore footprint. This approach can lower costs but raises coordination and commercial allocation complexities. Merchant transmission models are being floated to attract private capital for export infrastructure. Grid reinforcement timelines remain a core risk. Long approval and construction cycles for onshore reinforcement can force developers to accept conditional commissioning or constrained operations, which affects project returns. Planners may need to assess interim solutions such as dynamic export limits and on-site storage. Technology choices, including AC vs HVDC and submarine cable designs, are also under scenario review to balance capital intensity against operational flexibility. HVDC can offer longer-distance, lower-loss exports but requires converter stations with long lead times and specialized skillsets. Policy and market design will be decisive. Expectations are that regulators will consider queue-management reforms, staged connection agreements and clearer attribution of curtailment risk between generator and transmission owners. How these reforms take shape will influence project financing. What to monitor are queue reform signals, requests-for-information for HVDC corridor assessments, and merchant transmission pilot announcements. These will indicate whether policy and capital are aligning to solve export bottlenecks. Scenario watch: coordinated corridor planning and merchant transmission vehicles could unblock offshore clusters, but timelines hinge on permitting, converter-station supply and allocation of curtailment risk.

What to watch
  • HVDC corridor RFIs and procurement notices
  • queue-management or curtailment-allocation policy signals
Action

Map project interconnection risk and explore corridor-based commercial structures or interim storage options to mitigate export constraints.
