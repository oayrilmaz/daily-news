Seasonal renewables swings feeding short-term price volatility

Market signals point to amplified short-term price swings as seasonal renewable variability intersects with constrained thermal availability and transmission bottlenecks.

Renewable output seasonality can create predictable windows of tight supply that markets still struggle to price efficiently. When transmission constraints prevent geographic smoothing, price spikes can intensify locally. Ancillary service markets are being tested for new products that better match fast variability. Traders and hedgers may shift toward shorter-duration contracts and structured products that hedge intra-day exposure. Liquidity in those instruments will be critical to prevent market dislocations. Regulators may consider measures to dampen excessive volatility but interventions carry their own distortions. Monitor intraday spread movements, ancillary market volumes, and new product rollouts for signs of shifting market structure.

What to watch
  • Intraday spreads and ancillary market bids
  • Launches of new hedging products for short-duration risk
Action

Ensure trading and risk teams have plans for higher intraday volatility and assess hedging instrument availability.
