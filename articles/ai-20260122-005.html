Ancillary market volatility expectations rise amid tight reserve signals

Market participants are preparing for increased ancillary service volatility as tight reserves and changing resource mixes create scenarios for price spikes and non-linear scarcity bids.

Traders and asset owners are flagging scenarios where fast-responding storage and demand response capture elevated scarcity rents. The intelligence framing is that volatility will be episodic and concentrated, rather than uniform across the year. Capacity value calculations and forward hedges may need to be revisited if the frequency of extreme pricing events increases. Market design changes to remunerate fast ramping and inertia-like services are being discussed and could alter revenue stacks for BESS and flexible gas plants. Participants with flexible bidding strategies and AI-driven price forecasting may gain short-term advantage. Regulatory attention to market power and settlement rules could follow periods of intense price movement. Portfolio risk managers should stress-test models against clustered scarcity events.

What to watch
  • Intraday and real-time price dispersion during high-demand events.
  • Regulator statements on settlement rules after any price excursions.
Action

Stress-test asset revenue models for clustered scarcity events and review bidding strategies for fast-response resources.
