Scenario watch: Capacity-market price swings driven by distributed resources

Market signals point to a scenario where growing participation of distributed energy resources and aggregated demand-response could amplify short-term capacity-price volatility in regional markets.

Aggregators are demonstrating that DER portfolios can meet peak obligations, creating uncertainty about traditional capacity forecasting. Market operators may respond with revised accreditation tests or new performance gates. Price discovery could become more episodic as DER participation grows, especially during tight reserve events. This could incentivize short-duration storage at commercial and industrial sites to chase high capacity payments. Conversely, uncertainty around accreditation may reduce appetite for long-term capacity commitments. Traders and asset owners will need to model higher variance scenarios in capacity price forecasts. Watch for consultation papers or rule changes around DER performance measurement in capacity markets.

What to watch
  • market operator notices on DER accreditation and testing
  • spikes in short-duration storage procurement tied to capacity campaigns
Action

Incorporate DER performance uncertainty into capacity revenue models and stress-test long-term commitments under volatile price scenarios.
