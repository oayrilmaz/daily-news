Nearshoring and Capacity Shift Signals

Signals from contract portfolios and procurement tenders point to a scenario of OEMs and EPCs nearshoring some manufacturing capacity to reduce lead times and exposure to distant supply chains.

Tender notices and capital allocation hints show a shift toward locating critical manufacturing closer to major markets. This could reduce transportation risk and shorten delivery windows for heavy electrical equipment over time. Near-term transition may increase prices as new facilities ramp and workforce training is required. EPC scheduling may see mixed outcomes: shorter logistics but possible initial production bottlenecks. The scenario also raises the prospect of regionalized standards and certification complexity. Contractors and owners should expect renegotiation points around delivery and warranty as supply chains reconfigure. Long-term resilience gains may offset near-term cost inflation for many buyers.

What to watch
  • new facility announcements and capital allocation reports
  • changes in standard lead-time tables from major suppliers
Action

Engage early with OEMs on production schedules and review contract terms for delivery and escalation during transition periods.
