Curtailment and flex-market demand rise as renewables capacity grows

Expectations are forming that rising renewable penetration will increase curtailment events and heighten demand for flexible balancing services, prompting new commercial strategies for asset owners.

Grid operators and market designers are reportedly exploring mechanisms to capture and compensate flexibility from storage, demand response, and dispatchable generation. Renewables owners face scenarios where negative prices and curtailment force reconsideration of firming assets and market participation strategies. There is an expectation that hybrid project designs combining PV/wind with storage will proliferate to reduce merchant exposure. Battery and converter capacity constraints are being monitored as potential bottlenecks for aggregation strategies. Market evolution may introduce locational signals that change project siting economics and transmission use planning. Operational coordination between distribution-connected resources and system operators is a practical challenge being addressed in pilot programs. Lastly, new commercial intermediaries are expected to emerge to pool small assets and bid aggregated flexibility into balancing markets.

What to watch
  • Pilot market products for distributed aggregation
  • Changes to locational pricing or congestion charges
Action

Evaluate hybridization and aggregation strategies for renewable assets and model curtailment risk under multiple market scenarios.
