Renewable integration friction ahead of storage scale-up

Expectations point to localized curtailment and congestion as generation growth outpaces near-term transmission upgrades and storage deployment in several Asian grids.

Rapid renewable capacity additions are creating pockets of oversupply at predictable times of day, increasing curtailment risk. Transmission expand timelines and permitting friction are often slower than generation procurement cycles, creating mismatches in dispatchability. Storage procurement economics remain sensitive to raw material price shifts and permitting timelines for land and interconnection. Developers are evaluating co-located storage and inverter control strategies to reduce curtailment and capture ancillary revenue. Grid operators are experimenting with market mechanisms for voluntary curtailment and dynamic pricing to manage congestion. Distribution-level hosting limits are emerging as a practical cap on further rooftop or local solar in some jurisdictions. These dynamics create a near-term scenario where some projects lose revenue to curtailment until storage and network reinforcement catch up. Project financiers are revising stress cases to reflect these operational headwinds.

What to watch
  • Interconnection queue notes or curtailment reports from system operators.
  • Developer contract clauses addressing curtailment compensation or co-located storage.
Action

Stress-test project revenue against curtailment scenarios and prioritize storage or flexible arrangements in early-stage designs.
