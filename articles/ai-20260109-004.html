Long-duration storage (LDS) moving from concept conversations to procurement posture

Expectation signals point to increased procurement interest in long-duration storage as a mechanism to firm multi-day variability from renewables.

Procurement frameworks are being rethought to allow services beyond hourly capacity, reflecting an interest in multi-day firming profiles. This is still largely exploratory across regions. Technology diversity is part of the scenario set, with flow batteries, thermal storage, and power-to-X framed as possible solutions. No single technology has emerged as dominant in these signals. Economic models are being stress-tested against high renewable-penetration scenarios. Buyers are examining levelized cost trade-offs against the value of avoiding curtailment and capacity shortfalls. Policy and market designs that recognize multi-day services are a common enabler cited in planning discussions. Absent clearer revenue signals, adoption timelines are expected to be cautious. Developers and offtakers are exploring pilot procurements and staged contracts with performance milestones. These enable technology validation without full commercial exposure. Supply chain and siting considerations are flagged as secondary constraints, particularly for large integrated thermal or hydrogen-based approaches.

What to watch
  • monitor procurement RFP language for multi-day performance metrics
  • watch regulatory conversations that could create revenue streams for firming services
Action

Model multi-day firming needs into long-term capacity plans and test sensitivity to different LDS technologies.
