HVDC corridor utilization scenarios signal potential congestion windows

Signals indicate HVDC corridors may face episodic congestion as cross-border flows interact with high-frequency market dispatch and increasing inverter-based generation profiles.

HVDC links are key to moving bulk renewable energy across regions, but they are not immune to congestion dynamics driven by market behavior and localized generation swings. Market-driven dispatch that seeks short-term price arbitrage can create flows that saturate corridor capacity during peak windows. Additionally, rapid shifts in inverter-based resource output can change flow patterns faster than traditional thermal ramping. The result is episodic congestion that requires coordinated curtailment protocols or dynamic reassignments. Operational coordination across control areas becomes more critical, as does real-time visibility into inverter fleet behavior and HVAC contingencies. The scenario warrants investment in enhanced monitoring and adaptive flow control mechanisms. Early indicators include narrowing margins on contingency headroom and more frequent re-dispatch events.

What to watch
  • Increases in re-dispatch frequency and narrowing contingency margins on corridor manifests.
  • Operational coordination notices between adjacent transmission operators.
Action

Enhance cross-border operational coordination and model HVDC response under high inverter penetration scenarios.
