Contractor consolidation and skills gaps create delivery risk for large substation projects

Signals of contractor consolidation combined with localized skills shortages suggest scenario risks for schedule slippage and higher EPC margins on large substation projects in LATAM.

Procurement teams are encountering fewer qualified bidders for turnkey substation and transmission packages in certain markets, increasing dependency on a small set of large contractors. Localized shortages of high-voltage technicians and commissioning engineers are extending project timelines and increasing mobilization costs. Contractors are pricing in uncertainty through higher contingency and extended lead times for specialized equipment. Local content requirements in some jurisdictions further complicate sourcing and may require subcontractor development programs. Financing terms can be sensitive to schedule risk, raising the cost of capital if perceived delays are likely. Operators should consider phased commissioning and clearer milestone incentives to reduce single-point schedule risk. Workforce development partnerships and targeted training investments could alleviate medium-term capacity constraints.

What to watch
  • monitor bidder counts and bid prices for major transmission tenders
  • track local training program announcements and visa/workforce policy changes
Action

In tenders, include staged acceptance milestones and workforce capability assessments to reduce schedule and financial exposure.
