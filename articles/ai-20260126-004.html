Curtailment management and market design under scrutiny as renewables expand

Signals point to increasing instances of renewable curtailment in constrained regions, prompting scenario discussions on market mechanisms, storage incentives, and transmission prioritization.

Expanding wind and solar fleets in certain territories are outpacing transmission capacity growth, resulting in higher curtailment risk signals. Market participants are debating price signals and ancillary service reforms that would better value flexibility. There is growing interest in locational storage incentives to reduce congestion and firm output. Policy discussions under scenario watch include congestion pricing and queue reform to disincentivize speculative interconnection requests. Integration of distributed energy resources into operational markets is being piloted to provide fast flexibility. These dynamics could shift investment toward hybrid projects that pair generation and storage to guarantee deliverability. Monitoring of regional dispatch and curtailment statistics will reveal if market reforms accelerate.

What to watch
  • Regional curtailment and congestion metrics.
  • Policy signals on locational pricing and queue reforms.
Action

Model curtailment exposure for new renewable bids and prioritize hybrid or firmed solutions for contract competitiveness.
