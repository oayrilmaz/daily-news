Transmission chokepoints emerging near offshore wind clusters

Expectations are growing that transmission build timelines could become the gating factor for several offshore wind clusters, with onshore grid reinforcement and cross-border coordination forming key scenario risks.

Large-scale offshore wind projects are signaling concentrated delivery timelines that converge on limited onshore connection points. Landfall and onshore reinforcement projects are complex, often requiring permitting, wayleave acquisition, and multi-stakeholder coordination. The result is a scenario where generation is ready before transmission is available, elevating curtailment and merchant exposure risk. Coordinated planning across grid operators and project developers will be essential to manage queue risk and avoid stranded capacity. Storage and HVDC staging are being considered as mitigation but carry cost and financing implications. The timing and sequencing of incremental transmission capacity additions will determine whether projects can monetize expected outputs. Monitor permitting milestones and cross-jurisdictional coordination discussions as leading indicators.

What to watch
  • Permitting milestones and wayleave approvals for onshore reinforcement
  • HVDC project start-of-construction notices and interconnector commissioning plans
Action

Map critical transmission milestones against your commissioning schedule and consider staging options for generation dispatch.
