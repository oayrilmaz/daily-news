Interconnection queue reform discussions are feeding planning uncertainty

Signals from regulator consultations suggest interconnection queue reform remains a key policy lever, generating planning uncertainty for developers and system planners.

Consultation activity and stakeholder engagement indicate multiple reform proposals are under consideration to address backlog and deliverability. The expectation is that some jurisdictions may prioritize designs that accelerate proven projects while imposing new technical or financial milestones. Scenario watch: transitional rules could create temporary winners and losers, affecting project bankability and transmission build sequencing. What to monitor are draft rule language, milestone enforcement mechanisms, and short windows for compliance. For developers and planners, engaging in comment cycles and preparing adaptable project timelines and financing contingencies will reduce exposure to abrupt policy swings.

What to watch
  • draft rule releases and milestone enforcement dates
  • changes to deliverability and viability tests
Action

Engage with regulatory comment windows and prepare contingency schedules for interconnection milestones.
