Capacity market rule-change signals increase short-term volatility scenarios

Expectations of rule shifts in capacity mechanisms and explicit signals of hydrogen-offtake growth are creating market volatility scenarios that could affect dispatch economics for peak assets.

Signals of capacity market reform in several regions may change revenue stability for peaker plants and storage assets. One scenario sees shorter commitment windows that favor fast-response resources but penalize long-duration assets. Hydrogen demand signals could create new merchant power loads and different price shapes that affect what assets are economic. Market participants should anticipate increased forward volatility and a potential re-rating of asset revenue stacks. Hedging products may evolve to cover new shapes and hydrogen-related electricity demand. Regulators are likely to focus on ensuring reliability while managing cost impacts for end users. Traders and asset owners will need updated scenario models to capture capacity-rule permutations and new industrial loads.

What to watch
  • announcements on capacity market rule consultations
  • large new industrial offtake requests in power markets
Action

Re-run asset revenue forecasts under alternative capacity-rule scenarios and update hedging strategies.
