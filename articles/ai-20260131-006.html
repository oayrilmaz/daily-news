Refining and processing capacity signals shaping battery material timelines

Expect processing and refining capacity signals for nickel and cobalt to materially affect project timelines for battery supply, with potential acceleration of investment in regional refining hubs.

Players are reportedly evaluating midstream investments to reduce exposure to bottlenecks in primary refining. The economics of localizing processing are attractive where logistics costs and tariffs compress margins. Technology choices for hydrometallurgy versus pyrometallurgy remain a scenario variable that affects capital intensity and environmental permitting timelines. Recycling and circular supply-startups are gaining attention as a partial supply hedge, though scale remains limited. Refining project permitting and financing risk is likely to be a gating factor over the next two years. Monitor capital commitments to refining projects and shifts in trade flows for concentrated intermediates.

What to watch
  • capital commitments to refining capacity
  • trade flow shifts for intermediates
Action

Track midstream investment announcements and re-evaluate procurement timing for battery-material-dependent projects.
