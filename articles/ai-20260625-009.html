OT risk scenario as AI tools integrate into substation operations

As AI-derived diagnostics and control advisories are piloted in substations, signals indicate a risk trade-off between operational efficiency gains and expanded OT attack surfaces.

AI models can aggregate diverse telemetry and provide faster anomaly detection and dispatch advisories. These tools create scenarios where human-in-the-loop decision-making is expedited, improving response times. At the same time, integrating AI workflows introduces new interfaces and data flows between control systems and analytics platforms. Each interface represents an OT cyber risk that must be carefully managed. Data provenance, model update controls, and rollback procedures become important operational controls. Vendors and operators are signaling pilot programs, but full-scale deployments may reveal unforeseen failure modes. Rigorous testing, segregated networks, and strict change-management are prudent in early rollouts.

What to watch
  • pilot program scopes that expose control-system interfaces to analytics platforms
  • vendor controls for model update and rollback mechanisms
Action

Enforce strict network segregation and change-management for any AI integration pilots in substations.
