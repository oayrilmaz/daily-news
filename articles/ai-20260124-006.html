Contracting shift: fixed-price EPCs push risk back to owners

Recent contracting patterns suggest EPC firms are offering fixed-price bids while tightening clauses that shift schedule and supply risks back to owners, creating a negotiation pressure point for project sponsors.

EPC firms are signaling a move toward fixed-price frameworks to lock margins in an uncertain input-cost environment. In parallel, risk-transfer clauses for supply, customs delays, and force majeure are becoming more onerous for owners. This dynamic can accelerate dispute likelihood if timelines slip or component shortages occur. Owners with flexible procurement strategies are pushing back, requesting carve-outs for long-lead items and supplier-driven delays. Lenders and insurers are watching contract language closely; tighter clauses could translate to higher financing premiums or additional covenants. The trend favors owners who can modularize scope or pre-purchase critical equipment. Conversely, smaller owners with limited procurement scale may face higher cost exposure. Market participants should map contractual exposures into cashflow models to quantify risk.

What to watch
  • Recent EPC bid terms showing warranty and force majeure carve-outs
  • Lender covenant language tied to delivery dates
Action

Review contract drafts for long-lead and supply carve-outs and quantify potential cost exposure under delay scenarios.
