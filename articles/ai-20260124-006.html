Processing bottlenecks elevate cobalt and nickel supply risk scenarios

Signals of constrained processing capacity for battery-grade cobalt and nickel are increasing the plausibility of short-term tightness in upstream supply chains; downstream manufacturers may face material sourcing stress.

Mining output growth is being outpaced by limited refining and processing infrastructure in key producing regions, creating an upstream backlog signal. Environmental permitting and local infrastructure needs are adding months to new processing project timelines. Concentrated processing capacity amplifies geopolitical and logistical risk, as disruptions in a single facility can ripple widely. Battery manufacturers are beginning to model multi-sourcing and increased inventory as hedges against processing delays. Investment appetite for new processing facilities exists, but capital intensity and permitting cycles will cool the pace of rapid expansion. Secondary sourcing and recycling are being prioritized as stabilizing options, though scaling these pathways requires time and regulatory clarity. Scenario planning should assume periodic supply tightness and price volatility for specific chemistries until processing capacity meaningfully expands. Monitor refinery commissioning schedules, environmental permitting outcomes and upstream shipment backlogs for leading indicators.

What to watch
  • Refinery commissioning announcements and start-up progress
  • Shipment backlogs and port congestion for ore exports
Action

Evaluate inventory and multi-sourcing strategies for critical cathode materials and engage recycling partners where feasible.
