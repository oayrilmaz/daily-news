Interconnection queues and curtailment risk pressure solar-plus-storage project economics

High interconnection backlogs and localized curtailment risk are creating scenario pressure on solar-plus-storage project returns, prompting developers to revisit dispatch strategies and revenue stacking assumptions.

Signals suggest developers are refining models to account for higher-than-expected curtailment windows and longer wait times to connect. Expectations are that some projects will target merchant optionality through multi-market participation to diversify revenue. Scenario watch includes increased interest in transmission upgrades or cluster mitigation strategies to reduce curtailment exposure. What to monitor are queue clearances and any prioritization rules announced by grid operators. For financiers, stress-testing against lower effective capacity factors and delayed operation dates is becoming standard. The broader implication is a potential uptick in hybridization and colocated storage as mitigation, but that itself heightens demand for constrained components. Operationally, refined forecasting and more dynamic plant controllers will be required to optimize dispatch under uncertainty.

What to watch
  • updates to interconnection queue processing
  • announcements of targeted transmission upgrades
Action

Run downside scenarios on curtailment and delayed in-service dates before final investment decisions.
