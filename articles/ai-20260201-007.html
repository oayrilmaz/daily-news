Price volatility expectations rise with renewable ramp variability

Signals point to heightened short-term wholesale price volatility in regions with fast renewable ramping and limited intra-day flexibility.

Market operators are seeing more frequent intraday price swings tied to meteorological variability. Flexible resources that can monetize fast ramps may outperform baseload in near-term dispatch economics. Contracting teams should consider increased basis and imbalance risk in hedges. Capacity and ancillary markets may experience stress if volatility persists over multiple weeks. Demand-response and dispatchable distributed assets could capture premium value if market access is assured. This is a scenario framing intended to inform trading and procurement decisions rather than to forecast prices definitively.

What to watch
  • Intraday price dispersion and frequency of negative-price hours
  • Activation rates for fast-ramping thermal and demand-response assets
Action

Stress-test hedge positions for higher basis risk and evaluate short-duration flexibility procurement.
