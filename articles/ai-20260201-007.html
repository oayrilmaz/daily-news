Interconnection reforms remain a pivotal lever for project timelines and grid resilience

Regulatory signals indicate continued focus on interconnection queue reforms, which could materially change how renewables and storage projects are assessed and prioritized.

Regulators are wrestling with queue backlogs and are experimenting with reforms that emphasize deliverability and power-flow studies earlier in the process. These approaches aim to reduce speculative entries. If reforms accelerate, projects with clearer deliverability plans and stronger financial assurances are likely to move ahead while speculative or poorly financed projects are culled from queues. Transmission planning processes may be compressed to align with faster queue clearing, placing additional demands on system planners and potentially changing cost allocation outcomes. Interconnection reforms also have equity implications: faster-clearing projects may favor incumbents with resources to meet new requirements unless specific protections are designed. Developers and financiers will need to revisit deal timelines and underwriting criteria to match new interconnection expectations and possible deposit or performance obligations. What to monitor: filings and guidance from regional interconnection authorities, pilot reform outcomes, and policy statements on queue management. Project sponsors should reassess queue positions and readiness to meet enhanced deliverability and financial assurance requirements.

What to watch
  • regional queue reform filings and pilot outcomes
  • changes to financial assurance requirements
Action

Review interconnection readiness and secure financing contingencies to remain competitive under tighter queue requirements.
