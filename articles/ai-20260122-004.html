Offshore renewables and HVDC routing create new congestion scenarios for coastal transmission corridors

Signals suggest that accelerated offshore wind builds combined with limited HVDC landing sites are raising the likelihood of constrained coastal corridors and complex grid reinforcements.

Project clustering near favored ports and existing substations is increasing interconnection complexity. HVDC converters require specialized civil works that cannot be easily relocated at scale. The need to sequence offshore capacity against onshore reinforcements creates timing risk for commercial availability. Operators may need to adopt dynamic curtailment and phased commissioning as a pragmatic response. Planning authorities are also weighing land use trade-offs for converter stations and AC grid upgrades. Scenario planning should include temporary curtailment tariffs and contractual arrangements for partial dispatch. Early engagement between project developers and transmission planners will be a decisive factor in avoiding protracted congestion.

What to watch
  • Permitting decisions on converter station sites
  • Transmission reinforcement procurement timelines
Action

Align project timelines with transmission reinforcement schedules and include curtailment clauses in power contracts.
