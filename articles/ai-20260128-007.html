Interconnection reform drafts could shift DER queue outcomes

Draft policy proposals under consideration may recalibrate interconnection queue mechanics, creating scenarios where distributed energy resource projects face new evidence requirements and re-prioritization mechanisms.

Policymakers are signaling interest in faster, more granular interconnection processes to manage growing DER volumes. Proposed changes would likely emphasize readiness metrics, standardized technical studies, and increased use of staged interconnection. Projects that cannot meet new readiness thresholds may be deferred or re-scoped. The intent is to reduce queue backlogs and improve visibility for system planners, but transitional frictions are expected. Smaller developers could face higher administrative burdens without commensurate support, altering market composition. A phased implementation could mitigate disruption, but details on grandfathering and cost allocation are pivotal. Stakeholders should prepare for potential procedural changes and engage in regulatory comment windows to shape outcomes.

What to watch
  • Regulatory comment deadlines and draft rule detail on staging/readiness
  • Utility implementation timelines and grandfathering provisions
Action

Assess project readiness against emerging interconnection criteria and prepare submissions for regulatory comment periods.
