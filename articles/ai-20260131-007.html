Regulatory signals around reliability standards and interconnection timelines

Expect policy conversations to center on tightening reliability standards and clarifying interconnection timelines in response to growth in inverter-based resources and distributed generation.

Policy bodies appear focused on reducing interconnection backlog through procedural changes and clearer queue management. Reliability standards are being framed with a greater emphasis on inverter performance and fault-ride-through capabilities. Expect guidance that pressures developers to provide more detailed dynamic models at earlier stages of the queue. Resource adequacy constructs may be updated to reflect capacity value of storage and demand response. Implementation timelines will vary regionally and could create near-term project scheduling uncertainty. Monitor rulemaking dockets and consultation papers for forward-looking compliance expectations.

What to watch
  • rulemaking dockets on interconnection
  • guidance on inverter requirements
Action

Prepare detailed dynamic models earlier in the interconnection process and track queue management changes.
