Cyber rules for OT and AI: regulatory tightening scenario

Regulatory signals suggest a tightening posture toward operational technology and AI systems in the critical-infrastructure domain, creating compliance and procurement ripples for operators and OEMs.

Policymakers are increasingly focused on the intersection of AI controls and operational networks in electricity systems. Expectations are for stricter baseline requirements for model validation, access controls, and incident reporting for AI models used in operations. There is a scenario where auditability and explainability requirements become contractual prerequisites for vendor selection. This could increase development and validation costs for control-system integrators and OEMs. Operators may need to document model lifecycle management and incident-response playbooks more rigorously. What to watch are regulatory consultation papers, draft compliance timelines, and industry guidance on model assurance frameworks.

What to watch
  • Draft rules on AI model validation and OT cybersecurity
  • Vendor guidance on model lifecycle management and explainability
Action

Begin mapping AI/OT models to proposed compliance requirements and prioritize documentation for high-impact systems.
