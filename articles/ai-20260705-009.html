Gas-insulated switchgear lifecycle pressure

Expectations of higher demand for compact GIS solutions in urban and offshore projects is signaling potential lifecycle and aftermarket service pressure for SF6 alternatives and gas handling capabilities.

Compact GIS is increasingly specified where footprint and reliability drive project decisions. This scenario watch highlights aftermarket service needs for gas handling, leak detection, and end-of-life gas reclamation. There's also an expectation of stronger interest in low-GWP gas mixtures and solid-insulated alternatives. What to monitor are tender requirements referencing GWP limits and service contracts that include gas reclamation. OEMs and service providers may need to scale field gas-handling capabilities and training. Projects that lock into legacy SF6 systems could face higher future remediation costs and compliance complexity.

What to watch
  • Tender language on GWP limits and gas reclamation requirements
  • Availability of trained gas-handling service teams
Action

Include gas-handling and end-of-life clauses in GIS service agreements and evaluate low-GWP alternatives for new projects.
