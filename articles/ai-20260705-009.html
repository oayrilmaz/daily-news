Digital substation rollouts emphasize cybersecurity and interoperability

As digital substation initiatives accelerate, project stakeholders are reportedly prioritizing cybersecurity frameworks and interoperability standards to manage increased attack surface and vendor diversity.

Signals show specifications are increasingly including secure-by-design requirements and segmented network architectures. Expectations are that procurement will favor solutions with proven interoperability across protection, control, and SCADA layers. Scenario watch includes increased use of certified gateways and managed patching regimes to reduce operational risk. What to monitor are updates to procurement templates and any regulatory guidance on cybersecurity compliance for critical infrastructure. For integrators, the need to demonstrate secure lifecycle support is becoming a precondition for selection. Operational teams should anticipate higher commissioning complexity as security and interoperability verification are added to test plans. In parallel, legacy asset integration remains a technical and policy challenge that can extend timelines.

What to watch
  • revisions to procurement security requirements
  • certification of interoperability gateways
Action

Include cybersecurity and interoperability test cases in commissioning plans and require demonstrable vendor lifecycle support.
