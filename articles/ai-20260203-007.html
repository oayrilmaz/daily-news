Grid code revisions for inverter-based resources accelerate

Signals suggest regulators in parts of Europe are accelerating grid-code updates governing inverter behavior to improve system stability as inverter-based resources approach majority penetration in some areas.

Draft requirements increasingly focus on fault-ride-through, synthetic inertia emulation, and standardized telemetry from inverter-based plants. These changes are framed as necessary to maintain system stability without depending solely on synchronous generation. Compliance testing and certification requirements will add time and cost to project commissioning, particularly for export-focused developers. Equipment vendors are adapting firmware and control stacks to meet new behavioral specifications, which affects interoperability and legacy fleet upgrades. System operators are concurrently piloting operational frameworks that use coordinated inverter controls for voltage and frequency support. If adopted broadly, these revisions could materially alter project timelines and technical specifications for new inverter-based generation.

What to watch
  • Publication of draft grid-code text specifying inverter ride-through and telemetry standards.
  • Certification timelines or third-party labs announcing expanded inverter testing capacity.
Action

Review planned projects for compliance risk and engage with vendors now to align firmware roadmaps to expected grid-code changes.
