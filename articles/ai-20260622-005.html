Capacity and ancillary product value may shift with fast-asset proliferation

Market design signals and asset bidding patterns point to a potential revaluation of capacity and ancillary services as fast-response resources like batteries and demand response gain market share.

Fast assets change the temporal profile of reliability and can substitute for slower capacity in certain contingencies. Market operators may adjust product definitions to capture value from ramping, reactive power, and synthetic inertia. New procurement windows and shorter settlement intervals are being discussed to better remunerate sub-hourly performance. Incumbent assets could see revenue pressure if markets fail to fairly value long-duration characteristics. Investment decisions will hinge on forecasted price spreads between energy, capacity, and ancillary markets. Scenario watch: a regulatory nudge to redefine ancillary products could accelerate retirements of aging thermal units and shift investment to hybrid architectures.

What to watch
  • Regulatory consultations on ancillary product definitions
  • Settlement-interval changes or pilot programs
Action

Model revenue sensitivity to shorter settlement intervals and emerging ancillary products for asset valuation.
