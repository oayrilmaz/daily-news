Co-located Storage Economics Improve for Solar Projects

Economic signals suggest that co-located storage with solar installations in parts of Latin America could become an increasingly attractive scenario to capture higher value from energy and ancillary markets.

Developers are signalling better revenue stacking possibilities as market mechanisms for energy shifting and capacity remuneration mature. The expectation is that co-located storage will improve project bankability by enabling time-shifting of daytime solar value into peak demand hours. Transmission constraints and curtailment risks create a scenario where storage also acts as a congestion management lever. Financing structures that combine energy and ancillary service revenue streams are gaining traction among sponsors. There are practical barriers in permitting and interconnection queuing that could delay realization of these economics. Equipment availability and local installation capacity remain factors in deployment pacing. For developers and investors, an immediate focus should be on modeling aggregated revenue streams and pursuing flexible financing terms that reflect storage's multi-service role.

What to watch
  • changes in ancillary market participation rules
  • interconnection queue movement for solar-plus-storage projects
Action

Model combined energy and ancillary revenues for solar projects and prioritize sites with favorable interconnection prospects.
