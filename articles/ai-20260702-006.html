Supply signals: cobalt and nickel chain tightness and recycling tailwinds

Signals point to persistent tightness in cobalt and nickel supply chains alongside growing commercial interest in battery-material recycling as a medium-term stabilizer for cathode feedstocks.

New exploration and development projects are advancing but face permitting and financing timelines that keep physical tightness for several years. Demand from battery manufacturers continues to pressure midstream processing capacity, especially for high-purity nickel sulfate. Recycling is becoming commercially viable at increasing scales as economics improve and feedstock streams stabilize. Trade and logistics risks, including concentrated refining nodes, create single-point vulnerabilities that actors are seeking to diversify. Policy incentives for domestic processing in several markets are accelerating capital flows into local refining and recycling infrastructure. Scenario watch: a disruption at a major refining node could spike premiums and accelerate recycling of end-of-life batteries as an alternate feedstock source.

What to watch
  • Announcements of new refining capacity or recycling plant commissioning
  • Policy incentives for domestic processing or export controls
Action

Stress-test project supply chains for critical cathode materials and evaluate recycling partnerships to hedge feedstock risk.
