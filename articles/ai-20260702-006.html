Skilled labor shortages push EPCs toward automation and remote commissioning

Signals suggest EPC firms are adopting automated factory testing and remote commissioning tools to counter skilled labor constraints and accelerate deliveries in complex environments.

EPCs report increased investment in automated test benches for switchgear and protection systems to reduce onsite man-hours. The intelligence framing notes that remote commissioning reduces travel and safety risks but adds dependencies on secure telemetry and digital twin accuracy. Scenario watchers identify risks where remote diagnostics miss subtle onsite mechanical issues that only physical inspection would catch. There is an expectation that insurers and clients will require proof of equivalence between remote and onsite commissioning methods. The mid-term trend could normalize remote methods for routine tasks while reserving onsite teams for exceptions and mechanical fit checks. Workforce upskilling remains critical as technicians shift from manual assembly to automation oversight roles. Procurement timelines may shorten where factory acceptance tests are accepted as a substitute for lengthy onsite validation. Policy or local labor rules could slow this transition in some jurisdictions.

What to watch
  • Insurance and client acceptance of remote commissioning
  • Incidents tied to remote-only validation processes
Action

Document equivalence of FAT and remote commissioning and secure contractual acceptance before relying on remote approaches.
