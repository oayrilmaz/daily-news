Procurement strain and local-content rules complicate project delivery

Signals from bid cycles suggest EPCs are adjusting commercial terms to account for procurement strain and evolving local-content requirements, potentially extending delivery windows and increasing cost variance.

Procurement lead times for specialized HV equipment remain uneven, forcing EPCs to re-sequence construction activities or procure interim solutions. Local-content requirements in some jurisdictions are prompting redesigns to incorporate domestically sourced components, which can introduce integration and testing overhead. Contractors are signaling higher contingency allowances in bids to offset inventory and logistics uncertainty. Financing structures may shift to accommodate longer delivery milestones and staged handovers. Project owners should expect contractual negotiation on milestone definitions and acceptance testing. Scenario watch: stricter local-content enforcement combined with global supply delays could materially extend critical-path activities.

What to watch
  • bid addenda addressing lead times
  • local-content compliance notices
Action

Revisit contractual milestones and acceptance criteria with EPCs and validate supply-chain contingencies for critical long-lead items.
