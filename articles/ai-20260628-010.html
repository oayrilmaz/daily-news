AI-driven maintenance adoption: high upside with emergent operational caveats

Signals point to accelerated adoption of AI for predictive maintenance across substations and transmission assets, creating expectations of improved uptime but also introducing new model-validation and operational-integration risks.

Maintenance teams are piloting AI models that analyze PMU, SCADA, and sensor data to predict component degradation. Signals indicate initial gains in lead-time prediction and failure avoidance. However, integration with asset management systems and operator workflows remains a practical hurdle. Expectations are that organizations will need structured change programs and validation protocols. Model drift and data quality concerns are salient; AI outputs require continuous retraining and supervisory review to avoid false positives or missed events. The scenario watch includes degraded model performance under unusual system states. Cyber and data governance risks increase as models rely on larger, sometimes cloud-hosted datasets. Operators must balance performance benefits against data sovereignty and security requirements. Vendors are offering packaged analytics with explainability features, but buyers should insist on transparent performance metrics, backtesting, and on-site validation. Contractual terms should address model maintenance and liability. What to monitor: pilot performance metrics, data pipeline integrity, and change-management progress for operational adoption.

What to watch
  • Pilot model performance and backtesting results
  • Data pipeline and cybersecurity safeguards
Action

Run controlled pilots with clear validation metrics and contractual provisions for ongoing model maintenance and explainability.
