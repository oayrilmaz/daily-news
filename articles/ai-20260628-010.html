AI-driven asset management gains traction in HV operations

Signals suggest AI-based monitoring and predictive-maintenance tools are gaining purchase among OEMs and operators for high-voltage assets, offering scenario improvements in uptime and cost control.

Vendors are packaging AI models with sensor kits to enable earlier detection of insulation degradation and thermal anomalies.

Operators are running pilot programs to integrate AI alerts into existing SCADA and maintenance workflows to reduce false positives.

The quality and coverage of sensor data remain a gating factor for model accuracy, prompting investments in instrumentation upgrades.

Regulatory acceptance and auditability of AI-informed maintenance decisions are emerging considerations for asset owners.

If pilots scale, expected outcomes include longer inspection intervals for low-risk assets and prioritized interventions for high-risk ones.

Adoption scenarios hinge on demonstrable ROI in reduced outage frequency and extended asset life, and on integration with existing OEM service contracts.

What to watch
  • pilot results demonstrating ROI for predictive-maintenance programs
  • regulatory guidance on AI use in maintenance decision-making
Action

Start small AI pilots on critical HV assets with clear KPIs for outage reduction and maintenance cost savings.
