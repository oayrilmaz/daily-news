Capacity accreditation and storage valuation debates surface in regional markets

Signals from market stakeholders indicate renewed debate on how storage and aggregated resources should be accredited for capacity markets, with scenario implications for valuation, investment signals, and resource adequacy.

Market designers and participants are reportedly examining accreditation rules that balance short-duration storage operation with contribution to peak capacity. This scenario explores multi-hour and duration-based accreditation frameworks that could shift investment toward longer-duration assets. Aggregators and virtual power plant models are being evaluated for their capacity contributions and deliverability requirements. There is a risk of transitional mismatches where existing storage assets are undervalued under legacy accreditation approaches. Regulators may trial phased accreditation changes or pilot performance tests to refine metrics. The outcome will affect financing terms and the attractiveness of storage versus firm thermal or long-duration options. Watch stakeholder submissions and any pilot accreditation programs for near-term directionality.

What to watch
  • Pilot accreditation programs and performance-test designs for storage contributions.
  • Regulatory or market operator communications on capacity market rule changes.
Action

Model multiple accreditation outcomes for storage investments and prepare flexible contracting to preserve optionality.
