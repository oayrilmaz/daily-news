Grid cybersecurity and standards compliance scenarios are influencing procurement clauses

Procurement teams increasingly factor cybersecurity and standards compliance into RFPs, creating a scenario in which suppliers without demonstrable OT security capabilities may be sidelined.

Signals suggest new RFP templates emphasize end-to-end security assurances, including supply-chain integrity and firmware update processes. The scenario emerges from heightened attention to OT vulnerabilities and regulatory expectations for critical infrastructure. OEMs are being asked for detailed vulnerability disclosure practices and patch management timelines. Smaller suppliers may struggle to meet documentation requirements, shifting advantage to larger firms or those with third-party security certifications. Integration testing for security posture could extend procurement schedules. Compliance-related clauses may carry financial penalties or remediation obligations. Monitoring regulatory guidance updates will be important to anticipate changes in contractual thresholds.

What to watch
  • RFP templates and cybersecurity requirement updates
  • third-party OT security certification trends
Action

Ensure vendor security documentation and patch management plans meet heightened procurement requirements ahead of RFP responses.
