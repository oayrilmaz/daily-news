Colocation growth driving microgrid and backup-capacity design choices

Signals indicate colocation growth in key LATAM hubs is driving earlier adoption of microgrids and diversified backup generation designs, altering interconnection and resiliency planning.

Developers appear to prioritize configurations that combine UPS, short-duration batteries, and backup gensets with microgrid controls. This layered approach balances capital costs with operational resilience and market participation potential. Expectations include greater negotiation complexity with utilities over firm capacity and islanding rights. A scenario to watch is the use of aggregated colocation microgrids as a grid service provider for local ancillary markets. What to monitor are interconnection amendment filings, and municipal grid reliability announcements that affect contract terms. Operators that standardize microgrid control interfaces across campuses will have more flexibility to offer services or island during outages. The net effect may be stronger resilience for critical customers but higher upfront integration complexity for developers.

What to watch
  • Interconnection amendment filings for campus-level islanding.
  • Local market rules enabling aggregated microgrid participation.
Action

Update microgrid control and interconnection strategies for colocation projects to preserve flexibility for islanding and market participation.
