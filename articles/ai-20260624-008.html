EPCs signal increased preference for modular HV components

Signals from procurement and project bids indicate EPCs are favoring modular high-voltage equipment to reduce site scheduling risk and accelerate commissioning windows.

EPC bid patterns show higher weighting for turnkey modular solutions that minimize on-site assembly time. This operational signal reflects lessons learned from logistic variability and workforce constraints. Modular equipment adoption can shorten lead times and reduce complexity in testing and commissioning, creating a scenario where projects can tolerate longer upstream delivery times. The net schedule benefit is situation-dependent. Manufacturers are responding with factory-assembled substations and pre-integrated skids, which shifts quality assurance upstream. The commercial signal is toward prefabrication premiums and revised warranty structures. Project financiers are re-evaluating collateral approaches as modular assets change salvage value and replacement pathways. That financial signal could lower perceived project execution risk for certain lenders. Standardization of interfaces and rapid plug-and-play protection settings remain a technical barrier and a scenario watch area. Without agreement on standards, modular gains may be limited by integration workarounds. What to monitor: RFQ templates that reward modularity, factory acceptance test scope changes, and warranty language shifts in EPC contracts.

What to watch
  • RFQ language on modularity
  • FAT scope updates
Action

Assess vendor modular offerings against existing interface standards and adjust procurement specs accordingly.
