Processing bottlenecks and refining capacity shaping mineral supply scenarios

Signal patterns in procurement and permitting suggest that refining and processing constraints, more than raw mined volumes, will be a key determinant of near-term critical mineral availability for battery and HV equipment supply chains.

Mining output alone may not predict downstream availability as concentrate processing capacity is the gating factor. Investment cycles for new refineries are long and subject to permitting and financing uncertainty. OEMs dependent on refined inputs could face intermittent shortages even if ore production increases. Recycling streams are emerging as a partial hedge but require scale and regulatory clarity to meaningfully offset demand. Strategic stockpiling and offtake agreements may become more common to stabilize supply for high-priority projects. Scenario watch should include accelerated permits for refining and shifts in trade flows that reroute concentrates to available processing hubs.

What to watch
  • Permitting and financing updates for major processing facilities.
  • Changes in offtake contract terms prioritizing refined product delivery.
Action

Secure offtake or processing commitments and evaluate recycling partnerships to reduce downstream exposure.
