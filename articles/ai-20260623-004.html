Curtailment risk rises where transmission buildout lags VRE growth

Signals point to growing curtailment risk in areas where variable renewable energy additions outpace transmission upgrades, creating scenarios that could compress merchant project revenues and challenge grid balancing.

Rapid additions of wind and solar in constrained corridors increase the probability of negative pricing and strategic curtailment. Expectations include more frequent backdown events during low-load, high-VRE windows, which may shorten asset payback horizons for merchant developers. Scenario watch: persistent curtailment could incentivize local storage, demand response, or repowering choices that favor flexible resources. Grid operators may prioritize reinforcement projects, but lead times and permitting delays temper near-term relief. What to monitor are interconnection queue movements, curtailment notices, and shifting of investment to hybrid projects co-located with storage. The intelligence angle highlights a policy and capex trade-off between transmission expansion and distributed flexibility solutions.

What to watch
  • Curtailed generation reports and negative-price windows
  • Interconnection queue progress and transmission permit milestones
Action

Reassess revenue cases for new VRE projects in constrained areas and prioritize hybrid or co-located storage options.
