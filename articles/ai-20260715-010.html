Workforce and O&M signals affect substation resiliency planning

Expectations are that workforce availability and evolving O&M models will become central to substation resiliency as asset bases expand in arid and remote regions.

Operators are signaling the need for more remote-monitoring capabilities to cope with limited onsite engineering resources. Training pipelines for high-voltage technicians are variable across the region, prompting some owners to outsource specialized maintenance services. Scenario watch includes the rise of long-term O&M contracts that bundle analytics, spares provisioning, and rapid-response teams. Climate considerations, including dust and heat stress on insulation systems, are pushing condition-based maintenance pilots. What to monitor are contractor capacity indicators, service-level changes in O&M agreements, and adoption rates of remote diagnostic platforms.

What to watch
  • availability of certified HV technicians and contractor mobilization timelines
  • new long-term O&M contract announcements or pilot remote-monitoring rollouts
Action

Build contingency staffing plans and evaluate bundled O&M providers that include remote diagnostics and spares.
