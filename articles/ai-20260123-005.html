Capacity and energy market signals are evolving with high inverter-based resource penetration

Signals indicate market operators are testing new product definitions and settlement rules to better value fast-response services and inertia equivalents as inverter-based resources grow.

Market designers are exploring products that explicitly reward synthetic inertia and rapid frequency response from non-synchronous resources. Settlement rule pilots appear focused on creating clearer pathways for storage and advanced inverter services to monetize reliability contributions. Capacity market signals are being reinterpreted to account for declining traditional reserve margins in some regions. Traders and aggregators are recalibrating bidding strategies to reflect changing stress-event profiles and shorter duration scarcity events. Scenario watch: if product definitions lag technical capability, resource owners may underinvest in controls that could provide grid services. Expect increased dialogue between system operators, aggregators, and vendors to align telemetry and settlement standards.

What to watch
  • Regulatory consultations or pilots on synthetic inertia remuneration
  • Aggregator filings for new market products
Action

Map your resource's technical capabilities to emerging market products and engage with market operators to influence telemetry and settlement requirements.
