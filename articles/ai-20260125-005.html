Capacity and real-time price volatility scenario watch for next winter season

Market signals point to continued sensitivity in capacity and real-time prices as higher variable renewables meet tight thermal retirements, increasing the potential for short-duration price spikes under stress scenarios.

The combination of retirements and interconnection backlogs is creating tighter margins in several regional markets. Variable renewables reduce average energy prices but can increase volatility when forecast errors or low wind/solar periods coincide with demand peaks. Capacity market revenues and ancillary service pricing are being recalibrated to reflect changing reliability contributions, leading to evolving revenue stacks for generators and storage providers. Market design changes under consideration in some regions create scenario risk for investors and operators, altering capacity valuation. Dispatch optimization that incorporates storage and demand response is likely to shape intraday price dynamics. Risk managers are re-stressing portfolios under high-volatility scenarios to assess hedging needs.

What to watch
  • Capacity auction results and any market rule changes affecting capacity valuation.
  • Short-term forecast errors during high-demand periods that could trigger price spikes.
Action

Stress-test portfolios for short, sharp price shocks and evaluate hedging or demand-response options to mitigate revenue swings.
