Short-duration ancillary markets tightening; price spikes possible in stress scenarios

Signals from intra-day market activity indicate growing premiums for short-duration and fast-response ancillary services, suggesting a scenario where rapid-response products become the scarcest and most valuable commodity in stressed hours.

Market clearing outcomes in several zones show widening spreads between energy and fast-response ancillary services. This reflects both increasing demand for sub-hourly support and limited supply able to provide repeated, short-duration deployments. Price spikes have been observed in simulated stress runs where inverter-based resources curtail grid support due to firmware or ride-through constraints. Capacity markets remain focused on longer-duration adequacy and are less responsive to sub-hourly needs, leaving a gap filled by batteries, demand response, and fast gas. Traders and portfolio managers are recalibrating models to price higher expected utilization for these assets. Procurement strategies are shifting to bilateral arrangements for fast response, and there's increased interest in aggregating distributed assets. The scenario to monitor is elevated short-term volatility and potential premium capture by flexible resource owners.

What to watch
  • intra-day ancillary clearing prices and volatility measures
  • activation frequency for fast-response contracted assets
Action

Reprice portfolios and contracts to reflect higher utilization and value for fast-response capabilities.
