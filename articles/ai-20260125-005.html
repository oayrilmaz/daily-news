Capacity market signals and winter-spring forecasts stress-test firm dispatch assumptions

Market signals and seasonal forecasts are prompting traders and schedulers to reassess firmed renewable positions and the value of flexible peaking resources in capacity markets.

Capacity market cleared positions are being re-evaluated under shifted winter-to-spring demand curves. Traders are testing hedge structures that account for increased renewable curtailment and longer-duration storage value. Some asset owners are modeling switchable fuel contracts for peakers as a short-term resilience play. Market operators are being watched for rule clarifications around storage participation and state-of-charge accounting. Scenario analyses are focusing on extreme weather coincident with transmission constraints. Clearing prices in capacity auctions may reflect these updated risk assumptions. Portfolio managers are updating stress tests to include combined weather and transmission-limit events.

What to watch
  • capacity auction results and rule changes
  • transmission constraint advisories during peak seasons
Action

Re-run capacity stress scenarios incorporating storage state-of-charge rules and transmission contingencies.
