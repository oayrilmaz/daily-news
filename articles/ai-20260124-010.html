AI-driven trading strategies could amplify price swings in thin markets

Signals from trading desk behavior suggest AI-based algorithms are entering regional power markets, which may amplify price movements in low-liquidity intervals and create new operational considerations for market operators.

AI-driven strategies optimize across timeframes and can converge on similar signals, which raises the potential for correlated actions in low-liquidity windows. In smaller markets, this correlation can produce pronounced price spikes or troughs that stress settlement and credit frameworks. Market operators are considering enhanced circuit-breaker logic and improved telemetry as mitigations to rapid, automated excursions. Smaller participants and distributed resources could be squeezed if algorithmic strategies dominate bid stacks during tight periods. Transparency around models and access to historical training data is limited, complicating impact attribution and mitigation design. Regulatory dialogue is beginning in some jurisdictions to evaluate disclosure and fairness measures for automated trading agents. Scenario planning should include abrupt liquidity shifts triggered by algorithmic convergence and subsequent operator interventions. Monitor trading volumes, bid retraction frequencies and operator notices about unusual automated activity patterns.

What to watch
  • Intraday volume surges and bid retraction behaviors
  • Operator advisories on market stability measures or rule changes
Action

Review market participation safeguards and stress-test portfolios for algorithm-driven liquidity shocks.
