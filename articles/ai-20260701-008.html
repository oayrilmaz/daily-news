Factory digitization and modular EPC models are gaining traction, with integration risk the main limiter

Signals indicate OEMs and EPCs are investing in factory automation and digital twin capabilities to accelerate delivery, but integration across logistics, quality control, and site adaptation remains a key risk factor.

Expect modular, factory-built components to reduce on-site time and improve predictability, particularly for repeatable substation and plant elements. Digitization investments focus on supply visibility, predictive maintenance of factory equipment, and digital-twin-based validation before shipping. The value case improves where projects are standardized; bespoke civil works and site constraints blunt some of the speed advantage. Skilled labor shortages push more OEMs to automation, but this requires up-front capital and new vendor relationships that take time to mature. Interfacing factory-built assets with site-specific protection, control, and civil integrations will demand tighter systems engineering up front. Scenario watch: EPCs that can offer full-package factory acceptance, transport, and rapid site commissioning may capture premium market share. What to monitor are procurement language shifts toward fixed-scope, factory-delivered packages and the pace of digital-twin adoption by major contractors.

What to watch
  • Procurement documents favoring factory-delivered packages
  • Announcements of digital-twin pilots for factory QA/QC
Action

Assess which standard scopes in your portfolio can be modularized and begin vendor engagement for factory acceptance testing protocols.
