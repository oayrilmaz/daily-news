Concentration risk in battery and magnet supply chains prompting diversification pilots

Market participants are signaling accelerated efforts to diversify critical-mineral sourcing and to qualify alternative chemistries, creating near-term procurement friction and incentive shifts for long-duration storage projects.

Signals point to OEMs and buyers running qualification tests on alternative cathode and magnet chemistries to reduce geopolitical sourcing concentration. These tests slow procurement cycles and increase short-term unit costs while aiming to reduce medium-term systemic risk. Expectation: projects that can accept alternative chemistries or recycled feedstock may secure faster delivery but face performance trade-offs. Recycling and secondary supply pilots are being integrated into procurement to shorten lead times for key elements needed in batteries and motors. Scenario watch: certification timelines for recycled content or alternative chemistries will influence capital allocation and candidate project selection. What to monitor: changes in supplier qualification standards, procurement RFIs that emphasize recycled or alternative materials, and spot-price volatility. Action: evaluate alternative chemistry roadmaps and include flexible specification options in long-lead procurement contracts.

What to watch
  • supplier qualification updates for alternative chemistries
  • procurement RFIs prioritizing recycled content
Action

Include flexible material specifications and recycling pathways in procurement to reduce single-supplier exposure.
