Semiconductor stress extends HV equipment lead times

Signals point to persistent semiconductor constraints for protection relays and controls that may extend lead times for high-voltage equipment and influence procurement strategies.

Protection and control suppliers continue to integrate specialized silicon in relays, IEDs, and telecommunications modules. Sourcing complexity for these components can lengthen production timelines when global supply tightness appears. OEMs are reported to be dual-sourcing where possible and redesigning boards to accept alternate components, but requalification adds schedule risk. Buyers may experience longer queues and are considering earlier purchase commitments. Extended lead times create upstream scheduling pressure on EPCs and can amplify project contingency risk. Some teams are exploring modular retrofit strategies to stage installations while awaiting long-lead control hardware. Regulatory compliance and testing cycles for alternative components remain important gating items. Over time, localized assembly and qualification could shorten timelines, but the near-term effect is an operational squeeze on project schedules.

What to watch
  • Quoted lead times from major relay manufacturers
  • OEM notices on alternative component qualification
Action

Advance procurement timelines for protection and control hardware and evaluate modular retrofit options to decouple schedules.
