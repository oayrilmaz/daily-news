Cyber risk from AI-enabled control tools remains a scenario watch

Signals indicate growing attention to cyber risks introduced by AI-enabled control tools at substations, particularly as more edge devices gain autonomous decision capabilities.

Operators are evaluating how AI model failure modes could cascade into protection misconfigurations or false tripping. The main scenario of concern involves adversarial inputs or model drift leading to degraded situational awareness at critical nodes. There is a governance expectation that layered defenses, rigorous testing, and model explainability will be required to mitigate operational risk. Vendors are experimenting with hybrid architectures that keep protective relays on traditional control paths while AI sits in advisory roles. This staged approach reduces immediate operational exposure but creates long-term integration complexity. Watch for guidance on model assurance, cybersecurity certification for AI tools, and insurance requirements tied to AI adoption.

What to watch
  • model assurance standards activity
  • insurance riders related to AI tools
Action

Require AI model assurance clauses in procurement and validate hybrid control architectures in test environments.
