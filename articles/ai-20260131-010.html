HVDC and interconnection projects face scheduling and technical interaction questions

Signals from planning exercises indicate HVDC interconnection projects are revealing complex technical interaction considerations that could affect commissioning schedules.

Engineers note more extensive studies are needed to capture dynamic interactions between multiple converter stations and nearby AC systems. This increases pre-commissioning testing and simulation scope. Scenario watch: extended simulation and interoperability testing windows may delay energization and revenue start dates for linked projects. Project teams are considering staged commissioning to mitigate schedule risk while preserving system stability. What to monitor are grid code advisory updates and test-plan approvals from system operators. OEMs offering pre-validated converter control packages and test harnesses could shorten on-site integration work. Stakeholders should review project timelines and budget contingencies with an eye to extended pre-energization activities.

What to watch
  • Grid code advisory updates
  • Pre-commissioning test plan approvals
Action

Build extended simulation and pre-commissioning time into HVDC project schedules and prioritize pre-validated control solutions.
