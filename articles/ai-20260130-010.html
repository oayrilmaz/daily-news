African upstream expansions present both supply diversification and project execution uncertainty

Signals point to increased upstream capacity announcements in African jurisdictions for battery minerals, offering diversification benefits but carrying execution, permitting, and logistics uncertainties that could affect mid-term supply visibility.

Several development-stage projects are being highlighted in broader supply diversification strategies, reflecting interest in reducing geographic concentration risks. These projects bring potential new tonnages for nickel, cobalt, and lithium derivatives, which could ease some supply-side pressures over the medium term. Execution risk is non-trivial: permitting timelines, local infrastructure constraints, and processing capacity must align to realize exportable volumes. Logistics and refining footprint decisions will determine whether raw ore or refined products enter global markets, affecting downstream supply chains. International firms are pairing mining investments with local processing initiatives to capture additional value and reduce transport bottlenecks. The scenario of staggered ramp-ups is likely, where incremental tonnages help but do not fully offset near-term demand spikes.

What to watch
  • permitting milestones and local infrastructure commitments for new projects
  • announcements on local refining or processing capacity
Action

Incorporate staggered ramp-up timelines for new upstream projects into supply risk assessments and procurement plans.
