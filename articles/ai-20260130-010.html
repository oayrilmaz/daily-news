Generative-AI configuration risks for protection systems

Adoption of generative-AI tools for relay and protection configuration introduces scenario-level efficiency gains alongside configuration and validation risks that merit close scrutiny.

Early deployments of AI-assisted configuration are producing scenario signals of faster engineering cycles. Efficiency gains from automating routine configuration tasks could shorten project timelines and reduce manual errors. However, reliance on model outputs without rigorous validation increases the risk of misconfiguration in protection settings. Regulatory and compliance frameworks for protection-system validation lag tool adoption in many jurisdictions. Vendors and operators are running internal validation scenarios to reconcile AI outputs with engineering judgment. Cybersecurity profiling of AI toolchains is an additional vector that requires attention. What to monitor are validation protocols, failure-mode test results, and any guidance from standards bodies on AI-assisted configuration.

What to watch
  • Validation and failure-mode test outcomes from AI-assisted tools
  • Standards-body guidance or draft positions on AI in protection settings
Action

Implement rigorous validation regimes for any AI-generated protection settings and include cybersecurity assessments in pilots.
