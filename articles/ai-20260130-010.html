Liquid cooling and higher rack power density trends

Signals suggest rising interest in liquid cooling and higher rack power densities driven by AI workloads, creating scenarios where facility electrical and cooling designs must adapt rapidly.

Operators are modeling higher sustained rack power draws tied to next-generation AI clusters. Expectations include increased adoption of direct-to-chip and immersion cooling to manage thermal limits. Scenario watch: facilities that cannot adapt quickly may face costly retrofits or constrained deployment schedules. What to monitor are RFIs and RFPs for liquid-cooling infrastructure and changes in PUE assumptions. Electrical infrastructure will need re-evaluation for feeder sizing and UPS performance under denser loads. Mechanical systems, including chilled water loops and heat rejection, will come under review for capacity and control. Site selection criteria may shift toward locations with ample water cooling resources or access to low-temperature ambient cooling.

What to watch
  • RFPs specifying direct-to-chip or immersion cooling
  • changes in PUE and feeder sizing assumptions
Action

Re-evaluate electrical feeder capacity and cooling strategies for new AI-scale deployments.
