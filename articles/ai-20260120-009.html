DER aggregation models create distribution-transmission coordination challenge

Signals show distributed energy resource aggregation scaling in several markets, raising a scenario watch on co-optimizing distribution-level flexibility with transmission-level system needs.

Aggregators are increasingly bidding fleets of behind-the-meter assets into ancillary markets, creating attractive revenue streams and new operational flex points. This aggregation can mask localized congestion or voltage issues at the feeder level while delivering services at the transmission level. Distribution-system operators are seeking visibility and control interfaces to avoid unintended local impacts when assets follow market dispatch signals. Technical workstreams are exploring visibility protocols and hierarchical control schemes to reconcile local constraints with bulk-system optimization. Billing, settlement, and telemetric standards are practical bottlenecks that will shape how quickly coordinated operation can scale. Scenario outcomes vary: with good coordination, DER aggregation reduces overall system costs; without it, localized reliability events could increase. Regulatory and market design adjustments to align incentives between TSOs, DSOs, and aggregators will be central to long-term success. Stakeholders should monitor pilot programs that test hierarchical control and settlement mechanisms as leading indicators.

What to watch
  • pilots for hierarchical control between DSOs and aggregators
  • changes in settlement rules for aggregated assets
Action

Engage with aggregators and DSOs to test visibility protocols and incorporate local constraints into market dispatch logic.
