Grid integration of large solar-plus-storage projects under scenario review

Planners are running scenario analyses for high-capacity solar-plus-storage projects to evaluate ramping implications and transmission congestion during evening peaks.

Project developers are sizing storage to firm evening ramps and to deliver capacity premiums in capacity markets. System operators are modeling congested corridors where large solar clusters may saturate existing transmission assets. What to monitor are firming product definitions and potential curtailment rules that could alter revenue stacks for projects. Expectations include increased need for dynamic line ratings and topology control schemes to optimize flows. Scenario watch covers negotiated interconnection terms that include curtailment rights, redispatch obligations, and cost-sharing for network reinforcements. Developers should assess hybrid project locational premiums and active curtailment hedges when modeling bankability. Transmission owners may explore targeted upgrades or topology changes to unlock clustered solar capacity safely.

What to watch
  • Interconnection term revisions affecting curtailment
  • Dynamic line rating pilot announcements
Action

Model curtailment and firming scenarios into project financials and engage early with transmission owners on upgrade cost-sharing.
