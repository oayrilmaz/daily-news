Capacity and ancillary markets show volatility signals amid storage growth

Signals point to increased short-term volatility in capacity and ancillary service pricing as storage and demand response scale, creating scenario risks for hedging and revenue forecasts.

Market operators are adapting settlement rules to accommodate faster-response resources, which changes revenue stacks and bidding behavior. Storage owners face scenarios where high frequency of fast-ramping events shifts revenues from energy arbitrage to capacity and fast-response services. There is an expectation that price cannibalization could emerge in energy-only segments where many assets chase the same peak windows. Regulatory changes to co-optimization of energy and ancillary services are a watch factor because they materially affect market outcomes. Market participants are experimenting with new hedging instruments tailored to storage performance profiles. The rise of algorithmic trading tied to AI forecasts may amplify intraday price swings, creating both opportunities and counterparty risks. Finally, transparency around settlement and performance measurement is an operational priority to avoid disputes.

What to watch
  • Rule changes on co-optimization or settlement windows
  • New hedging products for fast-response assets
Action

Stress-test portfolio revenue models under scenarios with elevated ancillary-service price volatility and emerging co-optimization rules.
