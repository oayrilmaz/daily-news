Recycling and secondary sources emerge in scenarios to offset cobalt and nickel supply tightness

Signals point to increased investment interest in battery-material recycling and secondary sourcing as a plausible buffer against primary-supply disruptions for cobalt and nickel.

Concentration risks in primary supply chains for key battery metals are creating scenario incentives for recycling and hydrometallurgical recovery. Investors and OEMs are evaluating circular-supply models that can reduce exposure to raw-material shocks. Expectations include near-term pilots scaling to commercial recycling facilities and partnerships between OEMs and recycling firms. The economics hinge on recovery rates, capex for processing plants, and regulatory incentives for circular sourcing. What to monitor are announcements of collection programs, technology partnerships, and shifts in procurement specifications that accept recycled-content material. Longer term, improved recyclability of new battery chemistries could materially change feedstock dynamics. The trajectory will influence pricing volatility and supplier selection across the EV and storage supply chains.

What to watch
  • Commercial-scale recycling facility announcements
  • Procurement specs allowing recycled-content battery materials
Action

Assess potential partners in battery-material recycling and evaluate lifecycle sourcing clauses in supplier contracts.
