Curtailment and congestion signals grow as wind and solar exceed local capacity

Signals of rising curtailment and localized congestion are appearing in regions with rapid wind and solar buildouts; planners should recalibrate interconnection and market assumptions for distributed renewables.

High renewable penetration in constrained transmission corridors is producing more frequent intraday curtailment events. These occurrences are signaling limits in existing network topology and the need for targeted reinforcements. Market settlements increasingly reflect negative price periods during low-demand, high-generation windows, which alters project revenue profiles. Grid operators are experimenting with more granular locational signaling to reflect true constraint costs and incentivize flexible resources. Battery storage is being deployed as a mitigation layer, but storage sizing and dispatch strategies are still evolving relative to curtailment patterns. Distributed resources with smart inverter capabilities can provide localized voltage and frequency support, but their deployment requires updated protection and control schemes. Interconnection queue reform and dynamic hosting capacity assessments are potential levers to better align projects with grid capabilities. Watch for policy adjustments that change priority access or compensation for curtailed energy.

What to watch
  • negative price occurrences
  • interconnection queue notices
Action

Model plant-level revenue under curtailment scenarios and prioritize co-located storage or flexible offtake agreements.
