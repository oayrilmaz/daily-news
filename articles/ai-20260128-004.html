Inertia gap scenarios boost interest in synthetic inertia and fast frequency response

Expectations are rising that system operators will more actively trial synthetic inertia and fast frequency response as a scenario response to declining synchronous generation.

The balance between inverter-heavy renewables and traditional synchronous machines remains a central operational challenge. Signals show increasing procurement of services that emulate inertia from BESS and advanced inverters. Pilots are likely to focus on areas with high instantaneous renewable penetration where traditional frequency support is insufficient. There is a scenario where market frameworks evolve to remunerate synthetic inertia, shifting investment toward responsive power electronics. OEMs are adapting inverter firmware to provide validated ride-through and frequency support features. What to monitor are product certification timelines and grid codes that could mandate synthetic inertia capabilities. The expectation is a progressive integration path rather than a single fast transition.

What to watch
  • Grid code amendments requiring synthetic inertia
  • Certification and product rollout timelines from inverter OEMs
Action

Review inverter control capabilities and plan firmware upgrade pathways to meet emerging inertia service requirements.
