Cyber and physical convergence raises asset hardening scenario needs

Signals show increasing attention to integrated cyber-physical risk at substations, prompting scenario-based hardening and layered detection strategies for critical nodes.

Operators are reframing security programs to treat cyber and physical threats as linked failure modes rather than distinct silos. Hardening scenarios now encompass supply-chain integrity, firmware provenance, and access-control convergence with perimeter security. There is an expectation that asset owners will prioritize layered detection and rapid isolation capabilities at critical substations. Procurement may include stricter cybersecurity requirements for IEDs and substation controllers, including verifiable update and patch management provisions. Training and exercise programs are being adapted to simulate simultaneous cyber and physical incidents to test cross-team coordination. The scenario outcome is a higher bar for vendor certifications and a need for closer integration between operations and security teams.

What to watch
  • Procurement clauses on firmware and update provenance
  • Exercise schedules that combine cyber and physical incident simulations
Action

Review substation vendor cyber requirements and plan a combined cyber-physical exercise for critical sites.
