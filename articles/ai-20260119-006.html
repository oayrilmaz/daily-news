Supply-chain signal: artisanal and informal mining affecting copper and cobalt risk profiles

Signals from trading and procurement channels suggest increasing exposure to informal mining in certain jurisdictions, elevating traceability and continuity risks for copper and cobalt supplies.

Procurement teams are raising questions about provenance and operational continuity as sourcing footprints extend. Expectations include heightened due diligence requirements and more robust chain-of-custody controls from buyers. Scenario watch: enforcement actions or local disruptions could interrupt shipments unexpectedly, creating supply shocks for downstream OEMs. There is also reputational risk for companies unintentionally linked to informal extraction practices. Financial intermediaries may demand greater transparency and certifications before financing projects. What to monitor are changes in export volumes from high-risk regions and emerging traceability standards. Companies should map multi-sourcing options and evaluate metal-substitution feasibility for specific components. The near-term intelligence task is to quantify exposure and accelerate supplier audits.

What to watch
  • Export volume anomalies
  • New traceability or certification requirements
Action

Map supplier exposures for critical metals and initiate provenance audits for high-risk supply lines.
