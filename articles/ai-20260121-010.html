Generative AI pilots for predictive maintenance at EPC scale

Signals show EPCs and OEMs piloting generative-AI workflows to synthesize equipment failure narratives and accelerate root-cause analysis across complex project portfolios.

Generative models are being used to consolidate maintenance logs, vendor bulletins, and sensor streams into prioritized diagnostic hypotheses.

Expectations emphasize time savings in troubleshooting and faster confidence-building for repair crews when combined with physics-based models.

Scenario watch includes hallucination risks and the need for strict validation against telemetry and test results before acting on model recommendations.

Governance frameworks and human-in-the-loop checkpoints are being trialed to balance speed with safety and contractual accountability.

If validated, generative-AI augmentation could shrink mean-time-to-repair and inform spare-parts inventories across EPC programs.

However, adoption will depend on rigorous evaluation frameworks and the ability to integrate model outputs into existing maintenance management systems.

What to watch
  • Pilot outcomes comparing AI-suggested diagnostics to traditional root-cause analysis
  • Integration milestones with maintenance-management systems
Action

Pilot generative-AI on low-risk maintenance tasks and implement strict validation gates before operational deployment.
