AI predictive maintenance for HV equipment shows promise amid data-quality constraints

Expectations for AI-driven predictive maintenance are high, but scenario watchers note that gains depend on data quality, labeling practices, and cross-vendor telemetry harmonization.

Early deployments of machine learning for transformer and circuit-breaker health monitoring show potential to reduce unplanned outages and optimize maintenance windows.

However, models are sensitive to sensor placement, sampling cadence, and historic failure labeling, which vary widely across fleets.

Model drift and false-positive rates are realistic operational concerns if training data is not representative of the installed base.

Vendors often propose federated learning and transfer learning to bridge data gaps, but those approaches require governance and careful validation.

There is a scenario where over-reliance on predictive scores without clear inspection protocols leads to missed maintenance or unnecessary interventions.

Operators should prioritize pilot programs with clear KPI definitions, model explainability, and integrated workflows for escalations and physical inspections.

What to watch
  • Training data representativeness and labeling practices
  • Model explainability and false-positive monitoring
Action

Run controlled pilots with clear KPIs and explainability checks; ensure inspection workflows align with model outputs.
