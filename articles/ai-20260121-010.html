Distributed energy resources create complex coordination scenarios for transmission planning

Expectations are that rapid behind-the-meter DER growth will complicate transmission planning models, requiring new coordination between distribution operators and central planners.

High DER adoption changes net load shapes and undermines traditional load-growth assumptions used for transmission investment decisions. Planners are signaling a need to integrate distribution-level data and probabilistic scenarios into capacity models. The coordination boundary between distribution system operators and transmission planners is becoming a friction point for interconnection timelines. Market designs and incentive structures may need adjustment to reward DERs that provide grid services during critical windows. Without updated data flows and joint planning protocols, misaligned investments and stranded assets are plausible. Scenario work is increasingly focused on interoperable telemetry and shared forecasting standards to reduce planning friction.

What to watch
  • Interconnection queue updates showing DER clustering
  • Emerging protocols for distribution-transmission data exchange
Action

Engage distribution counterparts to establish data-sharing protocols and align forecasts for near-term transmission planning cycles.
