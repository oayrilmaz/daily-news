Sourcing complexity for battery minerals remains an operations risk

Signals suggest procurement teams are grappling with geopolitical and environmental complexity in battery mineral sourcing, elevating operational and reputational risk for projects reliant on low-cost supply chains.

Buyers face a scenario set where price pressure drives interest in alternative sources with less mature governance frameworks. Environmental and community risks are prominent in several prospective supply regions, creating potential for project delays or social conflict. Firms are consequently experimenting with blended sourcing strategies that combine long-term contracts, recycled content, and premium-priced certified material. Certification schemes are emerging as a mitigation lever but add cost and lead time. There is an expectation that OEMs will increasingly demand traceable supply chains, shifting risk back to miners and refiners. Monitoring should focus on certification uptake rates, local permitting tensions, and refiners' capacity expansion plans.

What to watch
  • certification program adoptions
  • local permitting disputes at mine sites
Action

Map supply chains to identify single-source exposures and begin sourcing recycled or certified material as guardrails.
