Capacity signals shift as DERs and demand-side resources enter markets

Expectations are building that capacity market dynamics will change materially as distributed energy resources and aggregated demand-side assets increase participation, altering price formation and resource adequacy models.

Market administrators are experimenting with new product definitions and measurement frameworks to include aggregated behind-the-meter assets. This shift signals potential compression of scarcity pricing during peak periods and could lower average capacity prices over time. Scenario watch includes increased complexity in accreditation and performance verification, which could temporarily reduce liquidity. Investors may respond with new business models that bundle DERs and storage to capture capacity revenues. There is also an expectation that traditional large-scale thermal assets will face stiffer competition for capacity payments. What to monitor are rulemaking dockets and pilot program outcomes regarding aggregation standards.

What to watch
  • market operator rule changes for aggregation
  • pilot program performance and accreditation updates
Action

Assess aggregation pathways and measurement strategies to position DER portfolios for capacity market participation.
