Spot price volatility and ancillary market stress scenarios persist

Signals of elevated spot-price volatility and tighter ancillary service spreads are creating expectations of short-term procurement stress for large electricity buyers and independent power producers.

Day-ahead and intra-day price spreads in multiple regions are showing behavior consistent with thin reserve conditions. Traders and risk desks are reflecting this in hedging posture adjustments and increased use of short-dated instruments. Ancillary service prices are sensitive to reserve activation probability; market participants are modeling increased scarcity pricing as a plausible near-term scenario. This can alter dispatch economics for peakers and storage. Capacity market signals and potential emergency procurement announcements remain scenario variables that could reprice forward curves. Buyers with inflexible loads may face elevated short-term charges. Renewable curtailment risk under congestion can alter revenue certainty for project owners, changing merchant dark-period expectations. Some developers are modeling optionality through firming contracts and capacity products. Liquidity in some regional markets is a constraint; thinner markets see sharper price moves for small incremental demand. Trading desks are increasingly valuing execution certainty and counterparty credit posture. These are expectation-driven signals rather than deterministic forecasts, intended to highlight where market participants may need to adjust hedging and dispatch strategies.

What to watch
  • Expect intra-day price spikes and announcements of emergency reserve procurements.
  • Monitor capacity market notices that could alter forward curve pricing.
Action

Review short-term hedging positions and re-evaluate exposure to ancillary service price spikes.
