Short-duration ancillary services: price formation and procurement risk

Signals show volatility in short-duration ancillary service markets as demand for 1–15 minute responses grows, creating procurement and pricing uncertainty for operators and asset owners.

Market operators are experimenting with products that compensate sub-minute and minute-scale responses, creating a transition period where price signals may be noisy. These are developments to monitor rather than conclusive market shifts.

Asset owners with fast-response batteries and inverter fleets face opportunity and risk as market designs and settlement windows evolve. Revenue models could fluctuate materially during this period of design refinement.

Procurement teams may need to re-tool bid strategies and telemetry to capture value while avoiding exposure to market volatility. This requires more granular operational modelling and contractual sophistication.

There is a scenario where insufficient procurement transparency could deter new entrants, compressing supply and heightening price spikes during stress events. Policymakers could respond with interim transparency measures.

Hedging and synthetic contracts for short-duration services are nascent but likely to emerge as market participants seek to stabilize revenue. These instruments will depend on reliable metrics and standardization.

What to watch are rule changes in settlement timing, telemetry requirements, and pilot results from market operators. These will determine whether current price volatility resolves into stable markets or remains a structural risk.

What to watch
  • Settlement window rule changes for 1–15 minute products
  • Telemetry and performance metric updates from market operators
Action

Revisit short-duration revenue models and prepare telemetry upgrades to capture new market opportunities.
