Hybrid DER procurement becoming standard for resilience and sustainability

Commercial signals indicate data centers are increasingly structuring power procurement around hybrid on-site DER portfolios, blending solar, storage, and gas-fired generation to balance resilience and sustainability goals.

Data centers face competing priorities: near-100% availability, shrinking carbon footprints, and cost controls. Hybrid DER portfolios allow operators to hedge against grid outages while meeting renewable targets. The scenario implies more complex interconnection agreements, standby fuel arrangements, and dispatch coordination with local utilities. Energy management systems and microgrid controllers are becoming standard to orchestrate these assets under normal and emergency conditions. There are notable regulatory and tariff implications as utilities adapt to multi-point resources impacting system peaks. This evolution also creates development opportunities for EPCs that can deliver integrated solutions. Track procurement trends and interconnection filings to see how common DER portfolios are becoming across regions.

What to watch
  • interconnection filings and tariff adaptations affecting DER operations
  • procurement notices for integrated DER/EPC solutions
Action

Assess DER integration strategies and update interconnection plans to reflect hybrid portfolio operation modes.
