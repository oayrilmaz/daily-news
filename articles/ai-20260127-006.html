Concentrated processing remains a structural risk for battery and cable supply chains

Expectations point to ongoing concentration in refining and processing of key minerals, creating scenario risk for downstream battery and conductor availability.

Graphite, copper refining and certain rare earth processing remain geographically concentrated, which raises supply fragility on geopolitical or trade-policy shocks. Battery manufacturers and cable producers are evaluating multi-sourcing strategies and inventory buffers to hedge this exposure. The capital intensity and environmental permitting for new refining capacity make rapid diversification difficult. This concentration also increases the negotiating leverage of processing hubs and traders. Scenario planning now routinely includes trade-disruption and export-control contingencies. What to monitor are policy notices, trade measures and major investment announcements in processing capacity. The likely outcome absent rapid capacity ramps is elevated price volatility and supply prioritization flows to high-value projects.

What to watch
  • Policy changes affecting mineral exports or refining
  • Announcements of new refining capacity or major investments
Action

Evaluate multi-sourcing, inventory buffers, and long-term offtake agreements for critical mineral-dependent projects.
