Processing capacity expansions for battery minerals showing uneven progress

Signals from project pipelines suggest processing capacity expansions for cobalt and nickel are proceeding unevenly, with funding and permitting variability creating strategic supply risk for battery supply chains.

Several mineral producers are advancing downstream processing projects to capture more value and reduce export dependency. Projects that secure financing and expedited permitting are moving faster than others, creating regional asymmetry in processing capacity. Downstream capacity constraints are increasing interest in recycling and alternative chemistries. OEMs and battery manufacturers are reportedly diversifying feedstock contracts to hedge against single-source disruptions. Geopolitical and local permitting frictions remain a risk vector for timely project startups. Scenario watch: if a major processing hub experiences delays, buyers may accelerate contracts with alternative suppliers, potentially raising raw-material prices. What to monitor: financing closings, permitting milestones, and announcements about refinery commissioning plans.

What to watch
  • Refinery financing closures and commissioning schedules
  • Permitting outcomes for major processing projects
  • OEM announcements on feedstock diversification
Action

Stress-test supply chains for processing delays and increase engagement on recycling and alternative chemistries.
