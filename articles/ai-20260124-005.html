Ancillary service dynamics shift as inverter fleets alter dispatch

Market structures for ancillary services are under pressure as inverter-based resources change the timing and magnitude of reserve needs, creating potential pricing and product-definition frictions.

Inverter-dominated systems provide fast-ramping capabilities but reduce rotating inertia, shifting the profile of frequency and voltage support needs. Market operators are reassessing products for fast frequency response, synthetic inertia, and ramping capacity. Pricing volatility may increase in short-duration products as resources with ultra-fast response capture new revenue streams. There is a scenario risk where legacy market rules insufficiently value synthetic response, leaving operators short during high-penetration events. Aggregators and storage portfolios are positioning to arbitrage new products, but participation rules and telemetry requirements are evolving. Capacity accreditation for hybrid assets remains an open question in many regions. The expectation is for incremental rule changes rather than wholesale redesigns, but interim mismatches could produce transient price dislocations.

What to watch
  • ISO/RTO consultations on fast frequency response products
  • New accreditation rules for hybrid storage-plus-generation assets
Action

Model potential revenue shifts from fast-response products and engage with market-design consultations to influence product definitions.
