Inverter-dominated grids amplify synthetic inertia demand

Signals from network operators suggest growing emphasis on grid-forming inverters and synthetic inertia as inertia shortfalls emerge with high renewable penetration.

High inverter-based resource penetration reduces conventional rotating inertia. Operators are testing synthetic inertia and grid-forming controls to stabilize frequency and fault response. Expect pilot schemes linking storage and wind inverter capabilities to ancillary service markets. Scenario risk centers on interoperability and protection coordination during fault events. What to monitor are new procurement requests for grid-forming functionality and interconnection requirement updates. Deployments will likely be incremental, with early adopters focused on constrained zones and large offshore clusters.

What to watch
  • interconnection requirement changes that mandate grid-forming capabilities
  • announcements of ancillary market products for synthetic inertia
Action

Prepare technical specifications and testing plans for grid-forming inverter pilots.
