Offshore wind growth highlights transmission pinch points

Signals from planning and grid studies suggest that rapid offshore wind additions are creating localized transmission bottlenecks and are likely to increase calls for coordinated corridor and HVDC planning.

Intermittent offshore generation clusters are putting pressure on coastal grid nodes and the radial radial collection strategies currently in place.

Developers and TSOs appear to be exploring pooled transmission approaches to reduce duplication and manage system stability.

HVDC link considerations are rising in studies as a means to move large blocks of power to demand centers while managing stability across synchronous areas.

Permitting, seabed rights and coordinated routing are likely to emerge as major scheduling variables for integrated transmission solutions.

EPC timelines will be sensitive to how corridor decisions evolve and whether standardized platform and cable procurement can be scaled.

Scenario watch: If corridor planning lags project development, expect increased curtailment risk and contractual disputes between developers and network owners.

What to monitor: national transmission planning updates, corridor allocation signals, and HVDC development pilots.

What to watch
  • national transmission corridor decisions
  • HVDC pilot announcements and route selections
Action

Engage early in corridor planning consultations and evaluate HVDC options for connecting large offshore clusters to load centers.
