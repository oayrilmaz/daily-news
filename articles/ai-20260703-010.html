Curtailment and battery revenue stacking scenarios

Signals show growing instances of renewable curtailment in congested regions, which is increasing the strategic value of batteries and complex revenue-stacking models for projects looking to monetize multiple market products.

Curtailment signals are most pronounced where transmission build-out lags behind project interconnections. Scenario watch: batteries that can arbitrage energy, provide frequency services, and capture congestion rents may realize layered value but require sophisticated market participation capabilities. Project developers are modeling alternative revenue streams to justify storage sizing and placement. Merchant risk profiles change materially based on market settlement rules and the granularity of nodal pricing. What to monitor: curtailment rates, ancillary-service price behavior, and any rule changes around aggregated battery participation. Operationally, asset schedulers will need advanced optimization tools to switch modes and maximize stacked revenues.

What to watch
  • curtailment statistics in constrained regions
  • rule changes on aggregated storage market participation
Action

Model multiple revenue streams for storage projects and ensure control systems can switch between market products rapidly.
