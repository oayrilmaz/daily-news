Grid-code revisions target inverter behavior

Signals indicate regulators are moving toward stricter grid-code requirements for inverter-based resources, prompting retrofit and compliance scenarios for existing fleets.

Draft revisions being discussed focus on fault-ride-through, reactive-current support, and standardized communications. The intelligence framing is that enforcement timelines could create a wave of retrofits for in-field inverters and turbines. OEMs and field service providers may face compressed schedules to deliver firmware and hardware changes. Grid operators are likely to publish compliance test procedures that require reproducible test logs. Procurement and contracting for retrofit work could compete with greenfield deployments for limited installation crews. Compliance uncertainty may affect interconnection timelines and project financing decisions. The scenario includes possible carve-outs for small-scale distributed assets to ease administrative load.

What to watch
  • publication of final grid-code text and compliance schedules
  • test-procedure announcements and auditing requirements
Action

Assess inverter fleet compliance against draft requirements and plan retrofit capacity with vendors and EPC partners.
