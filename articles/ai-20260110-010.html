Protection relay cybersecurity surfaces as a maintenance priority

Signals suggest utilities are elevating cybersecurity considerations for protection relays and substation control networks as firmware updates and third-party integrations increase attack surface.

Many substations are integrating modern relays with IP-based communications and remote update capabilities, which streamline operations but expand cyber exposure. Operators are flagging the need for stricter patch management, code signing, and vendor accountability processes. Field teams are also concerned about the operational risk of applying patches without validated rollback plans. There is growing interest in hardened gateway appliances that isolate protection devices from broader IP networks while preserving telemetry needs. Training for relay firmware management and incident response is being prioritized alongside technical controls. Regulatory guidance around operational technology (OT) cyber hygiene is expected to tighten in some jurisdictions, increasing compliance burden.

What to watch
  • Vendor commitments on code signing, rollback, and documented update processes
  • Regulatory or audit guidance updating OT cyber hygiene expectations
Action

Require signed firmware deliveries, documented rollback procedures, and conduct tabletop exercises that include relay update failure scenarios.
