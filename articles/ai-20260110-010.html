AI for predictive maintenance: adoption and cyber risk trade-offs

Signals of accelerated AI adoption for asset health forecasting suggest scenarios where predictive maintenance gains operational efficiency but also raises cyber and model-risk considerations for critical infrastructure.

Early-adopter signals indicate predictive models are successfully identifying emergent failure modes ahead of traditional inspection cycles. This promises reduced unplanned outages and optimized spare parts logistics. However, increased model reliance creates new operational dependencies and potential single points of failure if models are not robust or if data integrity is compromised. Cyber-risk signals grow as predictive systems often require expanded telemetry and remote-access pathways, increasing the attack surface. Governance frameworks that combine model validation, explainability checks, and segmented access controls will be critical in managing these trade-offs. The scenario of improved reliability paired with elevated cyber and model risks means operators must invest in both analytics and hardened operational security.

What to watch
  • Telemetry expansion projects and remote-access configuration changes
  • Model performance drift indicators and key-data-source integrity checks
Action

Integrate model governance and cybersecurity controls into predictive-maintenance rollouts and run tabletop exercises on model-failure scenarios.
