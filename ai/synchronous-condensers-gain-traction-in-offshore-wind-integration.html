Synchronous Condensers Gain Traction in Offshore Wind Integration

Medium confidence — Emerging project data and pilot installations indicate growing adoption, though widespread deployment is nascent.

TL;DR

Synchronous condensers are increasingly integrated into offshore wind projects to provide grid support services and enhance system stability.

Why it matters

  • Addresses inertia and reactive power needs in offshore grids.
  • Facilitates reliable connection of large-scale wind farms.
  • Supports compliance with stringent grid codes.

Key points

  • Offshore synchronous condensers can be platform-mounted or floating.
  • They provide voltage support and fault current contribution.
  • Integration challenges include marine environment durability.
  • Costs are higher compared to onshore solutions but justified by grid benefits.
  • Projects in Europe are leading adoption trends.
  • Potential to reduce curtailment of offshore wind output.
  • Complementary to HVDC offshore transmission systems.
