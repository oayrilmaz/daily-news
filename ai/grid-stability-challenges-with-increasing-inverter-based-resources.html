Grid Stability Challenges with Increasing Inverter-Based Resources

High confidence — Operational data and studies consistently identify stability issues linked to IBRs.

TL;DR

High penetration of inverter-based resources (IBRs) introduces grid stability challenges requiring new control and protection strategies.

Why it matters

  • Traditional grid inertia is reduced, affecting frequency response.
  • Protection schemes may malfunction due to altered fault currents.
  • Grid operators must adapt to maintain reliability.

Key points

  • IBRs include solar PV, wind turbines, and battery storage systems.
  • Reduced system inertia leads to faster frequency deviations.
  • Fault current contributions from IBRs differ from synchronous machines.
  • Advanced inverter controls can provide synthetic inertia and grid support.
  • Grid codes are evolving to mandate enhanced inverter functionalities.
  • System operators invest in enhanced monitoring and control tools.
  • Hybrid solutions combining synchronous condensers and IBRs are under evaluation.

Watchlist (next 24–48 hours)

  • Deployment of grid-forming inverter technologies.
  • Updates to protection and control standards.
  • Pilot projects integrating synthetic inertia solutions.

Known unknowns

  • Scalability of synthetic inertia to large grid segments.
  • Long-term operational impacts on grid equipment.
  • Cost implications of required grid upgrades.
