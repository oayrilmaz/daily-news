Synchronous Condensers Gain Traction as Grid Inertia Solutions

High confidence — Multiple utility-scale installations and pilot projects confirm effectiveness.

TL;DR

Synchronous condensers are increasingly deployed to provide inertia and voltage support in grids with high renewable penetration.

Why it matters

  • Compensates for reduced inertia from inverter-based resources.
  • Improves voltage stability and fault ride-through capabilities.
  • Supports grid reliability during high renewable output periods.

Key points

  • Synchronous condensers provide rotating inertia without power generation.
  • Deployment rising in regions with high wind and solar capacity.
  • Can be retrofitted from decommissioned generators or purpose-built.
  • Complementary to battery storage and advanced inverter controls.
  • Costs and footprint vary depending on design and capacity.
  • Grid codes increasingly require inertia services for system stability.

Watchlist (next 24–48 hours)

  • Technological improvements reducing size and cost of synchronous condensers.
  • Integration with grid-forming inverter strategies.
  • Regulatory incentives for inertia and voltage support services.

Known unknowns

  • Optimal sizing and placement within complex grid topologies.
  • Long-term operational costs compared to alternative solutions.
  • Interaction effects with emerging grid technologies.
