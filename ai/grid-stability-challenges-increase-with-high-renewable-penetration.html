Grid Stability Challenges Increase with High Renewable Penetration

TL;DR

Utilities report growing grid stability challenges as inverter-based renewable generation replaces conventional synchronous machines.

Why it matters

  • Maintaining grid stability is essential for reliable power delivery.
  • Inverter-based resources lack inherent inertia.
  • New technologies and operational practices are required.

Key points

  • Frequency deviations have increased in grids with >50% renewables.
  • Voltage regulation becomes more complex with distributed generation.

  • Real-time grid monitoring and control systems are critical.
  • Demand response programs help balance variability.
  • Coordination between transmission and distribution operators is improving.
