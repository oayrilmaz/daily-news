Grid Stability Challenges Increase with High Renewable Penetration

High confidence — Widely observed operational data and grid operator reports confirm challenges.

TL;DR

As renewable energy sources constitute a larger share of generation, maintaining grid stability requires enhanced frequency and voltage control solutions.

Why it matters

  • Ensures continuous power supply and prevents blackouts.
  • Necessitates investment in advanced grid control technologies.
  • Influences regulatory frameworks and market designs.

Key points

  • Reduced system inertia from inverter-based resources affects frequency response.
  • Voltage fluctuations increase with distributed generation variability.
  • Grid-forming inverters and synchronous condensers are key solutions.
  • Energy storage systems provide fast frequency regulation.
  • Enhanced forecasting improves operational planning.
  • Grid codes are evolving to address stability requirements.
  • Cross-sector coordination is critical for resilience.
