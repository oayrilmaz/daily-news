Grid Stability Challenges Increase with High Renewable Penetration

High confidence — Empirical data from grids with >50% renewables confirm increased frequency variability.

TL;DR

High renewable energy penetration is creating new grid stability challenges related to inertia and frequency control.

Why it matters

  • Maintaining frequency stability is critical to avoid blackouts.
  • Traditional inertia sources decline as fossil plants retire.
  • Necessitates deployment of new technologies and grid services.

Key points

  • Reduced system inertia leads to faster frequency deviations.
  • Grid operators deploy synthetic inertia via inverter controls.
  • Synchronous condensers and battery storage provide ancillary services.
  • Grid codes are evolving to require new stability capabilities.
  • Advanced forecasting and demand response improve operational flexibility.
  • Research continues on grid-forming inverter technologies.
  • Coordination among balancing authorities becomes more complex.
