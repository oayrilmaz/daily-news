Grid Stability Challenges Increase with High Renewable Penetration

High confidence — Grid operator reports and academic studies document frequency and voltage stability issues.

TL;DR

Higher shares of inverter-based renewable generation are creating operational challenges for grid stability, requiring new solutions.

Why it matters

  • Risk of frequency deviations and voltage fluctuations.
  • Necessitates investment in grid-supportive technologies.
  • Impacts reliability and security of electricity supply.

Key points

  • Inverter-based resources lack inherent inertia present in synchronous machines.
  • Grid codes are evolving to require synthetic inertia and grid-forming capabilities.
  • Energy storage and synchronous condensers are deployed to mitigate stability issues.
  • Advanced grid controls and forecasting improve operational flexibility.
  • Frequency response services markets are emerging.
  • Coordination between transmission and distribution operators is critical.
  • Research continues on enhancing inverter functionalities.

Watchlist (next 24–48 hours)

  • Grid code updates and compliance timelines.
  • Deployment rates of grid-supportive technologies.
  • Operational data on high renewable grids.

Known unknowns

  • Limits of inverter-based generation penetration without stability compromises.
  • Cost-effectiveness of various mitigation technologies.
  • Impact of extreme weather on grid stability with high renewables.
