Grid Stability Challenges Increase with High Renewable Penetration

High confidence — Extensive operational data and grid studies support this trend.

TL;DR

High levels of inverter-based renewable generation are introducing new grid stability challenges requiring advanced control solutions.

Why it matters

  • Traditional grid inertia is reduced, increasing frequency volatility.
  • Voltage regulation becomes more complex with distributed generation.
  • Necessitates investment in grid-forming inverters and ancillary services.

Key points

  • Grid operators report increased frequency deviations.
  • Synchronous condensers and battery storage are deployed to mitigate issues.
  • Advanced inverter functions are being standardized.
  • Dynamic grid modeling tools are essential for planning.
  • Regulatory frameworks are evolving to address new stability needs.
  • Coordination between distributed resources and grid operators is improving.
  • Research into synthetic inertia and fast frequency response is ongoing.
