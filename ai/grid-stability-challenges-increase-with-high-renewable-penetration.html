Grid Stability Challenges Increase with High Renewable Penetration

High confidence — Grid operator reports and academic studies consistently highlight these stability issues.

TL;DR

High shares of inverter-based renewable generation reduce system inertia, complicating frequency and voltage control on power grids.

Why it matters

  • Necessitates new grid management and ancillary services.
  • May require investment in synthetic inertia technologies.
  • Impacts reliability and security of electricity supply.

Key points

  • Traditional synchronous generators provide natural inertia; renewables generally do not.
  • Frequency response times lengthen as inertia declines.
  • Voltage regulation becomes more complex with variable generation.
  • Grid codes are evolving to mandate inverter capabilities.
  • Energy storage and synchronous condensers are key mitigation tools.
  • Advanced forecasting improves grid operator situational awareness.
  • Cross-border interconnections help balance variability.

  • Regulatory updates on grid code requirements.
  • Research on inverter control strategies.

Known unknowns
