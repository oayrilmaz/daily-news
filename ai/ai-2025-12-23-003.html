TL;DR

Policy moves, export controls, and new refining capacity announcements can rapidly swing NdFeB magnet availability. Procurement teams should prepare for volatility and dual-sourcing pressure.

Why it matters

  • Magnets are critical inputs for wind, EVs, industrial motors, robotics, and many grid-adjacent supply chains.
  • Supply shocks can propagate into lead-time extensions and price spikes that disrupt project delivery.
  • Dual-sourcing and qualification timelines can be longer than typical procurement windows.

Key points

  • Policy sensitivity: export rules and licensing can change quickly and impact downstream buyers.
  • Processing concentration: refining and separation capacity constraints can drive volatility.
  • Qualification drag: alternate supplier qualification can take months due to performance and reliability testing.
  • Contract strategy: index-linked pricing and allocation clauses may matter more in tight markets.
  • Inventory posture: safety stock levels can reduce schedule risk but increase carrying cost.
