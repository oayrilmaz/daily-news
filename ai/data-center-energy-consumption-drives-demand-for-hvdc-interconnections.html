The surge in data center capacity is accelerating the deployment of high-voltage direct current (HVDC) links to meet localized power demands efficiently.

Why it matters

  • HVDC enables efficient long-distance power transmission with lower losses.
  • Supports reliability for energy-intensive data centers.
  • Facilitates integration of renewable energy sources into data center supply.

Key points

  • HVDC links reduce transmission losses compared to AC lines.
  • Projects are concentrated near major data center hubs in the US and Asia.
  • HVDC supports asynchronous grid interconnections.
  • Enables direct renewable power sourcing for data centers.
  • Capital costs are high but offset by operational efficiencies.
  • Grid operators are adapting planning to accommodate HVDC.
