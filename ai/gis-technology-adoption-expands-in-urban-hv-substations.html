GIS Technology Adoption Expands in Urban HV Substations

High confidence — Industry reports confirm rising GIS installations in metropolitan grids.

TL;DR

Gas-Insulated Switchgear (GIS) is increasingly favored for high voltage substations in dense urban areas due to its compact footprint and reliability.

Why it matters

  • Enables efficient use of limited urban space.
  • Reduces maintenance needs and improves safety.
  • Supports grid modernization and capacity upgrades.

Key points

  • GIS uses SF6 gas for insulation, enabling compact design.
  • Installation costs are higher than air-insulated switchgear but offset by space savings.
  • GIS is preferred in environments with space or environmental constraints.
  • Technological advances aim to reduce SF6 emissions.
  • GIS offers higher reliability and longer service life.
  • Integration with digital monitoring systems is increasing.
  • Urban grid expansion projects prioritize GIS for new substations.
