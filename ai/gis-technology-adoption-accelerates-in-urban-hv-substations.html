GIS Technology Adoption Accelerates in Urban HV Substations

TL;DR

Gas-insulated switchgear (GIS) is increasingly favored for high-voltage substations in urban areas due to space constraints and reliability.

Why it matters

  • GIS reduces substation footprint, enabling deployment in space-limited areas.
  • Improves system reliability and reduces maintenance requirements.
  • Supports urban grid modernization and resilience efforts.

Key points

  • GIS uses SF6 gas for insulation, enabling compact design.
  • Modular GIS designs facilitate faster installation and upgrades.
  • SF6 alternatives are under development to address environmental concerns.
  • GIS offers higher reliability under harsh environmental conditions.
  • Cost premium over air-insulated switchgear is decreasing.
  • Urban infrastructure projects increasingly specify GIS.
  • Integration with digital monitoring enhances asset management.
