TL;DR

Gas-insulated switchgear (GIS) is increasingly adopted in urban high-voltage substations to optimize space and improve reliability.

Why it matters

  • GIS reduces substation footprint, critical in dense urban areas.
  • Improves system reliability with sealed, maintenance-light equipment.
  • Supports integration of distributed energy resources in cities.

Key points

  • GIS uses SF6 gas for insulation, raising environmental considerations.
  • Modular designs facilitate faster installation and upgrades.
  • Higher upfront costs offset by long-term operational savings.
  • Enhanced safety features reduce outage risks.
  • Growing demand aligned with urban grid modernization efforts.
