Grid Stability Challenges Rise with Increasing Distributed Energy Resources

High confidence — Grid operators and studies consistently report increased variability and complexity due to DER integration.

TL;DR

The proliferation of distributed energy resources (DERs) introduces new challenges for maintaining grid stability and requires advanced control strategies.

Why it matters

  • DERs can cause voltage fluctuations and frequency instability.
  • Grid operators must adapt protection and control schemes.
  • Investment in advanced grid technologies is necessary for reliability.

Key points

  • High DER penetration reduces system inertia.
  • Voltage regulation becomes more complex with bidirectional flows.
  • Advanced inverter functionalities help mitigate stability issues.
  • Energy storage and synchronous condensers are used for support.
  • Real-time grid monitoring and control systems are essential.
  • Regulatory frameworks are evolving to manage DER impacts.
  • Coordination between distribution and transmission operators is increasing.
