Synchronous Condensers as a Solution for Inertia Deficit

High confidence — Multiple grid operators have successfully commissioned synchronous condensers recently.

TL;DR

Synchronous condensers are increasingly deployed to provide inertia and reactive power support in grids with high renewable penetration.

Why it matters

  • Maintains grid frequency stability amid declining synchronous generation.
  • Improves voltage regulation and fault current levels.
  • Enables higher renewable energy integration without compromising reliability.

Key points

  • Synchronous condensers provide rotating inertia without active power generation.
  • They support grid voltage through reactive power injection.
  • Installation costs are significant but justified by grid stability benefits.
  • Retrofit of existing generators as synchronous condensers is an option.
  • Deployment is concentrated in regions with rapid coal and gas plant retirements.
  • Complementary to battery energy storage systems for grid services.
  • Operational experience is growing, informing best practices.

Watchlist (next 24–48 hours)

  • New projects in high-renewable regions like California and Germany.
  • Technological improvements reducing synchronous condenser costs.
  • Policy incentives supporting inertia provision technologies.

Known unknowns

  • Long-term maintenance costs and lifecycle of synchronous condensers.
  • Optimal sizing and placement within transmission networks.
  • Integration with emerging grid-forming inverter technologies.
