TL;DR

High Voltage Direct Current (HVDC) technology is increasingly favored for new cross-border interconnections to enhance capacity and reduce losses.

Why it matters

  • Enables efficient long-distance power transfer with lower losses.
  • Facilitates integration of diverse energy markets and renewables.
  • Strengthens grid resilience through interconnections.

Key points

  • HVDC lines can transmit power over thousands of kilometers with minimal losses.
  • Projects are underway in Europe, Asia, and North America.
  • Voltage source converter (VSC) technology improves controllability.
  • Costs are high but offset by operational efficiencies.
  • Enables asynchronous grid connections.
  • Supports offshore wind integration.
  • Regulatory frameworks are evolving to accommodate HVDC.
