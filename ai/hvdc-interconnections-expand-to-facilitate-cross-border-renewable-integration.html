HVDC Interconnections Expand to Facilitate Cross-Border Renewable Integration

High confidence — Several HVDC projects are in advanced stages or recently commissioned in Europe and Asia.

TL;DR

High Voltage Direct Current (HVDC) links are increasingly deployed to connect national grids and optimize renewable energy flows across borders.

Why it matters

  • Enables efficient long-distance power transmission with lower losses.
  • Supports balancing of variable renewable generation across regions.
  • Enhances energy security through diversified supply sources.

Key points

  • HVDC technology reduces transmission losses compared to AC lines over long distances.
  • Projects like North Sea Link and China’s cross-provincial HVDC corridors are benchmarks.
  • Converter station costs remain a significant portion of project budgets.
  • Multi-terminal HVDC systems are emerging but face technical challenges.
  • Integration with existing AC grids requires advanced control systems.
  • Policy frameworks are evolving to support cross-border HVDC development.

Watchlist (next 24–48 hours)

  • Progress on multi-terminal HVDC pilot projects.
  • Regulatory harmonization for cross-border power trading.
  • Innovations in HVDC converter technologies.

Known unknowns

  • Scalability of multi-terminal HVDC networks.
  • Long-term reliability of HVDC components under high renewable penetration.
  • Impact of geopolitical factors on cross-border HVDC projects.
