Energy Storage Integration Critical for HVDC System Flexibility

Medium confidence — Emerging projects demonstrate benefits, but large-scale deployment data remain limited.

TL;DR

Coupling energy storage with HVDC systems enhances grid flexibility, enabling better management of variable renewable generation and grid contingencies.

Why it matters

  • Improves ramping capability and frequency regulation of HVDC links.
  • Increases resilience against grid disturbances and outages.
  • Facilitates higher renewable energy penetration and grid stability.

Key points

  • Battery energy storage systems (BESS) provide fast response ancillary services.
  • Storage can smooth power flow and manage congestion on HVDC corridors.
  • Hybrid HVDC-storage projects are under development globally.
  • Integration challenges include control coordination and cost optimization.
  • Storage extends operational flexibility during maintenance or faults.
  • Regulatory frameworks are evolving to recognize storage’s grid value.
  • Economic viability depends on multiple revenue streams.
