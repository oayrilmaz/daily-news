HVDC Interconnection Projects Accelerate to Support Cross-Border Power Trade

High confidence — Multiple confirmed projects and government-backed initiatives support HVDC expansion.

TL;DR

High Voltage Direct Current (HVDC) links are expanding globally to enable efficient long-distance power transmission and cross-border energy exchange.

Why it matters

  • Facilitates integration of diverse renewable resources across regions.
  • Reduces transmission losses over long distances.
  • Enhances grid reliability and energy market flexibility.

Key points

  • HVDC technology allows asynchronous grid interconnection.
  • Projects in Europe, Asia, and North America are in advanced stages.
  • Voltage levels commonly range from ±320 kV to ±800 kV.
  • Modular multilevel converters (MMC) improve efficiency and control.
  • Permitting and environmental assessments remain key challenges.
  • Cost per km remains high but justified by long-term benefits.
  • Integration with offshore wind farms is a major driver.

  • Policy support for cross-border transmission infrastructure.
  • Advances in converter station technology.

Known unknowns

  • Grid impact of multiple HVDC links operating concurrently.
  • Standardization of HVDC control protocols.
