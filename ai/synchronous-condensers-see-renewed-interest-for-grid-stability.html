Synchronous Condensers See Renewed Interest for Grid Stability

Medium confidence — Emerging deployments and pilot projects indicate growing adoption, but scale remains limited.

TL;DR

Utilities are increasingly deploying synchronous condensers to provide inertia and reactive power support amid growing renewable penetration.

Why it matters

  • Helps maintain voltage stability and frequency control.
  • Mitigates reduced inertia due to inverter-based resources.
  • Supports integration of variable renewable energy sources.

Key points

  • Synchronous condensers provide rotating inertia without generating power.
  • Several grid operators have initiated pilot installations.
  • They complement fast-acting inverter controls for grid support.
  • Retrofit of existing generators as synchronous condensers is being explored.
  • Costs and space requirements remain considerations.
  • Alternative technologies like grid-forming inverters are also advancing.
  • Regulatory recognition of inertia services is evolving.

Watchlist (next 24–48 hours)

  • Deployment rates of synchronous condensers in key markets.
  • Regulatory developments on inertia and reactive power compensation.
  • Advances in inverter technology as alternatives.

Known unknowns

  • Long-term cost-effectiveness compared to alternatives.
  • Optimal sizing and placement within grids.
  • Impact on overall system reliability metrics.
