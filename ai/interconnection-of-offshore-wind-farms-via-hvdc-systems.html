HVDC technology is increasingly utilized for connecting offshore wind farms to onshore grids, improving transmission efficiency and reliability.

  • Enables large-scale offshore wind development in distant locations.
  • Reduces transmission losses compared to AC alternatives.
  • Facilitates multi-terminal grid connections and power flow control.

Key points

  • Voltage source converter (VSC) HVDC is preferred for offshore applications.

  • Project costs are high but justified by long-term operational benefits.
  • Integration with onshore grid requires advanced control systems.
  • Environmental considerations influence route and installation methods.
  • Standardization efforts aim to reduce costs and improve interoperability.
  • Grid codes are evolving to accommodate HVDC-connected offshore assets.
