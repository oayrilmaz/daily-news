TL;DR

High-voltage direct current (HVDC) interconnections are increasingly utilized to link national grids and facilitate renewable energy trading across borders.

Why it matters

  • Enables efficient long-distance power transmission with lower losses.
  • Supports grid balancing by sharing renewable resources regionally.
  • Enhances energy security and market integration.

Key points

  • HVDC technology allows asynchronous grid interconnections.
  • Voltage source converters (VSC) improve controllability and black start capability.
  • Projects target linking offshore wind hubs to mainland grids.
  • Costs have decreased due to technological maturity and scale.
  • Regulatory cooperation is critical for cross-border HVDC projects.
  • Integration with AC grids requires complex converter stations.
  • Environmental impact assessments are integral to project approvals.
