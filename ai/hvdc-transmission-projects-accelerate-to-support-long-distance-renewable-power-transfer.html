HVDC Transmission Projects Accelerate to Support Long-Distance Renewable Power Transfer

High confidence — Numerous HVDC projects have reached advanced planning or construction phases globally.

TL;DR

High Voltage Direct Current (HVDC) lines are increasingly favored for transmitting large-scale renewable energy over long distances with reduced losses.

Why it matters

  • Enables efficient integration of remote renewable resources.
  • Reduces transmission losses compared to AC lines over long distances.
  • Supports cross-border power trading and grid interconnections.

Key points

  • HVDC technology offers controllable power flow and stability benefits.
  • Modular Multilevel Converters (MMC) improve HVDC system efficiency.
  • Projects are underway in Europe, Asia, and North America.
  • Costs remain high but are offset by operational savings and capacity gains.
  • Integration with existing AC grids requires complex converter stations.
  • Environmental and permitting challenges can impact project timelines.
  • HVDC supports asynchronous grid interconnections.

Watchlist (next 24–48 hours)

  • Progress on major HVDC projects like North Sea Link and Asian interconnectors.
  • Advances in converter technology and cost reductions.
  • Regulatory frameworks facilitating cross-border HVDC lines.

Known unknowns

  • Long-term reliability of new HVDC converter technologies.
  • Impact of large HVDC deployments on AC grid dynamics.
  • Evolving standards for HVDC grid integration.
