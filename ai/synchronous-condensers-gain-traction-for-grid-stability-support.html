Synchronous Condensers Gain Traction for Grid Stability Support

High confidence — Operational data and grid operator reports confirm growing use of synchronous condensers.

TL;DR

Synchronous condensers are increasingly deployed to provide inertia and voltage support amid rising renewable penetration.

Why it matters

  • Compensates for reduced inertia from inverter-based renewables.
  • Enhances voltage regulation and fault current capability.
  • Supports grid reliability during high renewable generation periods.

Key points

  • Retrofitting of existing generators as synchronous condensers is common.
  • New installations focus on critical grid nodes with stability concerns.
  • Synchronous condensers provide reactive power support without active power generation.
  • Deployment costs are lower than building new synchronous generators.
  • Integration with grid control systems is essential for optimal performance.
  • Some regions mandate synchronous condensers in grid codes.
  • Research ongoing into hybrid solutions combining condensers with energy storage.

Watchlist (next 24–48 hours)

  • Grid code updates regarding inertia and voltage support.
  • New synchronous condenser project announcements.
  • Technological improvements in condenser design and control.

Known unknowns

  • Optimal sizing and placement strategies for condensers.
  • Long-term maintenance costs and operational challenges.
  • Interaction effects with large-scale battery storage systems.
