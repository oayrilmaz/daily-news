Grid Stability Concerns Rise with Increased Renewable Penetration

High confidence — Grid operators report increased frequency and magnitude of voltage and frequency deviations.

TL;DR

Higher shares of variable renewable energy are raising grid stability challenges, prompting investment in ancillary services and advanced controls.

Why it matters

  • Maintaining reliability is essential for energy security.
  • Ancillary services markets are evolving to address new needs.
  • Investment decisions must balance cost and grid resilience.

Key points

  • Inertia reduction due to retiring synchronous generators affects frequency stability.
  • Fast frequency response and synthetic inertia technologies are deployed.
  • Voltage regulation increasingly relies on power electronics and synchronous condensers.
  • Grid codes are updated to require new stability capabilities from renewables.
  • Energy storage systems play a growing role in ancillary services.
  • Coordination between transmission and distribution levels is critical.

  • Deployment of grid-forming inverters and synchronous condensers.
  • Market mechanisms incentivizing ancillary service provision.
