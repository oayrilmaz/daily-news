HVDC Transmission Gains Traction for Long-Distance Renewable Integration

High confidence — Multiple large-scale HVDC projects are operational or under construction globally.

TL;DR

High Voltage Direct Current (HVDC) technology is increasingly adopted to transmit renewable energy over long distances with reduced losses.

Why it matters

  • Enables efficient transfer of remote renewable resources to load centers.
  • Supports grid decarbonization by facilitating large-scale clean energy integration.
  • Reduces transmission losses compared to AC systems over long distances.

Key points

  • HVDC lines can transmit power over thousands of kilometers with minimal losses.
  • Technology supports asynchronous grid interconnections, enhancing stability.
  • Costs have decreased due to technological advancements and scale.
  • Projects in Europe, China, and North America demonstrate operational viability.
  • Integration with offshore wind farms is a key growth driver.
  • Challenges include converter station costs and complex grid integration.

  • Technological innovations in converter efficiency and modular designs.
  • Regulatory frameworks facilitating cross-border HVDC links.

Known unknowns

  • Market acceptance and tariff structures for HVDC-transmitted power.
