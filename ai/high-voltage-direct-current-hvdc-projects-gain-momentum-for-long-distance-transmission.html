High Voltage Direct Current (HVDC) Projects Gain Momentum for Long-Distance Transmission

High confidence — Multiple large-scale HVDC projects are under construction or planning stages worldwide.

TL;DR

HVDC technology adoption is accelerating globally to enable efficient long-distance and inter-regional power transmission.

Why it matters

  • HVDC reduces transmission losses over long distances compared to AC lines.
  • Facilitates integration of remote renewable energy resources.
  • Supports grid interconnections enhancing energy security and market efficiency.

Key points

  • New HVDC links planned between regions with complementary generation profiles.
  • Voltage Source Converter (VSC) technology improves controllability and grid support.
  • Modular Multilevel Converters (MMC) increase scalability and reliability.
  • Significant capital investment required but offset by operational savings.
  • HVDC enables asynchronous grid interconnections.
  • Growing interest in multi-terminal HVDC networks for complex grids.
  • Environmental impact generally lower than overhead AC lines.

Watchlist (next 24–48 hours)

  • Commissioning timelines of major HVDC projects in Asia and Europe.
  • Technological advances in converter station equipment.
  • Regulatory frameworks facilitating cross-border HVDC interconnections.

Known unknowns

  • Cost reductions potential through standardization and mass deployment.
  • Impact of HVDC on existing grid protection schemes.
