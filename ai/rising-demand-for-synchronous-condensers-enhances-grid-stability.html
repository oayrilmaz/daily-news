Rising Demand for Synchronous Condensers Enhances Grid Stability

High confidence — Multiple grid operators report increased installations and positive operational outcomes.

TL;DR

Synchronous condensers are increasingly deployed to support grid stability amid higher renewable penetration and phase-out of conventional generators.

Why it matters

  • Supports voltage regulation and inertia in renewable-heavy grids.
  • Reduces risk of frequency instability and blackouts.
  • Enables smoother integration of intermittent energy sources.

Key points

  • Synchronous condensers provide reactive power and inertia without fuel consumption.
  • Deployment accelerated in regions with high wind and solar capacity.
  • Equipment suppliers report growing order books for synchronous condensers.
  • Grid codes increasingly mandate inertia support services.
  • Installation lead times remain a challenge for rapid deployment.
  • Costs are competitive compared to battery-based alternatives.
  • Maintenance requirements are well understood from conventional generator heritage.

  • Market mechanisms for inertia and reactive power compensation.
