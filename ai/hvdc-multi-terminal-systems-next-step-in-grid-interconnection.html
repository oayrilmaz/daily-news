HVDC Multi-Terminal Systems: Next Step in Grid Interconnection

Medium confidence — Several demonstration projects exist, but large-scale commercial adoption is still emerging.

TL;DR

Multi-terminal HVDC systems are progressing from pilot projects to commercial deployment, enabling more flexible and resilient grid interconnections.

Why it matters

  • Facilitates integration of diverse renewable resources across regions.
  • Enhances grid reliability through multiple power flow paths.
  • Reduces transmission losses compared to AC alternatives.

Key points

  • Multi-terminal HVDC allows multiple injection and withdrawal points on the DC network.
  • Control complexity increases with the number of terminals.
  • Standardization of protection and control schemes is ongoing.
  • Cost remains higher than point-to-point HVDC but offers operational benefits.
  • Projects in Europe and Asia are leading in multi-terminal HVDC deployment.
  • Technology advances include voltage source converters and modular designs.
  • Grid operators are developing operational protocols for multi-terminal systems.

Watchlist (next 24–48 hours)

  • Commercial commissioning of multi-terminal HVDC projects.
  • Development of industry standards and best practices.
  • Economic analyses comparing multi-terminal HVDC to traditional solutions.

Known unknowns

  • Scalability limits of multi-terminal HVDC networks.
  • Long-term operational reliability and maintenance requirements.
  • Market mechanisms to support multi-terminal HVDC investments.
