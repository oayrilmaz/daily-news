Rare Earth Element Supply Risks Pose Challenges for HV Equipment Manufacturing

Medium confidence — Industry analyses highlight supply vulnerabilities, but diversification efforts are ongoing.

TL;DR

Dependence on rare earth elements for high voltage equipment components exposes manufacturers to supply risks amid geopolitical tensions.

Why it matters

  • Potential shortages could delay production of critical grid equipment.
  • Price volatility affects capital expenditure planning.
  • Encourages investment in material recycling and alternative technologies.

Key points

  • Rare earths are essential for permanent magnets in HVDC converters and synchronous condensers.
  • China remains the dominant supplier, controlling a large share of global production.
  • Efforts to develop alternative materials and recycling methods are underway.
  • Stockpiling and strategic reserves are being considered by some countries.
  • Supply chain transparency is limited, complicating risk assessment.
  • Manufacturers face pressure to reduce rare earth content without compromising performance.
  • Regulatory scrutiny on mining and export policies is increasing.
