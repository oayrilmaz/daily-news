HVDC Interconnections Expand to Facilitate Cross-Border Renewable Trade

High confidence — Confirmed commissioning of multiple HVDC projects and planned expansions in Europe and Asia.

TL;DR

High-voltage direct current (HVDC) links are increasingly deployed to enable efficient cross-border electricity exchange, supporting renewable energy integration.

Why it matters

  • Supports balancing of variable renewable generation across regions.
  • Reduces transmission losses over long distances compared to AC lines.
  • Enables energy market integration and security of supply.

Key points

  • HVDC technology allows asynchronous grid interconnection.
  • Projects like North Sea Link and China’s HVDC corridors are operational.
  • Costs remain high but justified by enhanced grid flexibility.
  • Converters and control systems are critical for stable operation.
  • HVDC facilitates offshore wind integration by connecting remote sites.
  • Policy frameworks increasingly support cross-border HVDC development.
  • Challenges include permitting, environmental impact, and grid compatibility.
