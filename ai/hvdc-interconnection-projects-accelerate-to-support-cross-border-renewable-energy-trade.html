HVDC Interconnection Projects Accelerate to Support Cross-Border Renewable Energy Trade

High confidence — Confirmed project pipelines and government support for regional grid integration initiatives.

TL;DR

High Voltage Direct Current (HVDC) interconnections are expanding globally to enable efficient cross-border transmission of renewable energy.

Why it matters

  • Facilitates large-scale renewable energy sharing between regions with complementary resources.
  • Reduces transmission losses over long distances compared to AC lines.
  • Enhances grid resilience and energy security through diversified supply.

Key points

  • HVDC technology allows asynchronous grid interconnection and precise power flow control.
  • Europe, Asia, and North America lead in HVDC project development for renewable integration.
  • Projects include subsea cables, overhead lines, and hybrid AC/DC corridors.
  • Government policies and climate targets are key drivers for HVDC investments.
  • Technical challenges include converter station costs and grid compatibility.
  • Emerging modular multi-level converter (MMC) technology improves efficiency and scalability.
  • HVDC links support grid stability by controlling power flows and mitigating congestion.
