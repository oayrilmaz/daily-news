GIS Technology Adoption Expands in Urban Power Networks

High confidence — Utility procurement data and project announcements confirm growing GIS installations in dense urban areas.

TL;DR

Gas Insulated Switchgear (GIS) is increasingly adopted in urban distribution and transmission networks due to space constraints and reliability benefits.

Why it matters

  • GIS reduces footprint compared to traditional air-insulated switchgear.
  • Enhances system reliability with sealed, maintenance-light design.
  • Supports urban grid modernization and resilience efforts.

Key points

  • GIS is preferred in space-limited substations and underground installations.
  • Advancements in SF6 alternatives are underway to reduce greenhouse gas impact.
  • GIS equipment costs remain higher than conventional switchgear but justified by space savings.
  • Utility investments in GIS are aligned with urban infrastructure upgrades.
  • Integration with digital monitoring systems improves operational visibility.
  • GIS facilitates higher voltage levels in constrained environments.
  • Lifecycle assessments show GIS benefits in maintenance and downtime reduction.

  • Regulatory pressures on SF6 emissions.

Known unknowns
