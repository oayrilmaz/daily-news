TL;DR

High penetration of inverter-based resources (IBRs) such as solar and wind is prompting grid operators to address stability and control challenges.

Why it matters

  • IBRs lack inherent inertia, affecting frequency response.
  • Voltage regulation becomes more complex with distributed generation.
  • Grid protection schemes require adaptation to new dynamics.

Key points

  • Grid operators are deploying advanced inverter controls and synthetic inertia.
  • Frequency and voltage ride-through requirements are being updated.
  • Energy storage systems are increasingly integrated to support stability.
  • Dynamic grid modeling tools are evolving to simulate IBR impacts.
  • Coordination between transmission and distribution operators is critical.
  • Standards for inverter performance are under revision globally.
  • Investment in grid automation and sensing technologies is rising.
