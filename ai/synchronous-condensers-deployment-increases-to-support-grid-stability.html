Synchronous Condensers Deployment Increases to Support Grid Stability

High confidence — Documented procurement and commissioning of synchronous condensers in multiple grids.

TL;DR

Utilities are accelerating synchronous condenser installations to provide inertia and reactive power support amid rising renewable penetration.

Why it matters

  • Compensates for inertia loss as conventional generators retire.
  • Enhances voltage stability and fault ride-through capability.
  • Facilitates higher renewable energy integration without compromising reliability.

Key points

  • Synchronous condensers provide grid inertia without generating active power.
  • Projects underway in the US, Europe, and Australia.
  • Retrofit of existing generators and new installations both common approaches.
  • Grid codes increasingly require inertia and reactive power support.
  • Synchronous condensers help manage frequency fluctuations.
  • Capex and Opex considerations influence deployment scale.
  • Integration with energy storage systems is being explored.

Watchlist (next 24–48 hours)

  • Technological innovations reducing synchronous condenser costs.

Known unknowns
