HVDC Transmission Projects Accelerate for Cross-Border Power Exchange

TL;DR

High Voltage Direct Current (HVDC) lines are increasingly favored for cross-border interconnections to enhance grid flexibility and renewable integration.

Why it matters

  • Facilitates efficient long-distance power transfer with lower losses.
  • Enables integration of variable renewable energy across regions.
  • Strengthens energy security through diversified supply sources.

Key points

  • HVDC lines reduce transmission losses compared to AC over long distances.
  • Projects link grids across countries with asynchronous systems.
  • Technology supports bidirectional power flow and grid balancing.
  • Costs remain high but are offset by operational savings and capacity benefits.
  • Modular Multilevel Converters (MMC) improve HVDC system reliability.
  • Environmental permitting remains a key challenge for new corridors.
  • Digital control systems enhance HVDC operational flexibility.
