HVDC Transmission Projects Expand to Facilitate Long-Distance Renewable Integration

TL;DR

High-voltage direct current (HVDC) lines are increasingly deployed to connect remote renewable generation sites with load centers efficiently.

Why it matters

  • Enables efficient bulk power transfer over long distances.
  • Reduces transmission losses compared to AC alternatives.
  • Supports grid decarbonization by linking renewables to demand hubs.

Key points

  • HVDC systems provide controllable power flow and stability benefits.
  • Used in submarine cables, cross-border interconnections, and remote wind farms.
  • Technical challenges include converter station costs and complexity.
  • Growing interest in multi-terminal HVDC networks.
  • Integration with existing AC grids requires advanced control schemes.

  • Policy support for cross-border grid interconnections.
