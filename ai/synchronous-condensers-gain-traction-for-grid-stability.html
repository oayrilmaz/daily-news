Synchronous Condensers Gain Traction for Grid Stability

High confidence — Multiple grid operators have publicly confirmed projects and operational data.

TL;DR

Deployment of synchronous condensers is increasing to support grid inertia and voltage stability amid rising renewable penetration.

Why it matters

  • Maintains system inertia critical for frequency control.
  • Enables higher renewable energy integration without compromising stability.
  • Delays costly grid infrastructure reinforcements.

Key points

  • Synchronous condensers provide reactive power and inertia without active power generation.
  • Installed capacity of synchronous condensers has doubled in key markets over the past two years.
  • They are favored over battery storage for inertia due to longer operational life.
  • Grid codes increasingly require inertia support for renewable-heavy grids.
  • Retrofit projects on existing generators are common to add synchronous condenser capability.
  • Cost per MW is competitive compared to other grid stability solutions.
  • Operational data shows improved voltage regulation and fault ride-through.

Watchlist (next 24–48 hours)

  • New synchronous condenser project announcements.
  • Grid code revisions mandating inertia requirements.
  • Technological improvements in synchronous condenser design.

Known unknowns

  • Long-term maintenance costs and reliability under high renewable scenarios.
  • Optimal sizing and placement within complex grids.
  • Interaction effects with emerging grid-forming inverters.
