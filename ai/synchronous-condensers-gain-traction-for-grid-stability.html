Synchronous Condensers Gain Traction for Grid Stability

High confidence — Multiple grid operators report successful synchronous condenser installations and performance data.

TL;DR

Synchronous condensers are increasingly deployed to provide inertia and voltage support amid rising renewable penetration.

Why it matters

  • Helps maintain grid frequency stability with reduced conventional generation.
  • Supports integration of variable renewable energy sources.
  • Provides reactive power support improving voltage profiles.

Key points

  • Synchronous condensers provide inertia without fuel consumption.
  • Projects underway in US, Europe, and Australia reflect growing adoption.
  • Cost-effective alternative to battery-based grid services in some cases.
  • Can be retrofitted from decommissioned generators or built new.
  • Operational flexibility supports grid resilience during disturbances.

  • Regulatory frameworks incentivizing inertia services.
  • Technological advancements reducing condenser costs.
