Synchronous Condensers Gain Traction for Grid Stability

High confidence — Confirmed installations and utility plans across multiple markets.

TL;DR

Utilities are increasingly deploying synchronous condensers to enhance voltage support and inertia amid rising renewable penetration.

Why it matters

  • Supports grid stability as conventional generators retire.
  • Enables higher renewable energy integration without compromising reliability.
  • Offers a non-emitting alternative to traditional synchronous generation.

Key points

  • Synchronous condensers provide reactive power and inertia.
  • Several large-scale projects commissioned in North America and Europe.
  • Cost-effective compared to battery storage for certain grid services.
  • Can be retrofitted from existing generator assets.
  • Improves fault current contribution and voltage regulation.
  • Deployment aligns with decarbonization goals.
  • Operational flexibility supports dynamic grid conditions.

  • Cost trends and technology improvements

Known unknowns
