Synchronous Condensers Gain Traction for Grid Inertia Support

High confidence — Confirmed installations and grid operator reports demonstrate growing adoption.

TL;DR

Synchronous condensers are increasingly deployed to provide inertia and voltage support in grids with high renewable penetration.

Why it matters

  • Helps maintain frequency stability as conventional generators retire.
  • Supports voltage regulation and fault current supply.
  • Enables higher renewable energy integration without compromising reliability.

Key points

  • Retrofitting existing generators as synchronous condensers is common.
  • New-build synchronous condensers deployed near critical grid nodes.
  • Complementary to battery storage and advanced inverter functions.
  • Cost and space considerations compared to alternative solutions.
  • Operational flexibility improvements with modern designs.
