Synchronous Condensers Gain Traction for Grid Inertia Support

High confidence — Multiple grid operators report successful integration and benefits of synchronous condensers.

TL;DR

Synchronous condensers are increasingly deployed to provide inertia and voltage support amid rising renewable penetration.

Why it matters

  • Helps maintain grid frequency stability with low inertia renewables.
  • Supports voltage regulation in weak grid areas.
  • Enables higher renewable energy integration without compromising reliability.

Key points

  • Synchronous condensers provide rotating inertia without active power output.
  • Deployment is growing in regions with high wind and solar penetration.
  • They can replace retiring synchronous generators to maintain grid stability.
  • Installation timelines are shorter compared to new transmission lines.
  • Costs are competitive relative to battery storage for inertia services.
  • Some utilities retrofit existing generators to operate as synchronous condensers.
  • Grid codes are evolving to recognize and incentivize inertia contributions.
