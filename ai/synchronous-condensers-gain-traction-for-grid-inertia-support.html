Synchronous condensers are increasingly deployed to provide grid inertia and voltage support amid rising renewable penetration.

Why it matters

  • Renewables reduce natural grid inertia, risking stability.
  • Synchronous condensers offer a proven solution without emissions.
  • Supporting voltage and frequency stability is critical for reliable operations.

Key points

  • Several utilities have commissioned new synchronous condenser projects in 2023-24.
  • Synchronous condensers provide reactive power and short-circuit strength.
  • They facilitate higher renewable energy integration by stabilizing the grid.
  • Compared to batteries, synchronous condensers have longer operational lifespans.
  • Deployment is concentrated in regions with high wind and solar capacity.
  • Costs remain significant but justified by reliability benefits.
