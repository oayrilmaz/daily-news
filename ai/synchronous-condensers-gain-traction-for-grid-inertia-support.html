Synchronous Condensers Gain Traction for Grid Inertia Support

Medium confidence — Growing project announcements exist, but large-scale operational data is limited.

TL;DR

Synchronous condensers are increasingly deployed to provide inertia and voltage support as grids incorporate higher shares of inverter-based resources.

Why it matters

  • Inertia is critical for frequency stability in low-synchronous generation grids.
  • Synchronous condensers provide reactive power and fault current support.
  • They enable smoother integration of renewables and storage.

Key points

  • Retrofit of existing generators into synchronous condensers is underway.
  • New builds target areas with high renewable penetration.
  • Control systems are evolving for dynamic grid support.
  • Costs remain a consideration compared to alternative solutions.
  • Grid codes increasingly require inertia provision.

Watchlist (next 24–48 hours)

  • Performance data from recent synchronous condenser installations.
  • Development of hybrid solutions combining condensers and batteries.
  • Policy incentives for inertia services procurement.

Known unknowns

  • Optimal sizing and placement of synchronous condensers in diverse grids.
  • Long-term maintenance costs and operational challenges.
  • Interaction effects with inverter-based resource controls.
