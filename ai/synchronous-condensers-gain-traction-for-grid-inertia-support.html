Synchronous Condensers Gain Traction for Grid Inertia Support

Medium confidence — Growing number of installations reported, but operational data remains limited.

TL;DR

Synchronous condensers are increasingly deployed to provide inertia and voltage support amid rising renewable penetration.

Why it matters

  • Compensates for inertia loss from retiring synchronous generators.
  • Enhances voltage stability and fault ride-through capabilities.
  • Supports grid reliability during high renewable output periods.

Key points

  • Synchronous condensers spin without generating power to provide inertia.
  • Retrofits of retired generator units are common implementation.
  • Alternative technologies like grid-forming inverters are emerging.
  • Cost and maintenance requirements are significant considerations.
  • Integration with existing grid protection schemes is critical.
  • Deployment is focused in regions with high wind and solar shares.
  • Grid codes increasingly mandate inertia requirements.

Watchlist (next 24–48 hours)

  • Performance metrics from recent synchronous condenser projects.
  • Development of inverter-based inertia solutions.
  • Policy incentives for inertia provision technologies.

Known unknowns

  • Optimal sizing and placement of synchronous condensers.
  • Long-term operational costs compared to alternatives.
  • Interaction effects with inverter-dominated grids.
