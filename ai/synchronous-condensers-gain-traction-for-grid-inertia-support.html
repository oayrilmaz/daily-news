Synchronous Condensers Gain Traction for Grid Inertia Support

Medium confidence — Deployment data is emerging but still limited to select regions and projects.

TL;DR

Synchronous condensers are increasingly deployed to provide inertia and voltage support as grids integrate more inverter-based resources.

Why it matters

  • Addresses reduced inertia caused by renewable penetration.
  • Enhances voltage stability and fault ride-through capability.
  • Supports compliance with grid codes requiring inertia.

Key points

  • Synchronous condensers spin without generating power but provide mechanical inertia.
  • They help maintain frequency stability during disturbances.
  • Projects are underway in Europe, North America, and Australia.
  • Alternative solutions include synthetic inertia from inverter controls.
  • Costs and maintenance requirements are significant considerations.

Watchlist (next 24–48 hours)

  • Performance data from recent synchronous condenser installations.
  • Development of synthetic inertia technologies.
  • Grid code updates related to inertia requirements.

Known unknowns

  • Long-term cost-effectiveness compared to inverter-based solutions.
  • Optimal sizing and placement within transmission networks.
  • Impact on overall system reliability under high renewable scenarios.
