Synchronous Condensers Gain Traction for Grid Inertia Support

Medium confidence — Growing number of installations reported, but long-term performance data is limited.

TL;DR

Synchronous condensers are increasingly deployed to provide inertia and voltage support amid rising renewable penetration and inverter-based resources.

Why it matters

  • Helps maintain grid frequency stability with low synchronous generation.
  • Supports voltage regulation and fault current provision.
  • Enables higher renewable energy integration without compromising reliability.

Key points

  • Synchronous condensers provide rotating inertia absent in inverter-based resources.
  • Retrofit projects converting old generators into synchronous condensers are underway.
  • Investment costs are lower compared to battery energy storage for inertia services.
  • Deployment primarily in grids with high wind and solar penetration.
  • Operational flexibility allows grid operators to manage transient events.
  • Technological improvements focus on reduced maintenance and footprint.
  • Policy recognition of inertia services is increasing.

Watchlist (next 24–48 hours)

  • New synchronous condenser installations in North America and Europe.
  • Grid code updates incorporating inertia requirements.
  • Emerging hybrid solutions combining synchronous condensers with storage.

Known unknowns

  • Optimal sizing and siting strategies for synchronous condensers.
  • Long-term operational costs and reliability metrics.
  • Impact on overall grid resilience under extreme conditions.
