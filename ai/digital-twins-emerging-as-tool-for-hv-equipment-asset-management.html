Digital Twins Emerging as Tool for HV Equipment Asset Management

Medium confidence — Early-stage deployments show promise but widespread adoption is nascent.

TL;DR

Digital twin technology is being adopted to simulate and optimize high-voltage equipment performance and maintenance.

Why it matters

  • Enables real-time performance monitoring and predictive analytics.
  • Improves maintenance planning and reduces unplanned outages.
  • Supports integration of complex grid assets and enhances resilience.

Key points

  • Digital twins replicate physical HV equipment behavior using sensor data.
  • Integration with SCADA and asset management systems is increasing.
  • Machine learning models improve anomaly detection and fault prediction.
  • Initial use cases focus on transformers, GIS, and switchgear.
  • Challenges include data quality, model accuracy, and cybersecurity.
  • Vendor partnerships and utility pilot projects are expanding.
  • Potential to optimize lifecycle costs and extend asset life.
