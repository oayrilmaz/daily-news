Synchronous Condensers Gain Momentum for Grid Inertia Support

High confidence — Growing number of projects and utility endorsements confirm their role in grid stability.

TL;DR

Synchronous condensers are being deployed to provide inertia and voltage support as grids incorporate more inverter-based resources.

Why it matters

  • Compensates for reduced inertia from renewable generation.
  • Improves voltage regulation and fault current availability.
  • Enables higher renewable penetration without compromising reliability.

Key points

  • Synchronous condensers provide rotating inertia without active power generation.
  • They help maintain frequency stability during disturbances.
  • Deployment is increasing in regions with high wind and solar penetration.
  • Retrofits of existing generators into synchronous condensers are underway.
  • Costs are lower compared to battery-based synthetic inertia solutions.
  • Integration requires coordination with grid protection schemes.
  • Operational lifespan aligns with traditional rotating equipment.
