Rising Demand for Synchronous Condensers to Support Grid Stability

High confidence — Multiple grid operators have publicly confirmed ongoing projects and investments in synchronous condensers.

TL;DR

Synchronous condensers are increasingly deployed to enhance grid stability amid rising renewable penetration and phase-out of conventional generators.

Why it matters

  • Improves voltage regulation and reactive power support on grids.
  • Facilitates integration of intermittent renewable energy sources.
  • Mitigates grid reliability risks from retiring thermal plants.

Key points

  • Synchronous condensers provide inertia and reactive power without energy generation.
  • Several US and European utilities have announced new synchronous condenser installations.
  • They help stabilize frequency and voltage fluctuations on weak grids.
  • Deployment is seen as a cost-effective alternative to battery storage for certain grid services.
  • Manufacturers report increased order backlogs for synchronous condenser equipment.
  • Grid codes in some regions are evolving to incentivize synchronous condenser use.
  • Operational experience from recent projects shows improved grid resilience.

Watchlist (next 24–48 hours)

  • Technological advances in synchronous condenser design.
