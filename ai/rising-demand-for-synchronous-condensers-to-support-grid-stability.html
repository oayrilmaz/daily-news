Rising Demand for Synchronous Condensers to Support Grid Stability

High confidence — Multiple grid operators report successful integration and benefits of synchronous condensers in recent projects.

TL;DR

Synchronous condensers are increasingly deployed to enhance grid inertia and voltage support amid growing renewable penetration.

Why it matters

  • Improves grid resilience against frequency fluctuations.
  • Facilitates higher renewable energy integration.
  • Reduces reliance on fossil-fuel-based spinning reserves.

Key points

  • Synchronous condensers provide reactive power and inertia without active power generation.
  • Deployment is rising in regions with high wind and solar capacity.
  • They help maintain voltage stability during grid disturbances.
  • Can be retrofitted from existing generators or installed as new units.
  • Support compliance with evolving grid codes demanding inertia.

  • Regulatory changes incentivizing inertia provision.

Known unknowns

  • Impact on overall grid economics compared to alternatives.
