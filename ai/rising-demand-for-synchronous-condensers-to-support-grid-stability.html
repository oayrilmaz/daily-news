Rising Demand for Synchronous Condensers to Support Grid Stability

High confidence — Multiple grid operators report synchronous condenser projects underway to address inertia deficits.

TL;DR

The integration of variable renewable energy sources is driving increased deployment of synchronous condensers to maintain grid inertia and voltage stability.

Why it matters

  • Ensures grid reliability amid renewable penetration.
  • Reduces risk of frequency instability and blackouts.
  • Supports compliance with evolving grid codes.

Key points

  • Synchronous condensers provide reactive power and inertia without active power generation.
  • Deployment is rising in regions with high wind and solar capacity.
  • Retrofit of existing generators is a common approach.
  • Costs are competitive relative to battery storage for inertia services.
  • Lead times for installation can impact grid upgrade schedules.
  • Grid codes increasingly mandate inertia support mechanisms.
