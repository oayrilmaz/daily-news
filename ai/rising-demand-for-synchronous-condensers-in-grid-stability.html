Rising Demand for Synchronous Condensers in Grid Stability

High confidence — Multiple utilities report ongoing projects and procurement plans for synchronous condensers.

TL;DR

Synchronous condensers are increasingly deployed to enhance grid stability amid rising renewable penetration.

Why it matters

  • Supports inertia and voltage control in grids with high renewable energy.
  • Enables integration of intermittent energy sources without compromising reliability.
  • Potentially reduces need for fossil fuel-based spinning reserves.

Key points

  • Synchronous condensers provide reactive power and inertia support.
  • Deployment is growing in regions with high wind and solar capacity.
  • Installation timelines typically range from 12 to 24 months.
  • Costs vary but are competitive with alternative grid stability solutions.
  • Equipment suppliers are expanding manufacturing capacity.
  • Grid operators emphasize synchronous condensers in modernization plans.
  • Some projects include repurposed synchronous machines from retired plants.
