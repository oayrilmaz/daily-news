Rising Demand for Synchronous Condensers in Grid Stability

High confidence — Multiple grid operators have publicly confirmed synchronous condenser projects in recent quarters.

TL;DR

Utilities are increasingly deploying synchronous condensers to enhance grid stability amid growing renewable penetration.

Why it matters

  • Improves voltage regulation and inertia on grids with high renewable energy share.
  • Supports integration of intermittent resources like wind and solar.
  • Reduces reliance on fossil fuel-based spinning reserves.

Key points

  • Synchronous condensers provide reactive power support and inertia.
  • Deployment is rising in regions with high wind and solar capacity.
  • Equipment suppliers report increased order backlogs.
  • Grid codes are evolving to incentivize synchronous condenser use.
  • Projects often repurpose retired generators for cost efficiency.
  • Operational benefits include improved fault ride-through capability.
  • Expected lifespan of synchronous condensers exceeds 30 years.
