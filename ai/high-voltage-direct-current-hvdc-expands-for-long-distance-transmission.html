High Voltage Direct Current (HVDC) Expands for Long-Distance Transmission

High confidence — Multiple projects under construction and planned confirm HVDC's growing role.

TL;DR

HVDC technology is increasingly adopted for efficient long-distance power transmission and interconnection of asynchronous grids.

Why it matters

  • Enables integration of remote renewable resources.
  • Reduces transmission losses over long distances.
  • Facilitates interconnection of different grid systems.

Key points

  • HVDC lines can transmit power over thousands of kilometers with lower losses than AC.
  • Used for submarine cables connecting offshore wind farms.
  • Allows asynchronous grid interconnections, improving reliability.
  • Modular multilevel converters improve efficiency and control.
  • Costs remain high but are offset by operational savings.
  • New projects in Asia, Europe, and North America are underway.
  • Grid operators are updating standards to accommodate HVDC.

Watchlist (next 24–48 hours)

  • Commissioning of large-scale HVDC projects.
  • Advances in converter technology.
  • Regulatory frameworks for cross-border HVDC links.

Known unknowns

  • Long-term performance and degradation of HVDC components.
  • Integration challenges with existing AC grid infrastructure.
  • Market mechanisms for HVDC capacity allocation.
