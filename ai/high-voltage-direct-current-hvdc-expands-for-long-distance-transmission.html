High-Voltage Direct Current (HVDC) Expands for Long-Distance Transmission

High confidence — Multiple large-scale HVDC projects are under construction or planning globally.

TL;DR

HVDC technology adoption is accelerating to enable efficient long-distance and interregional power transfer.

Why it matters

  • HVDC reduces transmission losses over long distances compared to AC lines.
  • It facilitates integration of remote renewable resources into grids.
  • HVDC enhances interconnection capacity between asynchronous grids.

Key points

  • New HVDC lines are being built in Europe, Asia, and North America.
  • Voltage source converter (VSC) technology enables multi-terminal HVDC grids.
  • HVDC supports grid stability through controllable power flows.
  • Costs remain high but are offset by efficiency gains and reduced right-of-way needs.
  • Integration with offshore wind farms is a major driver of HVDC deployment.
  • HVDC projects face regulatory and permitting challenges.
  • Advances in converter technology improve reliability and reduce footprint.

Watchlist (next 24–48 hours)

  • Commissioning of major HVDC interconnectors
  • Technological breakthroughs in HVDC converter efficiency
  • Policy incentives for cross-border HVDC projects

Known unknowns

  • Long-term operational performance under multi-terminal configurations
  • Market mechanisms for HVDC capacity allocation
  • Impact of HVDC on traditional AC grid planning
