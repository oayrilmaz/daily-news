HVDC Interconnections Expand to Support Cross-Border Renewable Power Transfer

High confidence — Several HVDC projects have reached advanced planning or construction phases in Europe and Asia.

TL;DR

High Voltage Direct Current (HVDC) links are increasingly used to enable efficient cross-border renewable energy exchange.

Why it matters

  • Enables balancing of renewable generation across regions.
  • Reduces curtailment and increases grid resilience.
  • Supports decarbonization through international power trade.

Key points

  • HVDC technology reduces transmission losses over long distances.
  • Voltage Source Converter (VSC) HVDC allows black start and grid support.
  • Projects like North Sea Link and China’s HVDC corridors are benchmarks.
  • Permitting and environmental approvals remain key challenges.
  • Costs remain high but are offset by improved grid efficiency.
  • Integration with existing AC grids requires complex control systems.

  • Regulatory frameworks for cross-border power trading.

Known unknowns
