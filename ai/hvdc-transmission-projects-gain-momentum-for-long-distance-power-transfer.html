HVDC Transmission Projects Gain Momentum for Long-Distance Power Transfer

High confidence — Multiple large-scale HVDC projects have reached advanced development stages globally.

TL;DR

High Voltage Direct Current (HVDC) technology is increasingly selected for interregional transmission projects to enhance capacity and reduce losses.

Why it matters

  • Enables efficient long-distance bulk power transfer.
  • Facilitates integration of remote renewable resources.
  • Supports grid interconnection and reliability.

Key points

  • HVDC lines have lower transmission losses compared to HVAC over long distances.
  • Projects in Europe and Asia are expanding cross-border power flows.
  • Voltage Source Converter (VSC) HVDC allows better grid control and black start capability.
  • Costs remain high but are offset by operational savings and capacity benefits.
  • Environmental impact is reduced due to smaller right-of-way requirements.
  • HVDC supports asynchronous grid interconnections.
  • Technology maturity is increasing with new converter station designs.

  • Innovations in converter technology and grid integration.
