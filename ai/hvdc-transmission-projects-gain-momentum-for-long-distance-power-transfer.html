HVDC Transmission Projects Gain Momentum for Long-Distance Power Transfer

High confidence — Multiple ongoing projects and technology maturity support this trend.

TL;DR

High Voltage Direct Current (HVDC) projects are increasingly deployed to enhance long-distance, high-capacity power transmission with lower losses.

Why it matters

  • Enables integration of remote renewable resources.
  • Reduces transmission losses compared to AC lines.
  • Supports grid interconnections across regions and countries.

Key points

  • New HVDC links are planned or under construction in Asia, Europe, and North America.
  • Technological advancements reduce converter station costs and improve reliability.
  • HVDC facilitates asynchronous grid interconnections.
  • Projects often face regulatory and permitting challenges.
  • HVDC supports offshore wind integration through subsea cables.
  • Grid operators prioritize HVDC for congestion relief.
  • Standardization efforts are underway to streamline HVDC deployment.

Watchlist (next 24–48 hours)

  • Regulatory frameworks enabling cross-border HVDC links.
  • Technological innovations in converter and cable materials.
