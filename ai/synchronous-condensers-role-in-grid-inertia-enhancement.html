Synchronous Condensers Role in Grid Inertia Enhancement

Medium confidence — Growing adoption observed but long-term operational data limited.

TL;DR

Synchronous condensers are increasingly deployed to provide inertia and voltage support as conventional generators retire.

Why it matters

  • Maintains grid frequency stability in low-inertia systems.
  • Enables higher penetration of inverter-based resources.
  • Supports voltage regulation and reactive power management.

Key points

  • Renewed interest in synchronous condensers as inertia sources.
  • Retrofitting of retired generators into synchronous condensers underway.
  • Deployment primarily in grids with high renewable penetration.
  • Cost and maintenance considerations influencing adoption rates.
  • Complementary to energy storage and advanced inverter controls.
  • Limited availability of manufacturers and skilled workforce.
  • Regulatory recognition of synchronous condensers in ancillary services.
