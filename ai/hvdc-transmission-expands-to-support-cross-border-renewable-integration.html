HVDC Transmission Expands to Support Cross-Border Renewable Integration

High confidence — Multiple ongoing projects and government-backed initiatives confirm HVDC expansion trends.

TL;DR

High Voltage Direct Current (HVDC) lines are increasingly deployed to facilitate large-scale renewable energy transfers across regions.

Why it matters

  • Enables efficient long-distance power transfer with reduced losses.
  • Facilitates interconnection of asynchronous grids.
  • Supports decarbonization by linking renewable-rich regions with demand centers.

Key points

  • HVDC lines reduce transmission losses compared to AC lines over long distances.
  • Projects in Europe and Asia are connecting offshore wind farms to mainland grids.
  • Multi-terminal HVDC technology is advancing for complex grid interconnections.
  • Costs remain high but are offset by improved grid flexibility and capacity.
  • Integration challenges include converter station complexity and control coordination.
  • Policy support is critical for cross-border HVDC infrastructure development.
  • Environmental impact assessments focus on undersea and underground HVDC routes.
