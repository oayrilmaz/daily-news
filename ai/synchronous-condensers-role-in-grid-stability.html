Synchronous Condensers Role in Grid Stability

Medium confidence — Growing deployments observed but long-term operational data is limited.

TL;DR

Synchronous condensers are being deployed to provide inertia and reactive power support as grids integrate more inverter-based resources.

Why it matters

  • Helps maintain voltage stability and frequency regulation.
  • Mitigates reduced inertia from renewable generation.
  • Enables higher penetration of wind and solar power.

Key points

  • Synchronous condensers provide grid inertia without active power generation.
  • They support reactive power compensation and fault current contribution.
  • Retrofit of retired generators is a common approach.
  • Costs are significant but justified by grid stability benefits.
  • Integration requires coordination with grid protection schemes.
  • Deployment is increasing in regions with high renewable penetration.
  • Alternatives include synthetic inertia from inverter controls.

Watchlist (next 24–48 hours)

  • Performance data from recent synchronous condenser installations.
  • Development of synthetic inertia standards.
  • Grid codes evolving to mandate inertia support.

Known unknowns

  • Optimal sizing and placement of synchronous condensers.
  • Cost-benefit comparison with inverter-based solutions.
  • Long-term maintenance and operational challenges.
