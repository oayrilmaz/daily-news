Grid Stability Challenges with Increased HVDC Integration

High confidence — Technical studies and field data from recent HVDC projects confirm these challenges.

TL;DR

The growing deployment of HVDC links introduces new stability challenges for AC grids, requiring advanced control and protection schemes.

Why it matters

  • Ensures reliable power transfer across regions with diverse generation mixes.
  • Prevents cascading failures and blackouts in interconnected grids.
  • Supports integration of remote renewable resources.

Key points

  • HVDC converters can cause harmonic distortion and voltage fluctuations.
  • Coordination between HVDC controls and AC grid protection is critical.
  • Dynamic reactive power support from HVDC stations is increasingly utilized.
  • Fault ride-through capabilities are being enhanced in converter designs.
  • Grid codes are evolving to address HVDC-specific stability issues.
  • Simulation tools for HVDC-AC interactions are becoming more sophisticated.
  • Training for grid operators on HVDC system behavior is expanding.
