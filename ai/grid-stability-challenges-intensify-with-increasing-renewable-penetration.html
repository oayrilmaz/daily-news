Grid Stability Challenges Intensify with Increasing Renewable Penetration

High shares of inverter-based renewable generation are creating operational challenges for grid frequency and voltage stability.

Why it matters

  • Grid codes and operational practices must evolve.
  • Investment in ancillary services and grid-forming technologies is needed.

Key points

  • Reduced system inertia from retiring synchronous generators affects frequency response.
  • Voltage regulation becomes more complex with distributed generation.
  • Synthetic inertia and fast frequency response technologies are being deployed.
  • Energy storage systems play a growing role in stability support.
  • Advanced grid monitoring and control systems are essential.
  • Coordination between transmission and distribution operators is increasing.
  • Regulatory frameworks are adapting to new stability requirements.
