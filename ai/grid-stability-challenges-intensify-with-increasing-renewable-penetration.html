Grid Stability Challenges Intensify with Increasing Renewable Penetration

High confidence — Grid operators report measurable declines in inertia and frequency response capabilities linked to renewables.

TL;DR

Higher shares of inverter-based renewable generation are reducing system inertia, prompting new stability solutions.

Why it matters

  • Inertia reduction increases risk of frequency deviations and outages.
  • Necessitates investment in new technologies and grid services.
  • Influences future grid design and regulatory frameworks.

Key points

  • Conventional synchronous generators provide natural inertia.
  • Inverter-based resources lack inherent inertia but can emulate it.
  • Synchronous condensers and grid-forming inverters are deployed as solutions.
  • Frequency response markets are evolving to incentivize fast response.
  • Grid codes are updated to require new stability capabilities.
  • Energy storage systems contribute to frequency regulation.
  • Modeling tools improve understanding of inertia dynamics.
