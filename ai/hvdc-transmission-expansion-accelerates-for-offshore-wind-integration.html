HVDC Transmission Expansion Accelerates for Offshore Wind Integration

High confidence — Confirmed project pipelines and government targets support HVDC growth for offshore wind integration.

TL;DR

High Voltage Direct Current (HVDC) systems are expanding rapidly to connect offshore wind farms to onshore grids.

Why it matters

  • Enables efficient long-distance power transmission with reduced losses.
  • Facilitates integration of large-scale offshore renewable resources.
  • Supports grid reliability by controlling power flows precisely.

Key points

  • HVDC technology reduces transmission losses compared to HVAC over long distances.
  • Modular Multi-Level Converter (MMC) technology enhances system flexibility.
  • Projects in Europe and Asia lead HVDC offshore wind connections.
  • Costs remain high but are declining with technology maturation.
  • Interconnection standards are evolving to accommodate HVDC links.
  • HVDC links can provide black start capabilities and grid support.
  • Supply chain constraints for HVDC components may impact timelines.
