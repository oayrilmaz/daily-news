Interconnection Queues Lengthen, Delaying Renewable Project Integration

High confidence — Data from multiple regional transmission organizations confirm queue growth.

TL;DR

Increasing interconnection application backlogs are causing significant delays for renewable energy projects seeking grid access.

Why it matters

  • Slows renewable capacity additions critical for decarbonization goals.
  • Increases project development costs and financial risk.
  • Pressures grid operators to improve interconnection processes.

Key points

  • Average interconnection queue wait times exceeding 3-5 years in some regions.
  • High volume of solar and wind projects contributing to backlog.
  • Grid upgrade requirements further complicate interconnection timelines.
  • Regulatory efforts underway to streamline application and study processes.
  • Emergence of cluster study approaches to improve efficiency.
  • Developers exploring alternative grid connection points.
  • Potential for increased use of energy storage to mitigate delays.

Watchlist (next 24–48 hours)

  • Innovations in grid planning and modeling tools.
  • Market responses to interconnection delays, including project cancellations.
