HVDC Transmission Expands to Facilitate Offshore Wind Integration

High confidence — Multiple large-scale offshore wind projects have committed to HVDC links recently.

TL;DR

High Voltage Direct Current (HVDC) technology is increasingly deployed to connect offshore wind farms to onshore grids efficiently.

Why it matters

  • Enables long-distance, low-loss power transmission from offshore sites.
  • Supports grid decarbonization by integrating large renewable capacity.
  • Reduces congestion on existing AC transmission infrastructure.

Key points

  • HVDC systems offer better control over power flows than AC.
  • Modular Multi-Level Converter (MMC) technology advances HVDC capabilities.
  • Projects underway in Europe, Asia, and North America.
  • HVDC facilitates multi-terminal offshore grid configurations.
  • High upfront costs offset by operational efficiencies.
  • Improves grid reliability and flexibility.
