TL;DR

Gas-Insulated Switchgear (GIS) technology is advancing with compact designs and improved SF6 alternatives for urban substation applications.

Why it matters

  • Enables high-voltage equipment installation in space-constrained urban areas.
  • Reduces environmental impact through SF6 gas alternatives.
  • Improves reliability and safety of distribution networks.

Key points

  • Compact GIS designs reduce footprint by up to 50% compared to air-insulated switchgear.
  • Development of SF6-free insulation gases is progressing.
  • Modular GIS units facilitate faster installation and maintenance.
  • Enhanced monitoring and diagnostics improve asset management.
  • GIS equipment supports voltages up to 800 kV in some applications.
  • Costs remain higher than traditional air-insulated switchgear but justified by space savings.
  • Urban substations increasingly adopt GIS to meet growing demand.
