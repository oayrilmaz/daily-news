TL;DR

Gas-Insulated Switchgear (GIS) is increasingly deployed in urban substations due to space constraints and enhanced reliability needs.

Why it matters

  • GIS reduces substation footprint significantly.
  • Improves safety and reduces maintenance requirements.
  • Supports urban grid modernization efforts.

Key points

  • GIS uses sulfur hexafluoride (SF6) gas for insulation and arc quenching.
  • Compact design suits dense urban environments.
  • Higher initial costs offset by lower lifecycle expenses.
  • SF6 alternatives under development due to environmental concerns.
  • Integration with digital monitoring enhances operational efficiency.
