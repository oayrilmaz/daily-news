GIS Technology Adoption Expands in Urban Substations

High confidence — Strong market trends and procurement data support GIS growth in constrained urban environments.

TL;DR

Gas-insulated switchgear (GIS) is increasingly deployed in urban substations to optimize space and improve reliability.

Why it matters

  • Enables compact substation design where land is limited.
  • Improves operational safety and reduces maintenance needs.
  • Supports higher voltage levels in dense urban grids.

Key points

  • GIS uses SF6 gas for insulation, offering high dielectric strength.
  • Urban utilities prioritize GIS to minimize footprint.
  • Technological improvements reduce SF6 leakage risks.
  • GIS installation costs remain higher than air-insulated alternatives.
  • Modular designs facilitate faster deployment and upgrades.
  • Regulatory scrutiny on SF6 emissions is increasing.
  • Integration with digital monitoring systems enhances asset management.
