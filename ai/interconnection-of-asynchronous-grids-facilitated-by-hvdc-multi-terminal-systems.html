Interconnection of Asynchronous Grids Facilitated by HVDC Multi-Terminal Systems

Medium confidence — Technology is proven but large-scale multi-terminal deployments are still emerging.

TL;DR

HVDC multi-terminal systems enable interconnection between asynchronous grids, enhancing reliability and market integration.

Why it matters

  • Allows power exchange without synchronizing frequency or phase.
  • Supports regional grid resilience and renewable sharing.
  • Enables new market structures and cross-border trading.

Key points

  • Multi-terminal HVDC systems use voltage source converters for flexible control.
  • Projects in Europe and Asia are pioneering multi-terminal HVDC links.
  • Complex protection and control schemes are required.
  • Reduces need for costly AC grid reinforcements.
  • Facilitates integration of offshore wind and remote resources.
  • Operational experience is growing but still limited.
  • Standards and regulatory frameworks are evolving to support multi-terminal HVDC.

Watchlist (next 24–48 hours)

  • Commissioning of new multi-terminal HVDC projects
  • Advances in HVDC protection and control technologies
  • Regulatory developments for cross-border asynchronous interconnections

Known unknowns

  • Long-term reliability and maintenance requirements
  • Economic models for cost allocation among multiple stakeholders
  • Scalability of multi-terminal HVDC networks
