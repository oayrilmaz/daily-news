Grid Stability Challenges Rise with Increased Renewable Penetration

High confidence — Grid operators report operational challenges and are deploying solutions accordingly.

TL;DR

Higher shares of variable renewables are increasing grid stability challenges, prompting investment in advanced control technologies.

Why it matters

  • Maintaining frequency and voltage stability is critical for reliable power supply.
  • Advanced controls can reduce curtailment and improve renewable utilization.
  • Investment decisions hinge on balancing cost and reliability.

Key points

  • Inertia reduction due to inverter-based resources affects frequency response.
  • Grid-forming inverters and synthetic inertia are being tested.
  • Energy storage systems provide fast frequency regulation.
  • Demand response programs support grid balancing.
  • Regulatory frameworks evolve to incentivize stability solutions.
  • Coordination between transmission and distribution grids is increasing.
  • Data analytics and AI support predictive grid management.
