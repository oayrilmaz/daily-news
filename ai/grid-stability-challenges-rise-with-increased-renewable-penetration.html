Grid Stability Challenges Rise with Increased Renewable Penetration

High confidence — Supported by grid operator data and academic studies.

TL;DR

Higher shares of inverter-based renewable generation are creating new challenges for grid frequency and voltage stability.

Why it matters

  • Necessitates new grid management tools and technologies.
  • Impacts reliability and quality of power supply.
  • Drives investment in grid-forming inverters and ancillary services.

Key points

  • Reduced system inertia leading to faster frequency deviations.
  • Voltage regulation complexity increases with distributed generation.
  • Grid codes evolving to require synthetic inertia capabilities.
  • Energy storage and synchronous condensers used to mitigate issues.
  • Advanced forecasting and control systems being deployed.
