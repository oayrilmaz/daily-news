Rising Demand for Synchronous Condensers to Enhance Grid Stability

High confidence — Multiple utilities report active projects and procurement of synchronous condensers to address stability challenges.

TL;DR

Synchronous condensers are increasingly deployed to improve grid inertia and voltage regulation amid higher renewable penetration.

Why it matters

  • Supports integration of variable renewable energy sources.
  • Improves grid resilience against frequency fluctuations.
  • Delays costly grid infrastructure upgrades.

Key points

  • Synchronous condensers provide reactive power support without energy generation.
  • Deployment is growing in regions with high wind and solar capacity.
  • They help maintain voltage levels during grid disturbances.
  • Installation times range from 12 to 24 months.
  • Costs are competitive compared to battery storage for inertia services.
