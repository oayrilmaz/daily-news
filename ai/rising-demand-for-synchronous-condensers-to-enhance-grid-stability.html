Rising Demand for Synchronous Condensers to Enhance Grid Stability

TL;DR

Synchronous condensers are increasingly deployed to support grid inertia and voltage regulation amid rising renewable penetration.

Why it matters

  • Improves grid resilience against frequency fluctuations.
  • Facilitates higher integration of variable renewable energy sources.
  • Reduces reliance on fossil-fuel-based spinning reserves.

Key points

  • Synchronous condensers provide reactive power support without active power generation.
  • They help maintain grid frequency and voltage stability.
  • Deployment is growing in regions with high wind and solar capacity.
  • Can be retrofitted from decommissioned generators or purpose-built.
  • Cost and lead time are competitive compared to battery storage for inertia.
  • Grid operators prioritize synchronous condensers in system planning.
  • Technology maturity supports reliable operation over decades.
