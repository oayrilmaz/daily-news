Rising Demand for Synchronous Condensers to Enhance Grid Stability

High confidence — Multiple utilities report successful synchronous condenser installations and grid performance improvements.

TL;DR

Utilities are increasingly deploying synchronous condensers to manage grid inertia and support renewable integration.

Why it matters

  • Improves grid stability amid rising renewable penetration.
  • Reduces risk of frequency deviations and blackouts.
  • Supports compliance with evolving grid codes.

Key points

  • Synchronous condensers provide reactive power and inertia without generating electricity.
  • Deployment is accelerating in regions with high wind and solar capacity.
  • They help maintain voltage levels and system strength.
  • Installation costs are significant but offset by grid reliability gains.
  • Technology maturity reduces operational risks.
  • Can be retrofitted from existing synchronous machines.
  • Expected to play a key role in grids with low conventional generation.

  • Cost trends for synchronous condenser retrofits.
