Rising Demand for Synchronous Condensers to Enhance Grid Stability

High confidence — Multiple grid operators report successful synchronous condenser installations and plan expansions.

TL;DR

Utilities are increasingly deploying synchronous condensers to improve grid inertia and voltage stability amid rising renewable penetration.

Why it matters

  • Supports integration of intermittent renewables by stabilizing frequency.
  • Reduces reliance on fossil-fuel spinning reserves.
  • Enables compliance with stricter grid codes on inertia.

Key points

  • Synchronous condensers provide reactive power and inertia without fuel consumption.
  • Deployment is accelerating in regions with high wind and solar capacity.
  • Existing generators are being retrofitted or replaced with synchronous condensers.
  • Costs are competitive compared to battery energy storage for grid services.
  • Grid operators report improved frequency response after installations.

  • Technological advances in condenser design and controls.
