Grid Stability Concerns Rise with Increasing Distributed Energy Resources

High confidence — Grid operators report operational challenges and are implementing mitigation strategies.

TL;DR

High penetration of distributed energy resources (DERs) introduces challenges for maintaining grid frequency and voltage stability.

Why it matters

  • Risk of increased frequency deviations and voltage fluctuations.
  • Necessitates advanced grid management and control technologies.
  • Impacts planning and investment decisions for grid upgrades.

Key points

  • DERs often lack inertia and reactive power support.
  • Grid codes are evolving to require DERs to provide ancillary services.
  • Energy storage and smart inverters help mitigate stability issues.
  • Advanced distribution management systems improve real-time control.
  • Coordination between transmission and distribution operators is critical.
  • Pilot projects demonstrate technical feasibility of new control schemes.

Watchlist (next 24–48 hours)

  • Updates to grid interconnection standards for DERs.
  • Deployment of grid-forming inverter technologies.
  • Regulatory frameworks incentivizing DER grid support capabilities.

Known unknowns

  • Scalability of current mitigation technologies at high DER penetration.
  • Cost implications for utilities and consumers.
  • Long-term grid architecture adaptations required.
