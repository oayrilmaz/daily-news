TL;DR

High-voltage direct current (HVDC) lines are increasingly deployed to transmit renewable energy over long distances efficiently.

Why it matters

  • Enables large-scale renewable energy delivery from remote sites.
  • Reduces transmission losses compared to AC lines.
  • Facilitates interregional grid interconnections and market integration.

Key points

  • HVDC projects planned or under construction in Asia, Europe, and North America.
  • Technological advances improve converter station efficiency and reliability.
  • Cost remains a barrier but decreasing with scale and innovation.
  • HVDC supports asynchronous grid interconnections.
  • Permitting and right-of-way challenges persist.
