Medium confidence — Industry reports cite supply chain bottlenecks, but mitigation strategies are evolving.

TL;DR

Global rare earth element supply challenges are affecting production timelines and costs for high-voltage equipment manufacturers.

Why it matters

  • Rare earths are critical for permanent magnets in HV motors and generators.
  • Supply disruptions can delay HV equipment delivery and increase costs.
  • Encourages research into alternative materials and recycling.

Key points

  • China dominates rare earth mining and processing, creating geopolitical risks.
  • Manufacturers seek diversified sourcing and stockpiling strategies.
  • Recycling of rare earths from end-of-life equipment is gaining attention.
  • Substitution with ferrite magnets is limited by performance trade-offs.
  • Price volatility affects project budgeting and procurement planning.
  • Government policies increasingly support domestic rare earth production.
  • Technological innovation aims to reduce rare earth content in HV equipment.
