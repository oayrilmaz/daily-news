Synchronous Condensers Role in Modern Grids

High confidence — Grid operator reports and project deployments confirm growing adoption.

TL;DR

Synchronous condensers are increasingly deployed to provide inertia and voltage support in grids with high inverter-based generation.

Why it matters

  • Maintains grid frequency stability.
  • Supports voltage regulation in weak grids.
  • Enables higher renewable penetration without compromising reliability.

Key points

  • Synchronous condensers provide spinning inertia absent in inverter-based resources.
  • They help mitigate issues caused by rapid fluctuations in renewable output.
  • Installation projects are underway in North America, Europe, and Australia.
  • Retrofit of existing generators as synchronous condensers is a cost-effective option.
  • Integration with grid control systems enhances operational flexibility.
  • Synchronous condensers contribute reactive power without fuel consumption.
  • Their deployment complements energy storage and demand response solutions.

  • Grid code updates mandating inertia requirements.

Known unknowns
