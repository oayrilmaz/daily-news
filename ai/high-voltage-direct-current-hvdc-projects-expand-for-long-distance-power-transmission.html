High Voltage Direct Current (HVDC) Projects Expand for Long-Distance Power Transmission

High confidence — Numerous confirmed projects and technology maturity support this trend.

TL;DR

HVDC technology deployment is accelerating globally to enable efficient long-distance and cross-border electricity transmission.

Why it matters

  • Facilitates integration of remote renewable energy resources.
  • Reduces transmission losses compared to AC lines over long distances.
  • Enables interconnection of asynchronous grids and enhances energy trade.

Key points

  • New multi-gigawatt HVDC links under construction in Asia, Europe, and North America.
  • Voltage source converter (VSC) technology dominates recent projects.
  • HVDC supports grid stability by controlling power flows precisely.
  • Subsea HVDC cables are critical for offshore wind integration.
  • Costs remain high but are offset by efficiency gains and grid benefits.
  • Standardization efforts ongoing to reduce equipment and engineering costs.
  • Environmental permitting and right-of-way acquisition remain challenges.
