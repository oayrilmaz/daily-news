The Most Expensive Mistake in AI (And How to Avoid It)

Based on the available description… Marcus Oliver, lead of Google Delta professional services, says the most expensive mistake in AI is rushing to code without a strategy—not computing costs. He urges organizations to escape the "coding trap" by reimagining value chains and deploying multi-agent systems to achieve real business outcomes.

Key points
  • Most expensive AI mistake: rushing to code without a strategy (not compute costs).
  • Marcus Oliver is the featured speaker; he leads Google Delta professional services.
  • Warning against the "coding trap"—starting implementation before clarifying value.
  • Recommendation to reimagine value chains as part of AI initiatives.
  • Advocates deploying multi-agent systems to drive tangible business outcomes.
So what
  • Prioritize strategy and value definition before authorizing AI coding or deployments.
  • Assess how AI initiatives will change your facility's value chains and operational workflows.
  • Consider multi-agent system architectures where they align with concrete business goals.
  • Avoid sunk costs from premature development by requiring outcome-aligned plans first.
