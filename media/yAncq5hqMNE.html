Why your body shape doesn't define athletic abilities | Dominique Condo | TEDxDeakin Uni Melbourne

Based on the available description, Dr Dominique Condo examines how body-image pressures shape female athletes’ confidence, health, and performance and shows that women are often judged more for appearance than achievement. She outlines research-backed insights and practical strategies to build environments that value strength, resilience and capability across sport, workplaces, schools and families.

Watch on YouTube
Key points
  • Body-image pressures negatively affect female athletes’ performance, health and confidence.
  • Language, culture and expectations contribute to judging women by appearance rather than ability.
  • Dr Condo offers research-backed strategies to create supportive environments that prioritise performance over appearance.
  • Lessons extend beyond sport to workplaces, schools and families, calling for systemic cultural change.
  • Dr Condo is Head of AFLW at Geelong Football Club and a Senior Lecturer in Sports Nutrition at Deakin University.
So what
  • Prioritise measurable performance and capability metrics over appearance-based criteria in hiring, evaluation and promotion to retain technical talent.
  • Create supportive workplace culture and wellbeing programs to protect employee health and performance, reducing turnover risk in critical infrastructure teams.
  • Train leaders on language and bias to prevent appearance-focused assessments that can undermine confidence and productivity in engineering and operations staff.
  • Design uniform, PPE and workplace facilities with diverse body needs in mind to improve safety, comfort and inclusion for all employees.
