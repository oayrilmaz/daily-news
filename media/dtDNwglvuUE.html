You’re being lied to about electricity and AI data centers: the truth is dirtier and inefficient

The video argues that common narratives about AI and data-center electricity are misleading, claiming the true energy and emissions picture is "dirtier and inefficient." It challenges the idea that rapid expansion of AI data centers is benign or inherently low‑carbon and calls for closer scrutiny of electricity impacts.

Watch on YouTube
Key points
  • Asserts the public is being misled about AI/data-center electricity impacts.
  • Claims the reality of AI power use is dirtier and less efficient than portrayed.
  • Questions the narrative that building large numbers of new data centers is harmless.
  • Frames the issue around electricity demand, emissions accounting, and efficiency.
  • Calls for greater transparency and scrutiny of data-center energy claims.
So what
  • Revisit load forecasts and capacity planning assumptions for prospective AI data-center projects — advertised efficiency claims may understate actual grid impact.
  • Require transparent metrics (PUE, site energy, emissions attribution) in RFPs and contracts so utilities, EPCs, and owners can assess true power and carbon risks.
  • Plan for additional flexibility and firming (storage, demand response, grid upgrades) if AI demand proves higher or more variable than projected.
  • Prioritize siting and procurement that align with available low‑carbon capacity and realistic interconnection timelines to avoid stranded grid requirements.
