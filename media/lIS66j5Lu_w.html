Our ability is within our capacity | Jerome R.Wright | TEDxRochester

Based on the available description, Jerome R. Wright challenges conventional ideas of rehabilitation by urging people to use their capacities—as parents, advocates, neighbors—to uplift others and create choices rather than chances. He draws on his work ending solitary confinement (HALT Solitary Campaign, NYS Jails Justice Network), founding the MAN mentoring program, hosting a community-focused podcast, and consulting with a law clinic to promote humane, restorative approaches to justice.

Watch on YouTube
Key points
  • Frames rehabilitation disruption as a moral and practical question about community care and capacity.
  • Emphasizes joy through service and the everyday role of individuals in supporting families and communities.
  • Co-Director of HALT Solitary Campaign and leader in NYS Jails Justice Network advocating to end solitary confinement.
  • Founder of the MAN (Mentoring and Nurturing) Program supporting people impacted by incarceration.
  • Amplifies lived experience via The Wright Report podcast and consulting with University at Buffalo’s Criminal Law Clinics.
So what
  • Workforce resilience: Partnering with mentoring and reentry programs can expand local talent pipelines and support stable staffing without requiring outsized resources.
  • Community relations: Investing within organizational capacity in humane, restorative initiatives strengthens social license and community trust around critical infrastructure projects.
  • Operational continuity: Supporting mental-health and reintegration programs for affected populations can reduce local social risks that indirectly impact grid and facility operations.
  • Ethical supply chain & hiring: Incorporating lived-experience-informed training and fair-hiring practices aligns corporate responsibility with long-term workforce and community stability.
