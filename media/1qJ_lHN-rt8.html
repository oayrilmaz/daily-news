How I turned $27K of land into $700/night luxury cabin rentals

Based on the available description, full-time contractor Chris Broomfield bought a five-acre property in upstate New York in 2015 and transformed a $27K land purchase into luxury Airbnb cabins charging about $700 per night. He built an initial A-frame cabin himself over weekend commutes from Connecticut, added additional cabins over time, and by September 2025 the property had generated over $2.1 million.

Watch on YouTube
Key points
  • 2015 purchase of a five-acre lot (land cited as $27K) in upstate New York to develop Airbnb properties.
  • Chris Broomfield, a full-time contractor, commuted from Connecticut on weekends to build an A-frame cabin over three years.
  • Additional cabins were added after the initial build, creating multiple short-term rental units.
  • Cabins are marketed as luxury rentals commanding about $700 per night.
  • By September 2025 the property had produced over $2.1 million in revenue.
So what
  • High-value short-term rental conversions on rural land can create concentrated demand for reliable power and connectivity.
  • Incremental, owner-driven development suggests infrastructure needs may grow gradually as properties scale.
  • Service providers and planners should consider rural hospitality use cases when assessing capacity and redundancy requirements.
  • Opportunities exist to offer targeted solutions (backup power, robust broadband, remote monitoring) to support high-end remote rentals.
