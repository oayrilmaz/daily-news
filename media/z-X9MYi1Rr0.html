Leveraging tax credits and incentives for sustainability projects

Based on the available description, Randa Barsoum, Tax Partner at PwC US, highlights that energy tax credits are only part of the picture and draws attention to less obvious incentives—such as state and local abatements, location-based incentives, and R&D tax credits tied to innovation like reimagining packaging or processes. The core message: viewing sustainability investments through a broader tax lens can unlock meaningful tax value.

Watch on YouTube
Key points
  • Energy incentives are common but not the only tax opportunities for sustainability projects.
  • State and local abatements can support sustainability initiatives.
  • Location-based incentives may provide additional tax value tied to where projects are sited.
  • R&D tax credits can apply to innovation efforts, including reimagining packaging or processes.
  • A broader tax perspective can turn sustainability investments into meaningful tax value.
So what
  • Don’t limit assessments to energy credits—also evaluate state and local abatements and location-based incentives when planning projects.
  • Include potential R&D tax credits in the financial case for innovation-driven sustainability initiatives.
  • Screen available incentives across jurisdictions to maximize tax value from sustainability investments.
  • Treat sustainability investments as potential sources of tax value, not only as costs.
