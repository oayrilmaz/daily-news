Key points
  • Rising electrical demand from AI data centers and implications for grid capacity and peak loads.

  • Power quality, resilience, redundancy, and reliability needs unique to AI workloads.
  • Mitigation and opportunity areas: on-site generation, energy storage, demand response and flexible load management.

So what
  • Anticipate and plan transmission and distribution upgrades early when evaluating AI data center projects to avoid interconnection delays.
  • Incorporate energy storage, demand-response contracts and on-site generation into project design to manage peak demand and provide grid services.
  • Design for stringent power quality and resilience (dual feeds, UPS, fast-transfer generation) to protect latency- and availability-sensitive AI workloads.
  • Engage utilities and regulators early to align permitting, interconnection studies, and potential grid investment cost allocation.
