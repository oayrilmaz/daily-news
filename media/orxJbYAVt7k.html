Based on the available description, this re-release of the Reporting reset kickoff episode reviews foundational reporting principles and disclosure considerations for year-end reporting. Key points
  • Overview of foundational GAAP and SEC requirements for financial statement presentation.
  • Guidance on determining appropriate reporting periods and cutoffs.
  • Balance sheet presentation: classification, required disclosures, and best practices.
  • Income statement presentation: structure and key considerations.
  • Discussion of accounting changes, estimates, error corrections, and subsequent events, with links to PwC guidance chapters.
So what
  • Ensure balance sheet and income statement presentation, classification, and disclosures align with GAAP/SEC requirements to support accurate external reporting.
  • Confirm reporting periods and cutoff procedures are appropriate and consistently applied for year‑end reporting.
  • Document and disclose accounting changes, estimates, and any error corrections clearly to maintain transparency in reported results.
  • Establish processes to identify and evaluate subsequent events occurring after period‑end for appropriate recognition or disclosure.
  • Consider the notable differences between public and private company financial statements when preparing reports and disclosures.
