Key points

So what
  • For transmission and grid planners: data centers present steady, high-density loads—understand expected feeder capacity and resilience requirements when interfacing new or expanding sites.
  • For EPCs and utilities: coordination around UPS/generator integration, transfer schemes and site-level redundancy is critical to meet uptime expectations.
  • For renewable integration and energy-storage teams: data center load profiles and availability SLAs drive opportunities for onsite storage, demand response and behind-the-meter renewables.
  • For designers of AI and high-power facilities: anticipate high rack densities and cooling power needs that influence electrical distribution, transformer sizing and thermal-management strategies.
