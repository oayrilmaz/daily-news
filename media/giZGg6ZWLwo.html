So what
  • Creative AI use cases can drive increased compute demand—plan for scalable power and cooling capacity to support model training and inference workloads.
  • Data centers enabling creative AI may benefit from integrated renewables and storage to handle variable, high-power loads while managing operating costs and emissions.
  • Procurement and capacity planning should account for GPU-heavy hardware and the critical minerals supply chain that underpins AI acceleration.
  • Engage creative-sector customers early to size capacity, service-level expectations, and latency/collocation needs tied to artistic workflows.
  • Visibility into how creative applications consume compute can inform demand-response, load-shaping and contractual arrangements with grid operators.
