Key points
  • Rapid expansion of data center capacity is increasing electricity demand on local and regional grids.
  • Grid infrastructure and transmission/interconnection processes are coming under strain.
  • Workforce shortages are emerging across planning, construction, and operations roles.
  • Utilities, EPCs, and data center developers need closer coordination to manage load growth.
  • Potential consequences include delayed builds, extended interconnection timelines, and heightened reliability risk.
So what
  • Prioritize near-term capacity assessments and scenario planning for concentrated load additions.
  • Streamline interconnection and permitting workflows to reduce project delays.
  • Accelerate recruitment, training, and retention programs for grid and data-center-skilled trades.
  • Enhance coordination between utilities, developers, and EPCs to align build schedules and grid upgrades.
  • Consider short-term mitigations (temporary capacity, staged builds) while planning permanent upgrades.
