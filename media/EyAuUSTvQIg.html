The Hidden Cost of AI: How Much Power and Water Do Data Centres Really Consume

Based on the title metadata, the video examines the often-overlooked energy and water footprint of data centres that run AI workloads. It frames these resource demands as a "hidden cost" with implications for power supply, cooling water use, and infrastructure planning.

Watch on YouTube
Key points
  • Focus on power consumption of AI data centres (training/inference workloads implied)
  • Addresses water use linked to cooling and thermal management
  • Positions energy and water as "hidden" operational costs of AI
  • Implications for utilities, grid capacity, and site planning
  • Suggests need for better measurement or transparency on resource use
So what
  • If data-centre AI workloads drive substantial energy demand, grid planners and utilities must factor increased capacity, peak management and interconnection needs into short- and long-term planning.
  • Cooling-related water consumption raises site-selection and water-resource considerations for data-centre developers and EPC firms—water sourcing and sustainability should be evaluated alongside power.
  • Owners and operators should quantify and report power and water use to inform PPAs, renewable integration, and resilience planning; this informs opportunities for on-site generation and demand-side efficiency.
  • EPC, high-voltage and infrastructure teams should consider design choices (redundancy, efficient cooling systems, alternative cooling methods) that reduce both electrical load and water dependency.
