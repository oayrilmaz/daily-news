Audyt w erze AI: praktyczne zastosowania i realne korzyści | Katarzyna Chojniak, Alicja Barska

Based on the available description… AI is already a routine part of audit work at KPMG, helping audit teams automate repetitive tasks, analyse large datasets, and make better-informed decisions. Katarzyna Chojniak and Alicja Barska outline practical AI tools (like AI Transaction Scoring Tool and Clara AI), platform capabilities (KPMG Clara and Clara for Clients), the shift from sampling to full-population analysis, and discuss implementation challenges, data security/regulatory issues, and future directions.

Watch on YouTube
Key points
  • AI supports repetitive tasks and analysis of large data volumes to improve audit decision-making.
  • AI Transaction Scoring Tool enables analysis of full populations instead of sample-based testing.
  • Clara AI provides conversational assistance and specialised agents; KPMG Clara manages audits in real time and Clara for Clients modernises client communication.
  • Discussion covers how technologies change auditors’ roles, required competencies, and challenges in adoption.
  • Key concerns highlighted: data security, regulatory environment, and potential AI-related risks in audit.
So what
  • Expect more comprehensive audit coverage (full-population analysis) that can increase assurance and surface more issues than traditional sampling.
  • Prioritise vendors and audit teams with strong data security and regulatory-compliance practices when sharing operational or financial datasets.
  • Adopt platforms that support real-time audit management and client communication to speed reporting cycles and improve transparency.
  • Evaluate auditor teams for AI and data-analytics competencies to ensure effective use of advanced audit tools.
  • Require clear risk-mitigation and governance measures around AI use in audits to address potential threats and regulatory scrutiny.
