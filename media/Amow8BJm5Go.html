So what
  • Data centers represent concentrated, continuous electrical loads — planners must account for capacity, redundancy, and power quality.
  • Power and cooling infrastructure design, and coordination with utilities/EPCs, are critical to uptime and service-level obligations.
  • Opportunities exist to integrate renewables, on-site storage, and demand-response to manage peak power and emissions.
  • Grid operators and developers should treat data centers as strategic customers requiring advance coordination on interconnection and feeder capacity.
