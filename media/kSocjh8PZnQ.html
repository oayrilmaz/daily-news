Key points
  • Global trend: 'luxury fatigue' and a move from ownership to experiences (Gen Z prefers experiences).
  • Top 2% of clients account for roughly 40% of luxury market value.
  • Key themes: personalization, exclusive customer experiences, and new aspirational consumer categories.
  • ESG and responsible luxury gaining prominence (eco-leathers, certifications).
  • Poland: growing but dispersed market; luxury hospitality, tourism (Baltic, hotels, spas) and art scene noted; future shaped by AI and human craftsmanship.
So what
  • Growth in luxury hospitality and tourism (hotels, spas, events) in Poland may increase local peak electricity and resiliency needs—plan for higher capacity, backup power and flexible connections.
  • Rising use of AI and personalization in luxury services implies greater demand for data processing and low-latency IT — consider additional data centre capacity or edge compute solutions near premium venues.
  • ESG-driven demand for certified, low-carbon power offers opportunities for on-site renewables and storage projects targeted at luxury properties seeking sustainability credentials.
  • Fragmented but growing Polish luxury market suggests project-based, localized infrastructure opportunities (microgrids, standby generation) rather than only large centralized upgrades.
