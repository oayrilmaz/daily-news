Rynek dóbr luksusowych w obliczu transformacji | Andrzej Marczak, Tomasz Wiśniewski

Based on the available description, this KPMG podcast episode discusses current shifts in the global and Polish luxury goods market: growing emphasis on experiences over possessions, ESG and responsible-luxury practices, the rise of "affordable luxury," and the role of personalization and AI. Speakers highlight that Poland's luxury segment is still expanding despite global headwinds, with specific growth in luxury hospitality, tourism and art, and a future shaped by AI, craftsmanship and sustainability.

Watch on YouTube
Key points
  • Luxury demand shifting toward experiences, personalization and "affordable luxury."
  • ESG and responsible-luxury (e.g., eco-leathers, certifications) are rising priorities.
  • Poland’s luxury market is growing and geographically dispersed, with rises in hotels, spas and cultural sectors.
  • AI, craft and human-centered experiences are cited as key elements for the future of luxury.
  • A small share of the wealthiest customers drives a large portion of market value.
So what
  • Growing luxury tourism and premium hotels (e.g., Baltic coast, spas) can create localized, high-quality electricity demand—plan for reliable supply, resiliency and targeted substation/hardware upgrades in those areas.
  • Stronger ESG expectations among luxury brands and customers increase demand for renewable-backed power and verifiable energy certifications—consider green PPA options and renewable+storage offerings tailored to premium hospitality and retail clients.
  • Mention of AI as a future trend implies rising compute needs for personalization and immersive experiences—data center operators should anticipate higher capacity and prioritize low-carbon power and energy-efficiency measures to meet luxury-brand ESG preferences.
  • Personalized, experience-driven services often rely on low-latency connectivity and edge compute at venues—evaluate opportunities for edge data centers, microgrids or on-site storage to support critical guest services and digital experiences.
  • Market growth concentrated among high-value customers suggests demand hotspots rather than uniform load growth—use targeted capacity planning and demand-side solutions rather than broad, uniform network upgrades.
