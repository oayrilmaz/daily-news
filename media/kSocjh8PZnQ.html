Based on the available description, this KPMG ON AIR podcast episode features Andrzej Marczak and Tomasz Wiśniewski discussing current trends reshaping the luxury goods market — from experience-led consumption and "affordable luxury" to the rising importance of ESG. They compare Poland (a growing but fragmented market) to global dynamics and touch on areas like art, hotels, tourism and future drivers such as AI, craftsmanship and the human dimension.

Key points
  • Shift toward experience-driven luxury and personalization; Gen Z values experiences over ownership.
  • ESG and responsible-luxury practices gaining importance (e.g., eco-leathers, certifications).
  • ‘Affordable luxury’ and aspirational consumers expanding categories; top 2% of wealth accounts for ~40% of the market.
  • Polish market is growing despite global slowdowns but remains fragmented; art, hotels and luxury tourism are notable segments.
  • Future drivers include AI, craftsmanship and maintaining a human dimension in luxury offerings.
So what
  • Growing luxury demand and expanding hospitality/tourism in Poland imply increased need for reliable local digital infrastructure and low-latency services.
  • Emphasis on personalization and AI-driven experiences points to requirements for scalable compute and data-processing capacity in data centers.
  • Rising ESG expectations mean data centers should prioritize energy efficiency, low-carbon operations and sustainability certifications.
  • Regional/tourism-driven spikes (e.g., luxury hotels, coastal destinations) call for flexible capacity planning and resilience to handle seasonal load variations.
  • Support for digital art, exclusive experiences and secure client data necessitates high availability, strong security and robust backup/storage solutions.
