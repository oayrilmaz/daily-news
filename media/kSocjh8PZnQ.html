Based on the available description… This KPMG ON AIR podcast episode discusses how the luxury goods market is transforming, highlighting trends such as experience-focused consumption, ESG and responsible luxury, the rise of "affordable luxury," and the growing role of AI and craftsmanship. Key points
  • Global trends: "luxury fatigue," concentration of wealth (top 2% = 40% of market).
  • Shift toward experiences, personalization, and exclusive customer journeys.
  • ESG and responsible luxury: materials, certifications, and eco-leather discussed.
  • Poland-specific topics: growing yet dispersed market, luxury hotels, Baltic tourism, and emerging art scene.
  • Future drivers: AI, craftsmanship, and maintaining the human dimension in luxury offerings.
So what
  • ESG emphasis in luxury suggests increased demand for low-carbon/renewable energy and verifiable sustainability credentials in supporting infrastructure.
  • Growth of AI and personalization implies rising compute, storage, and low-latency requirements—plan capacity and scalability accordingly.
  • Luxury hospitality and tourism growth in Poland points to potential localized demand spikes—consider regional capacity and edge deployments.
  • High revenue concentration among few clients means services for luxury brands may require elevated reliability, security, and privacy guarantees.
  • A fragmented but growing Polish market indicates distributed, heterogeneous demand; flexibility and modular deployment models could be advantageous.
