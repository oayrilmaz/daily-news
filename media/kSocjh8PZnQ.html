Based on the available description, KPMG's podcast reviews how the luxury goods market is shifting — noting 'luxury fatigue', growth of experiential and personalized offerings, the rise of "affordable luxury," and stronger ESG/responsible‑luxury practices (e.g., eco‑leathers and certifications). Key points
  • Global trends: "luxury fatigue" and concentration of spending among the very richest (2% = 40% of market).
  • Demand shift toward experiences, personalization and "affordable luxury," with Gen Z prioritizing experiences over ownership.
  • ESG and responsible luxury gaining importance (eco‑leathers, certifications).
  • Luxury in hospitality, tourism and film as brand builders; Polish market growing but fragmented.
  • Future themes: AI integration, preservation of craftsmanship, and the human dimension in luxury offerings.
So what
  • Localized growth in luxury hotels, spas and premium retail can increase peak and reliability demands on local grids — plan for resilient supply and backup power at tourist hotspots.
  • Greater use of AI and personalization implies rising compute needs and potential demand for nearby data center capacity or edge compute to support low‑latency experiences.
  • ESG commitments by luxury operators create opportunities for on‑site renewables, energy storage and certification‑aligned energy procurement to support brand claims.
  • Premium art venues, galleries and luxury hospitality require stable power for climate control and sensitive equipment — prioritize UPS, microgrids or resilient infrastructure in projects serving these customers.
  • A fragmented but growing market means targeted infrastructure upgrades (distributed generation, EV charging, localized storage) may be more cost‑effective than broad, centralized investments.
