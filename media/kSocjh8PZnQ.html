Rynek dóbr luksusowych w obliczu transformacji | Andrzej Marczak, Tomasz Wiśniewski

Based on the available description, this KPMG podcast discusses how the luxury goods market is evolving—highlighting trends like “luxury fatigue,” growth in Poland despite global declines, the rise of affordable luxury, personalization and experiential offerings, ESG/Responsible Luxury, and emerging roles for AI and craftsmanship. Speakers note market concentration (2% of clients account for 40% of sales), shifts toward experiences (especially among Gen Z), and growth areas such as luxury tourism, hotels and the Polish art scene.

Watch on YouTube
Key points
  • Key trends: luxury fatigue, experiential focus, personalization, affordable-luxury segments, and ESG-driven product choices (e.g., eco-leathers, certifications).
  • Market structure: a small share of very wealthy clients (2%) drives a large portion (40%) of the market.
  • Poland: market is growing and fragmented, with opportunities in luxury tourism (coastal resorts, hotels, spas) and art.
  • Future drivers: AI-enabled services alongside continued emphasis on craftsmanship and human experience.
  • Podcast accompanies KPMG’s 2025 report “Luxury in the Midst of Change” for further detail.
So what
  • Concentrated demand from high-net-worth clients implies localized, high-reliability power needs for flagship stores, boutique hotels, galleries and private events—plan redundancy and premium power services accordingly.
  • Growth in luxury hotels, spas and tourism hubs can raise seasonal and localized grid/load requirements; consider capacity planning, microgrids, and targeted renewable + storage deployments in these areas.
  • ESG and certification trends increase demand for transparent, low-carbon energy and supply chains—opportunities for renewables, green tariffs, and traceability solutions tied to critical minerals and sustainable materials.
  • AI and personalization imply higher digital service and compute needs for luxury brands (CRM, AR/VR experiences); anticipate increased data center/edge compute capacity and robust connectivity for retail and experience venues.
  • Art and luxury goods storage/display require precise environmental controls and uninterrupted power—drive demand for high-quality UPS, HVAC-integrated energy solutions, and resilient backup power.
