Based on the available description, this KPMG ON AIR episode reviews trends reshaping the luxury goods market—shift to experience-first consumption, growth of "affordable luxury", rising ESG expectations, and personalization—while noting Poland's market growth despite global declines. Speakers also discuss luxury hospitality, the art market, aspirational consumers, and future drivers such as AI, craftsmanship and the human dimension.

Key points
  • Experience over ownership: Gen Z and many buyers prefer experiences; luxury fatigue is emerging.
  • Top-tier concentration: the wealthiest ~2% account for ~40% of the market.
  • ESG and responsible luxury are rising priorities (e.g., eco-leathers, certifications).
  • Poland: growing but fragmented luxury market with expanding aspirational segments and new product categories.
  • Luxury tourism, hotels, spas and the art market are notable growth areas; future themes include AI and bespoke craftsmanship.
So what
  • Rising luxury tourism and premium hospitality activity implies localized, high-quality power needs—plan for resilience (backup generation, storage, microgrids) and strict environmental controls.
  • Stronger ESG/ certification demand favors on-site renewables procurement and verified green power solutions for premium venues and brands.
  • Greater personalization and AI-driven services suggest higher data-processing needs—anticipate capacity growth and prioritize energy-efficient data-center deployments or edge/colocation close to luxury hubs.
  • Fragmented, local luxury growth and event-driven peaks argue for flexible demand management and distributed energy resources to mitigate grid stress.
  • ESG and technology shifts may affect supply chains (including materials/tech used in luxury goods); monitor critical-minerals exposure and recycling/ circularity options for risk mitigation.
