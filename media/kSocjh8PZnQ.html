Based on the available description, this KPMG podcast episode reviews current trends reshaping the luxury goods market—greater emphasis on experiences over ownership, ESG and responsible-luxury practices, “affordable luxury,” personalization, and Gen Z preferences. Hosts Andrzej Marczak and Tomasz Wiśniewski discuss Poland’s growing but fragmented luxury sector, luxury hospitality and tourism, the art market, and future drivers like AI, craftsmanship and human-centred experiences.

Key points
  • Global trends noted: "luxury fatigue," rise of experience-driven consumption, and "affordable luxury."
  • Concentration of spending: 2% of the wealthiest account for ~40% of the market.
  • Customer focus: personalization, exclusive experiences, and Gen Z preference for experiences over possessions.
  • ESG and responsible luxury highlighted (eco-leather, certifications); sustainability influences product and brand choices.
  • Poland-specific notes: market growing yet fragmented; luxury hotels, premium tourism and the contemporary art scene expanding; AI and craftsmanship named among future themes.
So what
  • Rising premium hospitality and tourism in Poland implies localized increases in reliable, high‑quality power demand for hotels, spas and flagship retail locations—plan for resilient supply and power quality measures.
  • Emphasis on AI and personalization in luxury suggests growing needs for edge computing, low‑latency connectivity and additional data‑center capacity to support real‑time services and analytics.
  • ESG focus among luxury brands increases the case for low‑carbon energy solutions (onsite renewables, storage, green procurement) and energy‑use transparency/certifications for corporate customers.
  • Pop‑up exhibitions, branded events and luxury retail experiences can drive variable, short‑term power peaks—design flexible distribution and temporary supply options (mobile generation, rapid deploy storage).
