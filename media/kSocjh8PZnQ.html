Key points
  • Global trends: "luxury fatigue", concentrated spending (top 2% = 40% of market) and growth of "affordable luxury".
  • Demand for personalized, exclusive customer experiences and Gen Z preference for experiences over possessions.
  • Growing importance of ESG and responsible luxury (e.g., eco-leathers, certifications).
  • Poland: market still growing despite global declines, but remains fragmented; opportunities in hotels, tourism, and art.
  • Future-facing themes: AI, craftsmanship, and maintaining a human dimension in luxury offerings.
So what
  • Experience- and AI-driven services mentioned as future directions imply planning for higher digital capacity and low-latency infrastructure to support immersive customer experiences.
  • ESG/responsible-luxury focus suggests prioritizing energy-efficient operations and renewable energy sources to meet client and certification expectations.
  • Personalization trends indicate growing need for secure, scalable data processing and privacy-compliant systems for customer analytics.
  • Growth in Poland’s luxury tourism and hotel segments points to demand for resilient, high-availability networking and localized edge services.
  • A fragmented but expanding market presents opportunities for targeted, localized data center deployments or partnerships to serve premium clientele.
