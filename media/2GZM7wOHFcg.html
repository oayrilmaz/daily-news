Follow the carbon, find the money: Turning sustainability into financial value

Based on the available description… Rich Goode, Sustainability Principal at PwC US, argues that sustainability resonates when framed in business terms by linking carbon reduction to financial impact. Translating emissions outcomes into cost savings and business value helps move sustainability from ambition to action, and leaders should understand carbon accounting to drive alignment, investment, and results.

Watch on YouTube
Key points
  • Link carbon reduction to financial impact to engage leadership.
  • A ton of carbon is abstract; financial value is concrete and persuasive.
  • Translate emissions outcomes into cost savings and business value.
  • Understand the fundamentals of carbon accounting.
  • Communicate sustainability in business terms to drive alignment and investment.
So what
  • Frame emissions reductions as measurable cost savings to justify grid/data center upgrades and efficiency projects.
  • Quantify carbon outcomes in financial terms to secure leadership buy-in and budget allocation.
  • Develop carbon accounting capabilities to track progress and demonstrate ROI.
  • Use business-language metrics (financial impact, cost avoidance, ROI) to align stakeholders and unlock investment.
